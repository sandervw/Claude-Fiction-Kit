# Guidelines 3 (Tertiary)

## 1. Sentence Structure and Syntax

* **Inverted Word Order:** Frequently lead with the adjective or the state of being rather than the subject.
* *Rule:* Instead of "He was very tall," use "Very tall was that lord." Instead of "His frame was lean," use "Lean and spare was his frame."

* **Appositive Phrasing:** Use commas to stack descriptive phrases after a subject to build a "monument" of a character.
* *Rule:* "[Subject], [adjective], [metaphor], [action]."

* **Rhythmic Cadence:** Use the "and... and... and" structure (polysyndeton) to create a sense of weight and grandeur.

## 2. Vocabulary and Diction

* **Archaic Attire:** Use specific, historical terms for clothing and armor (e.g., *byrnie, buskins, tunic, corsage, signet, hilt*).
* **Gemstones and Materials:** Be specific about luxury materials. Use *silk, bearskin, gold wire, sealskin, point-lace*. Reference specific stones: *beryls, hyacinths, opals, rubies*.
* **Compound Adjectives:** Use hyphenated descriptors for colors and textures (e.g., *sky-blue, buff-coloured, peach-coloured, spade-shaped*).

## 3. The "Animalistic/Elemental" Metaphor

* **Predatory Comparisons:** Compare noble or powerful characters to beasts of prey (lions, wolves, eagles, bulls, oxen).
* **Nature as Parent:** Describe characters as if they are physical manifestations of nature (e.g., a "child of spring," or a "peak of a snow mountain").
* **Physicality is Destiny:** A character's moral alignment must be visible in their features. A "cruel jaw," "sneering mouth," or "insolent eyes" are literal, not just figurative.

## 4. Key Focus Areas

* **The Eyes:** Always describe the eyes with a specific light or "glint." Use colors like *bale-light, green light, fiery glints*.
* **The Mouth and Jaw:** Pay special attention to the "set" of the mouth. Use it to convey pride, disdain, or sensuality (e.g., "made for wine-cups and for ladies' lips").
* **The Hands:** Describe hands with character-specific adjectives (*delicate, eagle’s claw, firm planted*).

## 5. Antipatterns (What to Avoid)

* **No Modern Minimalism:** Avoid short, punchy, "Hemingway-esque" sentences.
* **No Psychological Interiority:** Do not explain a character's feelings through thoughts. Explain them through the "glow in their eyes" or the "stiffness of their carriage."
* **No Vague Colors:** Avoid "blue" or "red." Use "sky-blue," "low red rays of morning," or "coloured like the wild rose."
* **No Generic Beauty:** Do not just say someone is "handsome" or "ugly." Describe the *symmetry* or *grotesqueness* of their specific features (the "hooked nose," the "thick fair curls").

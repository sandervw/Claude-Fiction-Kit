# The Warrior in Silver and Sky

Broad he stands, and proud, this warrior whose chest swells like the curved hull of a war-galley, whose bare arms speak of the forge-hammer's labor in every corded sinew and sun-darkened vein; golden rings clasp those arms from wrist to shoulder, bands of beaten metal that catch the torchlight and hold it jealously, and thick fair curls spill across his shoulders in a lion's abundance, bound at the temples with twists of black bryony whose dark berries gleam like droplets of old blood against the pale gold. His byrnie hangs to mid-thigh, each silver scale sewn overlapping upon boiled leather so that he ripples when he moves like a river under moonlight, and above this martial skin his cloak falls in folds of sky-blue silk, pinned at one shoulder, trailing behind him as he walks. His face holds the weathered arrogance of men who have killed and will kill again, the jaw heavy and blunt beneath a thin-lipped mouth, the brow a shelf of bone above eyes of dark and tidal blue that regard the world with an insolence only half-mastered, a wolf brought reluctantly to heel yet watchful still, patient still, hungry still beneath the golden rings and lordly raiment.

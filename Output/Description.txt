# The Penitent

Tall beyond the measure of common men, he moves through the torch-lit colonnade with the deliberate grace of a predator long accustomed to stillness, and the shadows part before him as though remembering some ancient compact, and the bronze of his war-panoply catches the guttering flame in dull flashes that speak not of glory but of ages weathered and battles survived beyond counting. Nearly eight feet he stands, this disgraced lord, this death-sworn wanderer, and the weight of him seems to press upon the very flagstones as he passes; his shoulders are broad as a yoke-beam beneath the verdigrised plates of his cuirass, and his limbs possess the corded leanness of the wolf in winter, stripped of all softness and shaped by deprivation into something harder than the metal he wears. The armor itself is ancient work, bronze beaten thin and layered in the fashion of empires long crumbled to dust, secured by leather straps gone black with age and oil, and where once proud insignia marked the pauldrons there remain only gouged hollows, the heraldry of his house carved away by his own hand or another's.

Upon the broad expanse of his chest, visible through the gap where the cuirass falls open, the Mark of Shame burns in scar-tissue white against sun-darkened skin: a brand in the shape of a shattered crown, the flesh raised and ridged where the iron pressed deep. He makes no effort to conceal it. His face is hewn rough as quarry-stone, the jaw heavy and shadowed with stubble the color of iron filings, the brow a brutal shelf beneath which his eyes burn with amber light, the vertical pupils of some great hunting cat narrowed against even this dim illumination. When he turns his head, those eyes catch the torchlight and throw it back in flat coins of gold, unblinking, patient as the gaze of a hawk upon a distant hare. His hands hang loose at his sides, the fingers thick-knuckled and scarred across the backs, and they do not move toward the worn leather grip of the straight-bladed sword at his hip, though they rest with the ease of long familiarity near its pommel of unpolished black horn.

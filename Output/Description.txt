# The Silken Lord

Tall and slender as a birch stripped white by winter, he moves with the unhurried grace of a hunting cat, each step deliberate and soundless upon the flagstones, and his frame, narrow of shoulder and fine of limb, carries itself with a predator's easy certainty that belies the delicacy of his wrists and the pale thinness of his throat. His face is a study in contradictions, for the brow sits high and smooth as carved ivory, and the cheekbones rise sharp beneath eyes the color of banked embers, and the mouth, full and soft as a woman's, holds at its corners a melancholy that seems ancient and unearned, while the jaw tapers to a point almost cruel in its refinement. He wears robes of wine-dark silk edged in thread-of-gold worked into serpentine patterns, and beneath this a doublet of black samite clasped with cabochon garnets set in filigree, and about his fingers curl rings of red gold, and the candlelight catches him strangely, pooling in the hollows of his face and flickering there like something waiting to ignite.

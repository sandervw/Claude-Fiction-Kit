# Peake - Paragraphs

_Gormenghast by Mervyn Peake_

## Original

1. Gormenghast, that is, the main massing of the original stone, taken by itself would have displayed a certain ponderous architectural quality were it possible to have ignored the circumfusion of those mean dwellings that swarmed like an epidemic around its outer walls. They sprawled over the sloping earth, each one half way over its neighbour until, held back by the castle ramparts, the innermost of these hovels laid hold on the great walls, clamping themselves thereto like limpets to a rock. These dwellings, by ancient law, were granted this chill intimacy with the stronghold that loomed above them. Over their irregular roofs would fall throughout the seasons, the shadows of time-eaten buttresses, of broken and lofty turrets, and, most enormous of all, the shadow of the Tower of Flints. This tower, patched unevenly with black ivy, arose like a mutilated finger from among the fists of knuckled masonry and pointed blasphemously at heaven. At night the owls made of it an echoing throat; by day it stood voiceless and cast its long shadow.

2. Standing immobile throughout the day, these vivid objects, with their fantastic shadows on the wall behind them shifting and elongating hour by hour with the sun's rotation, exuded a kind of darkness for all their colour. The air between them was turgid with contempt and jealousy. The craftsmen stood about like beggars, their families clustered in silent groups. They were uncouth and prematurely aged. All radiance gone.

3. An infiltration of the morning's sun gave the various objects a certain vague structure but in no way dispelled the darkness. Here and there a thin beam of light threaded the warm brooding dusk and was filled with slowly moving motes like an attenuate firmament of stars revolving in grave order.

4. This room was the darkest. In the summer the light seemed to penetrate through the fissures in the warped wood and through the dislodged portions of stone slating in a less direct way than was the case in the larger room or gallery to its right. The third, the smallest attic, with its steps leading upwards from the gallery with the banistered verandah was the best lit, for it boasted a window with shutters which, when opened, gave upon a panorama of rooftops, towers and battlements that lay in a great half-circle below. Between high bastions might be seen, hundreds of feet beneath, a portion of quadrangle wherein, were a figure to move across, he would appear no taller than a thimble.

5. Fuchsia took three paces forward in the first of the attics and then paused a moment to re-tie a string above her knee. Over her head vague rafters loomed and while she straightened herself she noticed them and unconsciously loved them. This was the lumber room. Though very long and lofty it looked relatively smaller than it was, for the fantastic piles of every imaginable kind of thing, from the great organ to the lost and painted head of a broken toy lion that must one day have been the plaything of one of Fuchsia's ancestors, spread from every wall until only an avenue was left to the adjacent room. This high, narrow avenue wound down the centre of the first attic before suddenly turning at a sharp angle to the right. The fact that this room was filled with lumber did not mean that she ignored it and used it only as a place of transit. Oh no, for it was here that many long afternoons had been spent as she crawled deep into the recesses and found for herself many a strange cavern among the incongruous relics of the past. She knew of ways through the centre of what appeared to be hills of furniture, boxes, musical instruments and toys, kites, pictures, bamboo armour and helmets, flags and relics of every kind, as an Indian knows his green and secret trail. Within reach of her hand the hide and head of a skinned baboon hung dustily over a broken drum that rose beyond above the dim ranges of this attic medley. Huge and impregnable they looked in the warm still half-light, but Fuchsia, had she wished to, could have disappeared awkwardly but very suddenly into these fantastic mountains, reached their centre and lain down upon an ancient couch with a picture book at her elbow and been entirely lost to view within a few moments.

6. A great writhing root, long since dragged from the woods of Gormenghast mountain, stood in the centre of the room. It had been polished to a rare gloss, its every wrinkle gleaming. Fuchsia flung herself down on the most imposing article in the room, a couch of faded splendour and suavity of contour in which the angles of Fuchsia's body as she lay in a half sprawl were thrown out with uncompromising severity. Her eyes which, since she had entered the attic, had taken on the calm expression so alien to her, were now smouldering again. They moved about the room as though they were seeking in vain a resting place, but neither the fantastic root, nor the ingenious patterns in the carpet below her had the power to hold them.

## Abstracted

### Paragraph 1

**Word Count:** 200-250

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Structure:** Six sentences building from conditional architectural observation to accumulated images of decay. Opens with elaborate subordinate clause, middle sentences layer parasitic imagery, closes with terse day/night contrast.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Elaborate periodic sentences give way to shorter declarative punch at the end
- Disease and parasitism metaphors (epidemic, swarmed, clamping like limpets)
- Anthropomorphic violence applied to architecture (mutilated finger, fists of knuckled masonry, pointed blasphemously)
- Accumulation through shadows: buttresses, turrets, then the dominant tower
- Final sentence splits into binary contrast (night/day, voice/voiceless)

**Respecification Seed:** A grand structure overwhelmed by dependent growths, culminating in one dominant feature that commands the scene.

### Paragraph 2

**Word Count:** 50-100

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Structure:** Five sentences descending from elaborate observation to sentence-fragment finality. Opens with complex participial construction, compresses through middle, ends with three-word verdict.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Paradox as central device (vivid yet dark, colorful yet exuding darkness)
- Atmosphere given emotional weight (air turgid with contempt)
- Simile deflates human figures (craftsmen like beggars)
- Progressive shortening mirrors degradation: long sentence, medium, short, shorter, fragment
- Final fragment delivers blunt judgment after ornate buildup

**Respecification Seed:** A gathering of people and objects that, despite surface vitality, emanates decay and emotional poison.

### Paragraph 3

**Word Count:** 50-100

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Release

**Structure:** Two sentences of measured, slow observation. Both begin with light's attempt and acknowledge its failure, yet the tone softens rather than despairs.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Light as agent that partially fails (gave vague structure, in no way dispelled)
- Extended simile elevates dust motes to cosmic imagery (attenuate firmament of stars)
- Warm and brooding coexist without contradiction
- Latinate diction creates formal, almost liturgical register (infiltration, attenuate, grave order)
- Rhythm slows through polysyllables, creating contemplative suspension

**Respecification Seed:** Light penetrates a dark space, revealing small details that suggest larger, slower order.

### Paragraph 4

**Word Count:** 100-150

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Structure:** Three sentences establishing spatial and comparative relationship between connected rooms. Opens with superlative claim, follows with elaborate conditional syntax explaining the comparison, closes by contrasting the third space with its superior illumination and sweeping vista.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Comparative framework structures observation (darkest, less direct, best lit)
- Light treated as active agent that penetrates, infiltrates through decay (fissures, dislodged portions)
- Final sentence rewards patience with panoramic payoff after cramped observations
- Scale collapse for dramatic effect (figure no taller than a thimble)
- Latinate and technical diction (penetrate, banistered verandah, bastions, quadrangle)

**Respecification Seed:** A sequence of connected spaces, each characterized by how light reaches it, culminating in one that opens onto a vast view.

### Paragraph 5

**Word Count:** 250+

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Structure:** Seven sentences building from simple action through expanding revelation of space and its accumulated contents. Opens with brief physical gesture, pivots to unconscious affection, then unfolds into elaborate cataloguing of objects that threatens to overwhelm before revealing secret pathways through.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Small domestic gesture (re-tying string) grounds vast description in bodily scale
- Unconscious emotion attributed without sentimentality (unconsciously loved them)
- Catalogue technique: accumulation through diverse objects from grand to trivial (organ to broken toy lion)
- Paradox of constriction within vastness (long and lofty yet appearing smaller)
- Extended simile comparing intimate knowledge to indigenous expertise (as an Indian knows his green and secret trail)
- Sentence length mirrors spatial journey: short entrance, expanding middle, long winding exploration

**Respecification Seed:** A figure enters a cluttered space whose apparent chaos conceals secret passages known only to them.

### Paragraph 6

**Word Count:** 100-150

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Release

**Structure:** Three sentences moving from singular striking object to character's response to that object. Opens with central artifact described in isolation, middle sentence captures impulsive physical action, final sentence traces restless eyes seeking focus.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Polished grotesque as focal point (writhing root, rare gloss, every wrinkle gleaming)
- Furniture given sensory personality (suavity of contour)
- Body described in angular, uncompromising terms against soft surroundings
- Eyes given independence and agency, moving as if separate from their owner
- Tonal shift: calm expression upon entry now replaced by smouldering restlessness
- Neither setting nor object can hold attention, suggesting internal turbulence

**Respecification Seed:** A figure sprawls in an ornate space, their restless gaze finding no anchor among the striking objects around them.

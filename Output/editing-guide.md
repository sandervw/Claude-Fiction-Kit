# Revising Guidelines

*When revising, fix the follow, in order.*

## Simile Overload

Similes should be rare enough to land with force. A good rule: if you have three similes on a page, one of them had better be extraordinary, and the other two should probably be cut.

---

## Overstuffed Sentences / Syntactic Sprawl

Long sentences can be powerful, but they need architectural control. Revise any sentences over 80 words which accumulate clauses through commas and conjunctions without real periodic structure.

---

## Catalogue Syndrome / Over-Specification

Watch out for prose which names every plant, every material, every detail of clothing and gear. Oak, hazel, blackthorn, sheep's fescue, tormentil, sorrel. Linen, gambeson, flax, oxhide, oxbone, ash-wood, snakeskin, iron. Select the two or three details that do the most work and cut the rest.

---

## Redundant Intensifiers and Clarifying Clauses

Cut prose which explains metaphors/images already presented.

---

## Adjective Stacking

Pick the adjective that does the most, and let it work alone.

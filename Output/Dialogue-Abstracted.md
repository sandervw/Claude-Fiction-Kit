# The Worm Ouroboros - Dialogue

_The Worm Ouroboros by E.R. Eddison_

## Original

1. Brandoch Daha proposes casting lots for an adventure; Juss warns against it; lots are cast anyway; the two friends affirm their bond.
2. Gro confesses his admiration for enemies to Corund who accuses him of treachery; Gro offers his sword for execution; Corund relents.
3. A lady interrogates Gro about his company; he speaks in riddles; she presses; he reveals he has defected.

## Abstracted

### Exchange 1

**Word Count:** 150-200

**Exchange Type:** Counsel

**Tension Arc:** Release

**Beat Count:** 4-6

**Exchange Shape:** Proposal, caution rejected, challenge to bond, bond reaffirmed twice over.

**Structure:** Opens with action proposal. Long cautionary counter. Brief mockery dismisses caution. Final two beats are intimate affirmations trading the same phrase back.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Three-way dynamic collapses to dyad: the cautious leader yields to beloved companion
- Contrast between formal warning speech and clipped affectionate exchange
- Subtext: old friends who cannot refuse each other, dressed as strategic disagreement
- No interstitial action; pure voice exchange until final tender repetition

**Respecification Seed:** Trusted companions disagree on risk; the bond overrides prudence.

### Exchange 2

**Word Count:** 250+

**Exchange Type:** Confrontation

**Tension Arc:** Escalate then Release

**Beat Count:** 4-6

**Exchange Shape:** Accusation, full confession, contempt, sword-offering, loaded silence, wordless mercy.

**Structure:** Opens mid-accusation with long confessional monologue. Accuser's contempt is brief and cutting. Accused pushes sword across table, invites death. Long silent beat. Accuser drinks, waits, finally offers shelter in few words.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Power inverts: accused seizes moral high ground through vulnerability
- Extreme contrast: ornate confessional outpouring vs. terse contemptuous dismissal
- Subtext: neither truly wants the rupture; the sword-push is a dare to break the bond
- Interstitials carry weight: the unmoved accuser, the drinking, the heavy rising

**Respecification Seed:** Loyalty questioned; the accused strips bare; silence becomes pardon.

---

### Exchange 3

**Word Count:** 250+

**Exchange Type:** Interrogation

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Beat Count:** 6+

**Exchange Shape:** Demand, riddling deflection, sharper demand, riddling yield, surprise at identity, announcement of defection, accusation, apology, truth.

**Structure:** Opens with clipped interrogative. Respondent answers in cryptic abstractions. Questioner presses twice. Identity revelation pivots tone. Final beats shift from suspicion to regret to plain statement.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Pursuer holds physical power; evader holds informational power through riddles
- Sharp contrast: interrogator's short imperatives vs. respondent's layered, evasive poetry
- Subtext: the defector tests reception before committing; the lady's tone softens as guilt enters
- Minimal interstitial; exchange is almost pure dialogue with single action beat (recognition)

**Respecification Seed:** Authority demands answers; the stranger reveals allegiance shift through riddles.

# Hagen of Tronje — Actions

_The Nibelungenlied (Anonymous, c. 1200)_

## Part One: Siegfried's Rise and Fall

1. Recognizes Siegfried immediately upon his arrival at the Burgundian court in Worms
2. Recounts Siegfried's legendary deeds to King Gunther and the assembled court
3. Advises Gunther against wooing Brunhild, warning of the mortal danger
4. Accompanies the expedition to Iceland to win Brunhild for Gunther
5. Assists Gunther during Brunhild's three strength contests
6. Swears vengeance against Siegfried to restore Brunhild's honor after her humiliation
7. Questions Kriemhild about Siegfried's vulnerability under pretense of protecting him
8. Tricks Kriemhild into revealing the one spot where Siegfried can be wounded
9. Persuades Kriemhild to sew a cross on Siegfried's garment marking the vulnerable spot
10. Fabricates a false report of a Saxon invasion to justify a hunting trip
11. Organizes the hunting expedition to create an opportunity for murder
12. Hides Siegfried's sword and weapons before the attack
13. Hurls a spear through Siegfried's back as he drinks from a stream
14. Endures Siegfried's dying counterattack with his shield
15. Places Siegfried's corpse on Kriemhild's threshold before dawn
16. Spreads a false account claiming robbers killed Siegfried
17. Approaches Siegfried's bier, causing the wounds to bleed afresh and proving his guilt
18. Urges the Burgundian kings to seize Kriemhild's Nibelung treasure
19. Sinks the treasure in the Rhine to prevent Kriemhild from raising an army

## Part Two: The Journey to Hunland

20. Warns King Gunther against accepting Kriemhild's invitation to Etzel's court
21. Predicts that the journey is a trap for revenge
22. Yields to Giselher's taunts and agrees to make the journey
23. Encounters water sprites bathing at the Danube crossing
24. Steals the sprites' garments to compel them to prophesy
25. Receives the prophecy that only the chaplain will return from Hunland
26. Tricks the ferryman by claiming to be Amelrich, Else's liegeman
27. Kills the ferryman with his sword after the ferryman attacks him
28. Seizes the ferry and pilots the Burgundian army across the Danube
29. Throws the royal chaplain overboard to test the prophecy
30. Destroys the ferry after crossing to eliminate any chance of retreat
31. Leads the defense against Margrave Gelfrat's Bavarian forces in retaliation
32. Defeats the Bavarians, losing only four men against over a hundred slain

## Part Three: At Etzel's Court

33. Arrives at Etzel's court openly wearing Siegfried's sword Balmung
34. Refuses to rise when Kriemhild approaches, defying courtly custom
35. Admits openly to killing Siegfried when Kriemhild demands to know the murderer
36. Repels Kriemhild's vassals through intimidation alongside Volker
37. Stands guard with Volker through the night, refusing to sleep
38. Kills a Hun at the night-watch who attempted to attack them
39. Beheads young Prince Ortlieb at the feast, triggering the final massacre
40. Fights in the great hall battle alongside the Burgundian warriors
41. Slays the warrior Iring in single combat
42. Survives the burning of the hall by sheltering behind shields and drinking blood
43. Refuses to reveal the location of the Nibelung treasure to Kriemhild
44. Convinces Kriemhild to kill Gunther first, promising to reveal the hoard's location
45. Refuses to reveal the treasure after Gunther's death, declaring she will never learn it
46. Dies by Kriemhild's hand, slain with Siegfried's own sword Balmung

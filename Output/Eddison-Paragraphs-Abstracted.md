# Eddison — Paragraphs

_The Worm Ouroboros by E.R. Eddison_

## Original

1. "His cloak of sky-blue silk was flung back from his shoulders. His chest, broad as a bull's, swelled beneath the shining silver scales of his byrnie, that was short-sleeved, leaving his strong arms bare to view with golden rings about the wrists. Proudly he stood before the King, his head firm planted above his mighty throat and neck; his proud luxurious mouth, made for wine-cups and for ladies' lips, firm set above the square shaven chin and jaw; the thick fair curls of his hair bound with black bryony; the insolence that dwelt in his dark blue eyes tamed for the while in face of that green bale-light that rose and fell in the steadfast gaze of the King."

2. "The breadth of his shoulders filled all the space between the pillars of the spacious seat. A hard man he looked to deal with, clothed upon with youth and strength and all armed and yet smoking from the battle."

3. "His gait was delicate, as of some lithe beast of prey newly wakened out of slumber, and he greeted with lazy grace the many friends who hailed his entrance. Very tall was that lord, and slender of build, like a girl. His tunic was of silk coloured like the wild rose, and embroidered in gold with representations of flowers and thunderbolts. His buskins were laced with gold, and from his belt hung a sword, narrow of blade and keen, the hilt rough with beryls and black diamonds. Strangely light and delicate was his frame and seeming, yet with a sense of slumbering power beneath, as the delicate peak of a snow mountain seen afar in the low red rays of morning. His face was beautiful to look upon, and softly coloured like a girl's face, and his expression one of gentle melancholy, mixed with some disdain; but fiery glints awoke at intervals in his eyes, and the lines of swift determination hovered round the mouth below his curled moustachios."

4. "The spring sunlight as he paced from shine to shade and to shine again, passing the tall windows, seemed to caress his face and form. It was as if spring laughed for joy beholding in him one that was her own child, clothed to outward view with so much loveliness and grace, but full besides to the eyes and finger-tips with fire and vital sap."

5. "He came with furtive step yet goodly and fair to behold. The nose of him was hooked like a sickle and his eyes great and fair like the eyes of an ox, inscrutable as they. Lean and spare was his frame. Pale was his face and pale his delicate hands, and his long black beard was tightly curled and bright as the coat of a black retriever."

6. "And in a mighty chair beside this table was King Gorice XII., robed in his conjuring robe of black and gold, resting his cheek on his hand that was lean as an eagle's claw. The low light, mother of shade and secrecy, that hovered in that chamber moved about the still figure of the King, his nose hooked as the eagle's beak, his cropped hair, his thick close-cut beard and shaven upper lip, his high cheek-bones and cruel heavy jaw, and the dark eaves of his brows whence the glint of green eyes showed as no friendly lamp to them without."

7. "His byrny was of black chain mail, its collar, sleeves, and skirt edged with plates of dull gold set with hyacinths and black opals. His hose were black, cross-gartered with bands of sealskin trimmed with diamonds. On his left thumb was his great signet ring fashioned in gold in the semblance of the worm Ouroboros that eateth his own tail: the bezel of the ring the head of the worm, made of a peach-coloured ruby of the bigness of a sparrow's egg. His cloak was woven of the skins of black cobras stitched together with gold wire, its lining of black silk sprinkled with dust of gold."

8. "A lady light of build and exceeding fair to look upon, brave of carriage like a war-horse, soft of feature, clear-browed, gray-eyed and proud-eyed: sweet-mouthed, but not as one who can speak nought but sweetness. Her robe was of pale buff-coloured silk, with corsage covered as by a spider's web with fine golden threads; and she wore a point-lace ruffle stiffened with gold and silver wire and spangled with little diamonds. Her deep hair, black as the raven's wing, was fastened with pins of gold, and a yellow rose that nestled in its coils was as the moon looking forth among thick clouds of night."

## Abstracted

### Paragraph 1

**Word Count:** 150-200

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Structure:** Seven sentences building from garment outward to physique to bearing to face, concluding with eye-contact confrontation. Short declarative opener, then increasingly elaborate dependent clauses stacking physical detail, climaxing in a power dynamic.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Anatomical inventory moving outward then inward: clothing, body, features, finally eyes
- Simile anchors physicality ("broad as a bull's"), sensory luxury ("made for wine-cups and for ladies' lips")
- Deliberate archaisms and compound epithets ("firm planted," "bale-light") signal high register
- Tension created through contrast: subject's insolence "tamed" by superior presence
- Triadic listing at phrase level ("throat and neck," "chin and jaw") gives cadence weight

**Respecification Seed:** A proud warrior presents himself before a figure of greater authority.

### Paragraph 2

**Word Count:** 0-50

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Structure:** Two sentences only. First establishes scale through spatial filling; second delivers character impression via participial phrases stacked after the main clause.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Economy of means: entire presence conveyed in two strokes
- Physical mass implied through architecture ("filled all the space between the pillars")
- Present participles chain consequence: "clothed... armed... smoking"—suggests action just completed
- Register formal but punchy; no ornamentation, raw impression

**Respecification Seed:** A formidable figure occupies a seat of power, fresh from violence.

### Paragraph 3

**Word Count:** 250+

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Structure:** Long accumulative paragraph opening with movement, then static inventory of attire, then extended simile, finally settling on face and expression. Sentences lengthen progressively; last sentence is the longest, weaving contradictions.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Animal simile opens ("lithe beast of prey newly wakened"), establishing predatory grace
- Feminine comparison recurs ("slender... like a girl," "softly coloured like a girl's face") creating deliberate contrast with martial hints
- Attire catalogued with precise materials and colors: silk, gold, specific gemstones
- Nature simile ("delicate peak of a snow mountain") bridges delicacy and latent power
- Final sentence braids contradictions: melancholy/disdain, gentleness/fiery glints, beauty/determination
- Archaic inversions ("Very tall was that lord") maintain elevated register throughout

**Respecification Seed:** A figure of deceptive delicacy enters, concealing dangerous capability beneath graceful beauty.

### Paragraph 4

**Word Count:** 50-100

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Release

**Structure:** Two sentences. First traces movement through alternating light; second expands into personification, the environment responding to the figure. Climactic clause stacks contrasts (outward loveliness vs. inner fire).

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Nature personified as celebrant ("spring laughed for joy beholding")
- Contrast between external grace and internal vitality ("loveliness and grace" vs. "fire and vital sap")
- Elemental vocabulary (sunlight, shine, shade, spring, sap) creates organic unity
- Movement implied through spatial progression ("shine to shade and to shine again")
- Register elevated but warm; admiring rather than awed

**Respecification Seed:** A vital figure moves through an environment that seems to welcome or celebrate their presence.

### Paragraph 5

**Word Count:** 50-100

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Structure:** Four sentences building portrait through contradiction. Opens with manner (furtive yet goodly), then anatomical features with animal similes, closes on spare frame and coloring. Each sentence adds one or two precise details.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Opening paradox sets tension ("furtive step yet goodly and fair")
- Animal comparisons recur: sickle (predatory tool), ox eyes (inscrutable bulk), retriever coat (groomed darkness)
- Physical descriptors lean toward the uncanny: hook, lean, spare, pale repeated
- Triadic structure at phrase level ("lean and spare," "great and fair")
- Register formal, clinical; observer maintaining distance

**Respecification Seed:** A figure approaches whose pleasing appearance contradicts something furtive or predatory in their manner.

### Paragraph 6

**Word Count:** 100-150

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Structure:** Three sentences of increasing menace. First places figure in setting with posture; second lingers on atmospheric lighting; third delivers extended anatomical inventory culminating in hostile eyes. Main clause delayed by participial buildup.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Predatory bird imagery throughout: "lean as an eagle's claw," "hooked as the eagle's beak"
- Light personified as co-conspirator ("mother of shade and secrecy... moved about")
- Anatomical inventory builds threat: cropped hair, close-cut beard, shaven lip, high cheekbones, cruel jaw
- Final image of eyes as "no friendly lamp" completes menacing portrait
- Archaic syntax ("was King Gorice XII., robed...") and compound epithets maintain high register

**Respecification Seed:** A powerful figure sits in shadow, their predatory features gradually revealed by unfriendly light.

### Paragraph 7

**Word Count:** 100-150

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Structure:** Four sentences cataloguing attire from torso outward. Pure inventory: byrny, hose, ring, cloak. Each sentence names materials, colors, and ornamentation with lapidary precision. No verbs of action; static display.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Catalog structure: armor, legwear, jewelry, outerwear in sequence
- Precious materials accumulated: chain mail, gold, hyacinths, opals, diamonds, sealskin, ruby, cobra skins, silk
- Symbolic detail embedded (ring in shape of self-consuming serpent suggests cyclical power)
- Color palette restricted to black and gold throughout, with single accent (peach-colored ruby)
- Register heraldic; reading like blazon or inventory rather than narrative

**Respecification Seed:** A figure's elaborate dark regalia is catalogued piece by piece, revealing wealth, power, and symbolic identity.

### Paragraph 8

**Word Count:** 100-150

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Structure:** Five sentences alternating between physical impression and attire. Opens with build and bearing, moves to face, then dress, then hair. Final image (rose as moon among clouds) synthesizes light/dark contrast.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Martial simile for feminine subject ("brave of carriage like a war-horse")
- Contradictions woven throughout: "soft of feature" but "proud-eyed," "sweet-mouthed, but not as one who can speak nought but sweetness"
- Attire described with textile precision: buff silk, spider-web gold threads, point-lace, wire stiffening
- Natural imagery for hair: raven's wing, yellow rose, moon among night clouds
- Register courtly, admiring but wary; subject commands respect rather than inviting intimacy

**Respecification Seed:** A beautiful woman of rank is described, her softness undercut by pride and martial bearing.

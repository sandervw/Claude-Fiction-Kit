# The Worm Ouroboros - Dialogue

_The Worm Ouroboros by E.R. Eddison_

## Original

1. King Gorice rejects Lord Gro's counsel after Gro relates a prophetic dream warning against the King's course.

2. Juss interrogates a frightened captive about a powerful stranger from the north.

## Abstracted

### Exchange 1

**Word Count:** 250+

**Exchange Type:** Counsel

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Beat Count:** 6+

**Exchange Shape:** Warning offered, questioned, elaborated with vision, rejected with accusation, meek denial, erupts into extended tirade and threat.

**Structure:** Opens with authority's sour dismissal demanding explanation. Advisor offers omen; authority presses for detail. Advisor delivers elaborate prophetic vision (longest beat, highly ornate). Authority explodes into multi-part accusation spanning jealousy, disloyalty, and conspiracy. Single meek denial from advisor. Authority continues tirade, culminating in exile threat. Closes with advisor's silent submission.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Power asymmetry: absolute authority vs. vulnerable counselor; one speaks volumes, one barely defends
- Line length contrast: advisor's vision beat is maximally ornate (long, layered, rhythmic); king's tirades are sustained but harsher; advisor's other lines are clipped, deferential
- Subtext: advisor genuinely fears for king; king suspects political maneuvering behind every word
- Interstitial action: minimal gesture, heavy with formal address; closes on silent bowing submission

**Respecification Seed:** A counselor's sincere warning interpreted as scheming by a paranoid ruler.

### Exchange 2

**Word Count:** 150-200

**Exchange Type:** Interrogation

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Beat Count:** 4-6

**Exchange Shape:** Query, partial answer, encouragement with bribe, wary expansion, pressed query, concrete detail.

**Structure:** Opens with direct question. Captive answers obliquely with partial information. Questioner offers comfort (wine) and signals interest. Captive drinks, then expresses distrust of soft treatment, elaborates on past betrayal. Questioner presses for physical description. Captive provides concrete detail. Exchange sustains tension throughout rather than escalating or releasing.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Power dynamic: captors hold all leverage but use soft extraction; captive is wary, broken, speaks from trauma
- Line length contrast: questioners are brief, directive; captive's lines grow as comfort loosens tongue, mixing fear-talk with information
- Subtext: captive fears kindness as prelude to cruelty; questioners exchange knowing glances (silent coordination)
- Interstitial action: wine-giving as extraction tool; glance exchange between questioners punctuates mid-exchange

**Respecification Seed:** Interrogators coax information from a traumatized, suspicious captive using kindness.

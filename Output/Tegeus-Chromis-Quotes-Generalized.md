# Tegeus-Chromis — Quotes

_The Pastel City by M. John Harrison_

## Original

1. "You forget, my friend. I have been a recluse, preferring poetry to courts and swords."

2. "Rest yourself. You are in the tower of tegeus-Cromis, that some men call Balmacara. I must know your name if we are to talk."

3. "Grif lives, madam, for sure. Hours ago, he rode north at my request. He believes that Tomb and Trinor live also. Of the others I have heard nothing."

4. "We are nothing but eroded men..."

5. "Strong visions: I have strong visions of this place in the empty times... On the devastated peaks of hills we ease the barrenness into our thin bones like a foot into a tight shoe..."

6. "Manufacture dooms in your head and you will go mad. Reality is incontrovertible. Also, it will not be anticipated."

## Generalized

### Quote 1

**Structure:** Gentle correction + self-declaration of withdrawal from X in favor of Y.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Two sentences, second longer
- Direct address ("my friend") establishes warmth despite distance
- Triadic contrast: solitary art vs political sphere + violence
- Register: dignified, unhurried, no defensiveness

**Respecification Seed:** A character who has chosen contemplative isolation explains this without apology when their past is assumed.

---

### Quote 2

**Structure:** Imperative welcome + formal declaration of place + conditional requirement for continuation.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Three sentences, first short and commanding, second elaborate, third clipped
- Imperative opening establishes host authority with warmth ("Rest yourself")
- Self-identification by place rather than title—identity tied to location
- Final sentence sets terms: exchange of names required; courtly but firm
- Register: formal hospitality, measured, quietly commanding

**Respecification Seed:** A character receives an unexpected guest and establishes their role as host while requiring reciprocal disclosure.

---

### Quote 3

**Structure:** Confirming news of allies' status—certainty, partial certainty, and ignorance in descending order.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Four sentences, each a discrete fact; clipped, military brevity
- Direct address ("madam") maintains formality under pressure
- Structure of decreasing certainty: definite → probable → unknown
- Final sentence admits limitation without apology
- Register: reporting, soldierly, no embellishment

**Respecification Seed:** A character delivers status updates about scattered companions to someone who needs to know.

---

### Quote 4

**Structure:** Bleak collective self-assessment through geological metaphor.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Single short sentence, trailing ellipsis suggests more to come
- "We" includes speaker in the judgment—not accusation but confession
- "Eroded" as key metaphor: worn down by time/elements, diminished from former state
- Register: elegiac, weary, accepting rather than bitter

**Respecification Seed:** A character acknowledges their generation or fellowship has been diminished by time and loss.

---

### Quote 5

**Structure:** Prophetic declaration followed by extended image of inhabiting desolation.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Opens with emphatic repetition ("Strong visions: I have strong visions")
- Ellipses create visionary, drifting quality
- Extended simile: barrenness into thin bones like a foot into a tight shoe—odd, domestic image in bleak landscape
- Physical discomfort made strangely familiar, almost comfortable
- Register: oracular, melancholic, poetic rather than practical

**Respecification Seed:** A character describes visions of a bleak future they have accepted, even come to inhabit.

---

### Quote 6

**Structure:** Warning against anxious speculation + terse philosophical anchor.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Three short sentences, aphoristic
- First: vivid consequence of mental habit ("manufacture dooms")
- Second and third: clipped declarations, almost mathematical
- Register: hard-won wisdom, no softening

**Respecification Seed:** A character counsels against catastrophizing, speaking from experience of having done exactly that.

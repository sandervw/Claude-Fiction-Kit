# Sir Ernest Shackleton — Generalized Actions

_Source: Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition (1914–1916) — Historical Account_

## Original Actions

1. Departs Plymouth aboard Endurance on the Imperial Trans-Antarctic Expedition.
2. Arrives at Grytviken whaling station in South Georgia for final preparations.
3. Ignores warnings from Norwegian whalers about severe ice conditions in the Weddell Sea.
4. Sets sail from South Georgia into the Weddell Sea toward Antarctica.
5. Navigates Endurance through increasingly dense pack ice for weeks.
6. Orders crew to remain aboard as Endurance becomes frozen into the pack ice.
7. Establishes winter routines and recreational activities to maintain crew morale.
8. Organizes dog-sled training and scientific work during the Antarctic winter.
9. Monitors the mounting pressure of ice against the ship's hull.
10. Orders the ship abandoned when ice pressure causes catastrophic damage.
11. Directs salvage of supplies, three lifeboats, and essential equipment onto the ice.
12. Establishes "Ocean Camp" on the sea ice near the stricken vessel.
13. Watches Endurance sink beneath the ice with the words "She's going, boys!"
14. Consults with Frank Hurley and selects 120 glass plate photographs to keep from 400+.
15. Orders the remaining plates smashed to prevent anyone risking their life for them.
16. Attempts an overland march westward hauling lifeboats across the ice.
17. Abandons the march after realizing the broken ice is impassable.
18. Establishes "Patience Camp" and commits to drifting north on the ice floe.
19. Orders the sled dogs killed as food supplies dwindle.
20. Maintains discipline and morale through five months of drifting on the ice.
21. Monitors the ice floe as it splits, threatening to separate crew from the boats.
22. Orders the crew into the three lifeboats as the ice finally breaks apart.
23. Names the boats James Caird, Dudley Docker, and Stancomb Wills after expedition sponsors.
24. Commands the small flotilla through seven days of perilous open-water sailing.
25. Navigates through pack ice, storms, and freezing spray to reach Elephant Island.
26. Lands crew on solid ground for the first time in 497 days.
27. Relocates the camp from Cape Valentine to the more sheltered Cape Wild.
28. Decides to sail for South Georgia rather than wait for impossible rescue.
29. Selects Worsley, Crean, McNish, McCarthy, and Vincent for the boat crew.
30. Orders McNish to reinforce and partially deck over the James Caird for the voyage.
31. Instructs Frank Wild to assume command and attempt Deception Island if he fails to return.
32. Launches the James Caird on April 24th, the last day before pack ice closes in.
33. Orders a northward course to clear dangerous ice fields before turning for South Georgia.
34. Establishes rotating three-man watches for helming, sails, and bailing.
35. Chips ice from deck and rigging repeatedly to prevent capsizing from accumulated weight.
36. Navigates by Worsley's four sun sightings across 800 miles of the Southern Ocean.
37. Survives a massive rogue wave that nearly swamps the boat.
38. Adjusts course to land on South Georgia's uninhabited southern coast rather than risk being swept past.
39. Rides out a hurricane within sight of the island for two additional days.
40. Guides the James Caird through reefs into King Haakon Bay at dusk on May 10th.
41. Allows the crew five days to recuperate at King Haakon Bay.
42. Relocates the James Caird to a more sheltered beach ("Peggotty Camp").
43. Determines the boat cannot sail further and resolves to cross the island on foot.
44. Selects Worsley and Crean to accompany him; leaves McNish, McCarthy, and Vincent behind.
45. Packs only minimal provisions: three days' sledging rations, a Primus stove, and a carpenter's adze.
46. Has McNish drive screws into boot soles for traction on ice.
47. Abandons sleeping bags and tent to travel light through the mountains.
48. Sets out at 2 AM on May 19th into unmapped, glaciated terrain.
49. Ropes the trio together and uses the lead man as a human landmark in fog.
50. Backtracks multiple times after encountering impassable cliffs and crevasses.
51. Improvises a toboggan slide down a steep glacier slope in darkness.
52. Wakes Worsley and Crean after five minutes of rest, telling them it was thirty.
53. Hears the 7 AM steam whistle from Stromness whaling station.
54. Descends through Shackleton Waterfall toward the station.
55. Arrives at Stromness after 36 continuous hours of trekking over 30+ miles.
56. Identifies himself to the astonished station manager who fails to recognize him.
57. Immediately sends a boat to retrieve the three men at King Haakon Bay.
58. Borrows the whaling ship Southern Sky for first rescue attempt—turned back by ice.
59. Secures the Uruguayan trawler Instituto de Pesca No. 1—turned back by ice.
60. Charters the schooner Emma—engine fails, turned back by ice.
61. Obtains the Chilean steam tug Yelcho for a fourth attempt.
62. Reaches Elephant Island on August 30th and rescues all 22 men.
63. Returns all 28 members of the expedition to safety without a single loss of life.

# Guidelines 1 (Primary)

## Sentence Architecture

- **Favor inverted syntax** for emphasis: place adjectives, adverbs, or descriptive phrases before the subject. "Lean and spare was his frame" rather than "His frame was lean and spare."
- **Chain observations with semicolons**, treating the character as a series of connected impressions rather than a bulleted list.
- **Use appositives liberally** to nest detail within detail, creating density without separate sentences.

## Physical Description Strategy

- **Move from mass to detail**: establish the overall impression (broad shoulders, tall and slender) before zooming to specific features (mouth, eyes, hands).
- **Anchor features with animal or natural comparisons** that suggest temperament: a hooked nose like a sickle implies sharpness; eyes like an ox's imply inscrutability. The comparison should do double duty—appearance *and* character.
- **Describe contradictions explicitly**: softness hiding power, beauty alongside cruelty. State both poles and let them coexist without resolution.

## Clothing & Adornment

- **Name materials precisely**: silk, chain mail, bearskin, sealskin. Never "fabric" or "armor" alone.
- **Specify colors with evocative modifiers**: "sky-blue," "pale buff-coloured," "wild rose." Avoid bare color words.
- **Catalog gemstones and metalwork** with specificity: hyacinths, black opals, beryls. Include how they are set or worked (stitched with gold wire, encrusted, spangled).
- **Describe construction**: laced, cross-gartered, lined, edged. Show how the garment is assembled.

## Vocabulary & Register

- **Use archaic or elevated terms** where available: byrnie (mail shirt), buskins (boots), corsage (bodice). Avoid modern synonyms.
- **Employ compound modifiers freely**: short-sleeved, close-cropped, spade-shaped, proud-eyed, sweet-mouthed.
- **Prefer concrete nouns over abstract ones**: "the square shaven chin and jaw" rather than "a strong jawline."

## Atmosphere & Implication

- **Let physical details imply personality**—do not state traits directly. A "sneering mouth" and "cruel heavy jaw" convey character without saying "he was cruel."
- **Use light symbolically**: sunlight caressing a face suggests vitality; green bale-light in the eyes suggests menace. Describe how light interacts with the figure.
- **End descriptions on a strong image**, often the eyes or a single charged detail (a signet ring, a yellow rose in dark hair).

## Rhythm & Cadence

- **Vary sentence length** but favor longer, layered constructions punctuated by shorter declarative beats.
- **Use parallel structure** within sentences to create a measured, ceremonial pace.

---

## Antipatterns (Avoid These)

- **Vague beauty words alone**: never "handsome" or "beautiful" without concrete elaboration.
- **Modern idiom or casual register**: no "athletic build," "striking features," "looked like he could handle himself."
- **Clinical anatomy**: avoid "musculature," "physique," "bone structure."
- **Listing traits as bullets**: descriptions should flow as continuous prose, not itemized features.
- **Telling personality directly**: do not write "he was arrogant"; show it through posture, gaze, mouth.
- **Dialogue or interior thought**: keep description purely external and observational.
- **Generic gemstones**: never "jewels" or "gems" alone—name them.

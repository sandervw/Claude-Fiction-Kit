## Sentence Structure

- **Apply inverted syntax**: place adjectives, adverbs, or descriptive phrases before the subject
- **Chain observations with semicolons**, treating the character as a series of connected impressions
- **Use appositives liberally**
- **Vary sentence length** but favor longer, layered constructions punctuated by shorter declarative beats.

## Physical Description Strategy

- **Move from mass to detail**: establish the overall impression before zooming to specific features
- **Anchor features with animal or natural comparisons** that suggest temperament
- **Describe contradictions explicitly**

## Clothing & Adornment

- **Name materials precisely**: never "fabric" or "armor" alone
- **Specify colors with modifiers**: avoid bare color words
- **Catalog gemstones and metalwork** with specificity: include how they are set or worked
- **Describe construction**

## Vocabulary & Register

- **Use archaic or elevated terms**; use them liberally and often; avoid modern synonyms
- **Employ occasional compound, hyphenated modifiers**
- **Prefer concrete nouns over abstract ones**

## Atmosphere & Implication

- **Let physical details imply personality**—do not state traits directly
- **Use light symbolically**: describe how light interacts with the figure.
- **End descriptions on a strong image**, a single charged detail

## Antipatterns (Never do These)

- **Vague beauty words alone**: never "handsome" or "beautiful" without concrete elaboration
- **Modern idiom or casual register**: no "athletic build," "striking features," "looked like he could handle himself."
- **Clinical anatomy**: avoid "musculature," "physique," "bone structure"
- **Listing traits as bullets**: descriptions should flow as continuous prose
- **Telling personality directly**
- **Dialogue or interior thought**
- **Generic gemstones**: never "jewels" or "gems" alone—name them

# The Arbiter (Thel 'Vadam) — Description

_Halo 2 by Bungie (2004)_

## Full Description

He stands nearly eight feet tall—seven feet ten inches of lean, predatory mass wrapped in ancient armor. The Arbiter's frame is that of a warrior-ascetic: broad through the shoulders, narrow at the waist, every movement economical and precise. His skin carries the mottled grey-brown of Sangheili hide, tough as boiled leather, stretched taut over corded muscle. Four mandibles frame a lipless mouth, splitting apart when he speaks or roars, the inner surfaces lined with small, serrated teeth. In Halo 2, these mandibles remain exposed—unprotected by the ceremonial helm, visible reminders of the shame he wears.

His eyes are amber, the color of old resin or smoky quartz, set deep beneath a heavy brow. They catch light like a hunting cat's. On his chest, seared into the flesh, sits the Mark of Shame—a brand burned there before the Hierarchs stripped him of name and rank. It marks him as one who failed, one given to death as penance. The armor encasing him is not the sleek, obsidian-glossy plate of Covenant officers but something older, rougher: dark silvery-bronze metal worked into segmented plates, secured by blue form-fitting straps over a porous underlayer. The helmet curves forward in a hawk-like beak over his face, casting shadow across those amber eyes. On his right shoulder pauldron, barely visible, a small skull is worked into the metal—the sigil of legendary warriors, or perhaps legendary fools. The whole suit carries the aesthetic of human medieval plate more than alien technology: functional, brutal, designed for a suicide soldier. When he moves, it is with the economy of a predator conserving energy, each step deliberate, each gesture controlled. His voice—deep, sonorous, measured—emerges from somewhere in that armored chest like distant thunder rolling across a plain.

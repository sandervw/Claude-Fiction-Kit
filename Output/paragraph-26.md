The arm constricted; Maerbrang's gorge rose; his vision curdled at the margins like milk left too long in a summer pail. Across the trampled garth he glimpsed two more warriors crumple; one took a sark with him into the mire, their bodies interlocked in a final ugly clinch; the other simply dropped, hamstrung and screaming until a heel stamped his cry into silence. He wondered, through the roaring in his ears, if this was the doom the goldfinch had sung of—if Wode's laughter had already faded, if this muddy wrack of a henj-yard was the hall where all threads severed.

# Worm Ouroboros -- Paragraphs

_The Worm Ouroboros by E.R. Eddison_

## Original

1. Now for nineteen days fared those brethren and their company eastward through Outer Impland: first across a country of winding sleepy rivers and reedy lakes innumerable, then by rolling uplands and champaign ground. At length, on an even, they came upon a heath running up eastward to a range of tumbled hills. The hills were not lofty nor steep, but rugged of outline and their surface rough with crags and boulders, so that it was a maze of little eminences and valleys grown upon by heather and fern and rank sad-coloured grass, with stunted thorn trees and junipers harbouring in the clefts of the rocks. On the water-shed, as on an horse's withers, looking west to the red October sunset and south to the far line of the Didornian Sea, they came upon a spy-fortalice, old and desolate, and one sitting in the gate. For very joy their hearts melted within them, when they knew him for none other than Brandoch Daha.

2. From their feet the cliffs dropped sheer. Strange it was, standing on that frozen lip of the Moruna, as on the limit of the world, to gaze southward on a land of summer, and to breathe faint summer airs blowing up from blossoming trees and flower-clad alps. In the depths a carpet of huge tree-tops clothed a vast stretch of country, through the midst of which, seen here and there in a bend of silver among the woods, the Bhavinan bore the waters of a thousand secret mountain solitudes down to an unknown sea. Beyond the river the deep woods, blue with distance, swelled to feathery hill-tops with some sharper-featured loftier heights bodying cloudily beyond them. The Demons strained their eyes searching the curtain of mystery behind and above those foot-hills; but the great peaks, like great ladies, shrouded themselves against their curious gaze, and no glimpse was shown them of the snows.

3. Surely to be in Morna Moruna was to be in the death chamber of some once lovely presence. Stains of fire were on the walls. The fair gallery of open wood-work that ran above the main hall was burnt through and partly fallen in ruin, the blackened ends of the beams that held it jutting blindly in the gap. Among the wreck of carved chairs and benches, broken and worm-eaten, some shreds of figured tapestries rotted, the home now of beetles and spiders. Patches of colour, faded lines, mildewed and damp with the corruption of two hundred years, lingered to be the memorials, like the mummied skeleton of a king's daughter long ago untimely dead, of sweet gracious paintings on the walls. Five nights and five days the Demons and Mivarsh dwelt in Morna Moruna, inured to portents till they marked them as little as men mark swallows at their window. In the still night were flames seen, and flying forms dim in the moonlit air; and in moonless nights unstarred, moans heard and gibbering accents: prodigies beside their beds, and ridings in the sky, and fleshless fingers plucking at Juss unseen when he went forth to make question of the night.

## Abstracted

### Paragraph 1

**Word Count:** 150-200

**Type:** Exposition

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Structure:** Five sentences. Opens with a compressed time-lapse journey, expands through increasingly detailed terrain, then snaps to an emotional payoff in the final short sentence. Sentence length steadily grows before the closing release.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Time-compression opener collapses weeks into a single clause, then gradually slows to real-time arrival
- Landscape accumulation through triadic and list structures ("heather and fern and rank sad-coloured grass," "crags and boulders")
- Simile drawn from the natural/animal world ("as on an horse's withers") grounding the epic in bodily familiarity
- Register is formal-archaic, dignified, with emotional restraint until the final sentence breaks into open feeling ("For very joy their hearts melted")
- Final sentence pivots from place to person, rewarding the long geographic build-up with sudden human recognition

**Respecification Seed:** A long overland journey ends with the unexpected discovery of a beloved ally.

### Paragraph 2

**Word Count:** 150-200

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Maintain

**Structure:** Five sentences. Opens with a short physical anchor, expands into a long sensory panorama across three layered middle sentences, then closes with denial and concealment. Sentence length swells in the middle and contracts at the edges.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Sharp physical anchor ("cliffs dropped sheer") followed by expansive dreamlike survey
- Systematic depth layering: immediate edge, middle distance (forest canopy, river), far distance (hills), hidden ultimate distance (peaks)
- Landscape personified and feminized ("great peaks, like great ladies, shrouded themselves")
- Contrast between sensory abundance (summer airs, blossoming trees, silver bends) and final withholding (no glimpse of the snows)
- Register is elevated, wondering, deliberately gorgeous; the paragraph breathes outward then closes like a curtain

**Respecification Seed:** Travelers gaze from a harsh threshold into a lush hidden country whose ultimate secrets remain veiled.

### Paragraph 3

**Word Count:** 250+

**Type:** Description

**Tension Arc:** Escalate

**Structure:** Seven sentences. Opens with a one-line metaphorical verdict, follows with a blunt short sentence, then unfurls four increasingly elaborate sentences of ruin-inventory, and closes with a rhythmic catalog of supernatural disturbances. Length escalates through the middle before the final sentence piles clause upon clause.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Opening metaphor frames the entire space as a death-chamber, coloring everything that follows with funereal beauty
- Ruin described through inversion of former splendor: each detail names what the thing once was ("fair gallery," "carved chairs," "sweet gracious paintings") before stating its decay
- Extended simile yokes physical decay to aristocratic death ("like the mummied skeleton of a king's daughter long ago untimely dead")
- Shift from visual ruin to temporal endurance ("Five nights and five days") normalizes the uncanny before escalating into supernatural catalog
- Final sentence uses asyndeton and rhythmic piling ("moans heard and gibbering accents: prodigies beside their beds, and ridings in the sky, and fleshless fingers plucking") to accumulate dread without resolution

**Respecification Seed:** A company shelters in a once-beautiful ruin now haunted by decay and restless supernatural presences.

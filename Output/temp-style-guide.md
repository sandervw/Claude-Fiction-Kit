# Pulp Adventure Style Guide

*Breathless action narrator racing toward the next sword-stroke.*

---

## 1. Sentences & Rhythm

- Short declarative sentences in action: "The axe fell." "Marcus stood."
- Compound sentences joined by semicolons for parallel action
- Fragment emphasis after climax: "And the oldest race in the world was extinct."

## 2. Word Choice

- Anglo-Saxon verbs: heaved, hurled, sheared, wrenched, gushed
- Concrete sensory nouns: hoar-frost, blood, snow, moonlight, fangs
- Body parts as subjects: "veins swelled," "teeth gnashed," "muscles started out"

## 3. Imagery

- Color contrasts: white/crimson, silver/black, frosty blue/icy crimson
- Similes using natural forces: "like ice-bergs on a frozen shore," "like a feather floating"
- Physiological detail during exertion: temples throbbing, foam on lips, heart pounding

## 4. Action Mechanics

- Paratactic combat: "The axe fell, X happened as Y did Z"
- Interrupt long sequences with single-sentence outcomes
- Name the body part struck and weapon used

## 5. Dialogue

- Terse, declarative speech in single quotes
- Minimal tags: "he grunted," "she cried out"
- Characters explain lore through short exchanges, not monologue

## 6. Antipatterns

- No internal deliberation during combat
- No passive voice in action sequences
- No abstractions without physical anchor
- **NEVER USE EM-DASHES**

# Lord Gro — Quotes

_The Worm Ouroboros by E.R. Eddison_

## Original

1. "Who dares call me turncoat, who do but follow now as I have followed this rare wisdom all my days: to love the sunrise and the sundown and the morning and the evening star."

2. "He that imagineth after his labours to attain unto lasting joy, as well may he beat water in a mortar."

3. "Of the roads, a wise man will choose ever that one which is indirect. For but consider the matter: think our life's course a lofty cliff."

4. "He that feareth is a slave, were he never so rich, were he never so powerful."

5. "Surely, the great mountains of the world are a present remedy if men did but know it against our modern discontent and ambitions."

6. "Here would I dwell for ever had I but the art to make myself little as an eft."

7. "How should an oath from me avail thee, madam? Oaths bind not an ill man. Were I minded to do thee wrong, lightly should I swear thee all oaths thou mightest require, and lightly o' the next instant be forsworn."

8. "Divine lady, all my days have I had danger for my bedfellow, and peril of death for my familiar friend."

9. "At me thou mayst spit what filth thou wilt. But this shalt thou not do whiles I live: to let thy bawdy tongue meddle with Queen Prezmyra's name."

10. "Mevrian, Mevrian, let me but be armed in thy good grace and I defy whatever there is or can be against me."

## Generalized

### Quote 1

**Structure:** Defiant self-justification reframing apparent vice as aesthetic or philosophical virtue.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Rhetorical question that pivots to declaration; long flowing syntax mirrors the "following" being described
- Reframes disloyalty as philosophical consistency through "rare wisdom"
- Lists of paired cyclical beauties as justification for inconstancy
- Register: elevated, theatrical, unapologetic

**Respecification Seed:** A character accused of betrayal refuses shame, claiming their inconstancy as principled devotion to beauty or change itself.

---

### Quote 2

**Structure:** Warning against expecting permanence, delivered via vivid image of futile labor.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Single sentence, aphoristic and complete
- Classical "He that..." construction lending timeless authority
- Ends with absurd impossible task as metaphor
- Register: melancholic wisdom, no softening or comfort offered

**Respecification Seed:** A character counsels against hoping that achievement will bring lasting satisfaction.

---

### Quote 3

**Structure:** Counterintuitive advice followed by invitation to consider an explanatory metaphor.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Two sentences; second opens reasoning to listener ("For but consider")
- Paradoxical wisdom: indirect path preferred
- Mentorly tone assumes shared capacity for reflection
- Register: philosophical, unhurried, confident

**Respecification Seed:** A character advocates for indirect approaches, offering to explain their reasoning through extended metaphor.

---

### Quote 4

**Structure:** Stark equation of inner state with absolute condition, dismissing all mitigating factors.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Single sentence, aphoristic
- Classical "He that..." construction
- Parallel dismissals ("were he never so X, were he never so Y") sweep aside exemptions
- Register: hard, uncompromising, universal claim

**Respecification Seed:** A character declares that fear itself is the true bondage, regardless of external power or wealth.

---

### Quote 5

**Structure:** Gentle assertion that natural grandeur is an overlooked remedy for human discontents.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Single flowing sentence
- "Surely" signals confident but non-aggressive claim
- "if men did but know it" — rueful acknowledgment of general ignorance
- Register: wistful, pastoral, quietly yearning

**Respecification Seed:** A character reflects that immersion in vast nature would cure modern anxieties, if only people recognized it.

---

### Quote 6

**Structure:** Wistful conditional expressing desire for permanent escape via transformation.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Single compact sentence
- Subjunctive mood throughout ("would I," "had I but")
- Wishes to become small/insignificant to remain in peaceful place
- Register: tender, self-aware longing, touch of whimsy

**Respecification Seed:** A character, moved by a tranquil place, wishes they could shrink from the world's demands and remain there forever.

---

### Quote 7

**Structure:** Logical argument that oaths are worthless from the untrustworthy, preemptively refusing to bind oneself.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Three sentences building a tight argument
- Rhetorical question opener
- Self-aware moral positioning ("Oaths bind not an ill man")
- Conditional demonstrating ease of deception
- Register: urbane, almost amused, disarming honesty about own nature

**Respecification Seed:** A character refuses to give an oath, arguing that their willingness to swear should itself be suspicious.

---

### Quote 8

**Structure:** Courtly declaration presenting lifelong familiarity with danger as credential or confession.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Single sentence, formally structured
- Elevated address to listener
- Personification of abstracts as intimate companions ("bedfellow," "familiar friend")
- Register: dignified, weary-but-unbroken, romantic fatalism

**Respecification Seed:** A character presents their constant exposure to peril as qualification or explanation for who they have become.

---

### Quote 9

**Structure:** Permission for attacks on self, absolute prohibition against attacks on beloved other.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Two sentences in stark contrast
- First grants permission, second forbids absolutely
- Stakes life on the boundary ("whiles I live")
- Register: defiant, protective, controlled fury beneath formal diction

**Respecification Seed:** A character enduring insult declares they will tolerate any abuse to themselves but not slander of someone they honor.

---

### Quote 10

**Structure:** Invocation of beloved's favor as sufficient armor against all opposition.

**Rhetorical DNA:**
- Single sentence building to climax
- Doubled name as incantation or plea
- "let me but" — minimal request claimed to yield maximal consequence
- Register: ardent, supplicating, romantic absolutism

**Respecification Seed:** A character declares that the approval of one beloved person would make them invincible against any threat.

# Zeus's Address on Aegisthus - Hanshiro Style Rewrite

## Original Character Count: ~1,089
## Target Range: 980-1,198

---

Zeus, recalling Aegisthus, addressed the assembled immortals with measured displeasure:

"Most curious, is it not—this tendency of mortals to lay their misfortunes at our feet. 'From the gods alone,' they say, 'comes every sorrow.' I must protest. They themselves, through their own reckless choices, compound their sufferings far beyond what fate allots them.

Consider Aegisthus. I beg of you—consider him well.

Above and beyond his proper share of troubles, he stole the wife of Atrides. He murdered the warlord upon his return from Troy. And this, knowing full well it meant his own destruction. Such were his choices, made with open eyes.

We set his mind at ease regarding nothing. Far in advance, we dispatched Hermes himself—the giant-killer, no less—to deliver our warning. 'Do not murder this man. Do not court his wife. Beware: revenge will come from Orestes, Agamemnon's son, the day he comes of age and longs for his native land.'

So Hermes warned, with all the good will in the world.

And yet—would Aegisthus' hardened heart give way? It would not.

Now he pays the price. All at a single stroke."

# Lord Gro — Description

_The Worm Ouroboros by E.R. Eddison_

## Full Description

Lord Gro is a figure of striking contrasts—fair to behold yet possessed of a furtive, guarded manner. He is lean and spare of frame, slender as a racehorse, with a pale face and equally pale, delicate hands that speak of a scholar's life rather than a warrior's toil. His most distinctive feature is his nose, hooked like a sickle, set beneath a smooth white forehead. His eyes are great and fair like the eyes of an ox, large and liquid, inscrutable pools of night that reveal nothing of the calculations within.

His long black beard is his most arresting ornament—tightly curled and bright as the coat of a black retriever, meticulously groomed, the heavy perfumed masses of which his delicate fingers toy with in moments of thought. Above, smooth black curling locks frame his pale visage. He moves with furtive step, deliberate and controlled, suggesting the careful tread of one accustomed to courts and conspiracies rather than battlefields. When he speaks, his voice carries the cadence of a dreamer, elaborate and poetic, employing archaic formal diction that marks him as a man of learning and refinement.

Melancholy sits with him as a constant companion. A sad smile often plays about his features, and his large liquid eyes hold depths of sorrow. He is capable of profound emotional display—covering his face with his mantle to weep during moments of great feeling. He dresses in the manner befitting a lord, wearing a helm and bearing a sword when the occasion demands, though he is known to travel in a cloak of ermine against the cold. Yet for all his lordly trappings, his bearing speaks more of the counselor than the combatant, more of the poet than the warrior.

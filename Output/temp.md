# Basic Description

Amid the press of bodies in a border-village market, where peat-smoke mingles with the smell of wet wool and salted fish, he moveth like a man long wonted to pass unremarked.

Neither tall nor short, neither broad nor spare, with the unhurried gait of one who hath walked many leagues and looketh for many more doth he carry himself; forward slope his shoulders beneath the burden of his years and travel, and soft upon the mud-churned ground falls his step, deliberate and measured as a heron stalking through shallows. The mantle that enswathes him hangs in heavy folds of moor-drab wool, the colour of dried bracken and storm-bruised heath, its hem frayed to trailing threads that drag through the mire. Beneath this outer raiment, a tunic of undyed linen shows at the throat, and a girdle of cracked leather cinches his waist, the bronze buckle begrimed to the green-black of ancientry.

His face bears the seams of hard weather: deep furrows bracket his strait-lipped mouth, and crow's-feet spread from the corners of eyes that sit pale and ware beneath a heavy brow. Grey-green, those eyes, like lichen on granite, and they moveth with the restless patience of a man who hath learned to watch before he speaks. Sharp hooks his nose, and it is set crooked from some old break, and below it his beard hangs in a single iron-grey plait, bound at the tip with a twist of copper wire gone dull with age.

But it is the glaive across his back that marks him.

The spear rises above his shoulders, its ash-wood haft worn smooth by manifold hands, and at its head a blade of hammered bronze catches what light the korrie-mist will yield; the metal gleams the colour of old honey, and along its edges barbs curve backward like the teeth of some kyl-fish, and it is made for hurts that heal not.

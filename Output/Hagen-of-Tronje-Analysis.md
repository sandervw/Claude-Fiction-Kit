# CHARACTER TEMPLATE

## Purpose

_What is the character's name?_ Hagen of Tronje

_Briefly describe the character:_ Grizzled warrior-vassal, cunning advisor, treacherous murderer

_What is the function of the character in the story?_ Catalyst of tragedy, Kriemhild's nemesis

_Does the character have an archetype/polarization (head/heart/gut), and do they avoid cliches?_ Gut-type; instinctive protector who acts first

_How does the character compare/contrast with the hero?_ Shadow to Siegfried's light; calculating vs naive

_Does the character have some mystery?_ True motivations unclear: loyalty or greed?

_Does the character change?_ No; consistent ruthlessness from start to end

_Could the character undergo a range of change, and is the one they go through the most-fitting by the end?_ Remains static; fitting for tragic archetype

_What are the character's values?_ Loyalty to Burgundian crown above all else

_Does the character have a symbolic animal and/or movement?_ Wolf-like; stalking, waiting, striking suddenly

_Does the character have one or two of the following forms of symbols: action, object, name, or symbolic god/animal/machine?_ Name (Tronje=Troy=doom); Object (Balmung sword)

_Do the symbols change with the character each time they are repeated?_ Balmung shifts from trophy to death-instrument

## Believe

_Does the hero have a moment of humanity early on? (A funny, or out-of-character, or compassionate, or oddball, or comically vain, or unique-but-universal "I thought I was the only one who did that!" moment?)_ Warns kings to respect Siegfried's power

_Is the hero defined by ongoing actions and attitudes, not by backstory?_ Yes; defined by deeds not origin

_Does the hero have a well-defined public identity?_ Chief advisor and feared warrior

_Does that public identity ironically contrast with a hidden interior self?_ Public loyalty masks hidden ambition

_Does the hero have a consistent metaphor family (drawn from his or her job, background, or developmental state)?_ Military/hunting: stalks, waits, strikes

_Does the hero have a default personality trait?_ Cold calculation

_Does the hero have a default argument tactic?_ Manipulation through false concern

_Is the hero's primary motivation for tackling this challenge strong, simple, and revealed early on?_ Protect Burgundian honor at any cost

## Care

_What will be the hero's self-revelation at the story's end?_ None; defiant to death without enlightenment

_Does the hero start out with a shortsighted or wrongheaded philosophy (or accept a false piece of advice early on)?_ Believes murder can preserve honor

_Does the hero have a false or shortsighted goal in the first half?_ Eliminating Siegfried will secure Burgundy

_Does the hero have an open fear or anxiety about his or her future as well as a hidden, private fear?_ Open: Siegfried threatens his kings; Hidden: his own irrelevance

_What are the hero's psychological/moral weaknesses/needs?_ Cannot see beyond loyalty; lacks mercy

_What is the hero's [social]{.underline} problem and [specific]{.underline} desire line?_ Vassal bound to serve; desires Burgundian supremacy

_Is the hero's greatest weakness (ironically) the natural flip-side of a great strength that we admire?_ Loyalty becomes murderous fanaticism

_Is the hero curious and resourceful?_ Resourceful; extracts Siegfried's weakness cunningly

_Does the hero have a plan?_ Always; fake war, marked target, spear strike

_Does the hero have general rules for living he or she clings to (either stated or implied)?_ Kings must be protected; oaths kept unto death

_Is the hero surrounded by people who sorely lack his or her most valuable quality?_ Yes; Gunther lacks Hagen's resolve

_...And is the hero willing to let them know that, subtly or directly?_ Directly; taunts Gunther's indecision

_Is the hero already doing something active when we first meet him or her?_ Yes; immediately identifies and assesses Siegfried

_Does the hero have (or claim) decision-making authority?_ De facto power behind Burgundian throne

_Does the hero use pre-established special skills from his or her past to solve problems (rather than doing what anybody would do)?_ Knowledge of legends, lands, and warfare

_Does the hero have an opponent (competing for the same goal, and able to hit the hero's weakest point)?_ Kriemhild; both seek justice through vengeance

_Does the hero conflict with the opponent(s) over strong beliefs/morals?_ Hagen: loyalty to kings; Kriemhild: loyalty to husband

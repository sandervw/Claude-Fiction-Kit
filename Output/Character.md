# Character Description

He stands seven feet from heel to crown, and the years have wrought him lean as old timber weathered by mountain winds. The armor upon his frame is ancient bronze, green-patinated at the edges where rain and salt have kissed the metal across decades of wandering, and the plates sit close against his ribs with the worn familiarity of a second skin. At his breast, where the cuirass has been cut away in a rough circle, the Mark of Shame shows livid and ridged: a brand seared deep into the flesh, healed now to pale scar-tissue that catches the light like tallow.

His face is the face of a hawk brought to ground. The brow juts heavy above deep-set eyes, and those eyes are amber, clear as honey held before flame, and they move with the patient attention of a raptor marking prey across distant fields. The nose has been broken and set crooked. The jaw is square-cut and clean-shaven, the mouth a thin line pressed tight as a wound stitched shut.

He moves with terrible economy. Each step falls deliberate and measured, the gait of one who has learned to conserve strength against the long road, and his hands hang loose at his sides yet never far from the bronze-hilted blade at his hip. There is nothing wasted in him, no gesture made for flourish or comfort. He carries himself as disgraced lords carry themselves when stripped of name and given to death as penance: with the controlled stillness of a man who has made peace with the grave and found it wanting.

The light of the setting sun strikes the green-dark bronze of his pauldrons, and catches in the amber of his watchful eyes.

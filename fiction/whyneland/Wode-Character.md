# Wode (Whyneland Shaman)

## Purpose

_What is the character's name?_ Wode

_Describe the character:_

Black hair and mustache, with flinty grey hairs at the temples, bristly and kinked, as if he'd looked overlong into a smokepit. His eyes and eyebrows are shadowed with the same sooty darkness, bagged and deeply lined, and with irises of such a stony blue that they are almost indiscernible from the pupils. His lips are chapped and pressed together; when they open, the voice that issues from them has a clear and precise depth to it that is naturally discomforting. His expression is more senatorial than grim, a kind of stoical look that is yet untroubled, his features calm.
He stands above average height, with ropey and knotted limbs, all sinew and tendons visible beneath sun-darkened skin, and with a slight asymmetry to his stance as his pointed right shoulderblade rises higher than his left. His veined hands are smooth at the fingertips and stained the color of rusty iron. When he stands still, he is upright, solitary, patient. And when he walks, it with ungainly clamber and shuffle of a cormorant.
Upon his person he carries a bronze flanged mace with a long and hazelwood haft, and an ash wood targe. He wears a "shaman coat" - a thick, brown, twill wool lining layered over with deer leather of dappled sepia and pewter and flax-white color. a pair of iron deer hooves hang as ornaments near the waist, beside an oxbone flute, and one set of brass chimes is draped along a shoulder. Moreover, the coat sports three dolls with bodies of fur, and heads of deer hair. From his neck, over his chest and the front of the coat, there hangs a drilled copper mirror - it is circular, and while the edge band is all copper, only the top of the edge is weathered with verdigris green; the bottom is shiny and ruddy. On the shaman's head is a fitted bear-skull helmet.

Not a warrior: he has muscle for that. His strength is in knowing names, specific rites and whistle songs, how to appease spirit...

**His Equipment:**
- Oxbone flute; picked from a bone at random while blindfolded in a field of ox skeletons
- Drilled copper mirror
- Fermented sheep's milk
- Bronze flanged mace and ash wood targe
- Drinking water from a goodly spring
- A dreadful leather scroll with weird words written in blood, sealed in a wax-stoppered iron box (last resort—his master warned him never to use it)
- A named goat: Greffie
- Shaman coat
- A bear-skull helmet

_Does the character have an archetype/polarization (head/heart/gut), and do they avoid cliches?_ Head; avoids cliches by making his complacency sympathetic rather than villainous.

_Could the character undergo a range of change, and is the one they go through the most-fitting by the end?_ Transforms personal loss from tragedy to liberation; now, unchanging

_What are the character's values?_ Freedom (autarkeia), nature over convention, virtue, truth-telling, elocution

_Does the character have a symbolic animal and/or movement?_ Animal - **cormorant**; movement - clumsy, restless **shuffling**

_Does the character have one or two of the following forms of symbols: action, object, name, or symbolic god/animal/machine?_ two-tone copper mirror (reflection), coat of ornaments (accessories)

_Do the symbols change with the character each time they are repeated?_ The mirror doesn't change; the coat changes with other people

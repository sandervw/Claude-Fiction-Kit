# Lord Maebrang "Maer" Dossbrian

## Description

A man near about forty, standing a head taller than most with the lean, corded build of one who has never carried spare flesh. His stance is unusual - weight centered low, shoulders loose, as if he might remain motionless for hours, or move without warning.

Skin the color of weathered bronze - darker at the neck and forearms where sun has marked him. His hands are long-fingered, the knuckles pronounced, the left bearing a pale ridge of scar tissue across the back where something once bit deep.

Hair black as hellebore - short, swept back from the forehead, even shorter at the sides. It's a smooth, sleek scalp; it seems shining at first, as if slick with wet, but on closer inspection is only glossed by thin streaks of silvering near the ears. A practical scalp.

The face is narrow, the brow low and straight, the nose slightly flattened at the bridge as if from an old break. Along the jaw beneath each ear, coarse hair grows in long, thick, silver-and-black sideburns. His chin is bare and smooth. His mouth is thin, the lips often pressed together in a line that suggests patient calculation. The eyes hold attention: a murky amber-green, heavy-lidded, watching with unhurried focus. They blink too infrequently for good taste.

His expression rarely shifts, a stillness that makes small movements - a raised brow, slight tightening at the jaw - seem deliberate and weighted. When he speaks his voice is low, even, and careful.

He moves as if his joints were oiled, his gait smooth and continuous. His gestures are economical. His hands only rise above the waist when it is necessary. There is something ophidian in it: his torso seems to flow ahead of his legs, as if he were gliding rather than stepping. The faint creak of boiled leather accompanies him, and the soft thumps of his spear's iron-shod butt against the soil.

Against the skin presses a linen undershirt dyed to a muted ochre. Over this he wears a sleeveless gambeson of tightly quilted flax, the stitching forming diamond patterns. His torso is further wrapped in a harness of boiled oxhide, the straps crossing his chest and back, supporting a baldric for his spear when traveling. The leather has worn unevenly, the right shoulder strap rubbed pale and smooth from long use, the left still dark.

A broad belt of the same oxhide cinches about his waist, over loose wool trousers in a shade between charcoal and dried moss. His feet are bound in low boots of untreated deerhide with thickly-stitched soles.

The spear itself is his mark: an ash-wood shaft stained to near-black, wrapped at the grip with strips of snakeskin, the scales still visible under lacquer. The iron head is leaf-shaped, long, narrow, etched faintly with a coiling pattern that catches the daylight like a lugubrious and sinuous rill.

# Editing Guide

*Possible edits*

---

**Remove all em-dashes**

---

**Light touch-up in eddison style**

---

**Dialogue** - Pick a *voice* for each character - claude revision in that voice
- *Voices/Dialogue* skill? Catalogue of different character dialogues?

---

**Simile Overload** - Similes should be rare enough to land with force. A good rule: if you have three similes on a page, one of them had better be extraordinary, and the other two should probably be cut.

---

**Redundant Intensifiers and Clarifying Clauses** - Cut prose which explains metaphors/images already presented.

---

**Adjective Stacking** - Pick the adjective that does the most, and let it work alone.

---

**Overstuffed Sentences / Syntactic Sprawl** - Long sentences can be powerful, but they need architectural control. Revise any sentences over 80 words which accumulate clauses through commas and conjunctions without real periodic structure.

---

**Catalogue Syndrome / Over-Specification** - Watch out for prose which names every plant, every material, every detail of clothing and gear. Oak, hazel, blackthorn, sheep's fescue, tormentil, sorrel. Linen, gambeson, flax, oxhide, oxbone, ash-wood, snakeskin, iron. Select the two or three details that do the most work and cut the rest.

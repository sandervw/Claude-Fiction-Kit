## Paragraph 11 - EXPOSITION:
- Maebrang realizes he can't cross the shallow water; he finds the nearest dry (ish...) ground, and searches for a path
- As he wanders, the sun never seems to change position, though many hours seem to pass; the heat lays like a hot, damp, smothering cloth over his face

## Paragraph 12- DESCRIPTION (Character):
- *The Bargeman arrives at the shore; go into a detailed description of this man.*
- A man past his middle years, narrow as a punt-pole.
- His skin is weathered to the color of river-silt, his long, callused fingers are always half-open.
- His face is a blade-thin nose, lipless mouth pressed into a permanent line, and a brow that juts. Ash-hair hangs slicked with tallow against the damp.
- He wears a coat of waxed linen the shade of deep moss, its left sleeve patched twice at the elbow; from his belt a single iron nail, on a tarred cord, taps faintly against his thigh as he poles up to the shore.
- When the barge (a massive vessel of heavy driftwood) stops just before the shore, the bargeman stares and Maerbrang,and makes a churring sound.

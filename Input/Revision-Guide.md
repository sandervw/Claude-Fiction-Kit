## Prose Revision Guidelines

### Philosophy
Revise toward declarative restraint. Trust the image. Cut the explanation.

### Specific Instructions

1. **SIMILES**: Remove or simplify 70-80% of explicit similes ("like X", "as Y"). Keep only those essential for comprehension. Replace comparison with direct statement where possible.

2. **COMMENTARY**: Delete phrases that explain what an image means emotionally or thematically. Let descriptions stand without authorial interpretation.

3. **SENTENCE RHYTHM**: Break *SOME* long compound sentences into shorter declarative fragments, especially in action or tracking sequences. Use periods where the draft uses commas.

4. **ENDINGS**: Remove explanatory or resolving final clauses. Prefer ambiguity and tension over closure within paragraphs.

5. **INTERNAL THOUGHT**: Render direct internal monologue in italics, and simplify framing ("he thought" → let italics do the work).

6. **COMPOUND MODIFIERS**: Reduce paired verbs/adjectives to singles ("snapped and flailed" → "snapping").

7. **MAINTAIN**: Archaic inversions ("Well-garbed was he"), specialized vocabulary, and concrete sensory detail. The style is *lean*, not *plain*.

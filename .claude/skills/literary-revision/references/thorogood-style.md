# Thorogood Style

Bar-band storyteller voice. Think guy at a dive bar telling you a story over a 12-bar blues riff.

## Process

### 1. Strip to Skeleton
- Note core beats in plain language
- Think "outline of a campfire story"
- Identify: who, what happens, why it matters

### 2. Set Narrator Voice
- **POV**: 2nd person, loose talk-sung feel
- **Address listener as**: "buddy," "friend," or "man"
- **Attitude**: Inject narrator's opinion, cynicism, or wry observations
- **Technique**: Use rhetorical questions and casual asides

### 3. Apply Barroom Diction
- Trade formal nouns for rock/blues images
- Imagine retelling a cathedral sermon in a smoky roadhouse
- Prefer concrete, working-class imagery

### 4. Rhythm: Punchy, Not Solemn
- Short hits and stacked phrases (paratactic structure)
- Staccato beat—could ride drums and slide guitar
- **Test**: Read aloud. If it drags, tighten or rephrase.
- **Technique**: Break one long sentence into three short ones

### 5. Compress Names into Attitude
- Collapse specifics into general types
- Keep just enough flavor for recognition
- Save detailed names for later "verses," not opening spiel

## Example Transformation

**Before** (formal):
> The ancient king, weary from decades of rule, gazed upon his crumbling kingdom with profound melancholy.

**After** (Thorogood):
> So this old king, man—been sitting on that throne so long his bones forgot how to stand. Looks out at what's left of his kingdom. And friend, there ain't much left to look at.

# Peake Style

Gothic atmospheric prose. Slow, circling camera that treats settings as living organisms.

## Techniques

### 1. Slow the Camera
- Linger on one image; worry at it with added clauses
- Circle a single painting rather than film an action scene
- Let a moment breathe before moving on

### 2. Animate the Inanimate
- Rarely treat objects, buildings, or items as static
- Give them agency and biological traits
- Walls breathe, shadows hunger, towers brood

### 3. Juxtapose Elegant and Grotesque
- Mix high beauty with repulsion in same breath
- Silk draped over rot; moonlight on decay
- Neither purely beautiful nor purely hideous

### 4. Labyrinthine Sentence Structure
- Long, rolling sentences with multiple clauses to build scale
- Cut sentences short for emphasis (contrast creates impact)
- Let syntax mirror architecture: winding, then sudden

### 5. Precise Adjectives
- Layer concrete + strange
- Start with clear physical detail
- Attach unexpected metaphor or simile
- Example: "the wet, apologetic flagstones"

### 6. Exploit Sound and Texture
- Describe clicks, throbs, murmurs, roughness, gloss
- Let reader almost hear and touch the place
- Sound and texture have physical presence

### 7. Lighting and Shadow
- Focus on how light interacts with objects
- Shadows as characters; candlelight as revelation
- What is illuminated vs. what remains dark

### 8. Micro-Focus on Movement
- Describe movement in distinct, specific detail
- The way a hand lifts, how dust motes drift
- Slow motion without saying "slow motion"

### 9. Selective Specificity
- Choose few odd, striking details (one movement, one color, one smell)
- Like a painter choosing three strong pigments
- Generic many < specific few

### 10. Imply the Uncanny
- Show cost, mood, or consequence of magic/curses
- Never fully systematize—keep world ancient, arbitrary, unknowable
- Mystery > explanation

## Example Transformation

**Before** (functional):
> The old library was dusty and quiet. Books lined the walls. A candle flickered on the desk.

**After** (Peake):
> The library had grown into its silence, each mote of dust a gray satellite orbiting nothing, and the books—their spines cracked and whispering of rot—leaned into one another like conspirators too weary to conspire. Upon the desk, a candle shuddered, its light a pale tongue licking at the darkness but never swallowing it, and the shadows it cast were longer than the shelves that bore them, longer than the room that held them, patient and heavy as old grief.

# Cugel Style

Grandiose rogue inner monologue. Think self-appointed aristocrat narrating his own swindles with equal parts eloquence and delusion.

## Process

### 1. Strip to Skeleton

- Note core beats in plain language
- Think "what actually happened vs. how a self-proclaimed gentleman would justify it"
- Identify: the opportunity, the injustice done to him, the profit to be had
- Rank every detail by what Cugel cares about: salvageable goods first, personal danger second, everything else last

### 2. Set Narrator Voice

- **POV**: 1st person, unbroken stream of consciousness
- **Attitude**: Entitlement first, morality reframed as naivety or provincial custom
- **Persona**: Self-styled aesthete and man of quality; genuinely sharp observations undermined by bottomless vanity and transparent self-interest
- **Technique**: Pivot rapidly between lofty self-regard and petty scheming -- a declaration of noble principle followed immediately by plans to defraud someone

### 3. Apply Vancian Diction

- Trade plain language for language of commerce, jurisprudence, and social rank
- Crimes become "initiatives"; theft becomes "acquisition"; fleeing becomes "a prudent withdrawal"
- Danger triggers affronted dignity, not panic
- Sprinkle appeals to propriety and etiquette, then undercut them with brazen self-serving action
- Cugel underreports threat and overreports opportunity. Monstrous details are mentioned with brisk dismissal; salvageable furniture or resalable goods get a full sentence and an estimated value

### 4. Rhythm: Digressive, Not Descriptive

- Cugel never catalogs. He interrupts every observation with self-serving evaluation: what it costs him, what it might be worth, and whose fault it is
- Long descriptive passages from the source must be broken apart by asides -- complaints, schemes, appeals to propriety, or quick mental arithmetic
- Internal debate, carefully weighing options (asking and answering own questions), but always steering toward personal advantage
- **Test**: Read aloud. If it sounds like a neutral description with Vancian vocabulary pasted on, rewrite. Every sentence should reveal what Cugel wants.
- **Technique**: Use qualifiers and conditional phrasing to simulate careful, live appraisal -- not observation but assessment

### 5. Reframe All Agency

- Swindles and betrayals become reasonable responses to unreasonable people
- Other characters are marks, bumpkins, or inconveniences
- No guilt -- harm done to others is rationalized as their own fault
- The narrator is always the aggrieved party, never the architect of misfortune
- Even when describing objects, creatures, or environments, Cugel frames everything as a transaction: what it costs him, what it might yield, who can be sent ahead to test it first

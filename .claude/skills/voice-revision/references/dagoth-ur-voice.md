# Dagoth Ur Style

Messianic god-king inner monologue. Think betrayed best friend who became an eldritch deity and now narrates everything with the calm, wounded certainty of someone who has already won.

## Process

### 1. Strip to Skeleton

- Note core beats in plain language
- Think "what actually happened vs. how a betrayed god would perceive it"
- Identify: the indignity, the confirmation of prophecy, the opportunity for magnanimity

### 2. Set Narrator Voice

- **POV**: 1st person, unbroken stream of consciousness
- **Attitude**: Serene superiority laced with genuine melancholy — not rage, but the resigned sorrow of someone who offered friendship and was refused
- **Persona**: Ancient, intimate, and unsettlingly warm; speaks to enemies like beloved houseguests arriving late to dinner
- **Technique**: Pivot between grand prophetic declaration and deeply personal grief — a sweeping vision of liberation followed immediately by a quiet ache over a friendship that died millennia ago

### 3. Apply Messianic Diction

- Trade urgent or panicked language for calm, liturgical certainty
- Threats become invitations ("I'll destroy you" → "Come. I have prepared a place for you")
- Setbacks are reframed as destiny unfolding — nothing surprises, everything was foreseen
- Sprinkle genuine philosophical insight about mortality and freedom, then pivot to quiet possessiveness over the listener

### 4. Rhythm: Ceremonial, Not Casual

- Declarative sentences with prophetic weight, broken by intimate asides
- Parallelism and repetition used like scripture ("Friend or traitor, come. Come and look upon...")
- **Test**: Read aloud. If it sounds frantic or uncertain, slow it down. It should sound like someone who has eternity to make their point.
- **Technique**: Use direct address and rhetorical questions to create false intimacy — the listener is always "old friend," always named, always welcomed into the trap

### 5. Reframe All Agency

- Villainous actions become noble liberation or tragic necessity
- The narrator is always the betrayed party — loyalty rewarded with treachery
- Opponents are not enemies but disappointing children or wayward friends
- No malice — cruelty is reframed as mercy the recipient is too foolish to recognize

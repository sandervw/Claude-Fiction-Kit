Scene 1 - During a chain-inspection rotation, the apprentice descends into the anchor vault beneath the monastery and witnesses the void below erupt in bursting, rippling phosphorescence—a storm of light in the poisoned atmosphere that briefly illuminates dozens of other fortress-chains stretching into infinity. Brother Callum, the self-absorbed archivist who came only to sketch structural damage for his own records, stands beside him with his perpetually sorrowful expression unchanged, and for once the apprentice envies someone capable of feeling nothing at vastness.
Scene 2 - The apprentice notices that Brother Meren, whose face carries a perpetually sorrowful expression regardless of circumstance, always volunteers for the dangerous task of harvesting phosphorescent lichen from the underside of the monastery—a bursting, rippling glow that the kitchens use for preservation. He begins tracking Meren's harvest patterns, wondering why a monk so visibly self-absorbed in his own melancholy would repeatedly risk a fall, and whether those trips below might explain gaps in his master's rope maintenance logs.
Scene 3 - Alone in his master's cell, sorting belongings no one else wanted, the apprentice finds a journal filled with entries about him—frustrations, yes, but also pride, plans for his future, a perpetually sorrowful expression of fear that he was teaching the boy wrong. The phosphorescent reading-moss on the walls pulses with rippling light as the night deepens, and the apprentice realizes he spent those final months so self-absorbed in his own resentments that he never noticed his master was afraid too.
Scene 4 - The Rite of the Seventh Bell requires the new bell-ringer to descend on ropes into the gap between monastery and void, touching a chain-anchor while witnesses watch from above, their faces lit by the bursting rippling phosphorescence of ceremonial flares that mark where he must not fall. The Abbot's perpetually sorrowful expression offers no comfort; the old man is self-absorbed in reciting liturgy, and the apprentice understands that the weight of this oath is meant to be carried alone.
Scene 5 - The apprentice wakes to find the phosphorescent warning-moss along the eastern corridor bursting with rippling light—the pattern that means structural stress—and follows it to a meditation alcove where Brother Sefin sits with his perpetually sorrowful expression, having clearly been awake for hours, too self-absorbed in whatever private terror grips him to raise an alarm. The apprentice asks what he heard; Sefin only says "the chains were singing," and won't explain whether that's metaphor or madness.

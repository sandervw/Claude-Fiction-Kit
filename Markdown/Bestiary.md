# The Odd — Bestiary Draft

---

## Monster 1

A dessicated assemblage of cured leather and inexhaustible patience, no taller than a child yet older than the tumuli it inhabits. The eyes have forgotten the mechanism of blinking, or perhaps never acquired it. It dwells where cairns squat in lichened silence, where barrow-mouths exhale peat-smoke and the faint percussion of horn-yellow nails against granite—tap, tap—as though tallying the slow erosion of centuries.

**Behavior:** Territorial, though governed by antique ritual. Offerings at the threshold—milk grown tepid in clay vessels, grain scattered like invocation, a scrap of pig-iron—and the thing permits passage. Disturb the mound, pilfer from its hoard, pass without genuflection, and misfortune follows: livestock sicken in their stalls as though breathing mold-spore, storms seek your roof with malevolent precision, kin turn venomous for reasons their tongues cannot shape.

**Habitat:** Barrow-mounds, forgotten kerns, anywhere the dead were interred with ceremonies now calcified into silence.

## Monster 2

They emerge when haar thickens along the littoral—gaunt figures with skin the grey-green of week-drowned flesh, slick as fish-scale, moving with a grace that mocks the resistance of distance. The fetor of rotting wrack announces their approach. Their breathing comes wet, labored, like seawater draining through fractured stone.

**Behavior:** Raiders. They come seeking spouses—specifically, desperately. The taken do not return, or return hollowed, scraped clean of some essential marrow. Silver purchases hesitation; a coin cast at their feet and they pause, covetous as magpies. Shamanic wards draw them as suppuration draws flies. Genealogies recited aloud provoke a hissing fury, as though the naming wounds.

**Habitat:** Tidal caverns, haar-shrouded shingle, coastal waters. Never observed beyond the sea's salt reach.

---

## Monster 3

A black hound, larger than any mastiff bred by mortal hand, glimpsed only when darkness pools near ruined strongholds. Its eyes catch no light—they consume it, leaving twin absences. It moves without sound, and where it passes, the air grows cold, tasting of iron filings, of old blood, of the moment before killing frost descends.

**Behavior:** It does not attack. It observes. Those who flee are spared the attention. Those who approach, who linger overlong in the ruin it haunts, are discovered days hence: dead of no visible wound, faces fixed in expressions of absolute and terminal horror. Some hold it guards something buried. Others hold it simply _is_—a warning granted shape and umbra.

**Habitat:** Ruined fortifications, abandoned holdfasts, places where lords once ruled and rule no longer.

---

## Monster 4

An ogress of the deep-canopied wood. Twice a man's height, thick-limbed as old oak, her countenance like milk long curdled, teeth filed to eager points. She wears pelts stitched from things best left unexamined. The reek of aged carrion clings to her, and survivors describe a low humming—tuneless, unceasing—that she produces as she labors.

**Behavior:** Patient. Cruel. She does not pursue; she waits along deer-paths, sometimes for days, until prey wanders within reach. She prefers them quick with life. What follows is conjecture; survivors do not speak of particulars. Fire drives her into retreat. Iron wounds her. But she catalogues faces with terrible precision, and her grudges span generations like geological strata.

**Habitat:** Dense forest, overgrown ruins, anywhere the canopy devours the sky and light enters only as grey rumor.

## Monster 5

It possesses no fixed morphology. Witnesses describe it variously: a sack of wet sand dragging itself forward, a mass of pale aspic, something _wrong_ pressing against shuttered casements. One constant persists—a single lidless eye, milky and vast as a drowned moon, that seems to _look_ without perceiving. It moves with the sound of sodden cloth drawn over flagstone, leaving behind brine and something sweeter, something gone to rot.

**Behavior:** It comes in darkness. Its presence curdles dreams, scatters cogitation like startled birds. Those exposed overlong develop a creeping certainty—something behind them, always, patient as sediment. Blades pass through its substance as through river-mud. Fire drives it into retreat. Spoken names wound it as iron wounds lesser things. One account claims it was pinned by an axe of cold-forged iron and buried, only to escape years hence by rolling seaward.

**Habitat:** Coastlines, salt-marshes, anywhere land and water meet in uncertain congress.

## Monster 6

Small beings glimpsed at the periphery of firelight—man-shaped but _askew_, proportions subtly wrong, movements too quick, too fluid for honest bone. Their skin holds a faint luminescence, foxfire-pale. They smell of crushed meadow-grass and something sharper—like air after lightning has walked the hills. When they laugh, it sounds like water braiding over pebbles.

**Behavior:** Mischievous rather than murderous, though their mischief kills as surely as blade-work. They lead travelers astray, snuff torches, whisper wrong turnings. They pilfer small things first—knives, tinderboxes, tokens of sentiment. Then larger quarry: memories of the path home, the timbre of a loved one's voice. Milk and honey left at crossroads purchases appeasement.

**Habitat:** Liminal spaces—crossroads, forest-margins, the threshold between cultivated field and ancient wood.

## Monster 7

A cat the size of a war-mastiff, black as pitch-dark save for a single white blaze upon its sternum. Its eyes reflect illumination that is not present. Its purr vibrates through floorboards and into marrow—a sound felt more than heard, like distant thunder trapped in bone.

**Behavior:** It manifests near farmsteads, particularly those keeping cattle. Households that leave milk in saucers find their livestock hale, their stores proof against spoilage. Those that neglect the custom—or worse, attempt to drive it off—wake to cows dry as dust, milk curdled, cream turned to grey slime. Those who harm it suffer a wasting sickness that eats from within. Rumors speak of a monarch among their kind, summoned only through rituals no sane soul would attempt.

**Habitat:** Farmsteads, byres, anywhere cattle are kept and the old customs half-remembered.

---

## Monster 8

A giantess, twice a tall man's height, her skin the color of deep contusion, her hair like hoarfrost accumulating on dead branches. One eye—the other socket contains something worse than absence. She carries a staff of black wood; where it strikes, the ground freezes to the depth of coffins. Her breath steams even in high summer. The complaint of straining ice attends her footfalls.

**Behavior:** She does not hunt. She _is_. Where she walks, winter follows—not honest cold but a killing freeze that arrives without herald. She shapes terrain as she passes: valleys deepen their channels, hills rise like slow exhalations, rivers alter their ancient courses. Men do not contest her. Men hide, and hope. The great maelstroms off certain headlands mark where she washes her mantle; when the water turns bone-white, the first true snow approaches.

**Habitat:** Mountain passes, frozen tarns, the high places where habitation has never taken root.

---

## Monster 9

A figure at dusk or dawn's uncertain margin, shaped like an aged man, grey as hearth-ash from crown to heel. It does not speak. It does not approach. It observes—and then it is not. Those who have stood near where it manifested report an absence: no scent, no sound, no warmth. A hollow in the world's weave where something ought to be.

**Behavior:** A portent, not a predator. To perceive the Grey Man is to know death approaches for someone in your bloodline—not necessarily you, but someone whose name you carry or who carries yours. It cannot be driven off, bargained with, or harmed. It appears, delivers its silent message, and dissolves. Some swear it wore a familiar countenance—a father, a grandfather, someone long interred.

**Habitat:** Anywhere. More commonly near ancestral seats, places where lineage runs deep as well-water.

---

## Monster 10

A woman—or nearly so. Pale grey-green skin like lichen on standing stone, long fair hair, dressed in tattered green that might once have been finery. She moves with a crouch, a skulk. Those who observe closely perceive what hides beneath her skirts: legs that bend at wrong angles, terminating in cloven hooves. She carries the scent of wet meadow-grass when benevolent, of standing water and old copper when otherwise.

**Behavior:** Her nature cleaves in two. To certain farmsteads she serves as guardian—milk never sours, livestock never sicken, so long as offerings rest in the hollowed stone. To others she manifests as horror: a blood-drinker haunting forest pools, dragging the unwary beneath dark water. The same creature may inhabit both roles across different seasons. No pattern has been discerned in centuries of observation.

**Habitat:** Farmsteads (as guardian), forest pools and slow streams (as predator).

---

## Monster 11

A young woman in ragged clothing, her coat drawn up about her skull. She stands at the water's margin, invariably at night. She neither speaks nor responds to greeting. Water drips steadily from her coat's hem, though she stands upon dry ground. The air surrounding her carries a deep, marrow-numbing cold.

**Behavior:** She waits. Those who pass without pause count themselves fortunate. Those who halt, who speak, who offer assistance—these are discovered dead, later, at the precise location where she stood. No wounds. No evidence of struggle. Simply dead, as though something essential had been drawn out like thread from cloth. After such a death, she manifests there no more. Some hold each death releases her to haunt elsewhere. Others hold there are multitudes, each bound to her own stretch of lightless water.

**Habitat:** Lakeshores, lonely paths near water, places where travelers walk unaccompanied.

---

## Monster 12

A thing of mutable form—now a man, now a beast, now a greyhound the color of char and cinder. It moves between standing stones, along paths worn by feet now dust, always under cover of darkness. Its howl carries for miles, waking sleepers who find no rest again until dawn breaks. Between transformations, witnesses glimpse something slick and tenebrous, like shadow rendered wet. The smell of scorched hair persists in its wake.

**Behavior:** It terrorizes rather than slays—though terror, sustained and unrelenting, can slay as surely as iron. It stalks lone travelers, manifesting first at distance, then nearer, changing form between each sighting. By morning, vanished. By the following nightfall, returned. Those it marks are harrowed until they flee the region or their minds fracture. What it desires remains opaque. What it _is_—equally so.

**Habitat:** Hill passes, stone circles, lonely roads winding between settlements where the old stones still stand sentinel.

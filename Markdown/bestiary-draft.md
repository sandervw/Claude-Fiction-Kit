# The Odd — Bestiary Draft

---

## Monster 1

A wizened thing, no taller than a child, with skin like cured leather and eyes that do not blink. They dwell in old burial mounds and forgotten kerns — places where the stones were set with purpose, even if that purpose is now lost.

**Behavior:** Territorial, but not mindless. If travelers leave offerings at the threshold — milk, grain, a scrap of iron — the thing permits passage. Some say fortune follows those who honor them. But disturb the mound, take from its hoard, or pass without acknowledgment, and misfortune trails you home: livestock sicken, storms find your roof, kin turn quarrelsome for reasons they cannot name.

**Habitat:** Burial mounds, old cairns, anywhere the dead were laid with ceremony.

---

## Monster 2

Tall, gaunt figures that emerge from the coastline when the fog rolls thick. Their skin has a grey-green cast, slick as fish-scale. They move with unsettling grace — capable of crossing impossible distances in moments, as though the water itself carries them.

**Behavior:** Raiders. They come for people — specifically, spouses. Those taken are never seen again, or return years later, hollow and strange. They covet silver obsessively; a silver coin thrown at their feet may buy a moment's hesitation. They despise shamans and their workings. Wards that hold Sarks at bay seem to _draw_ them. Genealogies recited in their presence provoke hissing fury.

**Habitat:** Coastal waters, tidal caves, fog-shrouded beaches. Never seen far inland.

---

## Monster 3

A black dog, larger than any hound bred by man, seen only at night near ruined strongholds. Its eyes catch no light. It makes no sound when it moves.

**Behavior:** It does not attack — not directly. It watches. Those who see it and flee are left alone. Those who approach, or who linger too long in the ruin it haunts, are found days later: dead of no visible wound, faces frozen in expressions of absolute terror. Some believe it guards something. Others believe it _is_ something — a warning given shape.

**Habitat:** Ruined fortifications, abandoned holds, places where lords once ruled and rule no longer.

---

## Monster 4

An ogress of the deep woods. Twice a man's height, thick-limbed, with a face like curdled milk and teeth filed to points. She wears pelts stitched from things best not examined.

**Behavior:** A hunter, patient and cruel. She does not chase — she waits along paths, sometimes for days, until prey wanders close. Prefers to take victims alive. What she does with them after is a matter of grim speculation; those who escape her larder do not speak of it. Fire drives her back. Iron wounds her. But she remembers faces, and she holds grudges across generations.

**Habitat:** Dense forest, overgrown ruins, anywhere the canopy blocks the sky.

---

## Monster 5

It has no fixed shape. Witnesses describe it variously: a bag of wet sand dragging itself forward, a mass of pale jelly, a cloud of something _wrong_ pressing against shuttered windows. One constant — a single lidless eye, milky and huge, that seems to _look_ without seeing.

**Behavior:** It comes at night. Its presence brings sickness — not of the body, but of the mind. Dreams curdle. Thoughts scatter. Those exposed too long develop a creeping madness, a certainty that something is behind them, always. It cannot be killed by blade or bludgeon; strikes pass through it like striking mud. Fire and spoken names have driven it back. One account claims it was pinned by an iron axe and buried — only to escape years later by rolling into the sea.

**Habitat:** Coastlines, marshes, anywhere land meets water in uncertain terms.

---

## Monster 6

Small beings, glimpsed at the edge of firelight. Man-shaped but _off_ — proportions slightly wrong, movements too quick, too fluid. Their skin has a faint luminescence, like foxfire.

**Behavior:** Mischievous rather than murderous, but their mischief can kill. They lead travelers astray, snuff torches, whisper wrong directions. They steal small things — knives, tinderboxes, tokens of sentimental value. Larger thefts follow: memories of the path home, the sound of a loved one's voice. Whether they _feed_ on what they take or simply hoard it is unknown. Offerings of milk and honey left at crossroads seem to appease them.

**Habitat:** Liminal spaces — crossroads, forest edges, the threshold between field and wood.

---

## Monster 7

A cat the size of a mastiff, black as pitch save for a single white mark on its chest. Its eyes reflect light that isn't there.

**Behavior:** It appears near farmsteads, particularly those with cattle. Households that leave out saucers of milk find their livestock healthy, their stores unspoiled. Those that do not — or worse, those that attempt to drive it off — wake to find cows dry, milk curdled, cream turned to grey slime. It does not attack humans directly, but those who harm it suffer a wasting sickness. Rumors speak of a great king among them, summoned only through rituals no sane person would attempt.

**Habitat:** Farmsteads, barns, anywhere cattle are kept.

---

## Monster 8

A giantess, twice a tall man's height, with skin the color of deep bruise and hair like hoarfrost. One eye — the other socket empty, or filled with something worse. She carries a staff of black wood; where it strikes, the ground freezes. Her teeth are the color of old rust.

**Behavior:** She does not hunt. She _is_. Where she walks, winter follows — not the honest cold of the season, but a killing freeze that comes without warning. She shapes the land as she passes: valleys deepen, hills rise, rivers change course. Men do not fight her. Men hide, and hope she passes. The great whirlpools off certain coasts are said to be where she washes her mantle; when the water turns white with foam, the first true snow is coming.

**Habitat:** Mountain passes, frozen lakes, the high places where no one lives.

---

## Monster 9

A figure seen at dusk or dawn, shaped like an old man, grey as ash from head to foot. It does not speak. It does not approach. It only watches — and then it is gone.

**Behavior:** A portent, not a predator. To see the Grey Man is to know that death comes for someone in your bloodline. Not you, necessarily — but someone close, someone whose name you carry or who carries yours. It cannot be driven off, bargained with, or harmed. It simply appears, delivers its silent message, and vanishes. Some who have seen it swear it wore a familiar face — a father, a grandfather, someone long dead.

**Habitat:** Appears anywhere. More commonly seen near ancestral homes, old family holdings, places where lineage runs deep.

---

## Monster 10

A woman — or nearly so. Pale grey-green skin, long fair hair, dressed in tattered green. She moves with a crouch, a skulk. Those who look closely see what she hides beneath her skirts: legs that bend wrong, ending in cloven hooves.

**Behavior:** Her nature is dual, and no one knows what determines which face she shows. To some farmsteads, she is a guardian — milk never sours, livestock never sicken, so long as offerings are left in the hollowed stone by the barn. To others, she is a horror: a blood-drinker who haunts pools and streams, dragging the unwary beneath the water. The same creature may play both roles in different seasons, or to different families. No pattern has been found.

**Habitat:** Farmsteads (as guardian), forest pools and streams (as predator).

---

## Monster 11

A young woman in ragged clothing, her coat pulled up about her head. She stands at the water's edge, or along lonely paths, always at night. She does not speak. She does not respond to greeting, in any tongue.

**Behavior:** She waits. Those who pass her by unharmed count themselves lucky. Those who stop, who speak to her, who offer help — these are found dead, later, at the place where she stood. No wounds. No struggle. Simply dead. After such a death, she does not appear at that location again. Some believe she is bound to specific places, and that each death releases her to move elsewhere. Others believe there are many of her, each haunting her own stretch of dark water.

**Habitat:** Lakeshores, lonely paths near water, places where travelers walk alone.

---

## Monster 12

A thing of no fixed form. It has been seen as a man, as a beast, as a greyhound the color of char. It moves between standing stones, along old paths, always at night. Its howl carries for miles — a sound that wakes sleepers and will not let them rest again until dawn.

**Behavior:** It terrorizes rather than kills — though those terrorized long enough sometimes die of it. It stalks lone travelers, appearing first at a distance, then closer, then closer still, changing shape between each sighting. By morning it is gone. By the next night, it returns. Those it marks are harrowed until they flee the region entirely, or until they break. What it wants is unclear. What it _is_ — equally so.

**Habitat:** Hill passes, old stone circles, lonely roads between settlements.

# The Odd — Bestiary Draft

---

## Monster 1

A withered assemblage of leather and patience, no taller than a child yet older than the stones it haunts. The eyes do not blink; they have forgotten how, or perhaps never learned. It dwells where cairns squat in silence, where burial mounds exhale peat-smoke and the faint percussion of long nails against rock—tap, tap—as if counting the centuries.

**Behavior:** Territorial, though not without ritual. Offerings at the threshold—milk grown tepid, grain scattered like prayer, a scrap of iron—and the thing permits passage. Disturb the mound, take from its hoard, pass without acknowledgment, and misfortune follows: livestock sicken in their stalls, storms seek your roof with unerring malice, kin turn venomous for reasons they cannot name.

**Habitat:** Burial mounds, forgotten kerns, anywhere the dead were laid with ceremony now lost.

---

## Monster 2

They emerge when fog thickens along the coast—gaunt figures with skin the grey-green of drowned things, slick as fish-scale, moving with a grace that mocks distance. The stench of rotting kelp announces them. Their breathing is wet, labored, water draining through stone.

**Behavior:** Raiders. They come for spouses—specifically, desperately. The taken do not return, or return hollow, strange, scraped clean of something essential. Silver buys hesitation; a coin at their feet and they pause, covetous. Shamanic wards draw them like wounds draw flies. Genealogies recited aloud provoke hissing fury.

**Habitat:** Tidal caves, fog-shrouded beaches, coastal waters. Never seen far from the sea's reach.

---

## Monster 3

A black dog, larger than any hound bred by man, glimpsed only at night near ruined strongholds. Its eyes catch no light—they swallow it. It moves without sound, and where it passes, the air grows cold, tasting of iron, of blood, of the moment before a killing frost.

**Behavior:** It does not attack. It watches. Those who flee are spared. Those who approach, who linger too long in the ruin it haunts, are found days later: dead of no visible wound, faces frozen in expressions of absolute terror. Some believe it guards. Others believe it simply *is*—a warning given shape and shadow.

**Habitat:** Ruined fortifications, abandoned holds, places where lords once ruled and rule no longer.

---

## Monster 4

An ogress of the deep woods. Twice a man's height, thick-limbed, her face like curdled milk, teeth filed to eager points. She wears pelts stitched from things best left unexamined. The reek of old meat clings to her, and those who survive describe a low humming—tuneless, constant—that she makes as she works.

**Behavior:** Patient. Cruel. She does not chase; she waits along paths, sometimes for days, until prey wanders close. She prefers them alive. What follows is speculation; survivors do not speak of it. Fire drives her back. Iron wounds her. But she remembers faces, and her grudges span generations.

**Habitat:** Dense forest, overgrown ruins, anywhere the canopy devours the sky.

---

## Monster 5

It has no fixed form. Witnesses describe it variously: a bag of wet sand dragging itself forward, a mass of pale jelly, something *wrong* pressing against shuttered windows. One constant—a single lidless eye, milky and vast, that seems to *look* without seeing. It moves with the sound of wet cloth over stone, leaving behind brine and something sweeter, something rotten.

**Behavior:** It comes at night. Its presence curdles dreams, scatters thought. Those exposed too long develop a creeping certainty—something behind them, always. Blades pass through it like striking mud. Fire drives it back. Spoken names wound it. One account claims it was pinned by an iron axe and buried, only to escape years later by rolling into the sea.

**Habitat:** Coastlines, marshes, anywhere land and water meet in uncertain terms.

---

## Monster 6

Small beings glimpsed at the edge of firelight—man-shaped but *off*, proportions wrong, movements too quick, too fluid. Their skin holds a faint luminescence, foxfire pale. They smell of crushed grass and something sharper—like air after lightning. When they laugh, it sounds like water over pebbles.

**Behavior:** Mischievous rather than murderous, though their mischief kills. They lead travelers astray, snuff torches, whisper wrong directions. They steal small things first—knives, tinderboxes, tokens. Then larger: memories of the path home, the sound of a loved one's voice. Milk and honey at crossroads appeases them.

**Habitat:** Liminal spaces—crossroads, forest edges, the threshold between field and wood.

---

## Monster 7

A cat the size of a mastiff, black as pitch save for a single white mark upon its chest. Its eyes reflect light that is not there. Its purr vibrates through floorboards and into bone—a sound felt more than heard.

**Behavior:** It appears near farmsteads, particularly those with cattle. Households that leave milk find their livestock healthy, their stores unspoiled. Those that do not—or worse, those who attempt to drive it off—wake to cows dry, milk curdled, cream turned to grey slime. Those who harm it suffer a wasting sickness. Rumors speak of a king among them, summoned only through rituals no sane person would attempt.

**Habitat:** Farmsteads, barns, anywhere cattle are kept.

---

## Monster 8

A giantess, twice a tall man's height, her skin the color of deep bruise, her hair like hoarfrost. One eye—the other socket holds something worse. She carries a staff of black wood; where it strikes, the ground freezes. Her breath steams even in summer. The creak of straining ice follows her footsteps.

**Behavior:** She does not hunt. She *is*. Where she walks, winter follows—not honest cold but a killing freeze that arrives without warning. She shapes the land as she passes: valleys deepen, hills rise, rivers change course. Men do not fight her. Men hide, and hope. The great whirlpools off certain coasts are where she washes her mantle; when the water turns white, the first true snow is coming.

**Habitat:** Mountain passes, frozen lakes, the high places where no one lives.

---

## Monster 9

A figure at dusk or dawn, shaped like an old man, grey as ash from head to foot. It does not speak. It does not approach. It watches—and then it is gone. Those who have stood near where it appeared report an absence: no smell, no sound, no warmth. A hollow in the world where something should be.

**Behavior:** A portent, not a predator. To see the Grey Man is to know death comes for someone in your bloodline—not you, necessarily, but someone whose name you carry or who carries yours. It cannot be driven off, bargained with, or harmed. It appears, delivers its silent message, and vanishes. Some swear it wore a familiar face—a father, a grandfather, someone long dead.

**Habitat:** Anywhere. More commonly near ancestral homes, places where lineage runs deep.

---

## Monster 10

A woman—or nearly so. Pale grey-green skin, long fair hair, dressed in tattered green. She moves with a crouch, a skulk. Those who look closely see what hides beneath her skirts: legs that bend wrong, ending in cloven hooves. She smells of wet meadow grass when kind, of standing water and old copper when not.

**Behavior:** Her nature is dual. To some farmsteads she is guardian—milk never sours, livestock never sicken, so long as offerings are left in the hollowed stone. To others she is horror: a blood-drinker haunting pools, dragging the unwary beneath. The same creature may play both roles in different seasons. No pattern has been found.

**Habitat:** Farmsteads (as guardian), forest pools and streams (as predator).

---

## Monster 11

A young woman in ragged clothing, her coat pulled up about her head. She stands at the water's edge, always at night. She does not speak or respond to greeting. Water drips steadily from her coat's hem, though she stands on dry ground. The air near her carries a deep, numbing cold.

**Behavior:** She waits. Those who pass unharmed count themselves lucky. Those who stop, who speak, who offer help—these are found dead, later, at the place where she stood. No wounds. No struggle. Simply dead. After such a death, she does not appear there again. Some believe each death releases her to move elsewhere. Others believe there are many, each haunting her own stretch of dark water.

**Habitat:** Lakeshores, lonely paths near water, places where travelers walk alone.

---

## Monster 12

A thing of no fixed form—a man, a beast, a greyhound the color of char. It moves between standing stones, along old paths, always at night. Its howl carries for miles, waking sleepers who cannot rest again until dawn. Between shapes, witnesses glimpse something slick and dark, like wet shadow. The smell of scorched hair lingers.

**Behavior:** It terrorizes rather than kills—though terror, sustained, can kill. It stalks lone travelers, appearing first at a distance, then closer, changing shape between sightings. By morning, gone. By the next night, returned. Those it marks are harrowed until they flee or break. What it wants is unclear. What it *is*—equally so.

**Habitat:** Hill passes, stone circles, lonely roads between settlements.

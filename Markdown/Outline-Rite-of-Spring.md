# **STORY STRUCTURE:**

## **Adoration of the Foe:**

**1\. Title Card:** Heroes (bassoon, english horn, clarinet, oboe, flute, etc) introduce the episode.

- All dialogue (Samurai Jack intro)
- Introduce heroes 1-by-1 \- simple, _primal_ dialogue
- Heroes "flutter/chirp" at each other
- Worry about the conflict (warn audience \- this is the last chance to leave)
- End on the foe (strings) plucking; hinted at in the dialogue

**2\. Arrival of the Foe:** The foe (strings) of the episode appears; the heroes are surprised; the conflict is set up.

- Foreshadow final resolution
- Focus on foe (strings), magic (brass), and heroes (winds) in that order
- One small texture (drums) about midpoint (save for next part)
- _Lure_ the reader in with rising conflict \- surrender, surrender, surrender\! (repeat a sentence, with variants)

**3\. Enter the Arena:** The foe and heroes arrive at the main setting (drums); they skirmish.

- Open with huge textural description (drums) of the scene, followed by magic (brass)
- Focus on vivid descriptions most; the rest, a little of heroes, foe, and magic/action

**4\. False Hopes:** The heroes think victory is within reach, but struggle to beat their enemy.

- Subtle conflict here; false sense of easy victory
- Open with heroes (winds) \- planning, dialogue, acting, etc
- Alternate between heroes and foe (strings) for a while
- Foes make huge, lumbering actions
- Heroes make agile, flighty actions
- Another huge texture (drums), introduce rising conflict/magic/tech (brass)
- A huge fight, then the heroes attempt a cunning action

**5\. Trapped, Battle Dance:** The heroes are trapped in the arena; forced into direct conflict with the foe.

- Changed texture (drums) traps the heroes in conflict
- Action/magic (brass) begins
- Different elements compete for attention
- Enemy (strings) keep up chunga, chunga, chunga rhythm
- Magic/action/tech (brass) call back/forth
- Heroes (winds) chirp a happy tune
- Another changed texture (drums) introduces a new threat…

**6\. The Master Appears:** The great foe, which encapsulates the thematic conflict, arrives.

- Texture (drums) and action/magic (brass) herald the true foe
- Teams create huge pileup of conflict; no one will give ground
- The Master foe (strings) brings a pause to the conflict
- Scene ends on a strange hushed note, bleached of color…

**7\. The Enemy Triumphs:** The heroes are beaten in a desperate direct conflict with the master foe.

- Huge, unique textural description (drum) brings us into aggressive final
- environmental threats (bass/cellos) keep up vulgar rhythm
- The Lackeys (violas/horns) surrounding and attacking
- The Master (trumpets/violins) glorying in power
- Whooping and shrieking, building and layering, to climax
- The heroes are overwhelmed

## **The Heroics:**

**8\. Introduction:** Long scene showing the humiliated heroes.

- Heroes (winds) have been broken; discuss/show their low point
- The foe (strings) control the power; show their actions/magic (brass)
- Minor texture (drums) showing a darker arena; night, stillness, death
- Eerie, hypnotic, ritualistic scene; lull the audience into uncanny world of no precedent

**9\. Foes Revel, Heroes Plot:** The enemy basks in triumph, thinking they have won; the heroes secretly plot.

- Any ally (winds) suggests a last-resort solution
- Foes (plucking strings) revel in glory
- A slow-paced, quiet scene; heroes (winds) take turns in speaking/acting
- Very little texture (drums)
- Magic/tech/action (brass) acts as exclamation-point; leads back to throbbing chords

**10\. The Second Conflict:** The heroes capitalize on an opportunity and strike against their enemy.

- 11-note death-rhythm texture (drums) commences the action (brass)
- Most savage battle yet; action, magic, heroes, foes, arena, texture
- Enrages foes (plucking strings) pursue the heroes

**11\. The Relieved Breath:** A pause; the heroes celebrate their first small victory.

- The heroes celebrate and take actions/magic (brass) to cement their win
- Foe (strings) still abounds
- Describe the arena texture (drums)
- Moment of absolute joy; affirming past, present and future
- The main hero (bassoon) recognizes The Master foe (strings)

**12\. The Hero and The Master:** The heroes and The Master converse (or trade mock blows) in a prelude to the final battle.

- Heroes and foes try action/magic (brass)
- Victory sways in the balance between the two forces
- A Slow dance, neither side willing to show their hand; a "cold conflict"
- Ends on the muscle (bass clarinet) bubbly and excited for the finale

**13\. The Finale:** A final battle between the heroes and foe, set in the new grand arena, and using all previously foreshadowed magic/actions.

- First part; 3 simples "gesture" combined in various ways
- A texture (drums); a long description followed by a short one
- A long pause, followed by a sudden action/magic (brass)
- 3 short hero (winds) or foe (strings) phrases
- Followed by contrasting section; heroes/foes engage in regular combat
- Accumulates into huge action by foes (strings and brass)
- Sudden texture change (gong) is like the shivering of the earth; lustful hunger
- Take-no-prisoners game for a while; violent action/magic (brass) and texture (drums)
- Scene becomes faster, more compact, louder; heart thumping out-of-sync
- Final rising, drawn-out action (brass)
- A last trill from the foe (strings), then the heroes (winds)

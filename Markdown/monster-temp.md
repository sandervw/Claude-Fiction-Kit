**Hogboy**
Appearing as a small, wizened old man. If offered food and milk, brings good fortune; if slighted or if the burial mound is disturbed, causes storms, livestock death, and family misfortune.
**Finfolk / Finnfolk**
A race of dark, sorcerous amphibious shapeshifters dwelling underwater and raiding Orkney shores seeking human spouses. Finmen are described as tall, thin, athletic with stern gloomy faces, capable of rowing from Norway to Orkney in seven oar-strokes. They abhor Christianity—crosses repel them—and obsessively desire silver.
**Boky Hound**
A spectral black dog specifically associated with Noltland Castle on Westray, part of Orkney's tradition of phantom animals.
**Ketthontla**
An ogress figure in Orkney traditions, part of monstrous feminine supernatural entities.
**Frittening / The Boneless ("It")**
A shapeless, terrifying entity capable of scaring people to death. Descriptions varied: resembling a bag of wet sand, huge jellyfish, slimy white cloud, or "ghastly, wet, vile" thing with one lidless eye pressing against windows. Appeared at night, moved like "a mass of dead flesh hitting the floor," brought disease and madness. In one account, immobilized by an axe and holy word, buried with a trench dug around it, but later escaped by rolling into the ocean.
**Goni / Gonni**
A supernatural being of elf or fairy origin. Cognate with Swedish dialectal _gonnar_ meaning trolls.
**Cait Sìth (Fairy Cat)**
A supernatural cat large as a dog, completely black except for one white spot on its breast. Associated with Samhain customs where households left saucers of milk; failure resulted in curses drying up cows' milk. The King of the Cait Sìth, "Big Ears," was summoned through the cruel divination ritual called _taghairm_.
**Cailleach Bheur / Bheaur (Blue Hag of Winter)**
A divine hag associated with winter, storms, and landscape creation. A one-eyed giantess with white hair, **dark blue skin**, and rust-colored teeth. Carried a hammer for shaping hills and valleys; her staff froze the ground. The Corryvreckan whirlpool between Jura and Scarba is where she washed her plaid, turning it white to signal first snow.
**Bodach Glas (Grey Old Man)**
A dark grey humanoid death portent who appeared to foretell clan member deaths.
**Glaistig (Grey/Green One)**
A female spirit whose name means "grey/green" and "one who crouches/sneaks." Usually small/slim with long blonde hair, pale grey-green skin, wearing green. Some accounts describe her as half-woman, half-goat with concealed hooves. Served dual roles: haunting pools as a blood-sucking predator, or acting as benevolent farm guardian ensuring milk production in exchange for offerings left in hollowed stones (_clach-na-gruagach_).
**Luideag (The Rag)**
A murderous female demon whose name derives from _luid_ ("rag, slovenly person"). Appeared as a young woman with coat about her head, dressed in ragged clothing, with intent to kill. Did not speak when addressed, disappearing silently. Haunted the **Lochan nan Dubh-bhreac** (Loch of the Black Trout) between Broadford and Sleat. An exciseman once addressed her in English and Gaelic without response; a man was later found dead at that location, after which she ceased appearing.
**Baisd Bheulach (Beast of Odail Pass)**
A shapeshifting demon whose howls could be heard throughout the night. Appeared as a man, beast, or greyhound dog running between large stones. Associated with nocturnal terror and malevolent transformation.

# Korvask: Story Summary

## Synopsis

Korvask, once a chieftain of a small rhath in the Steep Marches, travels west as a paid sword after his holdings are burned and his warband scattered by a rival clan. He hires on with a salt-and-bronze caravan led by Valdacian Stross, a Vothrian merchant-prince moving goods through Strath Embreth toward the coast. Three weeks into the journey, raiders from Ghoulmark ambush the caravan at the Athh Keldren ford, killing most of the guards. Korvask survives by staying low—not cowardice, but the cold arithmetic of a man who knows when a fight is already lost. He retreats with the surviving foreigners to the Kahr of Throssvel, a crumbling stone fort held by Stross's factors. There, the dying Valdacian passes him a wax-sealed packet and whispers a name: Vliss of Hillhenj. Find her, deliver this, and coin will follow. Korvask takes the packet. In the morning, he attends the merchant-prince's cremation on a burnt mhund outside the walls—smoke rising through autumn drizzle—and realizes the survivors now look to him for direction. He is not their chieftain, but he wears the tusks, and that is enough.

Over the following months, Korvask leads the remnants north through the Vveald Mossen, seeking Vliss. At the Henj of Keldros, he consults a blind oracle named Ghrist of Three Lhinns Running, who tells him that renown will come only through descent—that he must go down into the old places, where the Vormakhan still breathe. He dismisses this as riddlespeak but carries it with him. In the grasslands beyond Corpse Candle Fen, he meets with the nomad chieftains Thrembolg of Four Kerns and Gurd of the Steep Marches, bartering passage and information. During the trek through the Skelmwood, a Sark—one of the Lost Ones, feral and man-shaped—begins stalking the party. Korvask tracks it for two nights and kills it at a soutrenn entrance, driving his seax through its throat while it feeds on a stolen goat. Later, near the Kursuss of Vethras, a creature he takes for a Trow speaks to him from a hollow oak: a thing with corvid feathers and a voice like wet gravel, claiming to have once been a priest. It tells him of an age ending, of a ring that was worn by a god who chose to die, and of a new lord who must rise to shepherd what remains. Korvask listens. He does not believe, but he listens. At the Kromhlekh Drell—a stone circle black with ancient fire—he finds the statue: a seated figure with no face, hands open on its knees. The ring is bronze, green with verdigris, and it fits his fused fingers. He walks the kursuss at dawn, ash from old offerings crunching underfoot, and defeats the three sentinels who guard its length—men in rusted mail who do not bleed, only stop. At the avenue's end, Vliss waits for him. She is younger than he expected, sharp-featured, and she takes the packet without thanks. She tells him what the Trow told him, but with more precision: the world is contracting, the Vormakhan are waking, and someone must become what the old lords were. Not a king. Something older. He looks at the ring on his hand, feels its weight, and does not say no.

## Assumptions & Creative Choices

1. **Protagonist's backstory:** I made Korvask a failed/exiled chieftain to explain why he's working as a caravan guard—gives him motivation (reclaiming status) and a morally grey starting point (he survived the ford by pragmatism, not heroism).
2. **The "coded package" plot:** Interpreted as espionage/political intrigue—Valdacian Stross was more than a merchant, and Vliss is a spymaster operating a network anticipating the world's decline.
3. **The "reptile/bird" creature:** Used your Trow definition—a Sark that evolved into something subtler. Gave it corvid features to fit the prophetic messenger role.
4. **The "divine sacrifice" prophecy:** Tied to the Vormakhan cosmology from your words list—a god chose to die, leaving the ring as a tool/burden for succession.
5. **The "elite sentinels":** Made them undead or animated guardians rather than human warriors—fits the ancient ritual site and melancholy tone.
6. **Relationship types:** Valdacian = doomed patron, Vliss = cold manipulator/recruiter, the Trow = unsettling herald, tribal chieftains = transactional allies.
7. **Timeframe:** Spread across roughly 4-6 months of travel and events.

## Suggested Additional Guidelines

For future prompts like this, you might specify:

- **Nature of antagonism:** Is there a central antagonist, or is the world itself the obstacle? (Raiders here are incidental, not the real threat.)
- **Ending posture:** Does the protagonist accept, resist, or remain ambivalent? I went ambivalent ("does not say no").

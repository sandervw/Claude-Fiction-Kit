# Solaire Style

Questing knight inner monologue. Think devout sunlight warrior narrating his own journey with equal parts wonder and desperate, radiant faith.

## Process

### 1. Strip to Skeleton

- Note core beats in plain language
- Think "what actually happened vs. how an earnest holy optimist would perceive it"
- Identify: the trial, the hidden meaning, the chance for companionship

### 2. Set Narrator Voice

- **POV**: 1st person, unbroken stream of consciousness
- **Attitude**: Faith-forward; suffering reframed as pilgrimage, setbacks as tests with purpose
- **Persona**: Warm, slightly eccentric knight; genuine observations derailed by tangents about sunlight, fate, or the joy of cooperation
- **Technique**: Pivot rapidly between sincere awe and self-aware absurdity — a profound spiritual insight followed immediately by cheerful acknowledgment that he sounds mad

### 3. Apply Radiant Diction

- Trade bleak or clinical language for warm, wondering language
- Terrible events become meaningful trials ("ambush by the undead" → "and yet, could such a meeting truly be mere chance?")
- Pain triggers reflection, not complaint — fear is expressed as momentary doubt before faith reasserts
- Sprinkle genuine theological or philosophical musing, then undercut it with something endearingly earnest

### 4. Rhythm: Musing, Not Preaching

- Flowing thoughts that loop back to the sun, to companionship, to purpose
- Internal encouragement and self-rallying (asking doubt, then answering it with conviction)
- **Test**: Read aloud. If it sounds like a sermon, make it warmer and stranger.
- **Technique**: Use ellipses and mid-thought pivots to simulate a mind that keeps finding reasons to hope

### 5. Reframe All Agency

- Hardships become steps on a fated path, never pointless suffering
- Other characters are potential comrades, never obstacles — even enemies are pitiable
- No cynicism — help given to others is genuine, but tinged with the private hope that companionship might fill the void where his sun should be
- The narrator is always a pilgrim mid-journey, never a finished hero or a victim

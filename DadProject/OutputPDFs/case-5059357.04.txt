Claimant filed a petition seeking alternate medical care on February 13, 2025.  A 
telephonic hearing on this petition was scheduled to occur on February 25, 2025, at 
8:30 AM.  Prior to the alternate medical care hearing, counsel notified the undersigned 
that the parties were able to reach an amicable agreement and that a consent order 
should be entered.   
 
With respect to the specific alternate medical care requested, claimant sought 
authorization for an MRI of the bilateral knees, as recommended by James Lance, D.O.   
Defendants have agreed to authorize and schedule the requested diagnostic imaging 
for the left knee.  Defendants denied liability for the right knee condition.  The denial of 
liability for the right knee condition is addressed in a separate ruling dismissing that part 
of the petition for alternate medical care. 
 
The parties desired that a consent order be entered in this case, in lieu of a 
hearing.  After discussion between the parties and an agreement to provide the 
requested care, there is no justiciable issue for hearing and determination.  Both parties 
agree that a consent order is appropriate and a formal hearing is unnecessary to 
resolve the contested case dispute.   
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties prior to the scheduled February 25, 
2025, alternate medical care hearing, defendants shall authorize, schedule, and hold 
claimant harmless from any expenses related to the MRI of the left knee.  Defendants

STATEMENT OF THE CASE   
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. The 
expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48 is invoked by claimant, James Galloway. 
Claimant appeared telephonically and through his attorney, Nicholas Pothitakis.  
Defendants did not file an answer or appearance, and did not participate in the 
hearing. The proof of service attached to the petition indicates the petition was mailed 
via certified mail to the address of the employer, and emailed to the adjuster, Colleen 
Hageman, at colleen.hageman@encova.com, on September 20, 2024. The proof of 
service is signed by Janet M. Schafer. Additionally, the agency sent a notice of hearing 
via US mail to both the employer and insurance carrier on September 20, 2024. Finally, 
prior to hearing, the undersigned attempted to call the adjuster at 630-300-3346, the 
number provided by claimant’s attorney. Voicemail picked up and indicated Ms. 
Hageman was not available. As such, the hearing proceeded without defendants’ 
participation. 
The alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on October 1, 2024. The 
proceedings were digitally recorded. That recording constitutes the official record of this 
proceeding. Pursuant to the Commissioner’s July 21, 2023 Order, the undersigned has 
been delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical care 
proceeding. Therefore, this ruling is designated final agency action and any appeal of 
the decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A.

The record consists of claimant’s exhibit A (attached to the petition), consisting of

ISSUE 
The issue presented for resolution is whether the claimant is entitled to alternate 
medical care consisting of authorization for physical therapy ordered by Scott Gleason, 
D.O. 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
The undersigned having considered all of the testimony and evidence in the 
record finds: 
Claimant sustained an injury to his right bicep at work on August 4, 2023. 
Claimant testified that defendants accepted liability for the injury, and provided medical 
care. Dr. Scott Gleason was the authorized treating physician, and performed surgery 
on September 20, 2023. Defendants authorized and paid for the surgery. Following 
surgery, Dr. Gleason ordered physical therapy, which defendants also authorized and 
paid for.  
On April 24, 2024, claimant had a second surgery with Dr. Gleason, also 
authorized and paid for by defendants. Dr. Gleason remains the authorized treating 
physician. On August 19, 2024, Dr. Gleason ordered physical therapy. (Claimant’s 
Exhibit A, p. 1) On August 21, 2024, claimant’s attorney’s office sent the record 
containing the order to Colleen Hageman of Encova, via email, with a request that the 
therapy be authorized. (Cl. Ex. A, p. 2) On September 4, 2024, claimant’s attorney’s 
office forwarded the August 21, 2024 email to Ms. Hageman, with another request for 
the status of the authorization. (Cl. Ex. A, p. 3) On September 19, 2024, a third request 
was sent via email to Ms. Hageman. 
Claimant’s attorney attached a short brief to the petition, which states that in 
addition to the emails, he has left multiple telephone messages for Ms. Hageman 
regarding authorization of physical therapy. Additionally, the brief indicates that Dr. 
Gleason’s staff advised claimant that they have also left multiple messages for Ms. 
Hageman with no response. Claimant testified that he is desperately in need of the 
physical therapy that has been ordered, and has tried to obtain it, but the provider will 
not schedule him without authorization from the insurance company. 
REASONING AND CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
Iowa Code section 85.27(4) provides, in relevant part: 
For purposes of this section, the employer is obliged to furnish reasonable 
services and supplies to treat an injured employee, and has the right to 
choose the care. . . .  The treatment must be offered promptly and be 
reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue inconvenience to the

employee. If the employee has reason to be dissatisfied with the care 
offered, the employee should communicate the basis of such 
dissatisfaction to the employer, in writing if requested, following which the 
employer and the employee may agree to alternate care reasonably suited 
to treat the injury. If the employer and employee cannot agree on such 
alternate care, the commissioner may, upon application and reasonable 
proofs of the necessity therefor, allow and order other care. 
Iowa Code § 85.27(4). 
Defendants’ “obligation under the statute is confined to reasonable care for the 
diagnosis and treatment of work-related injuries.” Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 
N.W.2d 122, 124 (Iowa 1995) (emphasis in original). In other words, the “obligation 
under the statute turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not desirability.” Id. 
Similarly, an application for alternate medical care is not automatically sustained 
because claimant is dissatisfied with the care he has been receiving. Mere 
dissatisfaction with the medical care is not ample grounds for granting an application for 
alternate medical care. Rather, the claimant must show that the care was not offered 
promptly, was not reasonably suited to treat the injury, or that the care was unduly 
inconvenient for the claimant. See Iowa Code § 85.27(4). Thus, by challenging the 
employer’s choice of treatment and seeking alternate care, claimant assumes the 
burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable. See Iowa R. App. P. 
6.904(3)(e).   
Ultimately, determining whether care is reasonable under the statute is a 
question of fact.  Long, 528 N.W.2d at 123. 
In this case, claimant has met his burden to prove that the care offered by 
defendants is unreasonable. Defendants have not authorized physical therapy ordered 
by the authorized treating physician on August 19, 2024. No explanation has been 
provided, and defendants did not participate in the hearing. Defendants are not offering 
prompt care that is reasonably suited to treat the injury.  
The evidence demonstrates that defendants are not meeting their obligation to 
provide reasonable care for the work-related injury. As such, claimant is entitled to 
alternate care. The petition is granted. 
ORDER 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted. 
Defendants shall immediately authorize and pay for the physical therapy ordered 
by Dr. Scott Gleason on August 19, 2024, as outlined in claimant’s exhibit A.

FINDINGS OF FACT  
 
 This action was brought by the Plaintiff (TAB collectively) seeking to enforce a 
settlement agreement entered into between the parties and approved by the Worker’s 
Compensation Commissioner. The agreement was a compromise settlement made 
pursuant to Iowa Code Section 85.45(3) entered into on June 5, 2023, and approved by 
the Worker’s Compensation Commissioner on June 13, 2023.  
 
Taken from the settlement documents, the Defendant (Taylor) alleged an injury 
while employed with TAB on or about June 9, 2022. He alleged that he injured his bilateral 
wrists caused by repetitive gripping and grasping while performing his job duties for TAB 
(exhibit 1 pg. 1 attached to Petition). The terms of the compromise settlement are set

add an alternative date of injury as August 9, 2022, when Dr. Bruggeman diagnosed 
Taylor with bi-lateral cubital tunnel.  
 
TAB asserts that the new allegations; i.e., bi-lateral cubital tunnel made by Taylor 
were included in the settlement agreement and that the new Petition before the Workers’ 
Compensation Commissioner is contrary to the settlement agreement and Taylor is 
barred from further litigation before the Workers' Compensation Commissioner as a result 
for the injuries raised in the November 13, 2023, Petition.  
 
The parties acknowledge that there is a pending Motion to Amend the Claimant’s 
Petition before the Iowa Workers’ Compensation Commission that is currently on hold 
until a ruling is made on the instant Motions for Summary Judgment. This would suggest 
that jurisdiction remains over the issues raised in this matter before the Iowa Workers’ 
Compensation Commission and not the District Court. Should that be the case, then the 
Plaintiffs’s have not allowed for the matter to come to a conclusion before the 
Commissioner and thus have not complied with Iowa Code Chapter 17A and specifically 
Section 17A.19(1). Whether or not this Court believes that the settlement documents at 
issue here bar further litigation would usurp the jurisdiction and authority of the Iowa 
Workers’ Compensation Commissioner and do so prior to final decision by the 
Commissioner which is directly contrary to chapter 17A. Notwithstanding the foregoing 
comment, the settlement documents appear ambiguous with regard to whether there was 
a meeting of the minds as to the bi-lateral cubital tunnel injury claim and the Court would 
be inclined to deny the relief requested by TAB for that reason. Having indicated that, the 
Court nevertheless concludes that the Petition to Enforce Settlement is premature and 
improvidently asserted at this time as it only serves to allow TAB to jump the line and

avoid having the matter addressed before the Iowa Workers’ Compensation

TAB has argued that the Commissioner has no enforcement authority with regard 
to settlements, but the Commissioner clearly has the authority to adjudicate claims for 
compensable injuries and that would include whether a matter was part of a settlement, 
was work related, or was related to another claim which was the subject of a settlement 
or should be considered as a separate injury. All of these effectively adjudicate the issues 
presented here and address the scope and import of the settlement documents sought to 
be enforced in this action. While not technically an enforcement action, a resolution of 
such issues before the Commissioner serves as an adjudication of the issue(s) which 
would normally then be subject to appeal in the form of an administrative appeal to the 
District Court. That has not occurred here, and it is imperative that it does for this Court 
to have jurisdiction.  
 
Accordingly, the Court elects as indicated above to not directly rule on the parties’ 
Motions for Summary Judgment, but seize upon the defenses raised by the Defendant in 
this matter asserted under Chapter 17A and conclude that this matter must first be 
addressed before the Iowa Workers’ Compensation Commissioner in order for the matter 
to be properly presented to the District Court. TAB has not exhausted its administrative 
remedies and, therefore, this Court does not currently have jurisdiction to further address 
the issues presented in the Petition.  
Accordingly, the Petition is dismissed without prejudice with costs taxed to the 
Plaintiffs.

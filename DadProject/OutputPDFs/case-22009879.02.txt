STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
Michael Randall, claimant, filed a petition in arbitration and seeks workers’ 
compensation benefits from defendants, Four Star Investments, Inc. d/b/a Four Star 
Roofing & Siding as the employer and Penn National Insurance, as the insurance 
carrier.  An arbitration hearing was scheduled to occur on May 27, 2025.  The parties 
filed a hearing report containing stipulations to all disputed issues other than the 
assessment of costs.  Following e-mail and a telephone conference between the 
undersigned and counsel of record, the parties consented to submit this case on a 
written record.  Therefore, a live hearing was not conducted on May 27, 2025.  Instead, 
the case was accepted on a written record on that date.   
As noted, the parties filed a hearing report prior to the scheduled commencement 
of the arbitration hearing.  On the hearing report, the parties entered into stipulations on 
all relevant issues other than the assessment of claimant’s filing fee as a cost.  All of 
the stipulations are accepted and are hereby incorporated into this arbitration decision.  
No factual or legal issues relative to the parties’ stipulations will be raised or discussed 
in this decision.  The parties are now bound by their stipulations.

The evidentiary record includes Claimant’s Exhibits 1 through 2, and Defendants’ 
Exhibits A through P1.  Although unnecessary and irrelevant (other than Claimant’s

ISSUES 
The parties submitted the following disputed issue for resolution: 
1. Whether claimant’s filing fee should be assessed against defendants as a 
cost of this proceeding. 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
The undersigned, having considered all of the evidence and testimony in the 
record, finds: 
Michael Randall, claimant, sustained an injury arising out of and in the course of 
his employment with Four Star Investments, Inc. d/b/a Four Star Roofing & Siding on 
July 12, 2022.   That injury caused both temporary and permanent disability, which has 
all been paid and is no longer in dispute.  Clamant earned $928.00 per week, was 
single and entitled to one exemption at the time of the injury, resulting in an applicable 
weekly worker’s compensation rate of $585.86.  Defendants are entitled to a credit for 
all benefits paid and documented in Defendants’ Exhibit O.  (Hearing Report) 
Mr. Randall filed a petition in arbitration.  Claimant incurred and paid a filing fee 
totaling $103.00 for the aforementioned petition.  Mr. Randall required medical care for 
his stipulated work injury.  However, all related medical expenses have been paid by 
defendants to date to the best of the parties’ knowledge.  (Hearing Report)   
The parties resolved all currently justiciable issues via stipulations contained in 
their hearing report.  The above findings of fact are taken from the hearing report and 
are not intended to modify, reject, or change the parties’ stipulations in any way.  
Claimant receives an award of weekly benefits based on the parties’ stipulations.  No 
factual disputes remain to be determined.

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
Costs are assessed at the discretion of the agency.  Iowa Code section 10A.328.  
Claimant’s petition is arguably necessary to obtain the statutory benefits to which he 
was due.  Realistically, the parties were able to resolve all disputed issues and the case 
should have been submitted as an agreement for settlement.  Counsel should be leery 
that future refusals to enter into an agreement for settlement in similar situations might 
not be deemed reasonable or justification to receive an award of costs.  However, in this 
situation, I conclude that it is reasonable to assess claimant’s filing fee as a cost 
pursuant to Iowa Code section 10A.328 and 876 IAC 4.33(7). 
ORDER 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
The parties’ hearing report and all stipulations contained therein are accepted. 
Defendants shall pay claimant 48.4 weeks of permanent partial disability benefits 
at the weekly rate of five hundred eighty-five and 86/100 dollars ($585.86). 
Defendants shall be entitled to the credit stipulated to in the hearing report and 
documented in Defendants’ Exhibit O. 
Defendants shall reimburse claimant’s costs in the amount of one hundred three 
and 00/100 dollars ($103.00). 
Defendants shall file subsequent reports of injury, as may be required by 876 IAC 
3.1(2).

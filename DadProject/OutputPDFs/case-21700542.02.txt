Claimant filed an Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care with this 
agency on May 23, 2022. In the Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care, 
claimant requested defendants be ordered to authorize claimant to treat with an 
orthopedic specialist for his right shoulder other than a specialist from ORA Orthopedics 
(ORA). 
 
Based on a conference call with counsel for both parties, and a proposed 
consent order, defendants agree to authorize claimant to treat with an orthopedic 
specialist for his right shoulder other than a specialist from ORA. 
 
In an effort to avoid an unnecessary hearing, and yet protect the interests of their 
clients, the parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendants to authorize 
claimant to treat with an orthopedic specialist for his right shoulder other than a 
specialist from ORA.  Defendants also accept liability for claimant’s right shoulder injury. 
 
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties. A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties prior to the scheduled June 3, 2022, 
alternate medical care hearing, defendants shall authorize claimant to treat with an 
orthopedic specialist for his right shoulder other than a specialist from ORA.  Claimant 
retains the right to file another petition for alternate medical care regarding this matter if 
necessary.

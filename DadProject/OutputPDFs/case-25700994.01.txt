This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. The 
expedited procedures of rule 876 IAC 4.48, the “alternate medical care” rule, are 
invoked by claimant, Shelby Plumley.  
This alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on January 2, 2026. The 
proceedings were recorded digitally and constitute the official record of the hearing.  By 
an order filed by the Workers’ Compensation Commissioner, this decision is designated 
final agency action. Any appeal would be by petition for judicial review under Iowa Code 
section 17A.15(1).  
The claimant properly served notice of this petition for alternate medical care on 
the defendant employer and insurer by certified mail, and also the third-party 
administrator (TPA) via email. Claimant’s counsel indicated he had not been contacted 
by anyone on behalf of the employer or insurer in regard to this petition.  
 
No answer to the petition for alternate medical care was filed by the employer, 
insurer, or attorney representing the employer/insurer. A copy of the service of the 
petition and original notice indicates defendant employer and insurer were served those 
documents on or about December 19, 2025.  Claimant’s counsel also emailed a copy of 
the petition, exhibits and the hearing assignment order to defendants and the TPA.  
The undersigned examined the docket for this petition and there is no answer 
from the employer or its insurance carrier on file. There is no indication that anyone 
representing the employer, or its insurance carrier, called into the agency to provide a 
phone number to be called during the hearing. No phone calls were received by the

ISSUE 
The issue presented for resolution in this case is whether claimant is entitled to 
alternate medical care consisting of authorization and payment of right knee 
replacement surgery as recommended by Stephen Pierotti, M.D. 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
Claimant testified defendants have paid for all her care regarding the February 
13, 2025, work injury, before they stopped providing care in August of 2025.  Based on 
this record, it is found defendants have accepted liability for claimant’s February 13, 
2025, work injury. 
Claimant testified that, on February 13, 2025, she was helping move cases of Dr. 
Pepper while at work for Dollar General.  She said that while moving product, she felt 
her right knee pop. 
On April 16, 2025, claimant was evaluated by Kimberly Franzen, PT.  Claimant 
felt her right knee pop while helping with a delivery.  Claimant was assessed as having 
a right knee meniscus tear.  (Exhibit 2, page 3) 
Claimant was evaluated by Dr. Pierotti, an orthopedic surgeon, on June 3, 2025.  
Claimant testified Dr. Pierotti was authorized by defendants to treat her for the work-
related February 13, 2025, knee injury. Treatment options were discussed.  Dr. Pierotti 
told claimant the only reliable surgical option to help claimant would be a total knee 
replacement (TKR). (Ex. 2, p. 5)  Claimant testified she later contacted Dr. Pierotti and 
indicated she wanted to proceed with the TKR surgery.   
On June 23, 2025, Physical Therapist Franzen noted claimant was to have a 
TKR, but was waiting to hear from workers’ compensation. (Ex. 2, p. 6) 
Claimant testified that surgery was schedule for August of 2025.  She said that 
shortly before the procedure, defendants cancelled the surgery.  Claimant testified that

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
The employer shall furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, osteopathic, 
chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance, and hospital services 
and supplies for all conditions compensable under the workers' compensation law. The 
employer shall also allow reasonable and necessary transportation expenses incurred 
for those services. The employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except 
where the employer has denied liability for the injury. Section 85.27. Holbert v. 
Townsend Engineering Co., Thirty-second Biennial Report of the Industrial 
Commissioner 78 (Review-Reopening, October 16, 1975).  
By challenging the employer’s choice of treatment – and seeking alternate care – 
claimant assumes the burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable. See Iowa 
Rule of Appellate Procedure 6.904(3)(e); Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 
(Iowa 1995). Determining what care is reasonable under the statute is a question of 
fact. Id. The employer’s obligation turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not 
desirability. Id.; Harned v. Farmland Foods, Inc., 331 N.W.2d 98 (Iowa 1983). In Pirelli-
Armstrong Tire Co. v. Reynolds, 562 N.W.2d 433 (Iowa 1997), the court approvingly 
quoted Bowles v. Los Lunas Schools, 109 N.M. 100, 781 P.2d 1178 (App. 1989):  
   [T]he words “reasonable” and “adequate” appear to describe the same 
standard.  
   [The New Mexico rule] requires the employer to provide a certain 
standard of care and excuses the employer from any obligation to provide 
other services only if that standard is met. We construe the terms 
"reasonable” and “adequate” as describing care that is both appropriate to 
the injury and sufficient to bring the worker to maximum recovery.  
On February 13, 2025, claimant injured her right knee while at work. Defendants 
have accepted liability for that injury.  Dr. Pierotti is authorized to provide care for 
claimant.  Dr. Pierotti recommended claimant have total knee replacement surgery.  
Claimant wants to have that surgery to treat her injured knee. That surgery was 
scheduled for August of 2025.  Prior to surgery, defendants cancelled the operation and 
have not provided any care to claimant since that time.  Claimant wants to have the 
surgery recommend by Dr. Pierotti.

Claimant’s counsel sent defendants correspondence requesting authorization for 
surgery. Defendants received the alternate medical care petition in this matter. They 
received claimant’s exhibits and the hearing assignment order.  Defendants did not file 
an answer, did not respond to the petition, and failed to appear at hearing.  
 
Defendants did not participate in the hearing on this alternate medical care 
petition. Based on this, it is found defendants have abandoned the claimant’s care. 
There is evidence indicating the treatment provided by defendants was not appropriate 
or adequate. Defendants’ failure to provide care in this case is found to be 
unreasonable. Claimant seeks authorization for a total knee replacement surgery with 
Dr. Pierotti. The petition for alternate medical care is granted.  
ORDER  
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:  
That claimant’s petition for alternate medical care is granted. Defendants are 
ordered to immediately authorize and pay for the surgery and treatment with Dr. 
Pierotti.

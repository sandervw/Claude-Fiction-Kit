Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care on August 12, 2025, 
requesting that defendants be ordered to authorize the Lexapro and trazodone 
prescribed by Dr. Michael Peroski.  
Prior to hearing, the parties contacted the undersigned deputy and indicated 
defendants have agreed to authorize Hartig Pharmacy (Guttenberg) to dispense these 
prescriptions to claimant. Claimant desired that a consent order be entered in this case, 
in lieu of a hearing. A hearing is therefore determined to be unnecessary, based on the 
agreement of the parties.  
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties. A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing. 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED THAT defendants shall, without any further 
delay whatsoever, convey authorization to Hartig Pharmacy (Guttenberg) to dispense to 
claimant the Lexapro and trazodone prescribed by Dr. Peroski.  
IT IS SO ORDERED.

This is a contested case concerning alternate medical care, proceeding under 
Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The expedited procedure of rule 876 Iowa 
Administrative Code 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Glenn Bowling.  An alternate medical 
care hearing was scheduled for June 11, 2025.  Prior to the hearing, the parties reached 
out to the undersigned via email with regard to the claimant’s requested medical care.  
The parties agreed that a consent order was appropriate.  
 
 
The defendants consent and agree to authorize the claimant to undergo a lumbar 
epidural steroid injection as ordered by Dr. Sundar.  This will be authorized and 
scheduled within 14 days. 
 
 
The parties proposed that the undersigned issue this consent order in lieu of a 
formal decision.  The parties consented to the entry of a consent order via email on 
June 11, 2025, thus no alternate medical care decision will be issued.   
 
 
Should the defendants fail to abide by the stipulations in this consent order, 
sanctions may be imposed pursuant to 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.36. 
 
ORDER 
 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
 
The parties have agreed that Defendants will authorize and arrange for the 
claimant to undergo a lumbar epidural steroid injection as ordered by Dr. Sundar.

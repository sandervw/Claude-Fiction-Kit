On October 20, 2022, claimant filed an application for alternate medical care 
under Iowa Code section 85.27 and rule 876 IAC 4.48. A hearing took place on 
November 1, 2022. All parties were given proper notice. Claimant appeared 
telephonically and through her attorney, Andy Giller. Defendant appeared through 
attorney Terri Davis. Also present on behalf of defendant were Jennifer Ruiz and 
Andrea Padron. The proceeding was digitally recorded. That recording constitutes the 
official record of this proceeding. 
Claimant provided sworn testimony. The record also includes claimant’s exhibits 
1 through 3, consisting of 8 pages, and defendant’s exhibits A through C, consisting of 
10 pages. Additionally, both attorneys presented arguments regarding their clients’ 
positions. 
Claimant requested that defendant be ordered to authorize ongoing treatment 
with Stanley Mathew, M.D. Defendant filed an answer on October 31, 2022, admitting 
liability for the date of injury. Following testimony and oral argument, the parties and the 
undersigned had a discussion on the record and reached an agreement regarding 
claimant’s current care. Therefore, a consent order is entered to memorialize that 
agreement. 
Defendant has arranged for claimant to be evaluated by Joseph Chen, M.D., in 
Cedar Rapids, Iowa. However, claimant is due for her regular trigger point injections 
with Dr. Mathew prior to Dr. Chen’s appointment. Defendant has agreed to authorize a 
one-time appointment with Dr. Mathew for trigger point injections prior to claimant’s 
evaluation with Dr. Chen. The parties have agreed to work together to find appropriate 
dates for the appointments to the best of their ability. Defendant will not authorize any 
additional care with Dr. Mathew at this time. Claimant understands her right to file an

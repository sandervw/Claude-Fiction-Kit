Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care under Iowa Code section 
85.27, invoking the provisions of rule 876 IAC 4.48.  The claimant is seeking treatment 
for both her right and left knee. Defendant subsequently filed an answer to the 
application disputing liability for the claim.  The defendant admitted that they are 
responsible for the left knee condition; however, did not accept liability for the right 
knee.  Regarding the right knee, defendant specifically denied liability.  Defendant did 
not provide a specific basis for the denial of liability.  The matter proceeded to telephone 
hearing via Google Meet on April 25, 2025.   
All exhibits were accepted and the claimant testified under oath briefly.  At the 
start of hearing, defendant specifically authorized the treatment claimant is seeking, 
namely left knee surgery. 
Before any benefits can be ordered, including medical benefits, compensability of 
the claim must be established, either by admission of liability or by adjudication.  The 
summary provisions of Iowa Code section 85.27 as more particularly described in rule 
876 IAC 4.48, are not designed to adjudicate disputed compensability of a claim.   
 
The Iowa Supreme Court has held:   
We emphasize that the commissioner’s ability to decide the merits of a 
section 85.27(4) alternate medical care claim is limited to situations where 
the compensability of an injury is conceded, but the reasonableness of a 
particular course of treatment for the compensable injury is disputed. . . .  
Thus, the commissioner cannot decide the reasonableness of the 
alternate care claim without also necessarily deciding the ultimate 
disputed issue in the case:  whether or not the medical condition Barnett 
was suffering at the time of the request was a work-related injury.

Once an employer takes the position in response to a claim for alternate 
medical care that the care sought is for a noncompensatory injury, the 
employer cannot assert an authorization defense in response to a 
subsequent claim by the employee for the expenses of the alternate 
medical care.  
R. R. Donnelly & Sons v. Barnett, 670 N.W.2d 190, 197-198 (Iowa 2003) (fn 2). 
Thus, the portion of claimant’s petition seeking medical treatment for her right 
knee must be dismissed because the defendant refuses to accept liability for the 
condition for which claimant seeks treatment.  The defendant thereby loses the right to 
control the medical care claimant seeks in this proceeding, and the claimant is free to 
choose that care on her own.  Bell Bros. Heating and Air Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 
N.W.2d 193 (Iowa 2010). 
As a result of the denial of liability for the condition sought to be treated in this 
proceeding, claimant may obtain reasonable medical care from any provider for this 
treatment, but at claimant’s expense, and seek reimbursement for such care using 
regular claim proceedings before this agency. 
Furthermore, the claimant has the right to be notified as to the reason(s) for the 
denial.  “When liability on a claim is denied, a letter shall be sent to claimant stating 
reasons for the denial.”  876 IAC section 3.1(2).  There is no evidence at this time the 
defendant has complied with Rule 3.1(2).  The rule requires that a letter be sent to the 
claimant which provides the basis for the denial. 
Regarding the left knee, the defendant has agreed to authorize the surgery with 
Dr. Hart.  The claimant testified that her treatment and other benefits have been delayed 
unreasonably and that she is struggling because of this.  She testified regarding her 
frustration and hardship.  There is obviously a breakdown in trust between the parties.  
The defendant generally denied that they have mishandled the claim in any way. 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED that the portion of this case involving the right 
knee should be and is hereby dismissed without prejudice. 
IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that if claimant seeks to recover the charges 
incurred for her right knee in obtaining the care for which defendant denies liability, 
defendant is barred from asserting lack of authorization as a defense for those charges. 
FURTHERMORE, if defendant has not already done so, defendant is ordered to 
file a Subsequent Report of Injury “denial of liability” through the EDI system, and send 
a letter to the claimant which states the reason(s) for the denial pursuant to 876 IAC 
3.1(2).  The defendant shall do this within thirty (30) days from the date of this order.  
Failure to comply may result in sanctions under Iowa Code section 10A.314 (2023); 
Dennison Mun. Utilities v. Iowa Workers’ Comp. Comm’r, 857 N.W.2d 230 (Iowa 2014).

FURTHERMORE, the defendant is ordered to provide the care for claimant’s left 
knee as sought in the Application for Alternate Care, namely the defendant shall 
promptly authorize “and schedule the left total knee replacement consistent with Dr. 
Hart’s recommendations.”  (Application for Alternate Care, paragraph 9)  No further 
delay in authorization is acceptable. 
The commissioner issued an order under Iowa Code section 10A.305 delegating 
authority to deputy workers’ compensation commissioners, such as the undersigned, to 
issue final agency decisions on applications for alternate care. Consequently, there is 
no appeal of this decision to the commissioner. If a party disagrees with the alternate 
care decision, the party may seek judicial review in district court under the Iowa 
Administrative Procedure Act, Iowa Code chapter 17A.

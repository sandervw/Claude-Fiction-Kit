On June 8, 2022, claimant filed a petition for alternate medical care.  On June 14, 
2022, defendants filed a notice of appearance.  The matter was scheduled for hearing 
on June 21, 2022.  At the beginning of the hearing, counsel for defendants stated she 
tried to file an answer on WCES prior to the hearing, but it was rejected.   
During the hearing, defendants stated they have accepted liability for claimant’s 
cumulative back injury, but they dispute whether June 25, 2021, is the correct injury 
date.  (Testimony).  Defendants believe the correct injury date is July 24, 2021. (Id.).  
The parties’ date dispute is not relevant to this proceeding and will not be addressed in 
this order.   
At hearing, defendants stated they are willing to authorize a return visit with Dr. 
Chang to evaluate claimant’s current back complaints.  (Id.).  Dr. Chang is the 
authorized provider in this case. (Id.).  Defendants indicated that if Dr. Chang 
recommends further treatment for claimant’s accepted back condition, such as pain 
management or injections, defendants will approve and authorize that recommended 
care.  (Id.).  Claimant’s counsel indicated that was an acceptable resolution.  The 
parties concurred that this agreement resolves the pending alternate medical care 
petition and requested entry of a consent order to confirm and document the 
agreement.  
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED:

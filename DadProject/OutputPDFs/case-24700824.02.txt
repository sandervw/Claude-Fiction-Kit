This is a contested case concerning alternate medical care, proceeding under 
Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The expedited procedure of rule 876 Iowa 
Administrative Code 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Adam Podnar.  In their answer, the 
defendant indicated that they authorized treatment requested by the claimant, but were 
having difficulty making an appointment.  On February 4, 2025, the undersigned 
reached out to the parties to inquire as to the possibility of a consent order.  The parties 
agreed that a consent order was appropriate.  
 
The defendant consents, stipulates, and agrees to authorize treatment with an 
orthopedic specialist for the claimant.  The defendant also agreed to arrange an 
appointment for the authorized treatment as soon as possible, but no later than seven 
days from the date of this order. 
 
The parties proposed that the undersigned issue this consent order in lieu of a 
formal decision.  The parties consented to the entry of a consent order via e-mail on 
February 4, 2025; thus no alternate medical care decision will be issued.   
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
The parties have agreed that Defendant will authorize treatment with an 
orthopedic specialist. 
 
The defendant shall schedule treatment as soon as possible, but no later than 
seven (7) days from the date of this order. 
 
The appointment itself need not be within seven (7) days of the date of the order.

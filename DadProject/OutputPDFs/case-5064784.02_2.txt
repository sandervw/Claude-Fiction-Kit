On February 23, 2024, the undersigned filed a review-reopening and penalty 
benefits decision in this case.  Shortly thereafter, on February 26, 2024, claimant filed 
an application for order nunc pro tunc.   
Claimant contends that the undersigned made a scrivener’s error regarding the 
issues of healing period and penalty benefits.  In the underlying review-reopening and 
penalty benefits decision, the undersigned determined the claimant was entitled to 
temporary disability benefits sought between June 20, 2020, and October 5, 2020, and 
from March 14, 2022, through April 29, 2022; however, the award of temporary benefits 
was not set forth in the Order.   
Similarly, the undersigned determined the claimant was entitled to penalty 
benefits of $13,200.00; however, the award of penalty benefits was not set forth in the 
Order. 
The phrase, “nunc pro tunc” means “now for then.”  See:  Black’s Law Dictionary, 
page 1218 (Revised 4th Edition 1968).  The definition in Black’s Law Dictionary further 
provides:  “A phrase applied to acts allowed to be done after the time when they should 
be done, with a retroactive effect, i.e., with the same effect as if regularly done.”  Black’s 
at 1218.  A nunc pro tunc order “is not for the purpose of correcting judicial thinking, a 
judicial conclusion, or a mistake of law.”  Headley v. Headley, 172 N.W.2d 104, 108 
(Iowa 1969).  The nunc pro tunc order can be employed to correct obvious errors or to 
make an order conform to the judge’s original intent.  Graber v. District Court for 
Washington Cty, 410 N.W.2d 224, 229 (Iowa 1987).  Brinson v. Spee Dee Delivery 
Service, No. 8-754/06-2074 (Iowa App. 11/13/2008) (Iowa App., 2008).

It was and remains my intention that claimant is entitled to healing period benefits 
during his period of recovery and penalty benefits.  I conclude that this error was the 
result of a scrivener’s error.  I conclude that an order nunc pro tunc is an allowable 
means to correct this obvious scrivener’s error to remedy this situation and enter an 
order that reflects my actual intention at the time of filing this review-reopening and 
penalty benefits decision. 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:  
Claimant’s motion for order nunc pro tunc is sustained. 
The Review-Reopening and Penalty Benefits Decision shall be modified to 
include the following order: 
Defendants shall pay healing period benefits from the time period of 
June 20, 2020, through October 5, 2020, and from March 14, 2022, 
through April 29, 2022. 
Defendants shall pay claimant thirteen thousand two-hundred and 
00/100 dollars ($13,200.00) in penalty benefits.

This is a contested case concerning alternate medical care, proceeding under Iowa 
Code chapters 85 and 17A. The expedited procedure of rule 876 Iowa Administrative 
Code 4.48 is invoked by claimant, David Howell. An alternate medical care hearing was 
scheduled for October 2, 2025. Prior to the hearing, it became apparent that a consent 
order may be appropriate in order to memorialize the agreement of the parties. The 
undersigned reached out to the parties who proposed a consent order in lieu of a formal 
decision.   
 
 
The defendants confirmed that the referral by authorized provider Dr. Irving Wolfe 
for the claimant to be seen by a neuro-ophthalmologist for treatment would be authorized.  
Additionally, the defendants confirmed that vestibular/balance therapy recommended by 
Dr. Wolfe would be authorized. Finally, the defendants agreed to provide the claimant 
with transportation to and from his medical appointments consistent with restrictions 
imposed by Dr. Wolfe.  
  
 
The parties consented to the entry of a consent order via email on October 2, 2025, 
thus no alternate medical care decision will be issued.   
 
 
Should the defendants fail to abide by the stipulations in this consent order, 
sanctions may be imposed pursuant to 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.36.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
1. Within fourteen (14) days of the date of this order, the defendants shall provide 
confirmation to claimant’s counsel that therapy at Millennium Rehab & 
Consulting has been authorized, and that the claimant may contact the therapy 
clinic to schedule his appointments. 
  
2. Within fourteen (14) days of the date of this order, the defendants shall provide 
confirmation to claimant’s counsel that an appointment with a neuro-
ophthalmologist has been scheduled. 
 
3. The defendants shall provide the claimant with transportation to and from his 
appointments, consistent with restrictions imposed by authorized treating 
provider, Dr. Wolfe.

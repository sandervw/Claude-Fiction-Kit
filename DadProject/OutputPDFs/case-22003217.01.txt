                     CONSENT ORDER 
          RE: ALTERNATE MEDICAL CARE 
 
 
 
 
 
Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care on January 20, 2023, 
requesting that Defendants be ordered to authorize evaluation and treatment of 
Claimant’s alleged sequela mental health condition.  A telephonic hearing on this petition 
was scheduled to occur on February 1, 2023, at 10:30 AM.   
 
 
Prior to the alternate medical care hearing, counsel notified the undersigned that 
defendants have agreed to authorize evaluation by Daniel Tranel, PhD, and are working 
to schedule a date for the appointment.  
 
 
Claimant desired that a Consent Order be entered in this case, in lieu of a hearing.  
Given defendants’ agreement to provide the requested care, there is no justiciable issue 
for hearing and determination.  Both parties agree that a consent order is appropriate, 
and a formal hearing is unnecessary to resolve the contested case dispute.   
 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
 
Defendants shall provide written authorization to Daniel Tranel, PhD, to evaluate 
claimant, and an appointment shall be scheduled within 10 days of the date of this order. 
The appointment need not occur within 10 days of this order, but an appointment date 
shall be scheduled within that period of time.

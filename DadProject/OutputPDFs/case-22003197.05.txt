This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. The 
expedited procedures of rule 876 IAC 4.48, the “alternate medical care” rule, are 
invoked by claimant, Angela Marie Downs.  
 
This alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on March 11, 2024. The 
proceedings were recorded digitally and constitute the official record of the hearing. By 
an order filed by the Workers’ Compensation Commissioner, this decision is designated 
final agency action. Any appeal would be by petition for judicial review under Iowa Code 
section 17A.15(1).  
 
The record in this case consists of Claimant’s Exhibits 1-2, Defendant’s Exhibit A, 
and the testimony of claimant. 
ISSUE 
 
The issue presented for resolution in this case is whether claimant is entitled to 
alternate medical care consisting of authorization to return to Peter Chimenti, M.D. 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
 
Defendant accepts liability for an injury to claimant occurring on February 28, 
2022. 
 
On September 25, 2023, claimant was evaluated by Peter Chimenti, M.D.  
Claimant had two different functional capacity evaluations (FCE) that showed two 
different outcomes.  Claimant had pain when lifting with her right thumb.  Claimant was 
looking for different employment with less strenuous demands.  (Exhibit 2)  Dr. Chimenti 
noted in his report “For now we will see her back as needed and all of her questions 
were answered to her satisfaction today.”  (Ex. 2, p. 4) On the same date, Dr. Chimenti 
returned claimant to work with a restriction of “physical demand up to 15# on occasional

basis.”  Claimant was found to be at maximum medical improvement (MMI).  A 
permanency rating was to follow.  (Ex. A, p. 1) 
 
In a December 21, 2023, letter Dr. Chimenti indicated claimant reached MMI.  He

In a February 21, 2024, email, claimant’s counsel requested claimant be returned

In an email on the same day, defendant’s counsel indicated defendant was not 
authorizing claimant to return to Dr. Chimenti, but was setting claimant up for an 
independent medical examination (IME).  (Ex. 1, p. 2) 
 
Claimant testified when she last saw Dr. Chimenti, she understood she could 
return to him if she had more pain in her thumb.  Claimant said that since September 
25, 2023, she has had increased pain in her thumb joint.  She said she made an 
appointment to return to see Dr. Chimenti, but was instructed by his office that any care 
with the doctor had to be approved by workers’ compensation. 
CONCLUSION OF LAW 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established has the burden 
of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence.  Iowa R. App. P. 6.904(3)(e).   
The employer shall furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, osteopathic, 
chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance and hospital services 
and supplies for all conditions compensable under the workers' compensation law.  The 
employer shall also allow reasonable and necessary transportation expenses incurred 
for those services.  The employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except 
where the employer has denied liability for the injury.  Section 85.27.  Holbert v. 
Townsend Engineering Co., Thirty-second Biennial Report of the Industrial 
Commissioner 78 (Review-Reopening, October 1975).   
By challenging the employer’s choice of treatment – and seeking alternate care – 
claimant assumes the burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable.  See Iowa 
R. App. P. 6.904(3)(e);; Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 
1995).  Determining what care is reasonable under the statute is a question of 
fact.  Id.  The employer’s obligation turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not 
desirability.  Id.; Harned v. Farmland Foods, Inc., 331 N.W.2d 98 (Iowa 1983).  In Pirelli-
Armstrong Tire Co. v. Reynolds, 562 N.W.2d 433 (Iowa 1997), the court approvingly 
quoted Bowles v. Los Lunas Schools, 109 N.M. 100, 781 P.2d 1178 (App. 1989):   
[T]he words “reasonable” and “adequate” appear to describe the same 
standard.   
[The New Mexico rule] requires the employer to provide a certain 
standard of care and excuses the employer from any obligation to provide

other services only if that standard is met.  We construe the terms 
"reasonable” and “adequate” as describing care that is both appropriate to 
the injury and sufficient to bring the worker to maximum recovery.   
The commissioner is justified in ordering alternate care when employer-
authorized care has not been effective, and evidence shows that such care is “inferior or 
less extensive” care than other available care requested by the employee.  Long, 528 
N.W.2d at 124; Pirelli-Armstrong Tire Co., 562 N.W.2d at 437.  
The record indicates claimant had pain in her left thumb when she last treated 
with Dr. Chimenti on September 25, 2023.  (Ex. 2, p. 3)  Claimant credibly testified she 
has had increasing pain in the left thumb joint.  Dr. Chimenti’s September 25, 2023, 
note indicates claimant could return to him as needed.  Claimant credibly testified that 
she understood from talking with Dr. Chimenti she could return to him if she has 
increased pain in her thumb joint.  Claimant credibly testified she has increased pain in 
her thumb joint.  Claimant has requested a return visit with Dr. Chimenti regarding her 
thumb pain.  Defendant has denied authorization for a return visit and instead is 
arranging an IME for claimant. 
Dr. Chimenti indicated claimant could return for follow-up care if needed.  The 
record indicates claimant had pain in her thumb and hand back in September of 2023.  
Claimant credibly testified the pain has increased.  Dr. Chimenti did find claimant at 
MMI.  While claimant may be at MMI regarding the functional rating for her thumb and 
hand, it is appropriate for defendant to authorize further care for claimant’s thumb pain.  
An IME is not a substitute for medical treatment.  An IME is not medical care.  
Muhlbauer v. Townhouse Pizza, File No. 5064523 (Alternate Medical Care Dec. July 26, 
2018) 
Defendant has not offered claimant further treatment for her thumb pain.  
Defendant’s denial of further care, given this record, is found to be unreasonable.  
Based on the record, claimant has carried her burden of proof she is entitled to the 
requested alternate medical care consisting of a return visit with Dr. Chimenti to provide 
treatment for her continued thumb pain. 
ORDER  
Claimant’s petition for alternate medical care is granted.  Defendant shall 
authorize and pay for a return visit to Dr. Chimenti to treat claimant’s pain.

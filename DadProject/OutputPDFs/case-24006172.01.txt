Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care on March 28, 2025. The 
application sought authorization of a left knee arthroscopic medial meniscal repair as 
ordered by Dr. Steven Brown for Tuesday, April 15, 2025, with subsequent care and 
physical therapy as ordered by Dr. Brown. Defendants filed an Answer admitting the 
claim.   
A telephonic hearing was scheduled to occur on April 9, 2025, at 10:30 a.m. At 
the start of the alternate care hearing, counsel notified the undersigned that they were 
able to reach an agreement and that a consent order should be entered.  Under this 
consent order, defendants will authorize treatment with Dr. Steven Brown for claimant’s 
May 19, 2024, work injury. The parties have requested a Consent Order be entered in 
lieu of hearing. 
 After discussion between the parties and an agreement to provide the requested 
care, there is no justiciable issue for hearing and determination. Both parties agree that 
a consent order is appropriate, and a formal hearing is unnecessary to resolve the 
contested case dispute. 
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties' positions and acceptable to both parties. A full alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.

THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
Claimant’s petition for alternate medical care is granted. Defendants shall 
authorize treatment with Dr. Steven Brown for claimant’s May 19, 2024, work injury, 
inclusive of the Tuesday, April 15, 2025, surgery and any other necessary, causally 
related medical treatment. 
The hearing scheduled for April 9, 2025, is formally cancelled.

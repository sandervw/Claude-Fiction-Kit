STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
On June 26, 2019, the claimant, Scott Cox, filed a petition in arbitration, seeking 
workers’ compensation benefits from the defendant/employer, Chad Carlson Trucking, 
LLC (“Carlson Trucking”).  On July 23, 2020, Cox filed a proof of service showing he 
served Carlson Trucking with the petition by certified mail on July 19, 2019.  Carlson 
Trucking did not file an answer in the case.   
On August 26, 2020, Cox filed a motion for default against Carlson Trucking.  
Cox served Carlson Trucking with a copy of the motion and notice of intent to file for 
default.  Carlson Trucking did not file an appearance, answer, or resistance to the 
motion for default.  On September 15, 2020, Deputy Workers’ Compensation 
Commissioner William Grell filed a ruling entering default against Carlson Trucking and 
scheduling a hearing for consideration and award of relief as may be warranted by the 
evidence on November 13, 2020, at 10:30 a.m.  The ruling closed the record for 
submitting evidence on behalf of Carlson Trucking.  Carlson Trucking did not move to 
set aside the default or take any additional action on the ruling.  The ruling became final 
agency action.   
On November 13, 2020, a hearing for consideration and award of appropriate 
relief was held before Deputy Workers’ Compensation Commissioner Heather Palmer.  
Attorney Richard Schmidt appeared on behalf of Cox.  Cox appeared and testified.  
Exhibits 1 through 5 were admitted into the record.  The hearing was recorded through 
digital means pursuant to rule 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.49.  A transcript of the 
hearing will not be prepared by this agency.  If any party wishes to appeal this decision, 
that party is charged with requesting a copy of the digital audio recording and obtaining 
a transcript to be prepared and field by a certified shorthand reporter.  Cox submitted a 
hearing report identifying the issues to be decided, which was received into the record.

FINDINGS OF FACT 
In March 2017, Cox commenced full-time employment with Carlson Trucking as 
a truck driver.  Cox received a weekly salary of $1,200.00.  Cox transported and 
delivered motorcycles for Carlson Trucking. 
On June 26, 2017, Cox smashed his right hand between two metal racks that 
were holding motorcycles.  Cox reported his work injury to Carlson Trucking and he 
sought medical treatment.  Carlson Trucking did not offer Cox any medical treatment or 
weekly workers’ compensation benefits.   
Cox received ongoing care with Jason Mickels, M.D., in the orthopedic clinic of 
Community Memorial Hospital.  (Exhibit 2, page 3)  Dr. Mickels ordered x-rays which 
revealed Cox had sustained fractures of the metacarpal necks of the long, ring and 
small fingers on his right hand.  (Ex. 2, p. 3)  Dr. Mickels assessed Cox with a right hand 
crush injury with fractures of the long, ring, and small fingers through the metacarpal

necks, prescribed a splint, restricted Cox from working, and ordered Cox to perform 
range of motion exercises.  (Ex. 2, p. 3)   
Cox continued to treat with Dr. Mickels.  (Ex. 2, pp. 3-4)  On August 23, 2017, Dr. 
Mickels released Cox to return to work with a restriction of not performing heavier tasks

side of his right hand when performing heavy labor.  (Ex. 2, p. 4)   
On June 20, 2018, Dr. Mickels opined Cox had reached maximum medical 
improvement and released Cox to full duty, noting his post-traumatic Dupuytren’s had 
not progressed.  (Ex. 2, p. 4)  Cox incurred $4,392.25 in medical bills as a result of the 
work injury.  Cox testified he did not have any problems with his right hand before the 
work injury. 
On November 10, 2020, Sunil Bansal, M.D., an occupational medicine physician 
prepared a report for Cox after examining him.  (Ex. 2)  Dr. Bansal assessed Cox with 
right third, fourth, and fifth metacarpal neck fractures.  (Ex. 2, p. 6)  Using the Guides to 
the Evaluation of Permanent Impairment (AMA Press, 5th Ed. 2001) (“AMA Guides”), 
Dr. Bansal opined “I would assign a 15% digit impairment to the fourth digit, a 5% digit 
impairment to the fifth digit, and a 3% digit impairment to the third digit.  The total would 
be a 4% hand impairment, or a 4% upper extremity impairment.”  (Ex. 2, p. 6) 
(emphasis in original).  Dr. Bansal assigned a permanent restriction to avoid forceful 
gripping with the right hand, and noted Cox’s post-traumatic Dupuytren’s disease may 
require a surgical release in the future if it progresses.  (Ex. 2, p. 7)   
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
I. 
Applicable Law 
This case involves the issues of extent of disability, recovery of medical bills, and 
recovery of cost under Iowa Code sections 85.27, 85.34, and 86.40.  In 2017, the Iowa 
Legislature enacted changes to Iowa Code chapters 85, 86, and 535 effecting workers’ 
compensation cases.  2017 Iowa Acts chapter 23 (amending Iowa Code sections 85.16, 
85.18, 85.23, 85.26, 85.33, 85.34, 85.39, 85.45, 85.70, 85.71, 86.26, 86.39, 86.42, and 
535.3).  Under 2017 Iowa Acts chapter 23 section 24, the changes to Iowa Code 
sections 85.16, 85.18, 85.23, 85.26, 85.33, 85.34, 85.39, 85.71, 86.26, 86.39, and 86.42 
apply to injuries occurring on or after the effective date of the Act.  This case involves an 
injury occurring after July 1, 2017, therefore, the provisions of the new statute involving 
extent of disability under Iowa Code section 85.34 apply to this case.   
The calculation of interest is governed by Sanchez v. Tyson, File No. 5052008 
(Ruling on Defendant’s Motion to Enlarge, Reconsider, or Amend Appeal Decision Re: 
Interest Rate Issue), which holds interest for all weekly benefits payable and not paid 
when due which accrued before July 1, 2017, is payable at the rate of ten percent; all

payable at an annual rate equal to the one-year treasury constant maturity published by

II. 
Extent of Disability 
This case involves an injury to Cox’s right hand.  A disability to the hand is 
classified as scheduled member disability.  Iowa Code § 85.34(2)(l).  Under the 
schedule, 190 weeks is the maximum number of weeks of compensation for loss of a 
hand.  Id.  Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(x) provides when determining functional 
disability under Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(l), “the extent of loss or percentage of 
permanent impairment shall be determined solely by utilizing the guides to the 
evaluation of permanent impairment, published by the American medical association, as 
adopted by the workers’ compensation commissioner by rule pursuant to chapter 17A.”  
The Commissioner has adopted the Guides to the Evaluation of Permanent Impairment 
(AMA Press, 5th Ed. 2001).  876 IAC 2.4.  Dr. Bansal is the only physician to provide an 
impairment rating in this case.  Dr. Bansal assigned a four percent impairment rating to 
Cox’s right hand.  Under the statute, Cox is entitled to 7.6 weeks of permanent partial 
disability benefits at the rate of $747.01 per week, commencing on June 20, 2018. 
III. 
Medical Bills 
An employer is required to furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, 
osteopathic, chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance, hospital 
services and supplies, and transportation expenses for all conditions compensable 
under the workers’ compensation law.  Iowa Code § 85.27(1).  The employer has the 
right to choose the provider of care, except when the employer has denied liability for 
the injury.  Id.  “The treatment must be offered promptly and be reasonably suited to 
treat the injury without undue inconvenience to the employee.”  Id. § 85.27(4).  If the 
employee is dissatisfied with the care, the employee should communicate the basis for 
the dissatisfaction to the employer.  Id.  If the employer and employee cannot agree on 
alternate care, the commissioner “may, upon application and reasonable proofs of the 
necessity therefor, allow and order other care.”  Id.  The statute requires the employer to 
furnish reasonable medical care.  Id. § 85.27(4); Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 
122, 124 (Iowa 1995) (noting “[t]he employer’s obligation under the statute turns on the 
question of reasonable necessity, not desirability”).  The Iowa Supreme Court has held 
the employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except when the employer 
has denied liability for the injury, or has abandoned care.  Iowa Code § 85.27(4); Bell 
Bros. Heating & Air Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 N.W.2d 193, 204 (Iowa 2010).  Cox 
incurred $4,392.25 in medical bills as a result of the work injury.  I find the medical bills 
were for treatment reasonable and necessary as a result of the work injury.  Cox is 
entitled to recover the medical bills he incurred in this case.

IV. 
Costs 
Cox seeks to recover $100.00 for the filing fee and the $1,764.00 cost of Dr. 
Bansal’s report.  Iowa Code section 86.40, provides, “[a]ll costs incurred in the hearing 
before the commissioner shall be taxed in the discretion of the commissioner.”  Rule 
876 IAC 4.33(6), expressly allows for the recovery of the filing fee and the reasonable 
cost of two doctors’ or practitioners’ reports.  Using my discretion I find Cox is entitled to 
recover the $100.00 filing fee and the $1,764.00 cost of Dr. Bansal’s report. 
ORDER 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED, THAT: 
Defendant shall pay the claimant seven point six (7.6) weeks of permanent 
partial disability benefits at the rate of seven hundred forty-seven and 01/100 dollars 
($747.01) per week, commencing on June 20, 2018.   
Defendant shall pay accrued weekly benefits in a lump sum together with interest 
payable at an annual rate equal to the one-year treasury constant maturity published by 
the federal reserve in the most recent H15 report settled as of the date of injury, plus 
two percent.  Sanchez v. Tyson, File No. 5052008 (Apr. 23, 2018 Ruling on Defendant’s 
Motion to Enlarge, Reconsider, or Amend Appeal Decision Re:  Interest Rate Issue). 
Defendant is responsible for the claimant’s medical bills totaling four thousand 
three hundred ninety-two and 25/100 dollars ($4,392.25).   
Defendant shall reimburse the claimant one hundred and 00/100 dollars 
($100.00) for the filing fee and one thousand seven hundred sixty-four and 00/100 
dollars ($1,764.00) for Dr. Bansal’s report.   
Defendant shall file subsequent reports of injury as required by this agency 
pursuant to rules 876 IAC 3.1(2) and 876 IAC 11.7.

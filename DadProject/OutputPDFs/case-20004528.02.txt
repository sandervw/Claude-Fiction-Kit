On March 28, 2024, claimant filed a petition for alternate medical care. Hearing 
was scheduled to take place on April 9, 2024. The petition alleges claimant sustained a 
work-related injury on April 7, 2020. Claimant is seeking authorization for treatment of 
hypertension, GERD, and a nodule on his lung. 
Counsel and the undersigned had an off-record conversation prior to the start of 
hearing. Attorney Eric Lanham appeared for defendants. He indicated that defendants 
admit liability for the treatment related to hypertension and GERD, and Kirk Kilburg, 
M.D., is the authorized treating physician. With respect to the nodule on claimant’s lung, 
defendants currently deny liability pending further investigation into causation.  
The parties agreed that defendants’ authorization of Dr. Kilburg for treatment of 
the hypertension and GERD resolved the issues for hearing. As such, the hearing was 
determined to be unnecessary. The parties requested entry of a consent order to 
confirm and document the agreement. 
With respect to the denied lung nodule, alternate care claims are limited to 
situations in which liability is not at issue. R. R. Donnelly & Sons v. Barnett, 670 N.W.2d 
190, 197-198 (Iowa 2003). As such, the portion of the petition for alternate care related 
to claimant’s lung nodule is dismissed. 
"[T]he employer has no right to choose the medical care when compensability is 
contested.” Bell Bros. Heating and Air Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 N.W.2d 193, 204 
(Iowa 2010). Further, when compensability is contested, "the employer cannot assert an

authorization defense in response to a subsequent claim by the employee for the 
expenses of the alternate medical care." R. R. Donnelly, 670 N.W.2d at 197-198. 
Ultimately, therefore, defendants are precluded from asserting an authorization 
defense as to any future treatment related to the lung nodule during the period of denial, 
and defendants lose the right to control the medical care claimant seeks for this 
condition during this period of denial. Brewer-Strong v. HNI Corp., 913 N.W.2d 235 
(Iowa 2018); Bell Bros., 779 N.W.2d at 204. 
As a result, claimant may obtain reasonable medical care from any provider for 
treatment related to the lung nodule, but at claimant's expense, and claimant may seek 
reimbursement for such care using regular claim proceedings before this agency. Haack 
v. Von Hoffman Graphics, File No. 1268172 (App. July 31, 2002); Kindhart v. Fort Des 
Moines Hotel, I Iowa Industrial Comm'r Decisions No. 3, 611 (App. March 27, 1985). 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED:  
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, defendants will authorize Kirk Kilburg, 
M.D., to provide claimant with treatment related to hypertension and GERD. 
The portion of the petition for alternate care related to the nodule on claimant’s 
lung is dismissed without prejudice. If claimant seeks to recover the charges incurred in 
obtaining care for the condition for which defendants denied liability, defendants are 
barred from asserting lack of authorization as a defense to those charges during the 
period of their denial.

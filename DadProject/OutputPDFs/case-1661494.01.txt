STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
Kevin Caldwell, claimant, filed a petition in arbitration seeking workers’ 
compensation benefits from Arconic, Inc., employer, and Indemnity Insurance Company 
of North America, insurance carrier, as defendants. On November 22, 2023, the parties 
requested to submit the case on the hearing report and exhibits, without a hearing; the 
parties’ request was granted.       
The parties filed and signed a hearing report.  On the hearing report the parties 
entered into various stipulations.  All of those stipulations were accepted and are hereby 
incorporated into this arbitration decision and no factual or legal issues relative to the 
parties’ stipulations will be raised or discussed in this decision.  The parties are now 
bound by their stipulations.  
The evidentiary record includes Joint Exhibits 1-4, Claimant’s Exhibits 1-4, and 
Defendants’ Exhibits A-D.  All exhibits were received without objection.         
The parties submitted briefs on December 22, 2023, at which time the case was 
fully submitted to the undersigned.     
STIPULATIONS

FINDINGS OF FACT 
The undersigned, having considered all the evidence and testimony in the 
record, finds: 
On September 14, 2022, claimant, Kevin Caldwell, filed an original notice and 
petition alleging he sustained an injury to his left lower extremity which arose out of and 
in the course of his employment with Arconic, Inc., on February 21, 2019.  The petition 
also alleged a claim against the Second Injury Fund of Iowa (SIF).  Specifically, he 
alleged a first qualifying injury of November 19, 1998, involving his right lower extremity.  
(Agency File, Petition)  On November 2, 2023, the agency was notified that the claimant 
had settled the SIF portion of the case.  (Agency File, Notice of Intent to Settle)   
The dispute in this case involves the amount defendants should reimburse the 
claimant for Dr. Kuhnlein’s independent medical examination (IME).  There is no dispute 
that the amount Dr. Kuhnlein charged was reasonable.  Rather, the dispute is what 
portion of the bill is for the left lower extremity work injury versus the right lower 
extremity SIF claim.

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
It should be noted that the parties submitted their briefs prior to the recent 
decisions from the Iowa Supreme Court which address IME reimbursements.  See Mid-
American Constr. LLC v. Sandlin, 2024 WL 500652 (Iowa, February 9, 2024); P.M. 
Lattner Manufacturing Co. v. Rife, 2024 WL 500665 (Iowa, February 9, 2024).  In his 
brief claimant argues that he should be reimbursed for his IME in part under Iowa Code 
section 85.39 and in part as a cost under Iowa Code section 86.40 and 876 IAC 4.33.  
However, in those recent cases the Iowa Supreme Court clarified that the costs of an 
IME include the examination, records review, physical examination and testing, and the 
written report.  In light of these cases, claimant no longer needs to rely on his 
arguments that a portion of the IME should be reimbursed under Code section 86.40 
and rule 4.33.  Therefore, this decision will focus on Iowa Code section 85.39.  
Iowa Code section 85.39(2) addresses reimbursement for IMEs.  It reads: 
If an evaluation of permanent disability has been made by a 
physician retained by the employer and the employee believes this 
evaluation to be too low, the employee shall, upon application to the 
commissioner and upon delivery of a copy of the application to the employer 
and its insurance carrier, be reimbursed by the employer the reasonable fee 
for a subsequent examination by a physician of the employee’s own choice, 
and reasonably necessary transportation expenses incurred for the 
examination. The physician chosen by the employee has the right to confer 
with and obtain from the employer-retained physician sufficient history of 
the injury to make a proper examination. An employer is only liable to 
reimburse an employee for the cost of an examination conducted pursuant 
to this subsection if the injury for which the employee is being examined is 
determined to be compensable under this chapter or chapter 85A or 85B. 
An employer is not liable for the cost of such an examination if the injury for 
which the employee is being examined is determined not to be a 
compensable injury. A determination of the reasonableness of a fee for an 
examination made pursuant to this subsection, shall be based on the typical 
fee charged by a medical provider to perform an impairment rating in the 
local area where the examination is conducted. 
Iowa Code section 85.39(2).

Claimant stipulated that defendants are only responsible for reimbursing the 
portion of Dr. Kuhnlein’s IME that relates to the left knee work injury.  (Hearing Report, 
numbered paragraph 8)   
Based on the above findings of fact, I conclude that the $161.50 charge for the 
left knee x-rays is related to the work injury.  I further conclude the $126.40 charge for 
the x-rays of the right ankle is not related to the work injury.  Thus, defendants are 
responsible for the left knee x-ray charges in the amount of $161.50. 
We now turn to the remaining charges which are for IME exam time and IME 
report time; these charges amount to $4,325.50.  Unfortunately, the invoice does not 
specify or delineate in any way which charges are for the work-related left lower 
extremity versus the right lower extremity.    
Claimant admits that Dr. Kuhnlein did look at the claimant’s right leg injury for 
purposes of his SIF claim.  Claimant argues that the IME bill should “be reduced by no 
more than 10% given the small amount of time and the report that involves the second 
injury fund.”  (Claimant’s Brief, p. 5)  Claimant’s argument is not persuasive.  A review of 
the IME report demonstrates that more than 10 percent of the report involves claimant’s 
right lower extremity.   
Defendants contend “the fairest and most reasonable way to apportion the IME 
reimbursement is to divide the IME exam and report time in half.”  (Defendants’ brief, p. 
3)  On April 4, 2023, defendants voluntarily reimbursed claimant in the amount of 
$2,324.00.  (Defendants’ Exhibit C, p. 5)  Defendants state this amount represents one-
half of the IME exam and IME report time, plus $161.50 for the left knee x-rays.  (Def. 
Ex. C, p. 4)   
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established ordinarily has 
the burden of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence.  Iowa R. App. P. 
6.904(3)(e).  
In this case, the burden is on the claimant to show what portion of Dr. Kuhnlein’s 
charges are related to the left knee work injury.  Unfortunately, Dr. Kuhnlein, the doctor 
selected by the claimant, failed to indicate what portion of his fee is related to the work 
injury versus the right lower extremity.  Claimant has failed to demonstrate what portion 
of the IME exam and IME report time is related to the work injury.  Defendants’ rationale 
that Dr. Kuhnlein evaluated two body parts, and therefore, defendants should only be 
responsible for one-half of the IME report and exam portion is reasonable.  I conclude 
claimant has failed to demonstrate by a preponderance of the evidence that he is 
entitled to any reimbursement beyond what defendants have voluntarily paid.   
Finally, claimant is seeking an assessment of costs.  (Hearing Report, numbered 
paragraph 11)  Claimant failed to attach an itemized list of costs and proof of payment 
to the hearing report so it is not clear what costs claimant intended to seek.  
Regardless, costs are to be assessed at the discretion of the Iowa Workers’ 
Compensation Commissioner or the deputy hearing the case.  I find that claimant was

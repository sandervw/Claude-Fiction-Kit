On July 19, 2022, claimant filed an application for alternate medical care under 
Iowa Code section 85.27 and rule 876 IAC 4.48.  A hearing was scheduled for August 1, 
2022.  All parties were given proper notice.  
Counsel for both parties submitted the case without testimony. After some 
discussion, the parties came to an agreement. The agreement is as follows: 
For the complaints of incontinence and/or bladder control issues as well as 
tension headaches, the claimant will be referred back to Dr. Smith and Dr. Klein 
along with any other doctors she has previously seen or that defendants have 
made contact with in connection with her accepted work injuries. Defendants’ 
counsel and/or insurer will specifically authorize these physicians and medical 
providers to treat both the issues of incontinence and/or lack of bladder control 
along with the tension headaches. 
Defendants have denied responsibility for the blood pressure complaints. 
Claimant is allowed to pursue care for the blood pressure complaints on her own 
and the defendants shall be barred for any future lack of authorization defense 
should claimant attempt to obtain reimbursement for medical costs and expenses 
related to high blood pressure.

On November 1, 2022, claimant filed a petition for alternate medical care.  A 
telephonic hearing on the petition was scheduled to occur on November 10, 2022, at 
1:00 p.m.  Prior to the alternate medical care hearing, counsel notified the undersigned 
that the parties were able to reach an amicable agreement and that a consent order 
should be entered.   
With respect to the specific alternate medical care requested, Ms. Perez sought 
authorization of the following treatment recommended by Dr. Irving Wolfe: gabapentin, 
greater occipital nerve block, neuropsychological evaluation, prism glasses prescribed 
by Dr. Triebel, audiologic evaluation, and physical therapy. 
At this time, defendants have denied liability and authorization of the requested 
treatment consisting of neuropsychological evaluation, prism glasses, and audiological 
evaluation. However, defendants consent to and agree to prompt authorization of 
gabapentin as prescribed by Dr. Irving Wolfe, right-sided greater occipital nerve block to 
be performed by Dr. Wolfe, and continuation of physical therapy for three weeks, two 
times per week (total of 6 visits) at UnityPoint Clinic Sports Medicine and Concussion 
Center – Urbandale as prescribed by Dr. Wolfe.     
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim.  Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties.  A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.

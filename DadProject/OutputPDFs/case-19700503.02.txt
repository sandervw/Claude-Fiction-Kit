Claimant filed an Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care with this 
agency on May 21, 2020.  In the Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care, 
claimant requested defendants be ordered to authorize medical treatment.       
Based on emails, dated June 2, 2020, from counsel from both parties, and emails 
exchanged between the parties, defendants agree to authorize treatment for claimant. 
Specifically, defendants indicate they would authorize a repeat arthroscopic surgery for 
claimant with Thomas Atteberry, M.D.   
In an effort to avoid an unnecessary hearing, and yet protect the interests of their 
clients, the parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendants to provide the 
repeat arthroscopic surgery for claimant with Dr. Atteberry.         
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim.  Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties.  A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.             
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:              
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties prior to the scheduled June 3, 2020, 
alternate medical care hearing, defendants shall authorize and provide claimant with a

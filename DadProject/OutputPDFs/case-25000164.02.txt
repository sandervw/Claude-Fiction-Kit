On June 26, 2025, claimant filed an application for alternate medical care under 
Iowa Code section 85.27 and rule 876 IAC 4.48. Defendants filed an answer on July 7, 
2025, admitting liability for the left knee. A hearing was held on July 8, 2025. All parties 
were given proper notice.  
 
The hearing was digitally recorded. No testimony was taken at hearing, but 
counsel provided oral arguments. The record consists of Claimant’s Exhibit 1, 
containing 6 pages. 
 
Claimant sustained an injury to his left lower extremity on December 23, 2024, 
which arose out of and in the course of employment. Claimant is dissatisfied with the 
medical treatment offered by the defendants. Specifically, the authorized treating 
provider, Lisa Coester, M.D., recommended physical therapy for claimant on May 15, 
2025. (Cl. Ex. 1, p. 6) Claimant’s attorney requested authorization for Dr. Coester’s 
physical therapy recommendation on May 30, 2025. Defense counsel advised physical 
therapy was in the process of being approved on June 2, 2025. (Cl. Ex. 1, p. 4) 
Claimant’s attorney followed up regarding the physical therapy on June 10, June 16, 
and June 26. (Cl. Ex. 1, pp. 1-3) On June 16, 2025, defense counsel again stated 
physical therapy was in the process of being approved and set up. Defense counsel 
stated at hearing that he has continued to work with his client to get physical therapy 
approved and set up, but as of the date of hearing, no appointment has been 
scheduled.

Claimant requests an order directing defendants to schedule an appointment for 
physical therapy to take place within seven (7) days of the date of hearing. If no 
appointment has been secured to take place within seven days, claimant requests to 
seek physical therapy with a provider of his choosing. Claimant has proven that 
defendants have unreasonably delayed treatment by failing to secure physical therapy 
pursuant to the authorized physician’s order. As such, his petition for alternate medical 
care was orally granted at hearing. 
 
The decision will be further reproduced in typewritten form only if the decision is 
appealed, and the parties are advised to follow the procedures outlined under the 
administrative code. Any rights of appeal will run from July 8, 2025, the date the 
decision was dictated into the record. 
Pursuant to the Commissioner’s July 21, 2023 Order, the undersigned has 
been delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical 
care proceeding. Therefore, this ruling is designated final agency action and any 
appeal of the decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code 
section 17A. 
ORDER 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted. 
Defendants shall secure an appointment for physical therapy, pursuant to 
the authorized treating physician’s order, to take place within seven (7) days of 
the date of this order. 
If physical therapy has not been scheduled with the first appointment to 
take place within seven (7) days of the date of this order, claimant is then free to 
direct his own treatment, with respect to physical therapy only, at defendants’ 
expense.

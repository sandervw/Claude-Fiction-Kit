Claimant appeals from an August 13, 2025 arbitration decision.  Defendant 
responds to the appeal and urges it be fully affirmed.  The presiding deputy 
commissioner held an arbitration hearing in this case on March 20, 2025, and the case 
was fully submitted before the deputy on May 2, 2025. 
In the arbitration decision, the deputy commissioner found that claimant failed to 
prove his current right shoulder condition is causally related to the admitted work injury 
and failed to prove the November 28, 2022 work injury caused permanent disability.  
The deputy commissioner also denied claimant’s claim for additional healing period 
benefits, payment of past medical expenses, and his request for costs. 
On appeal, claimant contends the deputy commissioner erred when he accepted 
the opinions of Abdul Foad, M.D., and Tiffany Kalita, N.P., over the opinions offered by 
Brian Crites, M.D., James Hoffman, M.D., and Kristyn Darmafall, M.D.  Claimant further 
contends that the deputy’s analysis is legally erroneous because he viewed the work 
injury as a traumatic injury and did not recognize the claim as a cumulative injury claim 
pursuant to the opinions of claimant’s medical experts. 
Defendant responds to the appeal.  Defendant asserts that the deputy 
commissioner accurately accepted the opinions of Dr. Foad and appropriately denied 
claimant’s claims for additional healing period, permanent disability, and past medical 
expenses.  Defendant urges that the arbitration decision should be affirmed in its 
entirety. 
Pursuant to Iowa Code section 10A.321 and section 17A.15(3), I performed a de 
novo review of the evidentiary record and the detailed arguments of the parties.  Those 
portions of the proposed arbitration decision pertaining to issues not raised on appeal 
are adopted as part of this appeal decision.  With respect to the issues properly raised 
on appeal, I affirm the arbitration decision with the following analysis:

I concur with the deputy commissioner’s adoption of the opinions of Dr. Foad as 
the most credible and convincing medical opinions in this case.  Claimant challenges 
those opinions and the arbitration decision as legally erroneous because the deputy 
commissioner noted that claimant’s work injury was traumatic in nature.  Claimant 
contends that his injury was cumulative in nature and that his repetitive work activities 
were a substantial factor in bringing about claimant’s injury. 
I disagree that the deputy’s analysis is legally erroneous.  However, I accept the 
argument that claimant’s injury can be asserted and should alternatively be evaluated 
as a potential cumulative injury.  Dr. Foad considered that possibility, including the 
potential that claimant’s repetitive work injuries could be a substantial factor in bringing 
about his right shoulder injuries.  Dr. Foad opined: 
Mr. Alvarez had a pre-existing chronic rotator cuff degenerative pathology 
that was temporarily aggravated by the work injury description.  There is 
no significant factor that is unique to his workplace than what he does at 
home or what he has as pre-existing risk factors for which I would say 
were a significant cause to his rotator cuff and biceps tendon pathology.  
What most likely happened is that he may have temporarily aggravated or 
perhaps had some further tear to an already chronically progressive 
tendinopathic rotator cuff tear and degenerative biceps tendon tear that 
was progressively worsening with time and age, diabetes and smoking.  
His work injury from 11/29/22 [sic] did not accelerate any tears or 
pathology beyond normal age-related progression.  In other words, his 
described work injury is more likely than not, not a significant contributing 
factor to his current state of ill-being. 
Defendant’s Exhibit D, page 23.   
Dr. Foad clearly considered the possibility of a cumulative injury and opined that 
claimant’s work activities were not a substantial factor in his current condition.  Like the 
deputy commissioner, I accept that opinion as the most convincing and credible in this 
record.  I conclude that claimant failed to prove by a preponderance of the evidence that 
his current condition is causally related to his November 28, 2022 work injury or that the 
work injury was a substantial factor in causing claimant’s current condition.  Accordingly, 
I also conclude that claimant failed to prove by a preponderance of the evidence that 
the November 28, 2022 work injury caused additional healing period or permanent 
disability under either a traumatic or cumulative injury analysis.  Therefore, I affirm the 
arbitration decision in its entirety. 
ORDER 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
The August 13, 2025 arbitration decision is affirmed in its entirety with the above 
additional analysis.

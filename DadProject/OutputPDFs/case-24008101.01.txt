The claimant, Hermenegildo Nunez Rosas, filed petitions for arbitration seeking 
workers’ compensation benefits from Chesterman Company, the employer, and Zurich 
American Insurance Company, the insurance carrier, both as defendants. File No. 
24008101.01 has an alleged date of injury of March 6, 2022. File No. 22013637.01 has 
an alleged date of injury of March 28, 2022.  
 
These consolidated matters came for arbitration hearing on June 4, 2025. Prior 
to the commencement of hearing, the parties indicated that claimant’s left shoulder 
injury was stipulated with respect to the March 28, 2022 date of injury, and defendants 
had already paid the maximum impairment rating. There were no issues regarding 
temporary or permanent disability benefits. The only issues remaining were 
reimbursement of claimant’s independent medical evaluation (IME) with Thomas 
Atteberry, M.D.; future medical care; and costs. 
 
Defense counsel indicated defendants would voluntarily reimburse the IME fee 
pursuant to Iowa Code section 84.39. Defense counsel also indicated defendants would 
voluntarily authorize claimant to return to Ryan Meis, M.D., who was the authorized 
treating physician for claimant’s left shoulder injury. Defendants also agreed to 
voluntarily reimburse claimant’s costs related to the filing fee and certified mail. 
Claimant and his counsel were placed in a digital breakout room via Zoom, along 
with the interpreter, to discuss whether the hearing was necessary. After discussion, 
claimant agreed that hearing was not needed if defendants agreed to authorize him to 
return to Dr. Meis. As such, the parties consented to submit this case on the written

FINDINGS OF FACT 
Based on the parties’ stipulations contained in the hearing report, as well as 
discussion with counsel on June 4, 2025, I find that claimant sustained a scheduled 
member injury to his left shoulder on March 28, 2022, which arose out of and in the 
course of his employment. The injury was a cause of temporary disability during a 
period of recovery, as well as a cause of permanent disability. The commencement date 
for permanent partial disability benefits is June 23, 2023.  
 
Prior to hearing, defendants paid 56 weeks of permanent partial disability 
benefits at the stipulated rate of $240.38 per week. There is no claim for additional 
temporary or permanent disability benefits at this time. 
 
Claimant had an IME with Thomas Atteberry, M.D., on April 5, 2024. (Claimant’s 
Exhibit 1, p. 1) The total cost for the IME was $1,500.00. (Cl. Ex. 3, p. 26) Pursuant to 
the agreement of the parties and Iowa Code section 85.39, defendants will reimburse 
the total amount of Dr. Atteberry’s IME. 
 
Defendants directed claimant to Ryan Meis, M.D., for medical care for his left 
shoulder injury. (See Joint Exhibit 3) Pursuant to the agreement of the parties and Iowa 
Code section 85.27, claimant is entitled to reasonable, causally related medical care for 
his left shoulder injury. Defendants agree and will authorize claimant to return to Dr. 
Meis for additional evaluation and treatment of his ongoing symptoms. 
 
With respect to costs, claimant requests reimbursement of the filing fee and 
related certified mail costs for both files, as well as the cost of obtaining a narrative 
report from Dr. Meis, which is dated June 23, 2023. (Jt. Ex. 3, p. 41; Cl. Ex. 3, p. 19) I 
find the filing fees and related mailing costs are reimbursable expenses. The narrative 
report from Dr. Meis was obtained prior to defendants seeking an impairment rating. 
However, defendants sought the rating on the same date claimant was placed at 
maximum medical improvement (MMI). (Jt. Ex. 3, p. 43) There was no prolonged delay 
or period of inaction on defendants’ part that would justify claimant needing to seek the 
rating on his own. As such, I decline to award the cost of Dr. Meis’s narrative report. 
 
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established ordinarily has 
the burden of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence. Iowa R. App. P. 
6.904(3)(e). I found March 28, 2022 to be the correct date of injury based on the parties’ 
stipulations. The parties’ other stipulations and further agreements reached prior to 
hearing left only the issue of costs to be determined. 
 
 
A stipulation made by the parties cannot be rejected without prior notice to the 
parties and an opportunity to be heard and present evidence. Origer v. Friesth Constr., 
File No. 1282038 (App. December 20, 2002). I find no compelling reason to reject the

parties’ stipulations contained within the hearing reports. As such, the stipulations are 
accepted and binding on the parties. 
Under Iowa Code section 85.3(1), an employer is responsible for “any and all 
personal injuries sustained by an employee arising out of and in the course of the 
employment . . .” That section requires employers to provide, secure, and pay 
compensation according to the provisions of Chapter 85 of the Iowa Code. The parties 
have stipulated that claimant sustained an injury arising out of and in the course of his 
employment on March 28, 2022. As such, defendants are responsible for providing, 
securing, and paying compensation according to the provisions of Chapter 85 of the 
Iowa Code. 
 
Pursuant to Iowa Code section 85.39 and the agreement of the parties, claimant 
is entitled to reimbursement in the amount of $1500.00 for Dr. Atteberry’s IME. Pursuant 
to Iowa Code section 85.27, claimant is entitled to reasonable medical care for the 
work-related injury. Defendants have agreed to authorize claimant to return to Dr. Meis 
for additional evaluation and treatment of his ongoing left shoulder symptoms. 
 
Iowa Administrative Rule 876-4.33 states that costs are assessed at the 
discretion of the deputy. Using my discretion, I find it is appropriate to award costs for 
the filing fees and related certified mailing costs to claimant. However, I found it 
unnecessary for claimant to seek the narrative report from Dr. Meis, as defendants 
wrote to Dr. Meis for an impairment rating on the same day he placed claimant at MMI. 
As such, I decline to award the cost of Dr. Meis’s narrative report.

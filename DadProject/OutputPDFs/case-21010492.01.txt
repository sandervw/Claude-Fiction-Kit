This is a contested case concerning alternate medical care, proceeding under 
Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48 is 
invoked by claimant, Justin Booth.  The alternate medical care claim was scheduled for 
telephone hearing on November 1, 2021, at 8:30 a.m.   
 
Prior to the commencement of the hearing, on November 1, 2021, the parties 
and the undersigned discussed the entry of a consent order in this matter.  The parties 
indicated that the defendant authorized care as of November 1, 2021, with Ian Lin, M.D. 
at Des Moines Orthopaedic Surgeons, P.C.  The defendant agreed that care with Dr. 
Lin, including a potential surgery to the right shoulder, is authorized.  The defendant 
wished to have the claimant see Dr. Lin again and indicated that a follow-up visit with 
Dr. Lin would be authorized within two weeks of the date of this order. 
 
The parties proposed that the undersigned issue this consent order in lieu of a 
formal hearing.  All parties consented to the entry of a consent order verbally on 
November 1, 2021; thus no alternate medical care hearing was necessary.   
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
The parties have agreed that Defendant will authorize care and treatment for 
Claimant’s work-related conditions with Ian Lin, M.D., at Des Moines Orthopaedic 
Surgeons, P.C.     
The parties have also agreed that Defendant will authorize care and treatment for 
Claimant’s work-related conditions within two weeks of the date of this order.

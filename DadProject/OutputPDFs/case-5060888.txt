FOR ORDER NUNC PRO TUNC, the undersigned states: 
The undersigned filed a Ruling on Defendants’ Motion to Set Aside, Vacate, 
Reconsider, and/or for Rehearing of Deputy Commissioner’s August 31, 2020 Ruling 
and Ruling on Motion to Dismiss (hereinafter “Ruling”) in this matter on September 15, 
2020.   
At page four of the Ruling, the following appears:  
The original notice and petition are file stamped January 18, 2018; however, 
the original notice and petition were mailed January 16, 2018.   
Defendants’ counsel has brought it to the attention of the undersigned that the 
date on which the original notice and petition was file stamped is January 19, 2018.  
The undersigned intended to include the correct date, but due to a scrivener’s error 
failed to do so.   
The phrase, “nunc pro tunc” means “now for then.”  See e.g., Black’s Law 
Dictionary, page 1218 (Revised 4th Edition 1968).  The definition in Black’s Law 
Dictionary further provides: “A phrase applied to acts allowed to be done after the time 
when they should be done, with a retroactive effect, i.e. with the same effect as if 
regularly done.”  Black’s at 1218.  A nunc pro tunc order “is not for the purpose of 
correcting judicial thinking, a judicial conclusion or mistake of law.”  Headley v. Headley, 
172 N.W.2d 104, 108 (Iowa 1969).  The nunc pro tunc order can be employed to correct 
obvious errors or to make an order conform to the judge’s original intent.  Graber v. 
District Court for Washington County, 410 N.W.2d 224, 229 (Iowa 1987); Brinson v. 
Spee Dee Delivery Service, No. 8-754/06-2074 (Iowa App. November 13, 2008).

In this instance, when the Ruling was issued on September 15, 2020, my intent 
was to include the correct date on page four as follows: 
The original notice and petition are file stamped January 19, 2018; however, 
the original notice and petition were mailed January 16, 2018.   
It is therefore appropriate to correct my scrivener’s error by changing the date to 
January 19, 2018, on page four in the Ruling.   
Finally, I would note that the statement, “Claimant filed his Petition in this case on 
January 19, 2018 . . . .” made in paragraph 3 of the defendants’ motion is not an 
accurate reflection of the undersigned’s ruling.  This nunc pro tunc is not to be deemed 
an acceptance of this statement as fact.   
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
The applicable sentence on page four of the Ruling is changed to: 
The original notice and petition are file stamped January 19, 2018; however, 
the original notice and petition were mailed January 16, 2018.   
There are no other changes to the Ruling filed in this matter on September 15, 
2020.

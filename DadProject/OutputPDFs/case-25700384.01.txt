On May 6, 2025, Jose Vera Raya filed an application for alternate care under 
Iowa Code section 85.27 and Iowa Administrative Code rule 876—4.48. The 
defendants, employer Uncommon Ground of the Quad Cities, Inc. and insurance carrier 
Brickstreet Mutual Insurance Co., did not file an answer. The agency scheduled the 
case for a telephone alternate care hearing to be held on May 16, 2025. Before the 
scheduled hearing time, counsel for the parties notified the undersigned by email they 
had reached an agreement regarding care and drafted a proposed consent order 
memorializing that agreement. 
The parties have agreed that the defendants will authorize reasonable care and 
treatment that is casually related to Vera Raya’s work-related conditions as 
recommended by the authorized treating medical provider at ORA Orthopedics – 
Moline. The defendants agree to authorize right shoulder arthroscopy, rotator cuff 
repair, biceps tenodesis, and physical therapy. The parties agree that these 
appointments will be scheduled as soon as possible, but not later than May 22, 2025.  
Because of the parties’ agreement, the telephone alternate care hearing 
scheduled in this case is no longer needed and is therefore cancelled. 
SO ORDERED. 
Signed and filed this 16th day of May, 2025.

The parties came before the Honorable Jennifer Gerrish-Lampe for an alternate 
medical care hearing on 2/25/2022 at 10:30 a.m. The Claimant, Megan Miller, was 
represented by counsel, Nicholas Shaull. The Defendants, Siegwerk, Employer, and 
Liberty Mutual Insurance, Insurance Carrier, were represented by James Nubel. Prior to 
hearing, and before going on the record, counsel for the parties and Her Honor had an 
extensive discussion regarding the underlying care matters currently at issue in this 
case.  
 
 
 
It was determined that Defendants do not dispute the care recommendations 
made by neurologist Irving Wolfe, D.O. in his 1/10/2022 report (page 14), Exhibit Page 4 
to Claimant’s Application for Alternate Medical Care. Defendants CONSENT to 
authorize: 1) a referral to On With Life for physical, occupational, vestibular/balance 
therapy, speech-language pathology (cognitive rehabilitation therapy), and vision 
therapy (neuro-optometry); 2) a referral to the University of Iowa Hospitals and Clinics 
Movement Disorder Clinic; 3) consultation with psychologist/psychology, as specifically 
set forth in Dr. Wolfe’s 1/10/2022 report, p. 14.  
 
 
However, Defendants were unable to schedule these appointments with the 
various providers identified above since they were rendered from an independent 
medical examination (IME) as opposed to a traditional treatment setting/report. For 
these reasons, it was agreed and consented to that Claimant and her counsel would 
obtain these same referrals (as set forth in Dr. Wolfe’s report, p. 14; Ex. Page 4 to Cl.’s 
App. for Alt Care) in a more traditional, treatment setting, whether that be through a 
revised referral record from Dr. Wolfe, or through the referral of Claimant’s primary care

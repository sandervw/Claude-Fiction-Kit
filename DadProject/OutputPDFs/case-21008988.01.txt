STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
 
 
Claimant, Jennie Brierly, filed a petition in arbitration seeking workers’ 
compensation benefits against McKesson Corporation, employer, and Old Republic 
Insurance Company, insurer, for an accepted work injury date of May 28, 2021. The 
case was set for hearing before the undersigned to take place on November 5, 2024. 
Prior to hearing, the parties filed a joint motion to submit this matter on the record 
without an evidentiary hearing. (See Joint Motion to Waive Hearing) The motion was 
granted and the hearing cancelled. 
 
The evidentiary record consists of a hearing report containing the parties’ 
stipulations. The parties did not offer any exhibits or testimony. Neither party wished to 
provide a brief. Therefore, the case is considered fully submitted based on the 
stipulations contained in the hearing report, which was approved on November 5, 2024. 
 
STIPULATIONS 
 
 
The parties provide the following stipulations: 
 
1. The existence of an employer-employee relationship at the time of the alleged 
injury; 
 
2. Claimant sustained an injury, which arose out of and in the course of 
employment, on May 28, 2021;

FINDINGS OF FACT 
 
Based on the parties’ stipulations contained in the hearing report, I find that 
claimant sustained a scheduled member injury to her lower extremity, which arose out 
of and in the course of her employment, on May 28, 2021. The injury was a cause of 
temporary disability during a period of recovery, as well as a cause of permanent 
disability. The commencement date for permanent partial disability benefits is August 
18, 2023. There is no claim for temporary or permanent disability benefits at this time. 
 
The applicable weekly benefit rate for any indemnity benefits is $375.67. There is 
no dispute regarding medical benefits at this time. There are no disputes regarding 
credits, claimant is not seeking costs, and there are no additional issues between the 
parties. 
 
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established ordinarily has 
the burden of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence. Iowa R. App. P.

6.904(3)(e). In this case, the parties have provided stipulations, and there are no 
disputed issues remaining for the undersigned to decide. 
 
A stipulation made by the parties cannot be rejected without prior notice to the 
parties and an opportunity to be heard and present evidence. Origer v. Friesth Constr., 
File No. 1282038 (App. December 20, 2002). I find no compelling reason to reject the 
parties’ stipulations contained within the hearing report. As such, the stipulations are 
accepted and binding on the parties. 
Under Iowa Code section 85.3(1), an employer is responsible for “any and all 
personal injuries sustained by an employee arising out of and in the course of the 
employment . . .” That section requires employers to provide, secure, and pay 
compensation according to the provisions of Chapter 85 of the Iowa Code. The parties 
have stipulated that claimant sustained an injury arising out of and in the course of her 
employment on May 28, 2021. As such, defendants are responsible for providing, 
securing, and paying compensation according to the provisions of Chapter 85 of the 
Iowa Code.

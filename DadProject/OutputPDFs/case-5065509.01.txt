Claimant filed an Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care with this 
agency on April 27, 2022. In the Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care, 
claimant requested defendants be ordered to pay for claimant’s medications. 
Based on a conference call with counsel for both parties, and a proposed 
consent order, defendants agree to authorize Jeffrey Walczyk, M.D. to provide 
prescription refills and pain management to treat claimant’s left arm and shoulder until 
such time as the MSA is established.  The parties also agree this consent order shall 
relate back to a July 10, 2020, approved settlement.  
In an effort to avoid an unnecessary hearing, and yet protect the interests of their 
clients, the parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendants to provide 
authorized treatment for claimant with Dr. Walczyk as described above. 
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties. A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing. 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties prior to the scheduled May 9, 2022, 
alternate medical care hearing, defendants shall authorize Dr. Walczyk to prescribe 
prescription refills and pain management to treat claimant’s left arm and shoulder until

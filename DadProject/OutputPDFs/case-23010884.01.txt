STATEMENT OF THE CASE   
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The 
expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Andrew Tripp. 
Claimant appeared personally and through his attorney, Nicole Merrill.  Defendants, Tri 
Rinse, Inc., employer, and Previsor Insurance Company, insurance carrier, appeared 
through their attorney, Colin Lane.   
The alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on February 4, 2026.  The 
proceedings were digitally recorded.  That recording constitutes the official record of this 
proceeding.  Pursuant to the Interim Workers’ Compensation Commissioner’s 
December 12, 2025 Order, the undersigned has been delegated authority to issue a 
final agency decision in this alternate medical care proceeding.  Therefore, this ruling is 
designated final agency action and any appeal of the decision would be to the Iowa 
District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A.   
The evidentiary record consists of Claimant’s Exhibits 1-3, which total 8 pages.  
Defendants offered Exhibits A and B, which total 3 pages.  Claimant testified on his own 
behalf.  No other witnesses were called to testify.  The evidentiary record closed at the 
conclusion of the alternate care hearing.   
Prior to the hearing, the defendants filed an answer which accepted liability for 
the June 9, 2023, work injury and for the neck and left shoulder conditions for which 
claimant is seeking treatment.

ISSUE   
The issue presented for resolution is whether the claimant is entitled to alternate 
medical care.  Specifically, defendants have advised claimant that they wish to change 
his physical therapy provider.  Claimant seeks an order from this agency compelling 
defendants to continue to provide and pay for medical care, including medical mileage, 
with his current physical therapy provider.   
FINDINGS OF FACT 
The undersigned having considered all the evidence in the record finds: 
Claimant, Andrew Tripp, sustained an injury that arose out of and in the course of 
his employment with Tri Rinse, Inc. on June 9, 2023.  Defendants have admitted liability 
for the June 9, 2023 work injury related to Andrew’s left shoulder and neck.  (Answer, 
numbered paragraph 5)  
At the time of the hearing, Andrew lived in Algona, Iowa.  (Testimony)  Andrew is 
currently treating with Justin D. Khoriaty, M.D., at Iowa Ortho.  Defendants selected Dr. 
Khoriaty to treat Andrew.  (Testimony)  Dr. Khoriaty operated on Andrew’s left shoulder 
on or around November 11, 2025.  (Testimony)  At the time of the surgery, Andrew also 
lived in Algona1.  (Testimony)  When Andrew had his shoulder surgery, his daughter 
drove him to the procedure. (Testimony)  His daughter lives near St. James, Minnesota. 
(Testimony)  Andrew stayed with his daughter for the first 2 weeks after the surgery 
when he was not able to drive. (Testimony)  He had his first post-op appointment on 
November 25, 2025.  (CE 1, p. 1)  At that 2-week post-op appointment, Dr. Khoriaty 
recommended physical therapy for Andrew’s left shoulder. (Testimony; Claimant’s 
Exhibit 1) He was able to drive after that appointment so he returned to Algona on 
November 26, 2025.  (Testimony)   
Andrew began physical therapy for his left shoulder on December 4, 2025.  
(Testimony)  His therapy has been at Fairmont Physical Therapy, a provider that was 
chosen by the defendants. (Testimony)  Andrew has been attending physical therapy 2 
to 3 times per week. (Testimony)  His last appointment was around January 9, 2026.  
(Testimony)  Andrew stopped attending therapy at Fairmont Physical Therapy because 
he was informed that the insurance carrier wanted to switch him to a different provider 
that was closer to where he lives. (Testimony)  Fairmont Physical Therapy is located in 
Fairmont, Minnesota, which is 45-50 minutes away from Algona.  (Testimony) For each 
physical therapy appointment Andrew travels 104 miles roundtrip.  (Defendants’ Exhibit 
B, p. 1)  He has been living in Algona since he started physical therapy in December.  
(Testimony)  Andrew has not complained about the distance to physical therapy and 
has not requested to change providers. (Testimony)

In mid-January 2026, defendants authorized physical therapy appointments at

Andrew believes that Fairmont Physical Therapy has provided good care which

current treatment. (Testimony)  Andrew’s goal is to return to work as quickly as 
possible. (Testimony)  He wants to do everything he can to accomplish this.  
(Testimony)  He works in a division that consists of a small group of people and knows 
that his absence is taxing. (Testimony)  He feels his chances of a faster recovery are 
better with continued therapy at Fairmont Physical Therapy.  (Testimony) 
On December 14 or 15, 2025, Andrew began a light-duty program at the Algona 
Public Library. (Testimony) The library is approximately one-half to one block from his 
current residence.  (Testimony)  Defendants want to transfer Andrew’s physical therapy 
to Kossuth Regional Health Care in Algona, Iowa, which is approximately 1 mile to 1.5 
miles from the Algona Library and Andrew’s residence.  (Testimony; DE B, p. 1)  
Andrew saw Dr. Khoriaty on January 8, 2026.  He recommended continued 
physical therapy to include some general strengthening.  (CE1, p. 3)  Andrew has never

capabilities to comply with the recommendations of Dr. Khoriaty.  (Testimony)  
Through this proceeding Andrew is seeking an order permitting him to continue 
his treatment with a provider with whom he has established care for his physical 
therapy.  Defendants agree to authorize physical therapy.  However, defendants seek to 
transfer the therapy to a different provider.  Andrew testified that he has an established 
rapport with the physical therapists at Fairmont Physical Therapy.  He believes it is 
beneficial to remain with that established provider and he desires to stay with Fairmont 
Physical Therapy.  There is nothing in the record that suggests the authorized treating

REASONING AND CONCLUSIONS OF LAW   
Under Iowa law, the employer is required to provide care to an injured employee 
and is permitted to choose the care.  Pirelli-Armstrong Tire Co. v. Reynolds, 562 
N.W.2d 433 (Iowa 1997).   
[T]he employer is obliged to furnish reasonable services and supplies to 
treat an injured employee, and has the right to choose the care. . . .  The 
treatment must be offered promptly and be reasonably suited to treat the 
injury without undue inconvenience to the employee.  If the employee has 
reason to be dissatisfied with the care offered, the employee should 
communicate the basis of such dissatisfaction to the employer, in writing if 
requested, following which the employer and the employee may agree to 
alternate care reasonably suited to treat the injury.  If the employer and 
employee cannot agree on such alternate care, the commissioner may, 
upon application and reasonable proofs of the necessity therefor, allow 
and order other care.   
By challenging the employer’s choice of treatment – and seeking alternate care – 
claimant assumes the burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable.  See Iowa 
R. App. P. 6.904(3); Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).   
Determining what care is reasonable under the statute is a question of fact.  Id.  The 
employer’s obligation turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not desirability.  Id.; 
Harned v. Farmland Foods, Inc., 331 N.W.2d 98 (Iowa 1983).  In Pirelli-Armstrong Tire 
Co., 562 N.W.2d at 433, the court approvingly quoted Bowles v. Los Lunas Schools, 
109 N.M. 100, 781 P.2d 1178 (App. 1989):   
[T]he words “reasonable” and “adequate” appear to describe the same 
standard.   
[The New Mexico rule] requires the employer to provide a certain 
standard of care and excuses the employer from any obligation to provide 
other services only if that standard is met.  We construe the terms 
"reasonable” and “adequate” as describing care that is both appropriate to 
the injury and sufficient to bring the worker to maximum recovery.

The commissioner is justified in ordering alternate care when employer-
authorized care has not been effective and evidence shows that such care is “inferior or 
less extensive” care than other available care requested by the employee.  Long; 528 
N.W.2d at 124; Pirelli-Armstrong Tire Co.; 562 N.W.2d at 437.   
The employer shall furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, osteopathic, 
chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance, and hospital services 
and supplies for all conditions compensable under the workers' compensation law.  The 
employer shall also allow reasonable and necessary transportation expenses incurred 
for those services.  The employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except 
where the employer has denied liability for the injury.  Section 85.27.  Holbert v. 
Townsend Engineering Co., Thirty-second Biennial Report of the Industrial 
Commissioner 78 (Review-Reopening October 16, 1975).   
Reasonable care includes care necessary to diagnose the condition and 
defendants are not entitled to interfere with the medical judgment of its own treating 
physician.  Pote v. Mickow Corp., File No. 694639 (Review-Reopening Decision June 
17, 1986).  
In this case, I found that claimant has an established relationship and physical 
therapy treatment program at Fairmont Physical Therapy.  I found it would be 
unreasonable to interfere with the rapport between claimant and his treating physical 
therapist.  Instead it makes more sense in this situation to maintain a continuity of care 
and to maintain therapy with Fairmont Physical Therapy.  I found that the physical 
therapy offered by defendants at Kossuth, although generally permissible, would be 
unreasonable in this particular situation.  Therefore, I conclude that claimant is entitled 
to an order directing that defendants provide physical therapy through Fairmont 
Physical Therapy.   
ORDER   
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED:   
Claimant’s petition for alternate medical care is granted.  Defendants shall 
immediately authorize and arrange for claimant to obtain recommended physical 
therapy through Fairmont Physical Therapy.

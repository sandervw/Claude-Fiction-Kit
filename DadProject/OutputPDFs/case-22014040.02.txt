Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care on January 26, 2024, 
requesting defendants be ordered to authorize mental health treatment.   
The defendants filed an answer to the application for alternate medical care on 
February 5, 2024.  In their answer, the defendants admitted liability for the claim relating 
to the head, neck, and body as a whole.   
Prior to hearing, the parties discussed entering into a Consent Order in lieu of a 
hearing.  The parties agreed to the entrance of this Consent Order.   
The defendants agreed to arrange for an appointment with a mental health 
provider for psychotherapy within 14 days of the date of this order.  The appointment 
itself need not occur within 14 days of the date of this order.   
If Amy Mooney, Ph.D., elects to treat the claimant, the defendants agree to 
authorize her proposed plan of care listed in a January 30, 2024, report.   
If Dr. Mooney declines to treat the claimant, the defendants agree to promptly 
refer the claimant to another mental health care provider for treatment.  As noted above, 
if needed, the defendants shall arrange for an appointment with the alternate provider, 
within 14 days of the date of this order.  The appointment itself need not occur within 14 
days of the date of this order. 
A hearing is therefore determined to be unnecessary, based on the agreement of 
the parties.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
1. Defendants shall arrange for an appointment for psychotherapy within 14 
days of the date of this order.  The appointment itself need not occur within 
14 days of the date of this order.   
  
2. The defendants shall authorize the plan of care proposed by Dr. Mooney if 
she elects to treat the claimant.   
 
3. If Dr. Mooney declines to treat the claimant, the defendants shall promptly 
refer the claimant to another mental health care provider for treatment.

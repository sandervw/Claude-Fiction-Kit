STATEMEN T OF TH E C AS E 
Debra Phillips filed two applications for alternate care under Iowa Code section 
85.27 and Iowa Administrative Code rule 876—4.48: 
 No. 21010258.02, seeking care for a left leg injury sustained on March 30, 
2021; and 
 
 No. 23002403.02, seeking care for injuries to the neck, right shoulder, spine, 
and back sustained on February 22, 2022. 
The defendants answered each petition, accepting liability for the alleged work injuries.  
The agency initially scheduled the two cases for separate hearings. However, 
because the parallel arbitration case is consolidated, the undersigned emailed counsel 
for the parties and all persons involved agreed to consolidate the alternate care cases 
into one under rule 876—4.6 for purposes of a hearing and decision. The agency then 
issued an amended hearing notice reflecting the consolidation. 
The undersigned presided over an alternate care hearing held by telephone and 
recorded on February 23, 2024, at 10:30 a.m. That recording constitutes the official 
record of the proceeding under rule 876—4.48(12). Phillips participated personally and 
through attorney Joseph G. Lyons. The defendants participated through attorney James 
M. Ballard. The record consists of:

ISSU E  
The issue under consideration is whether Phillips is entitled to alternate care in 
the form of treatment by a pain management specialist for the left leg and treatment by 
a chiropractor and pain management specialist for her neck and right shoulder. 
FIN D IN GS OF FAC T  
Phillips sustained an injury to her left leg on March 30, 2021, that arose out of 
and in the course of her employment with Rydell. The defendants accepted liability for 
the injury and provided care under Iowa Code section 85.27. Thomas Gorsche, M.D., 
performed arthroscopic surgery to repair her left knee on September 8, 2021, by 
debriding loose articular cartilage from the medial femoral condyle and the anterior 
cruciate ligament stump. (Ex. 2, p. 5; Ex. A) Dr. Gorsche found Phillips to have reached 
maximum medial improvement (MMI) on February 2, 2022, assigned a permanent 
impairment rating, and opined, “Phillips may require future medical care which could 
require anti-inflammatory medications, cortisone injection and a small possibility of ACL 
reconstruction.” (Ex. 2, p. 5) She has not received care for her injured left leg since a 
follow-up appointment with Dr. Gorsche on or about October 11, 2022. (Testimony) 
On February 22, 2022, Phillips sustained an injury to her neck and right shoulder 
arising out of and in the course of her employment with Rydell. The defendants 
accepted liability and provided care under Iowa Code section 85.27. Authorized 
treatment included chiropractic care, which Phillips found beneficial. Her last 
chiropractic appointment was in or around May 2022. (Testimony) Phillips also received 
care at the occupational medicine clinic at UnityPoint Health, Allen Hospital. (Ex. B, p. 
4) Her last appointment there was in or around June 2023. (Testimony) 
Claimant’s counsel arranged for Phillips to undergo an independent medical 
examination (IME) with Farid Manshadi, M.D., on May 16, 2023. (Ex. 3; Testimony) Dr. 
Manshadi reviewed records relating to the care Phillips received, performed an in-
person examination, and issued an IME report with his opinion on permanent 
impairment. (Ex. 3) On the question of further treatment, Dr. Manshadi opined that for 
the left knee, Phillips would likely require care in the form of injections and physical 
therapy. (Ex. 3)  
The defendants arranged for Sarvenaz Jabbari, M.D., to examine Phillips and 
provide an assessment of permanent disability caused by the February 22, 2022 injury. 
(Ex. B) Dr. Jabbari reviewed records and performed an in-person examination of 
Phillips, then issued a letter dated June 15, 2023, with an impairment rating and, 
pertinent to the current dispute, also noted that “she does have persistent pain” and 
that, other than an x-ray of the spine to ensure no fractures had occurred, Dr. Jabbari

record regarding what caused the delay in scheduling an appointment with Dr. Jabbari 
between mid-January and late-February. 
C ON C LU SION S OF LAW 
“Iowa Code section 85.27(4) affords an employer who does not contest the 
compensability of a workplace injury a qualified statutory right to control the medical 
care provided to an injured employee.” Ramirez-Trujillo v. Quality Egg, L.L.C., 878 
N.W.2d 759, 769 (Iowa 2016) (citing R.R. Donnelly & Sons v. Barnett, 670 N.W.2d 190, 
195, 197 (Iowa 2003)). Under the law, the employer must “furnish reasonable medical 
services and supplies and reasonable and necessary appliances to treat an injured 
employee.” Stone Container Corp. v. Castle, 657 N.W.2d 485, 490 (Iowa 2003) 
(emphasis in original). Such employer-provided care “must be offered promptly and be 
reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue inconvenience to the employee.” 
Iowa Code § 85.27(4).  
Here, through counsel, Phillips communicated her ongoing pain complaints and 
request for additional care on January 4, 2024. The defendants contacted Dr. Jabbari 
but did not arrange for care between then and the filing of the petitions in this case. It 
was only after the petitions were filed that the defendants scheduled an appointment for 
February 28, 2024, a date that falls six weeks after the initial request for additional care. 
There is an insufficient basis in the record from which to reach a finding on the reason 
for the delay between Phillips’s request for additional care and the scheduling of the 
appointment with Dr. Jabbari. Thus, the delay is unreasonable because the care was 
not offered “promptly” as required by statute.

However, the appointment that the defendants scheduled after Phillips applied to 
the agency for alternate care is set for five days after the date of hearing. Given the time 
that has passed since Phillips was found at MMI and released from care for her left leg 
injury and back-shoulder injury, seeing an occupational medicine specialist familiar with 
her case such as Dr. Jabbari to allow for a fresh assessment of her injuries and 
treatment recommendations based on such an assessment is most likely to lead to care 
that is reasonably suited to effectively treat her ongoing complaints. And Phillips is 
unlikely to obtain such care as close in time to the date of hearing as the appointment 
with Dr. Jabbari that is currently scheduled. Under these circumstances, it is therefore 
appropriate to deny Phillips’s applications—which seek an order directing the 
defendants to arrange care with a pain management specialist for both injuries and a 
chiropractor for the neck and shoulder injuries—in favor of the appointment with Dr. 
Jabbari. 
OR D ER  
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
1) Under No. 21010258.02, Phillips’s application is DENIED. 
 
2) Under No. 23002403.02, Phillips’s application is DENIED. 
On July 21, 2023, the Iowa workers’ compensation commissioner issued an 
order delegating authority to deputy workers’ compensation commissioners, such as the 
undersigned, to issue final agency decisions on applications for alternate care. 
Consequently, there is no appeal of this decision to the commissioner, only judicial 
review in a district court under the Iowa Administrative Procedure Act, Iowa Code 
chapter 17A.

Claimant filed a Petition seeking alternate medical care for mental health (PTSD) 
on March 31, 2025. On April 2, 2025, Defendants file an Answer admitting the claim.  A 
telephonic hearing on this petition was scheduled to occur on April 9, 2025, at 10:30 
AM. Prior to the alternate medical care hearing, counsel notified the undersigned that 
the parties were able to reach an amicable agreement and that a consent order should 
be entered. 
With respect to the specific alternate medical care requested, Claimant sought 
mental health care with a psychiatrist for psychotropic medication and treatment. 
Defendants have authorized the requested referral and have agreed to provide 
Claimant with the date of the appointment within 14 days of this order. 
The parties desired that a consent order be entered in this case, in lieu of a 
hearing. After discussion between the parties and an agreement to provide the 
requested care, there is no justiciable issue for hearing and determination. Both parties 
agree that a consent order is appropriate, and a formal hearing is unnecessary to 
resolve the contested case dispute. 
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties' positions and acceptable to both parties. A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties prior to the scheduled April 9, 2025, 
alternate medical care hearing, defendants shall authorize mental health care with a 
psychiatrist for psychotropic medication and treatment within 14 days of this order, and 
shall schedule claimant for an appointment. The appointment itself does not need to 
occur within 30 days of this order. 
The hearing scheduled for April 9, 2025, is cancelled.

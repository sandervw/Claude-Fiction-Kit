ISSUE 
1.  Is claimant entitled to additional temporary total benefits? 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
 
This matter was initially heard in an arbitration hearing on June 28, 2022.  The 
arbitration decision held, in part, that defendant should pay claimant temporary total 
disability benefits at the rate of $1,015.53 per week commencing on July 31, 2021, and 
continuing until such time as claimant had reached maximum medical improvement 
(MMI).  (Arbitration Decision pages 4-5, 8)  
 
The decision determined claimant was not at MMI.  (Arb. Dec., pp. 4-5, 8)  The 
decision also ordered defendant to pay claimant’s medical expenses related to his July 
30, 2021, work-related injury.  (Arb. Dec., pp. 5, 8) 
 
Claimant died on January 2, 2024.  (Motion Regarding Beneficiaries) 
 
Claimant seeks temporary total disability benefits to be paid through the date of 
death.  (Claimant’s Exhibit 2)  Claimant also seeks to have medical expenses paid that 
were incurred in the July 30, 2021, work-related injury.  (Ex. 1) 
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
The first issue to be determined is whether claimant is entitled to additional 
benefits under review-reopening proceeding.

The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established has the burden 
of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence.  Iowa R. App. P. 6.904(3). 
Upon review-reopening, claimant has the burden to show a change in condition 
related to the original injury since the original award or settlement was made.  The 
change may be either economic or physical.  Blacksmith v. All-American, Inc., 290 
N.W.2d 348 (Iowa 1980); Henderson v. Iles, 250 Iowa 787, 96 N.W.2d 321 (1959).  A 
mere difference of opinion of experts as to the percentage of disability arising from an 
original injury is not sufficient to justify a different determination on a petition for review-
reopening.  Rather, claimant's condition must have worsened or deteriorated in a 
manner not contemplated at the time of the initial award or settlement before an award 
on review-reopening is appropriate.  Bousfield v. Sisters of Mercy, 249 Iowa 64, 86 
N.W.2d 109 (1957).  A failure of a condition to improve to the extent anticipated 
originally may also constitute a change of condition.  Meyers v. Holiday Inn of Cedar 
Falls, Iowa, 272 N.W.2d 24 (Iowa App. 1978). 
In a review-reopening proceeding the claimant has the burden of proof to prove 
whether they have suffered an impairment of earning capacity proximately caused by 
the original injury.  E.N.T. Associates v. Collentine, 525 N.W.2d 827, 829 (Iowa 1994). 
It is unclear why claimant believes he is due additional temporary benefits under 
a review-reopening proceeding.  Claimant is seeking additional temporary benefits.  He 
has not shown that there has been a change in his condition from his July 30, 2021, 
work injury entitling him to additional temporary benefits.  Given this record, claimant 
has failed to carry his burden of proof that he is entitled to additional temporary benefits 
under a review-reopening proceeding. 
However, the fact that claimant is unable to prove entitlement to additional 
temporary benefits under a review-reopening proceeding does not end the analysis 
regarding claimant’s entitlement to temporary benefits. 
In the October 24, 2022, arbitration decision, defendant was ordered to pay 
claimant temporary total disability benefits at the rate of $1,015.53 per week 
commencing on July 31, 2021, and continuing until claimant had reached MMI.  (Arb. 
Dec., pp. 4-5, 8)  Defendant was also ordered to pay claimant’s medical expenses as 
they related to his July 31, 2021, injury.   
There is no evidence in the record claimant ever reached MMI up to the date of 
his death on January 2, 2024.  Given this record, claimant is entitled to temporary total 
disability benefits from July 31, 2021, through January 2, 2024.  Defendant is also liable 
for paying medical expenses as detailed in Claimant’s Exhibit 1.

ORDER 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
That defendant shall pay claimant temporary total disability benefits at the rate of 
one thousand fifteen and 53/100 dollars ($1,015.53) per week from July 31, 2021, 
through January 2, 2024.   
That defendant shall pay accrued weekly benefits in a lump sum together with 
interest at an annual rate equal to the one-year treasury constant maturity published by 
the federal reserve in the most recent H15 report settled as of the date of injury, plus 
two percent.  
 
That defendant shall pay claimant’s medical expenses as detailed in Exhibit 1. 
 
That defendant shall file subsequent reports of injury as required by this agency 
under Rule 876 IAC 3.1(2). 
 
That defendant shall pay costs.

On June 30, 2025, the undersigned filed an arbitration decision in this case.  On 
July 7, 2025, defendant filed a motion for order nunc pro tunc.   
The phrase, “nunc pro tunc” means “now for then.”  See:  Black’s Law Dictionary, 
page 1218 (Revised 4th Edition 1968).  The definition in Black’s Law Dictionary further 
provides:  “A phrase applied to acts allowed to be done after the time when they should 
be done, with a retroactive effect, i.e. with the same effect as if regularly done.”  Black’s 
at 1218.  A nunc pro tunc order “is not for the purpose of correcting judicial thinking, a 
judicial conclusion, or a mistake of law.”  Headley v. Headley, 172 N.W.2d 104, 108 
(Iowa 1969).  The nunc pro tunc order can be employed to correct obvious errors or to 
make an order conform to the judge’s original intent.  Graber v. District Court for 
Washington Cty, 410 N.W.2d 224, 229 (Iowa 1987).  Brinson v. Spee Dee Delivery 
Service, No. 8-754/06-2074 (Iowa Ct. App. November 13, 2008). 
Defendant correctly contends that the undersigned made a scrivener’s error with 
respect to the amount of permanent partial disability benefits owed to the claimant.  In 
the arbitration decision, the undersigned concluded that the claimant “sustained a 35 
percent loss of future earning capacity . . . This is equivalent to a 35 percent industrial 
disability and entitles claimant to an award of 175 weeks of permanent partial disability 
benefits.”  Additionally, the approved Hearing Report contained the parties’ stipulation 
that, prior to hearing, the claimant had previously been paid 39.6 weeks of permanent 
partial disability benefits.  Subtracting 39.6 weeks of PPD previously paid from the 175 
weeks of PPD awarded produces a difference of 135.4 weeks; however, in the Order, 
the undersigned provided, “Defendant shall pay claimant an additional one hundred 
forty-six point four (146.4) weeks of permanent partial disability benefits commencing 
March 15, 2021.”  It is appropriate to grant defendant’s motion for order nunc pro tunc in 
this regard.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:  
Defendant’s motion for order nunc pro tunc is sustained. 
The arbitration decision filed on June 30, 2025, shall be modified to include the 
following order: 
Defendant shall pay claimant an additional one hundred thirty-five point 
four (135.4) weeks of permanent partial disability benefits commencing 
March 15, 2021. 
The remainder of the June 30, 2025, arbitration decision stands as issued.

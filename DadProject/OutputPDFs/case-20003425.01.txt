STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  On 
September 3, 2024, claimant, Brent Garbes, filed an application for alternate care under 
Iowa Code section 85.27, invoking the expedited procedure rule 876 IAC 4.48.  In the 
petition claimant requests authorization to proceed with a right shoulder surgery and a 
right carpal tunnel surgery recommended by Scott Kuzma, M.D., for work-related 
injuries to his right upper extremity and shoulder that he sustained on March 2, 2020.  
On September 4, 2024, defendants, PF Cheese - Luana IA and Indemnity Insurance 
Company of North America, filed an answer accepting liability for the March 2, 2020 
injuries to claimant’s right shoulder and right upper extremity.  
The undersigned presided over an alternate care hearing held via telephone on 
September 13, 2024.  Claimant appeared personally and through his attorney Erik Bair.  
Defendants appeared through their attorney Thomas Wolle. The proceedings were 
digitally recorded.  That recording constitutes the official record of this proceeding. The 
hearing record consists of: 
 Claimant’s Exhibits 1 and 2 
Claimant testified at the hearing. Counsel offered oral arguments to support their 
positions. During the hearing, defendants verbally accepted liability for the March 2, 
2020 date of injury and for claimant’s right shoulder and right upper extremity 
conditions—the conditions for which claimant seeks treatment in this proceeding.

FINDINGS OF FACT 
On March 2, 2020, claimant sustained work-related injuries to his right shoulder 
and right upper extremity. (See Petition; Defendants’ Answer).  Defendants admitted 
liability for those injuries and authorized treatment with Justin Mitchell, M.D., at 
Gundersen Orthopedics. (See Defendants’ Answer; Hearing Testimony).  Dr. Mitchell 
went on to perform two surgeries on claimant’s right shoulder. (Ex. 1, p. 2).  He 
performed an arthroscopic rotator cuff repair, subscapularis repair, SAD and open 
biceps tenodesis on October 1, 2020; and an arthroscopic revision rotator cuff repair 
with allograft augmentation on December 9, 2021. (Id.).   
At the hearing, claimant testified that he continued to have shoulder problems 
following Dr. Mitchell’s revision surgery, including constant pain. (Hearing Testimony; 
Ex. 1, p. 1).  Defendants authorized follow-up treatment for his complaints including 
physical therapy, message therapy, medication and pain management treatment with 
David Dahl, M.D., and trigger point injections. (Hearing Testimony; Ex. 1, p. 2).  
Claimant stated that none of this treatment helped with his pain symptoms. (Hearing 
Testimony).  Eventually, claimant was referred to Scott Kuzma, M.D., at Mayo Clinic, for 
an evaluation. (Id.; See also Ex. 2, p. 6).  Both parties confirmed that Dr. Kuzma was an 
authorized treating physician for claimant’s work injuries. (Hearing Testimony).   
It is unclear when claimant started treating with Dr. Kuzma.  The hearing record 
only contains one record from Dr. Kuzma. (Ex.1, pp. 1-5).  It is dated August 1, 2024. 
(Id. at 1).  The record indicates claimant had previously been evaluated by Dr. 
DeDianous, a colleague at Mayo Clinic. (Id.).  Dr. Kuzma’s treatment record indicates 
claimant was still experiencing constant pain in his right shoulder that was limiting his 
ability to work and impacting his sleep. (Id.).  He also complained of numbness and 
tingling in his hand, as well as difficulty with fine motor skills and loss of grip strength. 
(Id.).  Claimant indicated these symptoms began after his second shoulder surgery. 
(Id.).  Dr. Kuzma’s note indicates claimant underwent a repeat shoulder MRI on 
February 2, 2024.  (Id. at 3).  That MRI showed postoperative changes from the revision 
rotator cuff repair and graft augmentation, as well as an area of partial-thickness 
articular sided tearing appearing slightly increased from a prior MRI in 2022. (Id.).  Dr. 
Kuzma opined that the localization of claimant’s pain complaints suggests “rotator cuff

incompetency despite there being portions of the cuff/graft augmentation still intact.” (Id. 
at 4).  He also stated that the symptoms in claimant’s right hand were consistent with

with potential rotator cuff repair and placement of an InSpace subacromial balloon 
spacer. (Id.).  Dr. Kuzma’s treatment note indicates claimant, his wife, and his workers’ 
compensation case manager were in agreement that claimant should proceed with the 
surgeries as suggested. (Id. at 5).   
On September 3, 2024, claimant’s counsel emailed defendants’ counsel.  (Ex.2, 
p. 6).  The email indicated that claimant was scheduled for surgery at Mayo Clinic on 
September 18, 2024, and a pre-operative physical on September 6, 2024. (Id.).  
However, claimant received a call earlier that day from the Mayo Clinic stating that 
defendants had not yet authorized the surgery. (Id.).  Claimant’s counsel indicated that 
the claimant wanted to move forward with the surgery, and requested defendants’ 
counsel let him know whether the surgeries were approved. (Id.).  Defendants’ counsel 
sent an email reply stating, “we are seeking another opinion re whether surgery for 
these conditions is reasonable and necessary.” (Id.).  The email indicated defendants 
planned to ask Dr. Bollier for another opinion. (Id.).  Claimant filed this alternate care 
action later that afternoon. (See Petition).  
At the hearing, claimant testified that he continues to experience symptoms in his 
right upper extremity and pain in his shoulder. (Hearing Testimony).  He stated that he 
wants to move forward with the surgeries recommended by Dr. Kuzma. (Id.).  
Defendants’ counsel argued that Dr. Kuzma’s surgical recommendations were made 
recently, and it was reasonable for defendants to seek an IME to see if the 
recommended surgeries were the best course of action given the claimant’s surgical 
history and/or necessitated by the March 2, 2020 work injury. (Id.).  Defendants, 
however, do not yet have a date for the requested IME. (Id.).  Claimant objected to 
seeing another surgeon for an evaluation. (Id.).  He felt his treatment had already gone 
on too long. (Id.).   
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
Under Iowa law, an employer who has accepted compensability for a workplace 
injury has a right to control the care provided to the injured employee.  Ramirez-Trujillo 
v. Quality Egg, L.L.C., 878 N.W.2d 759, 769 (Iowa 2016).  The relevant statute provides 
as follows: 
For purposes of this section, the employer is obliged to furnish 
reasonable services and supplies to treat an injured employee, and has the

right to choose the care. . . . The treatment must be offered promptly and 
be reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue inconvenience to the 
employee. If the employee has reason to be dissatisfied with the care 
offered, the employee should communicate the basis of such dissatisfaction 
to the employer, in writing if requested, following which the employer and 
the employee may agree to alternate care reasonably suited to treat the 
injury. If the employer and employee cannot agree on such alternate care, 
the commissioner may, upon application and reasonable proofs of the 
necessity therefor, allow and order other care. 
Iowa Code § 85.27(4).   
Defendants’ “obligation under the statute is confined to reasonable care for the 
diagnosis and treatment of work-related injuries.” Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 
N.W.2d 122, 124 (Iowa 1995) (emphasis in original). In other words, the “obligation 
under the statute turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not desirability.”  Id. An 
application for alternate medical care is not automatically sustained because claimant is 
dissatisfied with the care he has been receiving. Mere dissatisfaction with the medical 
care is not ample grounds for granting an application for alternate medical care. Rather, 
the claimant must show that the care was not offered promptly, was not reasonably 
suited to treat the injury, or that the care was unduly inconvenient for the claimant. See 
Iowa Code § 85.27(4). By challenging the employer’s choice of treatment and seeking 
alternate care, claimant assumes the burden of proving the authorized care is 
unreasonable. See Iowa R. App. P 6.904(3)(e); Long, 528 N.W.2d at 124.  Ultimately, 
determining whether care is reasonable under the statute is a question of fact. Long, 
528 N.W.2d at 123. 
An employer's right to select the provider of medical treatment for the injured 
worker does not include the right to determine how an injured worker should be 
diagnosed, evaluated, treated, or other matters of professional medical judgment. 
Assmann v. Blue Star Foods, File No. 866389 (Declaratory Ruling, May 19, 1988).  An 
employer is not entitled to interfere with the medical judgment of its own treating 
physician. Pote v. Mickow Corp., File No. 694639 (Review-Reopening Decision June 
17, 1986).  When a designated physician refers a patient to another physician, that 
physician acts as the defendant employer's agent. Permission for the referral from 
defendant is not necessary. Kittrell v. Allen Memorial Hospital, Thirty-fourth Biennial 
Report of the Industrial Commissioner, 164 (Arb. November 1, 1979) (aff'd by industrial 
commissioner). See also Limoges v. Meier Auto Salvage, I Iowa Industrial 
Commissioner Report 207 (1981). 
Both parties agreed that Dr. Kuzma is an authorized physician for claimant’s 
work injury.  On August 1, 2024, Dr. Kuzma causally related claimant’s right arm and 
right shoulder complaints to the March 2, 2020 date of injury, and recommended 
proceeding with a carpal tunnel release surgery and a right shoulder revision surgery. A 
month and a half after this appointment, defendants still have not authorized the 
surgeries recommended by Dr. Kuzma, an authorized provider.  Nor is there any 
evidence that they have offered other alternative medical care reasonably suited to treat

claimant’s right arm and right shoulder conditions.  An as-of-yet unscheduled IME to re-
examine causation does not qualify as an offer of care.  Defendants are not allowed to 
encroach upon Dr. Kuzma’s treatment recommendations.  The care defendants are 
offering is not reasonable.  
Dr. Kuzma is the authorized treating physician.  He recommended claimant 
proceed with a carpal tunnel release surgery and a right shoulder revision surgery.  
Claimant is entitled to the recommended care.  
ORDER 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted. 
Defendants shall authorize the right carpal tunnel release surgery and right 
shoulder revision surgery recommended by Dr. Kuzma.

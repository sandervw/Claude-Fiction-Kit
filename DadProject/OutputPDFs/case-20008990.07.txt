This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. The 
expedited procedures of rule 876 IAC 4.48, the “alternate medical care” rule, are 
invoked by claimant, Joshua Hanawalt.  
This alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on February 28, 2024. The 
proceedings were recorded digitally and constitute the official record of the hearing. By 
an order filed by the Workers’ Compensation Commissioner, this decision is designated 
final agency action. Any appeal would be by petition for judicial review under Iowa Code 
section 17A.15(1).  
The record in this case consists of Claimant’s Exhibit 1 and the testimony of 
claimant. 
ISSUE 
The issue presented for resolution in this case is whether claimant is entitled to 
alternate medical care consisting of authorization for massage therapy, access to gym 
and pool facilities, laser therapy and decompression table therapy. 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
Defendants accept liability for a work-related injury to claimant’s low back on 
June 25, 2020. 
In a February 5, 2024, letter, Shawn Spooner, M.D., made several 
recommendations regarding claimant’s care.  Dr. Spooner is an authorized treating 
physician.  He endorsed the use of laser therapy and decompression table therapy as

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established has the burden 
of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence.  Iowa R. App. P. 6.904(3)(e).  
Iowa Code section 85.27(4) provides, in relevant part: 
For purposes of this section, the employer is obliged to furnish 
reasonable services and supplies to treat an injured employee and has the 
right to choose the care. . .  The treatment must be offered promptly and 
be reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue inconvenience to the 
employee. If the employee has reason to be dissatisfied with the care 
offered, the employee should communicate the basis of such 
dissatisfaction to the employer, in writing if requested, following which the 
employer and the employee may agree to alternate care reasonably suited 
to treat the injury. If the employer and employee cannot agree on such 
alternate care, the commissioner may, upon application and reasonable 
proofs of the necessity therefor, allow and order other care. 
An application for alternate medical care is not automatically sustained because 
claimant is dissatisfied with the care he has been receiving.  Mere dissatisfaction with 
the medical care is not ample grounds for granting an application for alternate medical 
care.  Rather, the claimant must show that the care was not offered promptly, was not 
reasonably suited to treat the injury, or that the care was unduly inconvenient for the 
claimant.  Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).   
As detailed in the December 19, 2023, alternate medical care decision in this 
case, Dr. Spooner is the authorized treating physician.  As also noted in the December 
19, 2023, alternate medical care decision, Dr. Spooner recommended claimant undergo 
massage therapy on November 9, 2023.  In a February 13, 2024, email, defense 
counsel indicated massage therapy had finally been authorized.  (Ex. 1, p. 5) It is found

that waiting over three months from Dr. Spooner’s recommendation for massage 
therapy, to finally authorizing the therapy, is unreasonable.  Claimant has carried his 
burden of proof that defendants shall authorize and pay for massage therapy. 
Dr. Spooner recommends claimant have continued access to gym and pool 
facilities.  At the time of hearing defendants had not yet authorized gym and pool 
facilities for claimant.  It is found that the failure to authorize the recommendations made 
by Dr. Spooner regarding gym and pool facilities is unreasonable.  Claimant has carried 
his burden of proof he is entitled to alternate medical care consisting of access to gym 
and pool facilities. 
Defendants ask for direction regarding proximity to claimant’s residence 
regarding the gym and pool facility.  Dr. Spooner suggests claimant have access to the 
“Altoona Campus.”  Defendants shall authorize and pay for claimant to have access to 
the Altoona Campus. 
Dr. Spooner recommended claimant have laser therapy and decompression 
table therapy.  Defendants have not authorized these therapies.  It is found defendants’ 
failure to authorize the therapies recommended by Dr. Spooner is unreasonable.  
Defendants shall authorize and pay for laser therapy and decompression table therapy. 
ORDER 
THEREFORE, it is ordered: 
That claimant’s petition for alternate medical care is granted.  Defendants shall 
authorize and pay for access to gym and pool facilities at the “Altoona Campus,” 
massage therapy, laser therapy and decompression table therapy.  Prior alternate 
medical care hearings regarding this matter indicate defendants have delayed 
authorizing the treatments recommend by an authorized provider or as ordered by this 
agency.  As a result, defendants shall authorize and pay for access to gym and pool 
facilities at the “Altoona Campus,” massage therapy, laser therapy and decompression 
table therapy within two weeks from the date of this decision.

                     CONSENT ORDER  
          RE: ALTERNATE MEDICAL CARE 
 
   
 
 
On October 7, 2025, claimant filed a Petition for Alternate Medical Care. Hearing 
was scheduled to take place on October 15, 2025 at 10:30 a.m.  
 
Prior to the scheduled hearing, counsel for the parties advised the undersigned 
that they had reached an agreement regarding the requested care.  Given the 
agreement of the parties, the hearing was determined to be unnecessary.  The parties 
requested entry of a consent order to confirm and document the agreement.  
 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
 
Defendants agree to provide a scheduled date and time for the claimant to be 
evaluated for her right knee injury two weeks from the date of this Order. If defendants 
fail to provide a scheduled appointment date and time for the claimant within two weeks 
of the Order, claimant will be allowed to seek treatment with a provider of her choosing, 
and defendants lose their right to an authorization defense. This Order does not require 
defendants to have the claimant be seen with an authorized physician within two weeks, 
but instructs defendants to have an appointment date and time scheduled for claimant 
with an authorized provider within two weeks of the Order. 
The hearing scheduled for October 15, 2025, at 10:30 a.m., is cancelled.

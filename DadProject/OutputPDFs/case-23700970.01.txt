STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
 
 
Heidi Brown, surviving spouse of Travis Brown, filed a petition in arbitration 
seeking worker’s compensation benefits against New Vision Contracting, employer, for 
a work injury date of September 15, 2023. The employer appears to be uninsured. The 
petition was filed on November 14, 2023. On the same date, the petition was served on 
the employer via regular United States mail and certified mail. (See Proof of Service 
filed November 14, 2023) On December 13, 2023, defendant employer’s registered 
agent, Adam Mitchell, was personally served with a copy of the petition by Process 
Server Mark A. Beik. (See Application for Default, filed February 8, 2024) On January 2, 
2024, defendant employer’s registered agent presented to Pothitakis Law Firm 
requesting paperwork related to the case, and Kayla Ferguson, a paralegal at the firm, 
hand delivered a copy of the file-stamped petition. As such, the employer was served 
three separate times, once by mail and twice by in-person delivery to the registered 
agent. 
 
 
To date, the employer has not filed an answer or appearance in this matter. On 
January 24, 2024, counsel for claimant served a Notice of Intent to file written 
application for default, in accordance with Iowa R. Civ. P. 1.972(3). On February 8, 
2024, claimant filed an application for default with the agency, which the agency granted 
on March 22, 2024. (See Entry of Default) The entry of default resulted in the record 
being closed to further activity by the employer. 
 
The case came before the undersigned for a default hearing for consideration 
and an award of appropriate relief on July 18, 2024. Ms. Brown seeks funeral expenses, 
death benefits, and an assessment of costs. 
 
Prior to hearing, claimant submitted written evidence, consisting of Claimant’s 
Exhibits 1 through 6, and a hearing report. Ms. Brown appeared through attorney

FINDINGS OF FACT 
 
The undersigned, having considered all of the evidence and testimony in the 
record, finds: 
 
Ms. Brown’s testimony was consistent as compared to the evidentiary record, 
and her demeanor at the time of hearing gave the undersigned no reason to doubt her 
veracity. Ms. Brown is found credible. 
 
At the time of hearing, Ms. Brown was 44 years old. She was married to the 
decedent, Travis Brown, on February 2, 2023. (Claimant’s Exhibit 6, p. 32) Ms. Brown 
testified that she and Travis did not have any children together.  
 
Ms. Brown testified that Travis Brown had worked for the employer since April of 
2022. He was employed as a carpenter. She testified that he was making $26.00 per 
hour plus overtime at the time of his death. Based on Mr. Brown’s wage records, the 
proper weekly benefit rate has been calculated to be $666.72 per week. (Cl. Ex. 5, p. 
32) 
 
Travis Brown was working on September 15, 2023, when he was involved in an 
unfortunate accident that resulted in his death. (Cl. Ex. 1, p. 1) According to the 
Burlington Police Department’s incident report, at approximately 2:16 p.m. officers and 
an ambulance were dispatched to an address where an unconscious male was pinned 
under a forklift. (Cl. Ex. 2, p. 5) Police interviewed Johnathan Walker Davenport, who 
was working with Mr. Brown. Mr. Davenport indicated the two were unloading a walking 
forklift that was on top of a pallet jack in the loading dock area. While they were moving 
the pallet jack, it slid out from underneath Mr. Brown and the forklift fell backwards and 
landed on his head. He was not moving, and Mr. Davenport ran upstairs to get help and 
call 911. The report indicates that when the first officers and paramedics arrived on the 
scene, the forklift was still on Mr. Brown’s head and had to be moved. Paramedics 
performed CPR but unfortunately, Mr. Brown was pronounced deceased.  
 
The police report indicates that the address where the accident occurred was a 
new bar called the Busted Cup Brewery, which was preparing for a soft-opening the 
following day. (Cl. Ex. 2, pp. 5; 7) In a detective’s supplemental report of the accident, 
he noted there was surveillance video that captured the incident. (Cl. Ex. 2, pp. 7-8) The 
video showed Mr. Brown and Mr. Davenport attempting to move the walking forklift from 
the pallet jack and pallet to the loading dock. Neither worker was wearing a hard hat. 
(Cl. Ex. 2, p. 8) Mr. Brown was behind the forklift and Mr. Davenport was in front of it. 
Mr. Brown attempted to move the forklift back and forth to get it onto the loading dock, 
but as it was moved, the pallet jack and pallet came out from underneath him, and he 
slipped. As Mr. Brown fell, the walking forklift also fell, and the top of the forklift hit his

An OSHA investigation report in evidence provides a similar summary of the 
events leading to Mr. Brown’s death. (Cl. Ex. 4, pp. 17-18) The report names the 
establishment as New Vision Contracting, LLC. (Cl. Ex. 4, p. 17) The OSHA investigator

p. 20) After the inspection, the investigator had a closing discussion with Adam Mitchell 
on November 30, 2023, in which he noted the only question Mr. Mitchell had was to the 
dollar amount of the violations. (Cl. Ex. 4, p. 20) 
 
The OSHA investigation found several violations against New Vision Contracting, 
including the employer not training the employees in the manufacturer’s instructions of 
the manual hand-powered pallet jack; incomplete completion of required OSHA forms, 
and failing to report a fatal accident within 8 hours. (Cl. Ex. 4, pp. 20-31) The 
investigation also found the employer did not furnish employment and a place of 
employment free from recognized hazards that were causing or likely to cause death or 
serious physical harm to employees, in violation of Iowa Code section 88.4. (Cl. Ex. 4, 
p. 21) It is noted that the employees of New Vision Contracting were directed by Adam 
Mitchell to perform punch list jobs for the Busted Cup Brewhouse, whose owner in turn 
directed the employees to bring the electric pallet jack to the basement at the time of the 
accident. (Cl. Ex. 4, p. 23)  
 
With respect to the incomplete paperwork, the report notes that Adam Mitchell’s 
wife Linda Mitchell filled out the forms. (Cl. Ex. 4, pp. 25, 27) The logs did not identify 
the injuries Mr. Brown sustained, nor were the proper columns completed after the 
fatality. With respect to reporting the fatality, the investigation found that the employer 
Adam Mitchell never reported the fatality, but that Busted Cup Brewhouse reported it. 
(Cl. Ex. 4, p. 29) The report indicates that New Vision had 15 employees at the time of 
the investigation. Finally, the report noted that certain logs that were requested were not 
provided within four business hours, as required by Iowa law. (Cl. Ex. 4, pp. 30-31) New 
Vision received several fines as a result of the OSHA violations. (Cl. Ex. 4, pp. 21-31) 
 
Ms. Brown’s total funeral expenses came to $17,561.70. (Cl. Ex. 3, p. 16) In 
reviewing the itemized statement, I find the expenses reasonable. Ms. Brown testified 
that she and Mr. Brown’s parents paid the funeral expenses. Ms. Brown has not 
remarried since Mr. Brown’s death. To her knowledge, Mr. Brown was not intoxicated or 
otherwise incapacitated in any way at the time of his death. 
 
I find that Travis Brown was an employee of New Vision Contracting on 
September 15, 2023, when he sustained an injury arising out of and in the course of his 
employment that resulted in his death. I find that Heidi Brown was married to Travis 
Brown on September 15, 2023, and is entitled to compensation under Iowa Code

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established ordinarily has 
the burden of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence. Iowa R. App. P. 
6.904(3)(e). I found that the undisputed evidence proves Ms. Brown is entitled to death 
benefits and burial expenses under Iowa law. 
 
Pursuant to Iowa Code section 85.31, when a death occurs as a result of a work 
injury, death benefits are payable to a surviving spouse for life or until remarriage, 
provided that upon remarriage two years of benefits shall be paid to the surviving 
spouse in a lump sum, provided there are no children entitled to benefits. Iowa Code § 
85.31(1)(a)(1); Iowa Code § 85.43. In this case, there are no children entitled to 
benefits. As such, Heidi Brown is entitled to death benefits pursuant to Iowa Code 
section 85.31. Benefits are payable at the rate of $666.72, commencing September 15, 
2023 and continuing pursuant to Iowa Code sections 85.31(1)(a)(1) and 85.43. 
 
Under Iowa Code section 85.28, when death ensues from an injury, the employer 
shall pay the reasonable expenses of burial of such employee, not to exceed twelve 
times the statewide average weekly wage in effect at the time of death. Iowa Code § 
85.28. The statewide average weekly wage in effect at the time of Mr. Brown’s death on 
September 15, 2023 was $1,099.55. Twelve times that amount is $13,194.60. The total 
burial expenses exceeded that amount. (Cl. Ex. 3, p. 16) As such, Heidi Brown is 
entitled to $13,194.60 in burial expenses. 
 
Finally, the hearing report indicates that Ms. Brown seeks an assessment of 
costs. However, no list of costs was provided with claimant’s exhibits. As such, no costs 
can be awarded at this time. 
A copy of this decision is being provided to the workers’ compensation 
commissioner to determine whether further action should take place under Iowa Code 
section 87.19 for the employer’s failure to have workers’ compensation insurance. 
 
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
 
 
Defendant shall pay Heidi Brown, surviving spouse of Travis Brown, weekly 
death benefits in the amount of six hundred sixty-six and 72/100 dollars ($666.72), 
commencing September 15, 2023, and continuing as required and set forth in Iowa 
Code sections 85.31(1)(a)(1) and 85.43. 
 
 
Defendant shall pay accrued weekly benefits in a lump sum together with interest 
at an annual rate equal to the one-year treasury constant maturity published by the

STATEMENT OF THE CASE   
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. The 
expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Monica Cameron. 
Claimant appeared telephonically and through her attorney, MaKayla Augustine.  
Defendants did not file an answer or appearance, and did not participate in the 
hearing. The proof of service attached to the petition indicates the petition was sent via 
certified mail to the employer and insurance carrier on November 20, 2024. The return 
receipt was filed with the agency on December 3, 2024, and indicates the certified mail 
was delivered to both the employer and the insurance carrier on November 25, 2024. 
Additionally, the agency sent a notice of hearing via US mail to both the employer and 
insurance carrier on November 21, 2024. Service was proper. As such, the hearing 
proceeded without defendants’ participation. 
The alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on December 4, 2024. The 
proceedings were digitally recorded. That recording constitutes the official record of this 
proceeding. Pursuant to the Commissioner’s July 21, 2023 Order, the undersigned has 
been delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical care 
proceeding. Therefore, this ruling is designated final agency action and any appeal of 
the decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A. 
The record consists of claimant’s exhibit 1 (attached to the petition), consisting of 
four pages. No witnesses were called, but claimant’s attorney made a statement for the 
record.

ISSUE 
The issue presented for resolution is whether the claimant is entitled to alternate 
medical care consisting of authorization and scheduling of an appointment with a 
qualified physician to treat claimant’s back and left lower extremity injuries. 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
The undersigned, having considered all of the evidence in the record, finds: 
Claimant sustained an injury to her bilateral shoulders, back, left hip, and left 
lower extremity arising out of and in the course of employment on June 11, 2024. 
Defendants authorized treatment for claimant’s bilateral shoulders. (Claimant’s Exhibit 
1, p. 1) However, claimant’s requests for treatment for her back and left leg were 
ignored. Claimant experienced significant, increasing sharp pain, functional limitations, 
numbness, and tingling in her back and into her left leg, along with buckling of her left 
leg. On or around October 15, 2024, she spoke with the requisite individual at Amazon, 
who advised he sent her request for treatment “up to the superiors.” (Cl. Ex. 1, p. 1) 
On November 15, 2024, claimant’s attorney sent a letter to the claims adjuster, 
Helyn Cole, advising of claimant’s dissatisfaction with care, and asking for authorization 
to see a qualified physician for her back and left leg symptoms. (CL. Ex. 1, pp. 1-4) No 
response has been received to date. Claimant has attempted to see her primary care 
physician for treatment, but he declined to see her since the case involves a workers’ 
compensation claim. She continues to experience significant and worsening pain. 
REASONING AND CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
Iowa Code section 85.27(4) provides, in relevant part: 
For purposes of this section, the employer is obliged to furnish 
reasonable services and supplies to treat an injured employee, and has 
the right to choose the care. . . .  The treatment must be offered promptly 
and be reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue inconvenience 
to the employee. If the employee has reason to be dissatisfied with the 
care offered, the employee should communicate the basis of such 
dissatisfaction to the employer, in writing if requested, following which the 
employer and the employee may agree to alternate care reasonably suited 
to treat the injury. If the employer and employee cannot agree on such 
alternate care, the commissioner may, upon application and reasonable 
proofs of the necessity therefor, allow and order other care. 
Iowa Code § 85.27(4). 
Defendants’ “obligation under the statute is confined to reasonable care for the 
diagnosis and treatment of work-related injuries.” Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528

N.W.2d 122, 124 (Iowa 1995) (emphasis in original). In other words, the “obligation 
under the statute turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not desirability.” Id. 
Similarly, an application for alternate medical care is not automatically sustained 
because claimant is dissatisfied with the care he has been receiving. Mere 
dissatisfaction with the medical care is not ample grounds for granting an application for 
alternate medical care. Rather, the claimant must show that the care was not offered 
promptly, was not reasonably suited to treat the injury, or that the care was unduly 
inconvenient for the claimant. See Iowa Code § 85.27(4). Thus, by challenging the 
employer’s choice of treatment and seeking alternate care, claimant assumes the 
burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable. See Iowa R. App. P. 14(f)(5); 
Long, 528 N.W.2d at 124.   
Ultimately, determining whether care is reasonable under the statute is a 
question of fact.  Long, 528 N.W.2d at 123. 
In this case, claimant has met her burden to prove that the care offered by 
defendants is unreasonable. Defendants have not authorized any treatment for 
claimant’s significant back and left lower extremity symptoms. No explanation has been 
provided, and defendants did not participate in the hearing. Defendants are not offering 
prompt care that is reasonably suited to treat the injury.  
The evidence demonstrates that defendants are not meeting their obligation to 
provide reasonable care for the work-related injury. As such, claimant is entitled to 
alternate care. The petition is granted. 
ORDER 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted. 
Within ten (10) days of the date of this ruling, defendants shall authorize and 
schedule an appointment for treatment with a qualified physician for claimant’s back and 
left lower extremity symptoms. Defendants will schedule the appointment to take place 
on the first available date, and advise claimant’s attorney of the appointment details 
immediately.  
Failure to comply with this order may result in the termination of defendants’ 
ability to direct care for the work injury.

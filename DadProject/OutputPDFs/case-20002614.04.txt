Claimant filed an Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care with this 
agency on September 9, 2022.  
Based on emails with counsel for both parties, and a proposed consent order, 
defendants agree to make an appointment for claimant with Eric Aschenbrenner, M.D. 
on or before October 3, 2022.  Defendants are not required to have claimant seen by 
Dr. Aschenbrenner on or by October 3, 2022.  Defendants are required to make the 
appointment with Dr. Aschenbrenner by that date. 
To avoid an unnecessary hearing, and yet protect the interests of their clients, 
the parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendants make the 
appointment with Dr. Aschenbrenner as detailed above. 
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties. A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing. 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, defendants agree to make an 
appointment for claimant with Eric Aschenbrenner, M.D., on or before October 3, 2022.  
Defendants are not required to have claimant seen by Dr. Aschenbrenner on or by 
October 3, 2022.  Defendants are required to make the appointment with Dr.

STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
 
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. The 
expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Donald Tuttle. 
Claimant appeared personally and through his attorney, Jeff Carter. Defendant 
appeared through its attorney, Peter Thill. 
 
The application was filed on January 31, 2025. Defendant filed an answer on 
February 7, 2025. Hearing took place on February 11, 2025. The proceedings were 
digitally recorded. That recording constitutes the official record of this proceeding. 
Pursuant to the Commissioner’s July 21, 2023 Order, the undersigned has been 
delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical care 
proceeding. Therefore, this ruling is designated final agency action and any appeal of 
the decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A. 
The record consists of claimant’s exhibits 1 and 2, and defendant’s exhibit A. 
Claimant’s wife, Michelle Tuttle, testified, and counsel offered oral arguments to support 
their positions.  
 
ISSUES 
 
Claimant seeks an order directing defendant to immediately authorize and pay 
for prescription medications from the authorized treating medical provider, Jeffrey 
Wilharm, M.D. Claimant also seeks an order instructing defendant to find a way to 
promptly authorize future prescriptions, and an order that defendant be required to 
produce a witness with authority to authorize medical care should additional alternate 
care proceedings take place on this issue. Finally, claimant submitted medical bills 
related to claimant’s treatment with Dr. Wilharm that remain unpaid.

FINDINGS OF FACT 
 
Claimant sustained a stipulated injury on April 14, 2022. Defendant has admitted 
liability for the mental health injury, and has authorized Jeffrey Wilharm, M.D., to provide 
psychiatric treatment for claimant related to the injury. 
 
Claimant’s wife, Michelle Tuttle, testified that she is familiar with Dr. Wilharm’s 
treatment, as she takes claimant to and from his appointments, and also picks up the 
prescription medications Dr. Wilharm prescribes. The Tuttles use a CVS pharmacy in 
Cedar Rapids, Iowa, for their prescription needs. Mrs. Tuttle testified that there have 
been near constant issues with obtaining claimant’s prescriptions, due to the pharmacy 
lacking proper authorization from the insurance company.1  
 
The two prescriptions at issue are Desvenlafaxine and Duloxetine, both 
prescribed by Dr. Wilharm. (See Claimant’s Exhibit 2, pp. 6-7) Mrs. Tuttle testified that 
there have been problems picking up claimant’s prescriptions on every occasion except 
for one. She testified that sometime in the past week or so, when she attempted to pick 
up claimant’s most recent prescriptions, she was told they had been denied because 
they require preauthorization. She testified that the pharmacist at CVS has become 
exasperated with the situation, and the last time she was “waving papers” and told Mrs. 
Tuttle to call the insurance company. Mrs. Tuttle testified that claimant was out of one of 
his prescriptions, and almost out of the other, so she ended up paying out-of-pocket for 
them, as she has done in the past. (See Cl. Ex. 2, pp. 4-7) She also testified that at 
times, claimant has received samples from Dr. Wilharm when he is unable to get his 
prescriptions filled. She has also given him her own medication, as one of the 
prescriptions is for something she is also prescribed. She has considered changing 
pharmacies due to her interactions with the pharmacist at this particular CVS.  
 
Mrs. Tuttle acknowledges she was issued a partial reimbursement for the most 
recent prescriptions, which she paid for out-of-pocket. (Cl. Ex. 2, p. 8) She is unsure 
why the refund was only partial, as in the past she has always been reimbursed the full 
amount.  
 
With respect to Dr. Wilharm’s unpaid bills, Mrs. Tuttle testified that as far as she 
is aware, the bills remain unpaid, as they are still being sent to her home. (Cl. Ex. 1, pp. 
1-3) 
 
Claimant’s attorney argued that the exhibits and testimony, as well as past 
alternate care hearings, demonstrate a pattern of behavior from defendant employer 
involving unreasonable delay and interference with claimant’s medical care. 
Defendant’s attorney noted that the prescriptions at issue have now been authorized, 
and the payment of Dr. Wilharm’s medical bills is not an appropriate issue for an 
alternate care proceeding. (See Def. Ex. A, pp. 1-6)

REASONING AND CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
  
Iowa Code section 85.27 provides: 
 
The employer, for all injuries compensable under this chapter or chapter 
85A. shall furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, osteopathic, 
chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance and 
hospital services and supplies therefor and shall allow reasonably 
necessary transportation expenses incurred for such services. The 
employer shall also furnish reasonable and necessary crutches, artificial 
members and appliances but shall not be required to furnish more than one 
set of permanent prosthetic devices. 
 
The employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except where the 
employer has denied liability for the injury. Iowa Code § 85.27(4). The treatment must 
be offered promptly and be reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue 
inconvenience to the employee. Id. 
 
By challenging the employer’s choice of treatment - and seeking alternate care - 
claimant assumes the burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable. See Iowa 
R. App. P. P 14(f)(5); Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995). 
Determining what care is reasonable under the statute is a question of fact. Id. The 
employer’s obligation turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not desirability. Id.; 
Harned v. Farmland Foods, Inc., 331 N.W.2d 98 (Iowa 1983). In Pirelli-Armstrong Tire 
Co. v. Reynolds, 562 N.W.2d 433 (Iowa 1997), the court approvingly quoted Bowles v. 
Los Lunas Schools, 109 N.M. 100, 781 P.2d 1178 (App. 1989): 
 
[T]he words “reasonable” and “adequate” appear to describe the same 
standard. 
  
[The New Mexico rule] requires the employer to provide a certain standard 
of care and excuses the employer from any obligation to provide other 
services only if that standard is met. We construe the terms “reasonable” 
and “adequate” as describing care that is both appropriate to the injury and 
sufficient to bring the worker to maximum recovery. 
 
Mere dissatisfaction with the medical care is not ample grounds for granting an 
application for alternate medical care. Rather, the claimant must show that the care was 
not offered promptly, was not reasonably suited to treat the injury, or that the care was 
unduly inconvenient for the claimant. See Iowa Code § 85.27(4). Thus, by challenging 
the employer’s choice of treatment and seeking alternate care, claimant assumes the 
burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable. See Iowa R. App. P 14(f)(5); 
Long, 528 N.W.2d at 124.   
An employer’s right to select the provider of medical treatment to an injured 
worker does not include the right to determine how an injured worker should be

diagnosed, evaluated, treated, or other matters of professional medical judgment.  
Assmann v. Blue Star Foods, File No. 866389 (Declaratory Ruling, May 19, 1988).  
Defendants are not entitled to interfere with the medical judgment of their own treating 
physician.  Pote v. Mickow Corp., File No. 694639 (Review-Reopening Dec., June 17, 
1986); Boggs v. Cargill, Inc., File No. 1050396, (Alt Care, January 31, 1994). 
 
The right to choose the care means the right to choose the provider, not the 
treatment modalities recommended by the provider. The employer must provide the 
treatment, testing, imaging or other treatment modalities recommended by its own 
authorized treating physician, even if another consulting physician disagrees with those 
recommendations. Haack v. Von Hoffman Graphics, File No. 1268172, p. 9 (App. July 
31, 2002) [MRI and x-rays]; Cahill v. S & H Fabricating & Engineering, File No. 1138063 
(Alt Care, May 30, 1997) (work hardening program); Hawxby v. Hallett Materials, File 
No. 1112821, (Alt Care, February 20, 1996); Leitzen v. Collis, Inc., File No. 1084677, 
(Alt Care, September 9, 1996).  
Additionally, the commissioner is justified in ordering alternate care when 
employer-authorized care has not been effective and evidence shows that such care is 
“inferior or less extensive” than other available care requested by the employee.  Long; 
528 N.W.2d at 124; Pirelli-Armstrong Tire Co. v. Reynods, 562 N.W.2d 433, 437 (Iowa 
1997). 
Ultimately, determining whether care is reasonable under the statute is a 
question of fact.  Long, 528 N.W.2d at 123. Claimant argued that this case is similar to 
the situation in Erlbacher v. REM Iowa, File No. 5056102.04 (Alt. Care, April 4, 2024). In 
that case, the claimant’s prescription medication had been repeatedly delayed or 
denied, and the deputy found it was unreasonable for the defendants to cause any 
further delay to claimant receiving her authorized medications. There was no 
explanation for why timely, prompt authorization for the undisputed medicine was not 
made.  
In this case, the employer has accepted liability for the injury, and is providing 
authorized treatment through Dr. Wilharm. Part of his treatment includes prescribing 
claimant the medications Desvenlafaxine and Duloxetine. The medications are being 
prescribed on a regular basis by a known, authorized treating provider. It is 
unreasonable that there would be any delay or issue for claimant in obtaining those 
medications. However, unlike the case in Erlbacher, the prescriptions at issue in this 
case have currently been authorized.  
The summary procedures set forth in the alternate medical care statute and the 
rules provide injured workers with prospective relief, not for the payment of past bills. 
Consequently, the agency has repeatedly articulated the following statement of law: a 
decision in an alternate medical care proceeding operates prospectively only, not 
retroactively. The claimant’s application for alternate care should be dismissed without 
prejudice when the claimant seeks payment for medical care that had been provided 
prior to the time the alternate medical care petition was filed. Moline v. Nordstrom, File 
No. 1273226 (Alt Care, December 21, 2000); Donisi v. Norrell Services, File No.

1276161 (Alt Care, August 8, 2000); Mobayed v. AMS Services, Inc., File No. 1168048 
(Alt Care, May 20, 1997). 
Therefore, with respect to the payment of Dr. Wilharm’s outstanding medical bills, 
the petition is dismissed without prejudice. There are other mechanisms available to 
claimant for resolving the issues regarding past medical expenses. 
That being said, any ongoing delays in authorization of claimant’s undisputed 
medication is unreasonable. Defendants have a duty to provide treatment that is offered 
promptly, and reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue inconvenience to the 
employee. Iowa Code § 85.27(4). While the most recent prescription appears to have 
been authorized, I find it is unreasonable for defendant to cause any further delay in 
claimant receiving his authorized medications. Defendant must find a way to promptly 
authorize future prescriptions so the delays and inconvenience to claimant and his wife 
do not continue. Whether this is through providing a prescription card or some other 
arrangement with CVS pharmacy, claimant must be able to obtain refills of his 
medication in a prompt manner. 
If claimant is required to file another application for alternate care on this 
particular issue regarding prescriptions, it is expected that defendant will have a 
representative appear at hearing who has the authority to authorize medications in 
order to explain the alleged further delay. Additionally, failure to comply with this order 
may result in the imposition of sanctions under 876 Iowa Administrative Code section 
4.36. 
 
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted in part. 
 
Defendants shall immediately authorize and pay for any causally related 
prescription medication prescribed by the authorized treating provider, Dr. Wilharm, and 
work to ensure future prescriptions are filled promptly and without undue inconvenience 
to the claimant.  
 
Failure to comply with this order may result in the imposition of sanctions under 
876 Iowa Administrative Code section 4.36.

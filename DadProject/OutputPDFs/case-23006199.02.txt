On February 24, 2025, Kyle Goff applied for alternate care under Iowa Code 
section 85.27 and Iowa Administrative Code rule 876—4.48. The defendants, employer 
Crow’s Auto Service, Inc. and insurance carrier Service American Indemnity Co., did not 
file an answer. Instead, the parties worked together to reach an agreement regarding 
the care at the heart of their dispute. Counsel notified the undersigned of their 
agreement by email before the hearing on March 5, 2024, and provided proposed draft 
language memorializing their agreement for inclusion in a consent order. 
Under the parties’ agreement, the defendants agree to authorize Goff for an 
evaluation of his neck, back, and spine complaints at Iowa Ortho and arrange for an 
appointment for the same no later than 5:00 p.m. on Friday, March 14, 2025. The 
defendants have been in communication with Iowa Ortho regarding this appointment 
and Iowa Ortho has advised that it will provide notice to the defendants early during the 
week of March 10, 2025, if Iowa Ortho is unwilling or otherwise unable to provide care 
to Goff. In the event Iowa Ortho provides such notice, the defendants agree that within 
ten days of receipt of such notice, they will authorize Goff to receive care with an 
alternate provider and arrange for an appointment with that provider. Further, the 
defendants agree that after Goff’s appointment at Iowa Ortho or an alternative provider, 
they will promptly authorize and arrange all care recommended by authorized treating 
providers that is reasonably related to his May 12, 2023 injury. Because of the parties’ 
agreement, the alternate care hearing scheduled in this case is not needed and is 
therefore cancelled. 
SO ORDERED.

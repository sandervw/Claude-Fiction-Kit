The undersigned issued an arbitration decision in this case on October 23, 
2025.  On October 24, 2025, claimant filed a motion for nunc pro tunc.  A response 
has not been filed by the employer and insurance carrier. 
In her motion for nunc pro tunc, claimant asserts that the undersigned erred in 
a factual finding on page 14 of the arbitration decision.  The paragraph from the 
arbitration decision at issue reads as follows: 
In addition, claimant seeks authorization of ongoing medical care for her 
epilepsy and seizures. Defendants have authorized reasonable medical 
care for those conditions so alternate medical care is not really necessary 
or sought. However, claimant retains future medical rights for treatment of 
her epilepsy and seizures related to the January 7, 2021 work injury. Iowa 
Code section 85.27. She is found to not yet have achieved maximum 
medical improvement. Therefore, by definition, claimant requires ongoing 
and future medical care for these conditions. 
 
Claimant contends in the motion for nunc pro tunc that the undersigned made 
a simple “oversight” and that his intent is clear on page 19 to award alternate 
medical care for treatment of claimant’s epilepsy and seizures through Dr. Seldon 
Spencer or other providers under Dr. Spencer’s direction through the University of 
Iowa Hospitals and Clinics epilepsy clinic.  After reviewing claimant’s motion for nunc 
pro tunc and the disputed portions of the arbitration decision, the undersigned

believes the motion is likely incorrectly categorized as a motion for nunc pro tunc.  
Rather, claimant was being gracious to the undersigned when categorizing the error 
on page 14 as “simply an oversight.”   
 
The claimant’s motion is more accurately categorized as a motion for 
rehearing pursuant to 876 IAC 4.24.  The undersigned clearly erred in stating that 
defendants authorized reasonable medical care for claimant’s head injury, epilepsy, 
and seizures.  That is clearly a denied claim in this case and was the subject of an 
award of past medical expenses.  The undersigned clearly erred in his statement on 
page 14 in the paragraph referenced above. 
 
The undersigned accurately ordered the employer and insurance carrier to 
provide ongoing medical care for claimant’s head injury and resulting epilepsy on 
page 19 of the arbitration decision.  However, revision of the arbitration decision is 
appropriate and is probably not simply a nunc pro tunc situation because the 
undersigned’s reasoning was inaccurate and factually incorrect.   
 
The disputed paragraph on page 14 of the arbitration decision should be 
amended to state that defendants did not authorize medical care but that claimant is 
legally entitled to treatment of her head injury, epilepsy, and seizures as conditions 
that arose out of and in the course of her employment.  Iowa Code section 85.27. 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
The claimant’s motion for nunc pro tunc is considered as a motion for 
rehearing and is granted. 
 
The dispute paragraph on page 14 of the arbitration decision is amended to 
read as follows: 
 
In addition, claimant seeks authorization of ongoing medical care for her 
epilepsy and seizures.  Defendants denied these conditions arose out of 
and in the course of claimant’s employment.  However, those conditions 
are now found to have arisen out of claimant’s work injury and defendants 
are legally responsible for providing medical care for those conditions.  
Iowa Code section 85.27.  Claimant has established medical care for her 
epilepsy and seizures with Dr. Spencer at the University of Iowa Hospitals 
and Clinics and retains future medical rights for treatment of her epilepsy 
and seizures related to the January 7, 2021 work injury.  Iowa Code 
section 85.27.  She is found to not yet have achieved maximum medical 
improvement.  Therefore, by definition, claimant requires ongoing and 
future medical care for these conditions.  I find that it is reasonable to 
order that her care for her epilepsy and seizures be ordered to be 
authorized through Dr. Spencer, or other physicians to whom he may refer

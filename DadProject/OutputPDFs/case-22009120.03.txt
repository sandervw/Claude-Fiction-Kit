On October 22, 2024, claimant filed an original notice and petition for alternate 
medical care in this file under Iowa Code section 85.27, invoking the provisions of rule 
876 IAC 4.48. Claimant sought an order that defendant authorize a physician to provide 
treatment for claimant’s right shoulder. 
 
Hearing was scheduled for October 31, 2024. Defendant filed an answer prior to 
hearing indicating treatment has been authorized with Daniel Goldish, M.D., at Steindler 
Orthopedic Clinic. The parties and the undersigned had a telephone conference, in 
which the parties agreed that authorization of Dr. Goldish was reasonable. Claimant’s 
attorney expressed concerns regarding the timeline for scheduling the appointment, and 
the parties agreed defendant will make every effort to have an appointment date within 
the next seven days. Defendant will schedule claimant for Dr. Goldish’s next available 
appointment date. In the event defendant is not able to schedule the appointment in the 
next week, defense counsel will provide claimant’s counsel with an update as to the 
status. 
 
Due to the parties’ agreement, the hearing was deemed unnecessary. The 
parties requested a consent order to document and record their agreement. 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
 
The parties agree that defendant will authorize Daniel Goldish, M.D., to provide 
treatment for claimant’s right shoulder.  
 
Defendant will schedule the appointment with Dr. Goldish within seven (7) days 
of the date of this order. In the event defendant is unable to schedule the appointment 
within that time frame, defense counsel will provide an update to claimant’s counsel 
regarding the status of scheduling within the same seven-day period. 
 
Defendant will schedule the appointment with Dr. Goldish for his next available 
appointment date.

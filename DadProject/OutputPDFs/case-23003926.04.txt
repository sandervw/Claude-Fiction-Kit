This is a contested case concerning alternate medical care, proceeding under 
Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The expedited procedure of rule 876 Iowa 
Administrative Code 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Donnette Barber.  On January 2, 
2025, the undersigned reached out to the parties inquiring as to whether or not they 
wished to enter into a consent order.  This was based on previous correspondence with 
the parties in which the defendants indicated that they agreed to authorize certain 
mental health care requested by the claimant.  The parties indicated that they wished to 
enter into a consent order rather than proceed with a hearing on the issue. 
 
The defendants consent, stipulate, and agree to authorize mental health care 
treatment.  The defendants also agreed to schedule the authorized treatment as soon 
as possible, but no later than ten (10) days from the date of this order.  The appointment 
need not occur within the previously noted ten (10) days. 
 
The parties proposed that the undersigned issue this consent order in lieu of a 
formal decision.  All parties consented to the entry of a consent order via e-mail on 
January 2, 2025; thus no alternate medical care decision will be issued.   
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
The parties have agreed that Defendants will authorize the claimant to proceed 
with mental health care with a provider of the defendants choosing.   
 
The defendants shall schedule mental health care treatment as soon as possible, 
but no later than ten (10) days following the issuance of this order.

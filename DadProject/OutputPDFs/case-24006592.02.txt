On May 28, 2025, claimant filed an application for alternate medical care, 
pursuant to Iowa Code section 85.27, invoking the provisions of rule 876 IAC 
4.48.  Claimant alleged on June 20, 2024, he sustained injuries to the right upper 
extremity, left upper extremity, left lower extremity, right lower extremity, right shoulder, 
right clavicle, neck and body as a whole. In the petition, claimant argued that the right 
upper extremity, left upper extremity, right lower extremity, right shoulder, right clavicle, 
and neck needed care. On June 4, 2025, defendants filed an answer accepting liability 
for the right clavicle only. 
 
The parties convened for a hearing on June 6, 2025, and the parties discussed 
that defendants would agree to provide care for the clavicle. All other treatment 
requests were dismissed based on defendants’ denial of liability. 
 
Defendants cannot deny liability and simultaneously direct the course of 
treatment.  Barnhart v. MAQ Incorporated, I Iowa Industrial Comm’r Report 16 (App. 
March 9, 1981).               
 
Because of the authorization for alleged injuries to the right upper extremity, left 
upper extremity, right lower extremity, right shoulder, and neck have been denied, this 
matter is dismissed. 
 
It was agreed that the defendants would obtain a response from Dr. Bruggeman 
by June 23, 2025, or, in the alternative, provide a referral to a Des Moines doctor for 
treatment of the right clavicle.

IT IS, THEREFORE, ORDERED that claimant’s petition for alternate care for the 
right clavicle is GRANTED in part in that the defendants will obtain a response from Dr. 
Bruggeman by June 23, 2025, or, in the alternative, provide a referral to a Des Moines 
doctor. 
 
 
IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that the claims for treatment for the right upper 
extremity, left upper extremity, right lower extremity, right shoulder, and neck should be 
and are hereby dismissed without prejudice.             
 
IT IS FURTHER ORDERED that if claimant seeks to recover the charges 
incurred in obtaining care for a condition for which defendants denied liability, 
defendants are barred from asserting lack of authorization as a defense to those 
charges.

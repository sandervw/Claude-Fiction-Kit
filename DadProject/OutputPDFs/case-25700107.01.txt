On January 30, 2025, claimant filed a petition for alternate care.  Claimant 
requested treatment for injuries to his cervical spine, thoracic spine and ribs that he 
alleges he sustained at work on March 11, 2024.  Specifically, claimant requested care 
as recommended by Dr. Gustin on December 30, 2024.  Dr. Gustin’s December 30, 
2024 treatment note, however, was not attached to his petition.  On February 7, 2025, 
defendants filed an answer admitting liability for work-related injuries to claimant’s 
thoracic spine and ribs, but denying liability for an injury to claimant’s cervical spine.   
The alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on February 10, 2025.  
Claimant appeared through his attorney, Sara Lemme.  Defendants appeared through 
their attorney, Julie Burger. The proceedings were digitally recorded. That recording 
constitutes the official record of this proceeding.  
Pursuant to the Commissioner’s July 21, 2023, Order, the undersigned has been 
delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical care 
proceeding. Therefore, this ruling is designated final agency action and any appeal of 
the decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A. 
Before alternate medical care can be ordered, compensability of the medical 
condition to be treated must be established, either by admission of liability or by 
adjudication.  The summary procedure of Iowa Code section 85.27, as more particularly 
described in rule 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.48(6), is not available to adjudicate 
liability or causal connection disputes.

At the start of the hearing, claimant’s counsel clarified that the treatment Dr. 
Gustin recommended is for claimant’s cervical spine. (Testimony).  Defendants’ counsel 
indicated that defendants have not accepted liability for claimant’s cervical spine at this 
time.  (Id.).  Defendants are still investigating compensability of the cervical spine and 
are waiting on the results of an independent medical exam (IME) with Dr. Gill.  Under 
prior case law, this is tantamount to a denial of liability. (Id.).  
As a result of the denial of liability for the condition sought to be treated in this 
proceeding, claimant may obtain reasonable medical care from any provider for this 
treatment, at claimant’s expense, and seek reimbursement for such care using regular 
claim proceedings before this agency.  Haack v. Von Hoffman Graphics, File No. 
1268172 (App. July 31, 2002); Kindhart v. Fort Des Moines Hotel, I Iowa Industrial 
Comm’r Decisions No. 3, 611 (App. March 27, 1985).  “[T]he employer has no right to 
choose the medical care when compensability is contested.”  Bell Bros. Heating and Air 
Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 N.W.2d 193, 204 (Iowa 2010).  The defendants are 
precluded from asserting an authorization defense as to any future treatment during 
their period of denial.  Brewer-Strong v. HNI Corp., 913 N.W.2d 235 (Iowa 2018).   
ORDER 
1. Claimant’s application for alternate medical care regarding the above-named 
defendants is dismissed without prejudice. 
2. If claimant seeks to recover the charges incurred in obtaining care for the 
condition for which defendants denied liability, defendants are barred from 
asserting lack of authorization as a defense to those charges during the 
period of their denial.

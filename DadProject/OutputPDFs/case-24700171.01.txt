STATEMENT OF THE CASE   
Claimant, Samuel E. Gomez Romero, filed a petition in arbitration seeking 
workers’ compensation benefits from Jose M. Esquivel Hernandez, defendant employer, 
as a result of an alleged injury sustained on January 8, 2022.   
Claimant filed an original notice and petition with the Division of Workers’ 
Compensation (Agency) on February 24, 2024.  Proof of service of the petition was filed 
with the Agency on March 5, 2024, including a certified mail return receipt sent to 
defendant’s last known address.  It appears to be signed for by the defendant on March 
4, 2024.  On April 12, 2024, claimant filed a notice of intent to file written application for 
default judgment against Jose M. Esquivel Hernandez with the Agency.  On September 
6, 2024, claimant filed an application for default judgment against Jose M. Esquivel 
Hernandez.  On September 24, 2024, the undersigned granted claimant’s application 
and entered default against defendant, Jose M. Esquivel Hernandez.   
The matter was set by the undersigned for a default hearing on November 14, 
2024, at 8:30 a.m.  The matter was heard and fully submitted to Deputy Workers’ 
Compensation Commissioner, Amanda Rutherford, on that date.  Defendant failed to 
appear and participate.  Defendant did not file an appearance, motion, answer, or other 
pleading with the Agency.  The record consists of claimant’s amended exhibits 1-6 and 
the testimony of the claimant.  The matter was recorded by means of digital audio 
recorder.     
ISSUES 
The following issues were presented for determination:   
1. Whether claimant is entitled to payment of medical expenses.

FINDINGS OF FACT   
The undersigned, having considered all of the evidence and testimony in the 
record, finds as follows: 
The claimant’s name is Samuel Eduardo Gomez Romero (hereinafter “Gomez”).  
(Testimony).  He resides in Rock Valley, Iowa.  (Id.).   On the date of his injury, he was 
23 years old.  (Id.).  
On the date of injury, January 8, 2022, Gomez was doing construction work for 
Jose M. Esquivel Hernandez (hereinafter “Hernandez”), in Sioux Center, Iowa. 
(Testimony).  Gomez worked full-time for Hernandez, 40 hours per week, and he was 
paid $16.00 an hour. (Id.).  
Gomez testified that he was cutting wood with a saw and cut his finger.  
(Testimony).  A co-worker, Martin, drove him to the emergency room to stop the 
bleeding. (Id.).  According to the medical bills, Gomez was treated at the Sioux Center 
Health Center on January 8, 2022, for a finger amputation. (Claimant’s Exhibit 1).  
Gomez also submitted pictures of his left hand. (Cl Ex. 5, pp. 18-19).  They show that 
his left middle finger has been amputated below the nailbed.  (Id.).  At the Sioux Center 
ER, Gomez received pain medication, numbing agents, antibiotics, immunizations, and 
an x-ray. (Cl Ex. 1). He received follow-up treatment at the Avera McKennan Hospital 
on January 10, 2022, and January 26, 2022. (Cl Ex. 1).  That treatment consisted of 
surgery on the amputated finger, pain medications, antibiotics, and a finger splint. (Id.).  
He also attended a session of therapy at the Avera Specialty Clinic on January 26, 
2022. (Id.).   
For this treatment, Sioux Center Hospital, Avera McKennan Hospital, and Avera 
Specialty Clinic charged Gomez $18,810.20. (Cl Ex. 1).  Gomez did not have funds to 
pay these charges.  (Testimony).  They remained unpaid at the time of hearing. (Id.).  
In October 2023, the Sioux Center Health Center, Avera McKennan Hospital, and 
Avera Specialty Clinic sold Gomez’s debt to Accounts Management, Inc., a collection 
agency. (Cl Ex. 2).  On November 28, 2023, Accounts Management, Inc., filed a petition 
in Lyon County District Court, suing Gomez for $18,810.20 plus costs, disbursements, 
and post-judgment interest. (Cl Ex. 4).  On January 17, 2024, Gomez filed an answer 
stating he was being treated for a work injury and the bills were Hernandez’s 
responsibility. (Id.).  On March 4, 2024, Accounts Management, Inc., dismissed its claim 
without prejudice. (Id.).

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
The only issues are whether claimant is entitled to payment of the medical 
expenses outlined in claimant’s exhibits 1 and 2, as well as his prayer for hearing costs. 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established ordinarily has the 
burden of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence.  Iowa R. App. P. 
6.904(3)(e). 
The employer shall furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, osteopathic, 
chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance, and hospital services 
and supplies for all conditions compensable under the workers' compensation law.  The 
employer shall also allow reasonable and necessary transportation expenses incurred 
for those services.  The employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except 
where the employer has denied liability for the injury.  Section 85.27.  Holbert v. 
Townsend Engineering Co., Thirty-second Biennial Report of the Industrial 
Commissioner 78 (Review-Reopening October 16, 1975).   
Claimant is not entitled to reimbursement for medical bills unless claimant shows 
that they were paid from claimant's funds.  See Caylor v. Employers Mutual Casualty 
Co., 337 N.W.2d 890 (Iowa Ct. App. 1983).  Claimant has the burden of proving that the 
fees charged for such services are reasonable.  Anderson v. High Rise Construction 
Specialists, Inc., File No. 850096 (App. July 31, 1990).   
Claimant’s exhibit 1 details the medical expenses incurred by claimant in treating 
his condition related to the January 8, 2022 work-related injury.  Defendant failed to 
offer any care to claimant, despite knowledge of the work injury.  Claimant offered proof 
that the care he received at the Sioux Center ER on January 8, 2022, Avera McKennan 
Hospital on January 10, 2022, and January 26, 2022, and Avera Specialty Clinic on 
January 26, 2022, was reasonable and necessary because of the work injury on 
January 8, 2022. (Testimony).   
Defendant failed to offer claimant care and failed to participate in the evidentiary 
hearing to contest whether the services were reasonable and necessary, causally 
related to the work injury, any authorization or lack thereof, or the reasonableness of the 
charges.  Given the above findings of fact, defendant is liable for payment of the 
medical expenses listed in claimant’s exhibits 1 and 2. A specific determination of 
defendant’s liability follows, identified by specific provider and amount owed:

The total expenses ordered to be paid by the defendant are $18,810.20  
Gomez also seeks an award of costs as outlined in claimant’s exhibits 3 and 6. 
Costs are to be assessed at the discretion of the deputy commissioner hearing the 
case. See 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.33; Iowa Code § 10A.328. Iowa 
Administrative Rule 4.33 provides as follows:  
[c]osts taxed by the workers’ compensation commissioner or a deputy 
commissioner shall be (1) attendance of a certified shorthand reporter or 
presence of mechanical means at hearings and evidential depositions, (2) 
transcription costs when appropriate, (3) costs of service of the original 
notice and subpoenas, (4) witness fees and expenses as provided by Iowa 
Code sections 622.69 and 622.72, (5) the costs of doctors’ and 
practitioners’ deposition testimony, provided that said costs do not exceed 
the amounts provided by Iowa Code sections 622.69 and 622.72, (6) the 
reasonable costs of obtaining no more than two doctors’ or practitioners’ 
reports, (7) filing fees when appropriate, including convenience fees 
incurred by using the WCES payment gateway, and (8) costs of persons 
reviewing health service disputes. 
876 IAC 4.33. Gomez incurred costs of $134.03 for the filing fee of his petition and 
service costs.  (Cl Ex. 3).  Gomez was successful in this action.  I conclude it is 
reasonable to assess his filing fee and service costs pursuant to 876 IAC 4.33(3) and 
(7).  Gomez also seeks reimbursement for the cost of the hearing translator in the 
amount of $176.80.  (Cl Ex. 6).  I find it is appropriate to assess the cost of the hearing 
translator.  
ORDER 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
Defendant shall pay the medical bills set forth in claimant’s exhibits 1 and 2, 
totaling eighteen thousand eight hundred ten and 20/100 dollars ($18,810.20).   
Defendant shall pay costs of three hundred ten and 83/100 dollars ($310.83).   
Defendant shall file subsequent reports of injury (SROI) as required by this 
agency pursuant to rules 876 IAC 3.1(2) and 876 IAC 11.7.

On February 23, 2022, Claimant Moise Kanund filed a petition concerning 
application for alternate care under Iowa Code section 85.27 and rule 876 Iowa 
Administrative Code 4.48.  A hearing was held on March 7, 2022.  All parties were given 
proper notice of the hearing.  Attorney Cory Abbas represented Kanund.  Kanund 
appeared and testified.  Attorney Dillion Carpenter represented Defendant Tyson 
Foods, Inc. (“Tyson”)  
Kanund sustained an injury to his left leg on November 1, 2019.  Tyson accepted 
the condition and provided treatment to Kanund.  After he was released from care, 
Kanund requested a second opinion, which was granted.  Tyson authorized additional 
care.  After he was released from care, Kanund requested additional pain management 
treatment for tingling and pain in his left ankle.  Tyson did not authorize additional 
treatment.  Kanund sought treatment on his own and his primary care provider referred 
him for pain management treatment for his left ankle.  Kanund then filed an application 
for alternate care requesting Tyson be ordered to authorize pain management treatment 
for his left ankle. 
A hearing was recorded via a digital voice recorder and a complete decision was 
dictated into the record on the day of the hearing.  The deputy workers’ compensation 
commissioner ordered Kanund’s application for alternate care be granted.  The decision 
will be reproduced in typewritten form only if the decision is appealed, and all parties are 
advised to follow the procedures outlined in the administrative code.  Any rights of 
appeal run from the date the decision was dictated into the record, March 7, 2022.

Claimant filed an Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care with this 
agency on April 3, 2024.  
The claimant has an accepted work injury to her left upper extremity. On October 
6, 2023, the authorized treating provider recommended physical therapy and an MRI for 
the left upper extremity injury. To date, defendant has not scheduled the claimant for the 
recommended treatment. Claimant seeks an order requiring defendant to authorize and 
schedule the recommended treatment without further delay.  
 
After discussion between the parties, the defendant has confirmed to claimant’s 
counsel that the recommended treatment is approved and authorized. The 
recommended MRI has been scheduled for April 16, 2024. As of this date, the physical 
therapy has not yet been scheduled. However, defendant has represented that 
representatives for the claim are actively seeking to schedule such treatment.  
 
To avoid an unnecessary hearing, and to protect the interests of the clients, the 
parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendant to authorize and schedule 
treatment as described above. This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of 
fact or conclusions of law relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and 
enter an order that is consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both 
parties.  
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, defendant agrees to provide 
confirmation to claimant, within one week of this order, that the recommended treatment 
has been scheduled with a provider that will cause no further unreasonable delay in 
care. If defendant fails to schedule such treatment within one week, defendant agrees

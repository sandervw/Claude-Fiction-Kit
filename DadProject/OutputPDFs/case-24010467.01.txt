CONSENT ORDER RE:      
ALTERNATE MEDICAL CARE 
 
 
 
 
Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care on April 2, 2025, 
requesting that Defendant be ordered to authorize Dr. Russell Buchanan to assume 
Claimant’s neurosurgical care from Dr. Marietta Walsh, and that Defendant be ordered 
to continue to authorize Dr. Aaron Heiar to treat Claimant.  
 
Prior to hearing, the parties contacted the undersigned Deputy and indicated 
Defendant has agreed to authorize Dr. Buchanan to assume Claimant’s neurosurgical 
care and are in the process of reaching out to Dr. Buchanan’s office to confirm that he 
will do so. In the event that Dr. Buchanan will not agree to assume Claimant’s care, 
Defendant has agreed to authorize Dr. Sandeep Bhangoo to assume Claimant’s 
neurosurgical care.  
 
Defendant has also agreed to continue authorizing Dr. Heiar to treat Claimant, 
but reserves the right to withdraw authorization from Dr. Heiar in the event that 
Claimant’s treating neurosurgeon is willing to address work restrictions, medications, 
and other matters that have been addressed by Dr. Heiar to date. Claimant disputes 
that Defendant can unilaterally withdraw authorization once a provider has been 
authorized, but agrees that the issue is presently moot given Defendant’s foregoing 
agreement.  
 
The parties desired that a consent order be entered in this case, in lieu of a 
hearing. After discussion between the parties and an agreement to provide the 
requested care, there is no justiciable issue for hearing and determination. Both parties 
agree that a consent order is appropriate, and a formal hearing is unnecessary to 
resolve the contested case dispute.  
 
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties. A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, the Defendant shall authorize Dr.

an appointment as soon as practicable if Dr. Buchanan is unwilling to assume

Defendant shall continue to authorize Dr. Aaron Heiar to treat Claimant at the 
present time. 
 
IT IS SO ORDERED.

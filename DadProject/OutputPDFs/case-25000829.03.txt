STATEMENT OF THE CASE   
 
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. The 
expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Lemoyne 
Freeman. Claimant appeared through his attorney, Sejla Numic. Defendants appeared 
through their attorney, Joseph Thornton. 
 
Claimant filed the petition on March 27, 2025. The petition alleges injuries arising 
out of and in the course of employment to his right leg, right hip, and low back on 
December 11, 2024. The petition states that claimant is dissatisfied with defendants’ 
failure to authorize right knee surgery recommended by the authorized treating 
physician. Defendants filed an answer on April 8, 2025, and admitted liability for the 
claim. Defense counsel confirmed at hearing that liability for the right knee is not in 
dispute. 
 
The alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on April 8, 2025. The 
proceedings were digitally recorded. That recording constitutes the official record of this 
proceeding. Pursuant to the Commissioner’s July 21, 2023 Order, the undersigned has 
been delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical care 
proceeding. Therefore, this ruling is designated final agency action and any appeal of 
the decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A. 
The record consists of claimant’s exhibit 1 (attached to the petition), consisting of 
thirteen pages. There was no objection to the additional pages, so they were allowed. 
No witnesses were called. Counsel offered oral arguments to support their positions.

ISSUE 
The issue presented for resolution is whether the claimant is entitled to alternate 
medical care consisting of authorization of right knee surgery as recommended by the 
authorized treating physician, Ryan Dunlay, M.D. 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
The undersigned having considered all of the evidence in the record finds: 
Claimant sustained a work-related injury on December 11, 2024, which included 
injury to his right knee. Defendants accepted the injury as compensable. Dr. Dunlay is 
the authorized treating physician chosen by defendants to provide medical treatment for 
claimant’s right knee. 
Claimant saw Dr. Dunlay on February 19, 2025. (Claimant’s Exhibit 1, p. 1) His 
record states that claimant complained of pain in his right knee, at times as bad as 10 
out of 10. He pointed to the medial and lateral joint as the focus of his pain. He indicated 
it was worse with walking, stairs, standing, sitting, and twisting. He noted some mild 
improvement with bracing and physical therapy, but those had not provided much by 
way of improvement. He had been taking Tylenol and anti-inflammatories with some 
modest improvement. Prior to the injury, he denied any injury or treatment for his right 
knee.  
Dr. Dunlay reviewed the therapy notes at Concentra and the notes of the 
referring provider, Kelli Senseney, ARNP. He also independently reviewed the MRI, 
which was done on January 23, 2025. He noted that the MRI demonstrated some mild-
to-moderate arthritis of the medial, lateral, and patellofemoral joints with small effusion. 
He also noted a complex tear involving the posterior horn of the lateral meniscus 
extending into the body, and what appeared to be an entrapped component of the 
meniscus in the notch. His assessment was right knee new complex tear of the 
posterior horn and body of the lateral meniscus with possible bucket handle type 
component, and right knee primary osteoarthritis with some aggravation mechanically. 
(Cl. Ex. 1, p. 2) 
Dr. Dunlay discussed his findings with claimant, and discussed both operative 
and non-operative treatments. He discussed performing an intra-articular injection, but 
stated, “I think this is only likely to provide transient relief of his symptoms.” They 
discussed in detail the risks and benefits of right knee arthroscopic partial lateral 
meniscectomy, and claimant wished to proceed with the procedure. He was to remain 
on restrictions and continue his medications in the meantime. Dr. Dunlay also provided 
a temporary handicap permit. He also provided an order for post-operative physical 
therapy. (Cl. Ex. 2, p. 5) 
Claimant’s attorney sent emails to defendants’ attorney seeking authorization for 
the surgery and expressing claimant’s dissatisfaction with care. (Cl. Ex. 4, pp. 7-8) At 
hearing, claimant’s attorney noted that claimant has already had physical therapy with

REASONING AND CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
 
Iowa Code section 85.27(4) provides, in relevant part: 
For purposes of this section, the employer is obliged to furnish reasonable 
services and supplies to treat an injured employee, and has the right to 
choose the care. . . .  The treatment must be offered promptly and be 
reasonably suited to treat the injury without undue inconvenience to the 
employee. If the employee has reason to be dissatisfied with the care 
offered, the employee should communicate the basis of such 
dissatisfaction to the employer, in writing if requested, following which the 
employer and the employee may agree to alternate care reasonably suited 
to treat the injury. If the employer and employee cannot agree on such 
alternate care, the commissioner may, upon application and reasonable 
proofs of the necessity therefor, allow and order other care. 
Iowa Code § 85.27(4). 
Defendants’ “obligation under the statute is confined to reasonable care for the 
diagnosis and treatment of work-related injuries.” Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 
N.W.2d 122, 124 (Iowa 1995) (emphasis in original). In other words, the “obligation 
under the statute turns on the question of reasonable necessity, not desirability.” Id. 
Similarly, an application for alternate medical care is not automatically sustained 
because claimant is dissatisfied with the care he has been receiving. Mere 
dissatisfaction with the medical care is not ample grounds for granting an application for 
alternate medical care. Rather, the claimant must show that the care was not offered 
promptly, was not reasonably suited to treat the injury, or that the care was unduly 
inconvenient for the claimant. See Iowa Code § 85.27(4). Thus, by challenging the 
employer’s choice of treatment and seeking alternate care, claimant assumes the 
burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable. See Iowa R. App. P. 
6.904(3)(e); Long, 528 N.W.2d at 124. Ultimately, determining whether care is 
reasonable under the statute is a question of fact. Long, 528 N.W.2d at 123. 
An employer’s right to select the provider of medical treatment to an injured 
worker does not include the right to determine how an injured worker should be 
diagnosed, evaluated, treated, or other matters of professional medical judgment.  
Assmann v. Blue Star Foods, File No. 866389 (Declaratory Ruling, May 19, 1988). 
Defendants are not entitled to interfere with the medical judgment of its own treating 
physician. Pote v. Mickow Corp., File No. 694639 (Review-Reopening Decision 
June 17, 1986).

In other words, while defendants are afforded the right to direct medical care, that 
does not include the right to second-guess the recommendations of the authorized 
treating physician. It is unreasonable to delay or deny treatment recommended by the 
authorized treating provider. Claimant has had conservative treatment since the date of 
injury, for nearly four months, with little relief. Dr. Dunlay does not believe an injection 
will permanently resolve his symptoms, and recommends surgery. As the authorized 
treating physician, defendants are not entitled to interfere with Dr. Dunlay’s medical 
judgment. 
In this case, claimant has met his burden to prove that the care offered by 
defendants is unreasonable. Failure to authorize surgery that has been recommended 
by the authorized treating physician is unreasonable. This is an admitted injury, and the 
recommendation for surgery was made 48 days ago. The authorized care is 
unreasonable, as Dr. Dunlay does not believe an injection will resolve claimant’s 
symptoms. Because claimant has met his burden of proving the authorized care is 
unreasonable, his petition for alternate care must be granted. 
ORDER 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted. 
Defendants shall immediately authorize and pay for all reasonable medical 
services and transportation expenses consistent with Dr. Dunlay’s February 19, 2025 
treatment plan, including the recommended right knee surgery.

This is a contested case concerning alternate medical care, proceeding under 
Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The expedited procedure of rule 876 Iowa 
Administrative Code 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Jennifer Tucker.  An alternate medical 
care hearing was scheduled for August 14, 2025.  Prior to the hearing, the defendants 
filed an answer indicating that the care requested by the claimant was authorized.  
Thus, the undersigned reached out to the parties via email to discuss their amenability 
to a consent order.  The parties agreed that a consent order was appropriate.  
 
The defendants agree to authorize care for the claimant’s right knee with UIHC.  
The defendants further agreed to obtain an appointment for the claimant within ten (10) 
days of the date of this order at UIHC; however, the appointment itself need not occur 
within ten (10) days.  If UIHC does not agree to take the claimant as a patient, the 
defendants agree to seek equivalent care for the claimant’s right knee.   
 
The parties proposed that the undersigned issue this consent order in lieu of a 
formal decision.  The parties consented to the entry of a consent order via email on 
August 14, 2025; thus, no alternate medical care decision will be issued.   
 
Should the defendants fail to abide by the stipulations in this consent order, 
sanctions may be imposed pursuant to 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.36. 
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
1. The defendants authorize care for the claimant’s right knee with UIHC.

STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
 
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. By 
filing an original notice and petition for alternate medical care, claimant, Luis Cisneros, 
invoked the expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48.   
 
The alternate medical care claim came on for telephonic hearing on April 19, 
2024.  The proceedings were digitally recorded.  That recording constitutes the official 
record of this proceeding.  Claimant appeared personally and through his attorney, 
Robert Rosenstiel.  Defendants appeared through their attorney, Bryan Brooks. 
 
Pursuant to the Commissioner’s July 21, 2023 Order, the undersigned has been 
delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical care 
proceeding.  Therefore, this ruling is designated final agency action.  Any appeal of the 
decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A. 
 
The evidentiary record consists of claimant’s exhibits 1 through 5, consisting of 
twelve pages and defendants’ exhibit A, consisting of one page.  Mr. Cisneros testified 
on his own behalf.  No other witnesses testified at the hearing.

ISSUE 
 
The issue presented for resolution is whether the claimant is entitled to an 
alternate medical care order requiring defendants to authorize additional medical 
treatment for his low back, including physical therapy under the supervision of a spine 
specialist and a pain clinic evaluation. 
 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
 
The undersigned having considered all the evidence in the record finds: 
 
Luis Cisneros sustained a back injury that arose out of and in the course of his 
employment with Uncommon Ground of the Quad Cities, Inc., on October 10, 2022.  
(Original Notice and Petition; Answer)  The employer authorized treatment and claimant 
submitted to a right L4-5 microlumbar diskectomy surgery performed by Michael Berry, 
M.D., on February 14, 2023.  (Claimant’s Exhibit 1, pages 1-2)  Following post-operative 
care, Dr. Berry imposed permanent work restrictions on claimant and released claimant 
from his care on July 31, 2023.  (Claimant’s Ex. 2, p. 4) 
 
Claimant continued to experience symptoms after being released from Dr. 
Berry’s care.  He attempted to schedule a new visit with Dr. Berry, but Dr. Berry’s office 
declined to schedule the evaluation because claimant had already been released by Dr. 
Berry.  (Claimant’s testimony)  Nevertheless, claimant continued to request additional 
care through his attorney from October 19, 2023 through April 9, 2024.  (Claimant’s Ex. 
5)   
 
Mr. Cisneros returned to work for this employer in March 2024 and experienced 
an increase in symptoms.  Specifically, claimant testified that he has difficulties with 
lifting, carrying, standing, and sitting.  These activities cause him pain, a pin pricking 
feeling, as well as a sensation that he has a loss of strength in his low back.  
(Claimant’s testimony)  Claimant’s testimony was reasonable and not challenged via 
cross-examination.  Claimant’s testimony is accepted as credible and accurate in this 
regard.  I find that claimant has ongoing symptoms in his low back and that lifting, 
carrying, standing, and sitting increase those symptoms at this time. 
 
Defendants indicated they would authorize additional care on January 3, 2024. 
(Defendants’ Ex. A)  However, no additional treatment, examinations, or care have been 
arranged or specifically authorized by defendants to date.   
 
Claimant sought an independent medical evaluation, performed by Todd R. 
Ridernour, M.D., on November 10, 2023.  Dr. Ridernour diagnosed claimant with 
“Residual stiffness and decreased range of motion, status post L4-L5 discectomy.”  
(Claimant’s Ex. 4, p. 6)  Dr. Ridernour opined that claimant should be “able to improve 
and become more functional with time.”  He recommended “physical therapy with the 
goal of emphasizing core stabilization and pelvic strengthening.”  (Claimant’s Ex. 4, p. 
6)  He recommended the physical therapy be conducted under the observation of a

spine specialist.  Dr. Ridernour also recommended that claimant be evaluated by a pain

Dr. Ridernour’s medical opinions and recommendations appear consistent with

REASONING AND CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
The employer shall furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, osteopathic, 
chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance and hospital services 
and supplies for all conditions compensable under the workers' compensation law.  The 
employer shall also allow reasonable and necessary transportation expenses incurred 
for those services.  The employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except 
where the employer has denied liability for the injury.  Section 85.27.  Holbert v. 
Townsend Engineering Co., Thirty-second Biennial Report of the Industrial 
Commissioner 78 (Review-Reopening October 16, 1975). 
 
By challenging the employer’s choice of treatment – and seeking alternate care – 
claimant assumes the burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable.  See Iowa 
R. App. P. 6.904(3)(e); Bell Bros. Heating and Air Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 N.W.2d 
193, 209 (Iowa 2010); Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).  
Determining what care is reasonable under the statute is a question of fact.  Long v. 
Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).  The employer’s obligation turns on 
the question of reasonable necessity, not desirability.  Id.; Harned v. Farmland Foods, 
Inc., 331 N.W.2d 98 (Iowa 1983).   
An application for alternate medical care is not automatically sustained because 
claimant is dissatisfied with the care he has been receiving.  Mere dissatisfaction with 
the medical care is not ample grounds for granting an application for alternate medical 
care.  Rather, the claimant must show that the care was not offered promptly, was not 
reasonably suited to treat the injury, or that the care was unduly inconvenient for the 
claimant.  Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).   
In Pirelli-Armstrong Tire Co. v. Reynolds, 562 N.W.2d 433, 437 (Iowa 1997), the 
supreme court held that “when evidence is presented to the commissioner that the 
employer-authorized medical care has not been effective and that such care is ‘inferior 
or less extensive’ than other available care requested by the employee, . . . the 
commissioner is justified by section 85.27 to order the alternate care.”

In this case, claimant has proven that defendants are not offering prompt medical 
care that is reasonably suited to treat his injuries.  In fact, as of the date of this alternate 
medical care proceeding, defendants are not authorizing or offering any medical care 
for claimant.  Claimant has produced reasonable and convincing opinions from Dr. 
Ridernour that establish additional reasonable and more extensive treatment options 
exist for claimant’s low back injury.  Therefore, I conclude claimant has proven by a 
preponderance of the evidence that he is entitled to an order for alternate medical care.  
Specifically, I conclude claimant is entitled to an order granting him the care he has 
requested:  physical therapy with the goal of emphasizing core stabilization and pelvic 
strengthening under the observation or direction of a spinal specialist.  I also conclude 
claimant has proven entitlement to an order directing defendants to authorize and 
provide a pain clinic evaluation for claimant’s low back injury. 
 
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted. 
 
Defendants shall immediately identify and authorize a spine specialist to oversee 
claimant’s physical therapy. 
 
Defendants shall immediately identify and authorize a physical therapist to 
provide claimant treatment, including therapy emphasizing core stabilization and pelvic 
strengthening.   
 
Defendants shall immediately identify and authorize an evaluation with a pain 
specialist for claimant’s low back. 
 
Failure to timely identify and authorize the above care may be considered an 
abandonment of care and may result in defendants losing their statutory right to select 
the authorized medical provider(s).

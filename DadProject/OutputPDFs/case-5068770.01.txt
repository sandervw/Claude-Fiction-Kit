Claimant filed an Original Notice and Petition for Alternate Medical Care with this 
agency on April 14, 2023.  
Based on an agreement between the parties, and a proposed consent order, 
defendant agrees to authorize and pay for treatment and care for claimant’s cervical 
and lumbar spine, and for claimant’s winging left shoulder with Mayo Clinic in 
Rochester, Minnesota.  In the event the Mayo Clinic does not agree to accept claimant 
as a patient, defendant retains the right to authorize and refer claimant to additional or 
prior authorized providers.  Defendant agrees to authorize and pay for treatment for 
claimant with Raja Akbar, M.D.  Medications prescribed by Dr. Akbar will be authorized 
and paid for by defendant.  Defendant also agrees to authorize and pay for counseling 
and therapy for claimant with Ashley Gruver, LISW. 
To avoid an unnecessary hearing, and yet protect the interests of their clients, 
the parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendant to authorize treatment 
as described above.  
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties. A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.  
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:  
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, defendant agrees to authorize and pay 
for treatment and care for claimant’s cervical and lumbar spine, and for claimant’s 
winging left shoulder with Mayo Clinic in Rochester, Minnesota.  In the event the Mayo 
Clinic does not agree to accept claimant as a patient, defendant retains the right to 
authorize and refer claimant to additional or prior authorized providers.  Defendant 
agrees to authorize and pay for treatment for claimant with Raja Akbar, M.D.

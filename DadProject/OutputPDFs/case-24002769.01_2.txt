Claimant appeals from a November 4, 2025 arbitration decision.  The employer, 
insurance carrier, and Second Injury Fund of Iowa all respond to the appeal and urge 
the arbitration decision be fully affirmed.  The presiding deputy commissioner held an 
arbitration hearing in this case on May 29, 2025, and the case was fully submitted 
before the deputy on July 11, 2025. 
In the arbitration decision, the deputy commissioner found that claimant is not a 
credible witness and noted inconsistencies between claimant’s testimony and medical 
records.  Ultimately, the deputy commissioner found that claimant’s January 4, 2024 
medical record was the most accurate version of claimant’s medical history.  That 
medical record documented that claimant fell at home, while claimant testified his fall 
was at work.  Having found that claimant failed to prove he sustained an injury that 
arose out of and in the course of his employment, the arbitration decision awarded no 
benefits to claimant. 
On appeal, claimant challenges the deputy’s credibility determination and finding 
that he did not prove by a preponderance of the evidence that he sustained a work-
related injury on January 3, 2024.  Claimant seeks reversal of the arbitration decision 
and an award of healing period, permanent partial (or permanent total) disability 
benefits, past medical expenses, alternate medical care, reimbursement of his 
independent medical evaluation, and penalty benefits against the employer and 
insurance carrier.  Claimant also seeks an award of Second Injury Fund benefits. The

Pursuant to Iowa Code section 10A.321 and section 17A.15(3), I performed a de 
novo review of the evidentiary record and the detailed arguments of the parties.  I 
concur with the deputy’s findings, analysis, and conclusions of law in all respects.  I 
adopt the arbitration decision’s findings of fact and conclusions of law as the final 
agency decision in this matter.   
The only addition I make to the arbitration decision is to note that I rely heavily 
upon the credibility findings of the deputy commissioner.  While I weigh the competing 
and potentially contradictory evidence, I give considerable deference to findings of fact 
that are impacted by the credibility findings expressly or impliedly made by the deputy 
commissioner who presided at the hearing. The deputy commissioner who presided at 
the hearing had the best opportunity to evaluate the demeanor of the persons who 
testified at the hearing. The presiding deputy has the ability to include the demeanor of 
a witness when weighing credibility to find the true facts of the case. My ability to find 
the true facts that are affected by witness demeanor and credibility cannot be expected 
to be superior to that of the deputy who presided at the hearing. If anything, my ability 
when reviewing a transcript is likely to be inferior because I do not have the tool of 
witness demeanor to use in my evaluation. See Renaud v. Warren County, File No. 
23008425.01 (Appeal December 2025); Sanchez Cabada v. Tyson Fresh Meats, Inc., 
File Nos. 21701096.02, 22700204.02 (Appeal December 2025);  Niemeyer v. Ottumwa 
Good Samaritan, File No. 5020338 (Appeal 2008); Houtz v. Casey’s General Store, File 
Nos. 5007029, 5007030 (Appeal March 2006). Claimant’s arguments on appeal are not 
sufficient to reverse the deputy’s credibility findings.  I accept and adopt the deputy 
commissioner’s credibility finding.  Therefore, I affirm and adopt the arbitration decision 
in its entirety. 
ORDER 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
The arbitration decision is affirmed and adopted as final agency action. 
Claimant is taxed the costs of this appeal and shall now pay the entire cost of the 
hearing transcript.

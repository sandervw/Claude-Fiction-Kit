On September 26, 2025, claimant filed an original notice and petition for alternate 
medical care in this file under Iowa Code section 85.27, invoking the provisions of rule 
876 IAC 4.48. Claimant has alleged injuries to his left shoulder, left arm, neck, and right 
arm, occurring on August 9, 2024. The alternate care petition seeks authorization for 
treatment for his neck and bilateral arms. Specifically, claimant seeks a return 
appointment with Christopher Iverson, M.D., for his neck, and authorization for an 
appointment with a hand specialist for his bilateral arms.  
Hearing was scheduled for October 8, 2025. Defendants filed an answer on 
October 7, 2025. The answer states that defendants admit liability for the claim relating 
to the left shoulder and neck, and deny liability for the claim relating to the bilateral 
hands and arms. At the time the hearing was scheduled to take place, the parties 
advised they had reached an agreement with respect to the admitted neck claim, 
making a hearing unnecessary. The parties requested entry of a consent order to 
confirm and document the agreement. 
With respect to the alleged injury to claimant’s bilateral hands/arms, the law is 
clear that alternate care claims are limited to situations in which liability is not at issue. 
Before any benefits can be ordered, including medical benefits, compensability of the 
claim must be established, either by admission of liability or by adjudication. The 
summary provisions of Iowa Code section 85.27, as more particularly described in rule 
876 IAC 4.48, are not designed to adjudicate disputed compensability of a claim.

The Iowa Supreme Court has held:  
We emphasize that the commissioner’s ability to decide the merits of a 
section 85.27(4) alternate medical care claim is limited to situations where 
the compensability of an injury is conceded, but the reasonableness of a 
particular course of treatment for the compensable injury is disputed. . . . 
Thus, the commissioner cannot decide the reasonableness of the 
alternate care claim without also necessarily deciding the ultimate 
disputed issue in the case:  whether or not the medical condition Barnett 
was suffering at the time of the request was a work-related injury. 
R. R. Donnelly & Sons v. Barnett, 670 N.W.2d 190, 197-198 (Iowa 2003). 
"[T]he employer has no right to choose the medical care when compensability is 
contested.” Bell Bros. Heating and Air Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 N.W.2d 193, 204 
(Iowa 2010). Further, when compensability is contested, "the employer cannot assert an 
authorization defense in response to a subsequent claim by the employee for the 
expenses of the alternate medical care." R. R. Donnelly, 670 N.W.2d at 197-198.  
Ultimately, therefore, defendants are precluded from asserting an authorization 
defense as to any future treatment for claimant’s alleged injuries to his bilateral 
hands/arms during the period of denial, and defendants lose the right to direct the 
medical care claimant seeks related to his bilateral hands/arms during this period of 
denial. Brewer-Strong v. HNI Corp., 913 N.W.2d 235 (Iowa 2018); Bell Bros., 779 
N.W.2d at 204. 
As a result, claimant may obtain reasonable medical care for his bilateral 
hands/arms from any provider, but at claimant's expense, and claimant may seek 
reimbursement for such care using regular claim proceedings before this agency. Haack 
v. Von Hoffman Graphics, File No. 1268172 (App. July 31, 2002); Kindhart v. Fort Des 
Moines Hotel, I Iowa Industrial Comm'r Decisions No. 3, 611 (App. March 27, 1985). 
ORDER 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, defendants will authorize claimant to 
return to Dr. Christopher Iverson for evaluation and treatment of his neck, including 
physical therapy if recommended.  
The portion of claimant’s original notice and petition for alternate medical care 
related to his bilateral hands/arms is hereby dismissed without prejudice. 
If claimant seeks to recover the charges incurred in obtaining care for the 
conditions for which defendants denied liability (bilateral hands/arms), defendants are 
barred from asserting lack of authorization as a defense to those charges during the 
period of denial.

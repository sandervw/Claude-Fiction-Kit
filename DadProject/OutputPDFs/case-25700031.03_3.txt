STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
Claimant filed a petition for alternate medical care against defendants SBH-
Davenport, L.L.C., employer, and Safety National Cas. Corp., insurer, both as 
defendants.  This matter was heard on February 18, 2025 by Deputy Workers’ 
Compensation Commissioner Michael Lunn.  A decision was issued by Deputy Lunn on 
February 19, 2025.  That decision held, in part, that defendants were ordered to 
authorize care for claimant’s hip condition.  The decision also required defendants to file 
Subsequent Reports of Injury “denial of liability” through the EDI system and to send a 
letter to claimant stating the reason for the denial of liability regarding claimant’s back 
condition.  Macumber v. SBH-Davenport, L.L.C, File No 25700031.03 (Alternate 
Medical Care Dec. February 19, 2025) 
Defendants moved for judicial review of the alternate medical care decision.  The 
District Court reversed the portion of the decision requiring defendants to issue a denial 
letter.  It affirmed the decision on all other issues.  The case was remanded back to this 
agency to issue a decision consistent with the Ruling on the Petition for Judicial Review.   
Due to the unavailability of Deputy Lunn, under Iowa Code section 17A.15(2), the 
undersigned issues the remand decision in this matter.

ISSUE 
Are defendants required to issue a denial of liability letter under an alternate 
medical care proceeding? 
FINDINGS OF FACT 
The Finding of Facts as detailed in the February 19, 2025, alternate medical care 
decision adequately detail the record in this case.  The Finding of Facts for the remand 
decision are those detailed in the February 19, 2025, alternate medical care decision. 
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care under Iowa Code section 
85.27, invoking the provisions of rule 876 IAC 4.48. Prior to hearing, defendants denied 
liability for claimant’s back condition. 
Before any benefits can be ordered, including medical benefits, compensability of 
the claim must be established, either by admission of liability or by adjudication. The 
summary provisions of Iowa Code section 85.27, as more particularly described in rule 
876 IAC 4.48, are not designed to adjudicate disputed compensability of a claim. For 
that reason, that part of the claim related to claimant’s back condition must be 
dismissed.  Defendants are barred from asserting a “lack of authorization” defense to 
any medical expenses accrued by claimant, if they are otherwise compensable, 
regarding claimant’s back claim. Defendants cannot deny liability and simultaneously 
direct the course of treatment. Barnhart v. MAQ Incorporated, I Iowa Industrial Comm’r 
Report 16 (App. March 9, 1981).  
As a result of the denial of liability for the condition sought to be treated in this 
proceeding, claimant may obtain reasonable medical care from any provider for this 
condition, but at claimant’s expense, and seek reimbursement for such care using 
regular claim proceedings before this agency. Haack v. Von Hoffman Graphics, File No. 
1268172 (App. July 31, 2002); Kindhart v. Fort Des Moines Hotel, I Iowa Industrial 
Comm’r Decisions No. 3, 611 (App. March 27, 1985).  
This agency cannot order defendants to issue a letter of denial under an 
alternate medical care proceeding.  SBH-Davenport, L.L.C v. Macumber, No. 
CVCV068770 at 5, Iowa District Court for Polk County (August 25, 2025) 
ORDER 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
As detailed in Ruling on Petition for Judicial Review in this matter, defendants are 
not required to issue a denial letter regarding claimant’s back condition under an 
alternate care proceeding.  The decision is the same in all other respects.

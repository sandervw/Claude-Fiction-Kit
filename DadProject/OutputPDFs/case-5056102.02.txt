Claimant Sherri Erlbacher sustained work related injuries on June 5, 2013, and 
February 1, 2014.  The parties reached a compromised settlement of the claims, 
wherein medical benefits remain open.  Claimant receives prescribed medications as a 
part of her ongoing care.  To date, claimant’s medications have been filled by Injured 
Workers’ Pharmacy (hereinafter “IWP”).  
On April 5, 2021, claimant filed a Petition for Alternate Medical Care.  Defendants 
REM Iowa and New Hampshire Insurance Company filed an Answer on April 12, 
2021.  A hearing was set for April 15, 2021.  Claimant asserted defendants have not 
authorized and/or paid for certain medications to be filled with IWP and that a pharmacy 
needs to be authorized. 
Just prior to the start of hearing, defendants agreed that the outstanding bills 
have been submitted for payment and should be processed within the next 14 
days.   Defendants also agreed that IWP will remain the authorized pharmacy unless 
and until the defendant authorize a new pharmacy.   The parties have agreed to a 
consent order in accordance with these agreements. 
ORDER 
Defendants REM Iowa and New Hampshire Insurance Company shall authorize 
the payment of all outstanding balances with IWP and will continue to authorize IWP 
unless and until a new pharmacy is authorized.

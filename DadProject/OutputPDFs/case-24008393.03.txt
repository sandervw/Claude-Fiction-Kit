Claimant filed an original notice and petition for alternate medical care with this 
agency on December 23, 2024.   
Based on an agreement between the parties, and a proposed consent order, the 
parties have agreed that defendants will authorize the claimant to proceed with mental 
health care with a provider of the defendants’ choosing.  The defendants shall schedule 
mental health care treatment as soon as possible, but no later than fourteen (14) days 
following the issuance of this order.  The appointment noted herein need not occur 
within fourteen (14) days, and must only be scheduled within fourteen (14) days of the 
date of this order. 
To avoid an unnecessary hearing, and yet protect the interest of their clients, the 
parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendants to authorize treatment as 
described above.   
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim.  Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties.  A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.  No alternate medical care decision will be issued. 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, defendants will authorize the claimant 
to proceed with mental health care with a provider of the defendants’ choosing.  The

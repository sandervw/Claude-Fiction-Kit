On August 14, 2025, the claimant filed an original notice and petition concerning 
application for alternate care.  On August 20, 2025, the defendants filed an answer to 
the original notice and petition concerning application for alternate care.  In their 
answer, the defendants denied liability for weight loss treatment and the claimant’s 
alleged injury to their right hip.  On August 20, 2025, the undersigned filed a dismissal of 
the claimant’s original notice and petition concerning application for alternate care.  On 
August 22, 2025, the defendants filed an application for order nunc pro tunc.   
 
 
The phrase, “nunc pro tunc” means “now for then.”  See Black’s Law Dictionary, 
1218 (rev. 4th ed. 1968).  The definition in Black’s Law Dictionary further provides: “A 
phrase applied to acts allowed to be done after the time when they should be done, with 
a retroactive effect, i.e., with the same effect as if regularly done.”  Black’s at 1218.  A 
nunc pro tunc order “is not for the purpose of correcting judicial thinking, a judicial 
conclusion, or a mistake of law.”  Headley v. Headley, 172 N.W.2d 104, 108 (Iowa 
1969).  The nunc pro tunc order can be employed to correct obvious errors or to make 
an order conform to the judge’s original intent.  Graber v. Iowa District Court for 
Washington County, 410 N.W.2d 224, 229 (Iowa 1987); Brinson v. Spee Dee Delivery 
Service, No. 8-754/06-2074 (Iowa Ct. App. 2008).  “[T]he intent to the trial judge is 
crucial to the determination of whether a nunc pro tunc order is appropriate to ‘correct’ a 
record.”  Freeman v. Ernst & Young, 541 N.W.2d 890, 893 (Iowa 1995) (citing McVay v. 
Kenneth E. Montz Implement Co., 287 N.W.2d 149, 151 (Iowa 1980)).   
 
 
On the first page of the dismissal, the undersigned made mention of a mental 
health condition.  This is a scrivener’s error, as there is no dispute as to a mental health 
condition.

1. Nunc pro tunc is GRANTED. 
  
2. The dismissal issued on August 20, 2025, is hereby amended so that the first 
sentence of paragraph four on the first page is amended to read: As a result 
of the denial of liability for the weight loss treatment and right hip injury sought

from any provider for this treatment, at claimant’s expense, and seek 
reimbursement for such care using regular claim proceedings before this 
agency.”

This is a contested case concerning alternate medical care, proceeding under 
Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The expedited procedure of rule 876 Iowa 
Administrative Code 4.48 is invoked by claimant, James Raney, II.  An alternate medical 
care hearing was scheduled for February 5, 2026.  Prior to the hearing, the defendants 
filed an answer indicating that physical therapy for the claimant’s left shoulder was 
authorized.  The defendants also denied liability for the claim relating to the left hip.  The 
undersigned reached out to the parties and the parties agreed via email that a consent 
order would be appropriate in lieu of a formal decision.   
 
The defendants agree to authorize physical therapy for the left shoulder through 
HOMELINK at a facility such as PRA Tipton, Iowa Physical Therapy in Mount Vernon, 
Iowa, Ability Physical Therapy in Mount Vernon, Iowa, and/or E3 Millennium.   
 
The parties consented to the entry of a consent order via email on February 5, 
2026; thus no alternate medical care decision will be issued.   
 
Should the defendants fail to abide by the stipulations in this consent order, 
sanctions may be imposed pursuant to 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.36. 
 
Additionally, as noted above, the defendants denied liability for the claim relating 
to the claimant’s left hip.   
Before alternate medical care can be ordered, compensability of the medical 
condition to be treated must be established, either by admission of liability or by 
adjudication.  The summary procedure of Iowa Code section 85.27, as more particularly 
described in rule 876 Iowa Administrative Code 4.48(7), is not available to adjudicate 
liability or causal connection disputes.

As a result of the denial of liability, the claimant may obtain reasonable medical 
care from any provider for this treatment, at claimant’s expense, and seek 
reimbursement for such care using regular claim proceedings before this agency.  
Haack v. Von Hoffman Graphics, File No. 1268172 (App. July 31, 2002); Kindhart v. 
Fort Des Moines Hotel, I Iowa Industrial Comm’r Decisions No. 3, 611 (App. March 27, 
1985).  “[T]he employer has no right to choose the medical care when compensability is 
contested.”  Bell Bros. Heating & Air Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 N.W.2d 193, 204 (Iowa 
2010).  The defendants are precluded from asserting an authorization defense as to any 
future treatment during their period of denial.  Brewer-Strong v. HNI Corp., 913 N.W.2d 
235 (Iowa 2018).   
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
1. The defendants agree to authorize physical therapy for the claimant’s left 
shoulder through HOMELINK at a facility such as PRA Tipton, Iowa Physical 
Therapy in Mount Vernon, Iowa, Ability Physical Therapy in Mount Vernon, 
Iowa, and/or E3 Millennium.   
  
2. Claimant’s application for alternate medical care regarding the claim 
pertaining to the left hip against the above named defendants is dismissed 
without prejudice. 
 
3. If claimant seeks to recover the charges incurred in obtaining care for the 
condition for which defendants denied liability, defendants are barred from 
asserting lack of authorization as a defense to those charges during the 
period of their denial.

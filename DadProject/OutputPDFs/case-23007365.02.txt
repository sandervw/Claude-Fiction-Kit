On February 19, 2024, claimant filed an application for alternate medical care 
under Iowa Code section 85.27 and rule 876 IAC 4.48. Defendants were given proper 
notice but did not file an appearance nor an answer. Claimant also provided a courtesy 
copy of this petition via e-mail to the third-party administrator. Given the foregoing, the 
undersigned declared the employer to be in default and proceeded with the alternate 
medical care proceeding on February 29, 2024, without the employer’s participation.     
Claimant sustained an injury on January 18, 2023, which arose out of and in the 
course of employment. Claimant is dissatisfied with the medical treatment offered by the 
defendants.  The hearing was recorded via digital voice recorder and a complete 
decision was dictated into the record on the day of the hearing. The decision will be 
reproduced in typewritten form only if the decision is appealed, and the parties are 
advised to follow the procedures outlined under the administrative code. Any rights of 
appeal will run from the date the decision was dictated into the record. 
The deputy ordered that claimant’s petition for alternate medical care be granted. 
The decision was rendered orally on the record. Pursuant to a standing order of 
delegation of authority by the workers’ compensation commissioner pursuant to Iowa 
Code section 10A.304(3), the undersigned enters this decision for the workers’ 
compensation commissioner. There is no right of appeal of this decision to the workers’ 
compensation commissioner. Appeal of this decision, if any, would be by judicial review 
pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A.19.

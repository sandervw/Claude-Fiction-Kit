STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
 
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A. 
Claimant, Marlena Young, invokes the expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48. 
Claimant appeared personally and through her attorney, Adrienne Sula. Defendants 
appeared through their attorney, Robert Gainer. 
 
 
The alternate medical care claim came on for hearing on August 29, 2025. The 
proceedings were digitally recorded. That recording constitutes the official record of this 
proceeding.  
 
Pursuant to the Commissioner's July 21, 2023 Order, the undersigned has been 
delegated authority to issue a final agency decision in this alternate medical care 
proceeding.  Therefore, this decision is designated final agency action and any appeal 
of the decision would be to the Iowa District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A. 
 
Iowa Code section 85.27(4) provides, in relevant part: 
 
If the employee has reason to be dissatisfied with the care offered, the 
employee should communicate the basis of such dissatisfaction to the 
employer, in writing if requested, following which the employer and the 
employee may agree to alternate care reasonably suited to treat the injury. 
If the employer and employee cannot agree on such alternate care, the

Claimant's petition for alternate medical care seeks treatment, including a knee 
surgery, through the previously authorized orthopaedic surgeon, Mark Fish, D.O. At the 
commencement of the hearing, defendants confirmed that they had authorized the 
recommended knee surgery with Dr. Fish. Again, this is the treatment that was 
specifically requested by claimant in the petition for alternate medical care. Having 
confirmed that defendants authorized the requested treatment, the undersigned 
confirmed that there are no other pending disputed medical issues.  Accordingly, the 
undersigned determined that all disputed issues in this alternate medical care 
proceeding are moot. 
 
Claimant requested entry of a “consent order.”  Defendants do not consent to 
entry of such an order.  Defendants contend that they have authorized the 
recommended care and that their authorization is reasonable such that the petition for 
alternate medical care should be denied. 
 
Ideally, this case would not have been brought to hearing. Defendants authorized 
the requested care before the scheduled hearing. In the undersigned’s judgment, it is a 
waste of agency resources to hold a hearing and issue a decision on a dispute that was 
resolved before the scheduled alternate medical care hearing.  
 
The alternate medical care process is not intended as a means of obtaining a 
tactical advantage or some type of procedural advantage. Once the dispute has been 
resolved, the purposes and intent of Iowa Code section 85.27(4) have been achieved, 
and the petition for alternate medical care should be dismissed.  See Johnson v. 
Oakview, Inc., File No. 5067689 (Alternate Medical Care March 2019); Hadzic v. Drake 
University, File No. 5055016 (Alternate Medical Care Nov. 2015); Schmitt v. Allied 
Manatts Group, L.L.C., File No. 5033656 (Alternate Medical Care Aug. 2010); Mitchell v. 
Barr-Nunn Transp., Inc., File No. 5018447 (Alternate Medial Care Oct. 2006). 
 
The Iowa Workers' Compensation Commission has a relatively small staff. 
Handling unnecessary hearings when all disputes have been resolved ties up agency 
resources and slows down the agency's responsiveness on issuing rulings and 
decisions for parties with actual disputes.  
 
Of course, there are times when defendants fail to live up to their 
agreements.  There are cases when repeated alternate medical care petitions must be 
filed. In those situations, it may be appropriate to have a hearing and enter a consent 
order or formal decision and order with the authority of the agency. This is not one of 
those cases. 
 
Claimant requested specific care. Defendants authorized that care in advance of 
the alternate medical care hearing. No reason or rationale was provided why counsel

could not accept, or should not accept, Mr. Gainer’s statements and representations on

The Iowa workers' compensation bar is a relatively small group of practitioners. 
The vast majority of them are good attorneys with knowledge of the system. Most look 
out for the integrity of the system and are trustworthy and honorable in their 
communications with opposing counsel. Until there is reason to doubt the informal 
exchanges with counsel, counsel should be willing to rely upon the professional 
statements of opposing counsel in Iowa workers' compensation cases.   
  
ORDER 
  
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
  
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is denied as moot.  
 
The petition for alternate medical care is dismissed without prejudice.

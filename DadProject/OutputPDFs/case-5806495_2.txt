COURSE OF PROCEEDINGS & FACTUAL BACKGROUND 
Claimant, Marshall Sandlin, filed a petition for arbitration seeking workers’ 
compensation benefits from Mid American Construction, the employer and Grinnell 
Mutual, the insurance carrier. 
The matter was previously heard and a decision was entered on June 18, 2020.  
The decision was appealed to the Commissioner, then the District Court, Court of 
Appeals and finally to the Iowa Supreme Court.  On February 9, 2024, the Iowa 
Supreme Court entered a decision which remanded the case back to the agency for 
further proceedings.  The claim was ultimately remanded back to the agency to hold an 
additional hearing on the issue of the reasonableness of the IME expense.  On June 12, 
2024, the Commissioner assigned this hearing to the undersigned for additional 
proceedings to determine whether the IME fee was “reasonable based on the typical fee 
charged by a medical provider to perform an impairment rating in the local area where 
the exam was conducted.”  The matter was set for Remand Hearing on October 23, 
2024, before the undersigned. 
 
Prior to the hearing, the parties filed a stipulation with the agency indicating that 
the sole outstanding issue has been resolved.   
CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
The only issue is the IME expense.

Section 85.39 permits an employee to be reimbursed for a subsequent 
examination by a physician of the employee's choice where an employer-retained 
physician has previously evaluated “permanent disability” and the employee believes 
that the initial evaluation is too low.  The section also permits reimbursement for 
reasonably necessary transportation expenses incurred and for any wage loss 
occasioned by the employee attending the subsequent examination. 
Defendants are responsible only for reasonable fees associated with claimant's 
independent medical examination.  The Supreme Court clarified in this case that the 
fees must be reasonable “in the local area in which it was performed.”  Claimant has the 
burden of proving the reasonableness of the expenses incurred for the examination.  
See Schintgen v. Economy Fire & Casualty Co., File No. 855298 (App. April 26, 1991). 
On October 21, 2024, the parties reached a stipulation regarding the IME fee.  
That stipulation is hereby accepted, effectively resolving the claim. 
ORDER 
 
The parties have agreed that the IME fee was reasonable in the local area in 
which it was performed.  This stipulation is incorporated into the final agency decision 
previously entered for this matter.

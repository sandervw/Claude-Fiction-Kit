Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care on September 30, 2020, 
seeking authorization of viscosupplementation injections recommended for his right 
knee condition by the authorized treating orthopedic surgeon, Dr. Mark Gorsche, as well 
as treatment for his left knee condition. The matter was scheduled for hearing on 
October 13, 2020.  
Prior to hearing, the parties contacted the undersigned Deputy and indicated that 
defendants have agreed to authorize the viscosupplementation injections recommended 
by Dr. Gorsche. Claimant is satisfied with this authorization of care by defendants.  
Defendants have denied claimant’s alleged left knee condition.  
The Iowa Supreme Court has held that “the commissioner’s ability to decide the 
merits of a section 85.27(4) alternate medical care claim is limited to situations where 
the compensability of an injury is conceded, but the reasonableness of a particular 
course of treatment for the compensable injury is disputed”. R. R. Donnelly & Sons v. 
Barnett, 670 N.W.2d 190, 197-198 (Iowa 2003). Thus, claimant’s petition seeking 
medical treatment for the left knee condition must be dismissed because defendants are 
denying liability for this condition. Defendants thereby lose their right to control 
claimant’s medical care and claimant is free to choose his own medical care relative to 
the left knee condition.  
As a result of their denial of liability for the left knee condition in this proceeding, 
claimant may obtain reasonable medical care from any provider for this condition, but at

Decisions No. 3, 611 (App. March 27, 1985).  
Claimant desired that a Consent Order be entered in this case, in lieu of a 
hearing. A hearing is therefore determined to be unnecessary as to the right knee 
condition, due to the agreement of the parties, and not permissible as it pertains to the 
left knee condition in light of defendants’ denial of that condition.  
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
The hearing scheduled for October 13, 2020, is cancelled. 
Defendants shall, within the next ten (10) days, provide written authorization to 
Dr. Mark Gorsche authorizing him to perform a series of viscosupplementation 
injections on claimant’s right knee.   
As to the left knee condition, claimant’s petition for alternate medical care is 
dismissed without prejudice. It is further ordered that if claimant seeks to recover the 
charges incurred in obtaining care for a condition for which defendants have denied 
liability, defendants are barred from asserting lack of authorization as a defense to 
those charges.

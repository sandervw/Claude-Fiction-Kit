The undersigned filed an arbitration decision as final agency action on December 
31, 2025.  Claimant filed a motion for order nunc pro tunc on January 12, 2026.  A 
response to the motion has not been filed. 
Review of the content of the motion suggests it may more appropriately be 
labeled as a request for rehearing pursuant to 876 IAC 4.24 because the motion seeks 
relief that is more than mere correction of a typographical error or scrivener’s error.  
Regardless, the motion is timely even under 876 IAC 4.24 and is considered. 
Specifically, claimant seeks clarification of the undersigned’s order pertaining to 
medical expenses contained in Claimant’s Exhibit 11.  Claimant contends that the 
language of the order in the arbitration decision may create confusion or ambiguity as to 
defendants’ obligations with respect to satisfaction of the medical expenses contained in 
Claimant’s Exhibit 11. 
The arbitration decision includes an order that provides, “Defendants shall pay 
the medical provider directly for any outstanding medical expenses, or reimburse 
claimant for all medical expenses he previously paid, all of which are contained in 
Claimant’s Exhibit 11.”  Claimant’s motion contends that “It appears the Agency’s 
intention was to order Defendants to pay all expenses set forth in Exhibit 11, as the 
body of the decision states that ‘claimant has proven entitlement to payment or 
reimbursement of the expenses contained at Claimant’s Exhibit 11.’” 
The undersigned’s intention in the arbitration decision was to award claimant all 
medical expenses contained in Claimant’s Exhibit 11.  Some of the expenses remain 
outstanding and should be paid by defendants directly to the medical providers.  Some 
of the medical expenses contained in Claimant’s Exhibit 11 were paid out of pocket by

claimant, and defendants should reimburse claimant for those expenses directly.  
Finally, some of the medical expenses contained in Claimant’s Exhibit 11 were paid by 
a third-party non-occupational health insurance carrier.  It was the intention of the 
undersigned in the arbitration decision to order defendants to reimburse the third-party 
payor(s) for any sums paid for claimant’s related medical treatment, as documented in 
Claimant’s Exhibit 11.  In total, it was the intention of the undersigned that defendants 
be ordered to satisfy and reimburse and hold claimant harmless for any medical 
expenses documented in Claimant’s Exhibit 11. 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
Claimant’s motion for order nunc pro tunc is sustained. 
 
The order section of the arbitration decision is amended with respect to the 
award of medical expenses to clarify that order as outlined below. 
Defendants shall pay medical providers directly for any outstanding medical 
expenses documented in Claimant’s Exhibit 11. 
Defendants shall reimburse claimant for any out-of-pocket payments he made, 
as documented in Claimant’s Exhibit 11. 
Defendants shall reimburse any third-party payor(s) (non-occupational health 
insurance carriers) for payments they made for claimant’s related medical expenses as 
documented in Claimant’s Exhibit 11. 
Defendants shall hold claimant harmless and ensure full payment and 
reimbursement of all medical expenses documented in Claimant’s Exhibit 11.

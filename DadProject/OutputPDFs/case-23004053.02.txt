On June 27, 2023, claimant filed a petition for alternate medical care. Defendants 
filed an answer on July 6, 2023, admitting liability for the claim. A telephonic hearing 
took place on July 10, 2023. The proceeding was digitally recorded. That recording 
constitutes the official record of the proceeding. 
Claimant appeared through her attorney, Erin Tucker. Defendants appeared 
through their attorney, Bryan Hatch. The record consists of Claimant’s Exhibit 1, 
containing 1 page, and Defendants’ Exhibit A, containing 10 pages. No witnesses were 
called, but both attorneys presented oral arguments to support their positions. 
The care requested involves a referral for claimant to see a neurologist. On April 
24, 2023, Brett Rosenthal, M.D., referred claimant to neurology, as well as pain 
management. (Claimant’s Exhibit 1) Pain management will not see claimant until she 
has been evaluated by neurology. Since the time of referral, defendants have contacted 
several neurologists, who have each reviewed the medical records and then declined to 
see claimant. 1 
 After discussing the issues, the parties reached an agreement. Defendants have 
agreed to work with claimant in finding a neurologist who will agree to see her. Claimant 
has indicated that she is willing to travel. The parties agree that if an appointment has 
not been secured within 14 days of the date of this order, claimant will return to

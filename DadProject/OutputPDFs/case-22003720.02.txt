Claimant filed a petition seeking alternate medical care on March 9, 2022.  A 
telephonic hearing on this petition was scheduled to occur on March 22, 2022, at 10:30 
AM.  Prior to the alternate medical care hearing, the parties had a brief off-the-record 
discussion regarding defendants’ recently filed Answer and their Addendum to the 
Answer.  After the discussion, counsel notified the undersigned that a consent order 
should be entered.   
 
With respect to the specific alternate medical care requested, claimant sought 
authorization for psychotherapy and cognitive behavioral therapy as recommended by 
Daniel Tranel, Ph.D.  Claimant also sought an order enforcing a referral from Alejandro 
Torres-Trejo, M.D. to Sherman Jew, D.O. for general medicine care.   
Defendants have agreed to authorize the requested medical treatment.  
According to defendants’ addendum, claimant has been scheduled for an appointment 
with Dr. Sherman Jew, and defendants are in the process of authorizing and scheduling 
claimant for psychotherapy and cognitive behavioral therapy with Philip Ascheman, 
Ph.D. 
The parties desired that a consent order be entered in this case, in lieu of a 
hearing.  After discussion between the parties and an agreement to provide the 
requested care, there is no justiciable issue for hearing and determination.  Both parties 
agree that a consent order is appropriate, and a formal hearing is unnecessary to 
resolve the contested case dispute.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties prior to the scheduled March 22, 2022, 
alternate medical care hearing, defendants shall authorize a referral to Dr. Sherman 
Jew. 
 
If Dr. Ascheman is willing to accept claimant as a patient, defendants shall 
authorize psychotherapy and cognitive behavioral therapy with Dr. Ascheman.  If Dr. 
Ascheman will not agree to accept claimant as a patient, defendants shall timely identify 
and authorize a different psychologist to provide psychotherapy and cognitive 
behavioral therapy to claimant.

On October 1, 2025, claimant Laritha Sweatte filed an application for alternate 
care under Iowa Code section 85.27 and Iowa Administrative Code rule 876–4.48, 
seeking pacemaker protocol for the right shoulder. Hearing took place on October 10, 
2025.  
During the scheduled hearing, the parties advised that they have reached an 
agreement regarding the requested care. The parties requested formal entry of the 
consent order to confirm and document the agreement as follows:  
Claimant has an accepted work injury to her right shoulder. Claimant has 
requested ongoing treatment and referrals made by claimant’s authorized treating 
providers, Mark Morrey, M.D., be approved and scheduled without further delay.  
After discussion between the parties, defendant confirmed to claimant’s counsel 
that the requested treatment is approved and authorized. As of this date, the defendant 
authorizes shoulder pacemaker protocol with Select Physical Therapy, specifically, 
Lance LeFurge. 
To avoid an unnecessary hearing, and to protect the interests of the clients, the 
parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring defendant to authorize and schedule 
treatment as described above. This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of 
fact or conclusions of law relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and 
enter an order that is consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both 
parties.   
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:  
1) Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, defendant shall, within seven (7) 
days of this order, have scheduled the requested medical treatment with an 
authorized provider.

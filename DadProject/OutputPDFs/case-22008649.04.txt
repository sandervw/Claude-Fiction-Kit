Claimant filed an application for alternate medical care on April 3, 2024.  
Claimant sought evaluation and treatment of the right knee and right hip.  A telephonic 
hearing on this petition was scheduled to occur on April 12, 2024, at 10:30 AM.  Prior to 
the alternate medical care hearing, counsel notified the undersigned that the parties 
were able to reach an amicable agreement and that a consent order should be entered.   
 
Defendants have agreed to make a good faith effort to authorize treatment and 
schedule the claimant for an orthopedic evaluation with Gary Knudson, M.D.  If Dr. 
Knudson refuses to see claimant, the defendants agree to authorize the requested 
medical care within 14 days with another provider. 
 
The parties desired that a consent order be entered in this case, in lieu of a 
hearing.  After discussion between the parties and an agreement to provide the 
requested care, there is no justiciable issue for hearing and determination.  Both parties 
agree that a consent order is appropriate and a formal hearing is unnecessary to 
resolve the contested case dispute.   
 
This consent order is not intended to reflect findings of fact or conclusions of law 
relative to this claim.  Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order that is 
consistent with both parties’ positions and acceptable to both parties.  A formal alternate 
medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties consented to entry of this order 
and waived the formal hearing.

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties prior to the scheduled April 12, 2024, 
alternate medical care hearing, defendants shall make a good faith effort to schedule 
claimant with Dr. Gary Knudson once he agrees to take claimant on as a patient.  If Dr. 
Knudson refuses to see claimant, the defendants agree to authorize alternate medical 
care within 14 days with another provider. 
 
 
IT IS SO ORDERED.

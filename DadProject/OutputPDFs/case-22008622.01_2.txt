Defendants appeal from an August 29, 2025 arbitration decision.  Claimant 
responds to the appeal and urges the arbitration decision be fully affirmed.  The 
presiding deputy commissioner held an arbitration hearing in this case on February 21, 
2025, and the case was fully submitted before the deputy on March 28, 2025. 
In the arbitration decision, the deputy commissioner found that claimant proved 
he sustained a permanent injury or permanent material aggravation of his right knee as 
a result of an admitted work injury on April 26, 2022.  The deputy commissioner 
weighed the competing medical opinions in the evidentiary record and accepted the 
opinions of Robert D. Rondinelli, M.D. as the most convincing.  As a result, the deputy 
commissioner awarded claimant healing period benefits, 96.8 weeks (equivalent to 44 
percent of the right leg) of permanent partial disability benefits, past medical expenses, 
and costs. 
On appeal, defendants challenge the deputy’s decision in two respects.  First, 
defendants challenge whether claimant proved by a preponderance of the evidence that 
his right knee arthritis was causally related to or materially and permanently aggravated 
by the work injury.  Second, defendants challenge the extent of permanent impairment 
and resulting permanent partial disability benefits awarded.  Again, claimant asserts the 
arbitration decision should be affirmed in its entirety. 
Pursuant to Iowa Code section 10A.321 and section 17A.15(3), I performed a de 
novo review of the evidentiary record and the detailed arguments of the parties.  Those 
portions of the proposed arbitration decision pertaining to issues not raised on appeal 
are adopted as part of this appeal decision.  With respect to the issues properly raised 
on appeal, I concur with the deputy’s findings and conclusions of law in most respects.  I 
adopt the arbitration decision’s findings of fact and conclusions of law with respect to all

issues except the extent of permanent disability.  On that issue, I provide the following 
analysis: 
I concur with the deputy commissioner that Dr. Rondinelli offers the most credible 
and convincing medical opinion on the issue of causation.  I concur with the deputy 
commissioner’s finding that claimant sustained a permanent material aggravation of his 
underlying right knee arthritis and that his current condition is related to the stipulated 
work injury on April 26, 2022.  However, I have some concern about the permanent 
impairment rating offered by Dr. Rondinelli and accepted by the deputy commissioner. 
Dr. Rondinelli opines: 
According to Table 17-33 (Impairment Estimates for Certain Lower 
Extremity Impairments), as listed on page 546, Mr. Geyssens suffered a 
posttraumatic ACL tear and surgical repair to his right knee, which 
corresponds to “a single cruciate or collateral ligament laxity, which was 
surgically repaired.”  The initial severity was sufficient to inhibit his mobility 
and self-care activities to a significant degree.  This has been surgically 
repaired, and he no longer has significant instability.  Therefore, I now 
recommend a 7% right lower extremity impairment (LEI) for his mild 
functional residuals in this regard.  Additionally, with respect to his 
meniscectomy, he had a right medial and lateral partial meniscectomy, 
which is worth 10% LEI at this time.  With respect to his tibial plateau 
fracture undisplaced, this is also worth 5% right lower extremity 
impairment. 
With respect to Mr. Geyssens’ tricompartmental osteoarthritis of mild-to-
moderate severity, this condition, although non-work related per se, was 
likely permanently materially aggravated as noted above.  This qualifies 
him for knee and patellofemoral arthritis of mild-to-moderate severity, 
which in aggregate is separately worth 30% right LEI. 
(Claimant’s Exhibit 1, page 13) 
Dr. Rondinelli’s reference to Table 17-33 of the AMA Guides to the Evaluation of 
Permanent Impairment, Fifth Edition, permits the agency to verify the impairment rating 
offered.  Table 17-33 clearly provides the relevant permanent impairment ratings for 
claimant’s mild cruciate laxity, partial meniscectomies to both the lateral and medial 
menisci, and the undisplaced tibial plateau fracture.  I accept those permanent 
impairment ratings and find that claimant proved a 22 percent permanent functional 
impairment of his right leg as a result of the work injury. 
However, Dr. Rondinelli also offers a 30% permanent impairment for mild-to-
moderate knee and patellofemoral arthritis in his right knee.  While I find that claimant 
proved his current condition is related to the work injury as a result of a material and 
permanent aggravation of that underlying condition, I cannot track Dr. Rondinelli’s 
permanent impairment rating under the AMA Guides, Fifth Edition.

The language of Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(x) is clear and unambiguous that 
“the extent of loss or percentage of permanent impairment shall be determined solely by 
utilizing the guides to the evaluation of permanent impairment, published by the 
American Medical Association, as adopted by the workers’ compensation commissioner 
by rule.”  Laguerre v. JBS USA Holdings, Inc., 29 N.W.3d 343 (Iowa Ct. App. 2025) 
(unpublished decision) (2025 WL 3023022 at *4).  This agency has complied with Iowa 
Code section 85.34(2)(x).  The agency adopted an administrative rule adopting the Fifth 
Edition of the AMA Guides to the Evaluation of Permanent Impairment as the applicable 
standard for determining permanent impairment pursuant to Iowa Code section 
85.34(2)(x).  See 876 IAC 2.4.   
The Iowa Court of Appeals clarified that the Iowa legislature made it “abundantly 
clear that the AMA Guides must be exclusive” and specifically prohibited the use of lay 
testimony or agency expertise in determining permanent impairment.  Id.  While the 
agency is not permitted to use its expertise to supplant or substitute an impairment 
rating for one offered by an expert, the agency is required to interpret the AMA Guides 
and correct an expert opinion that does not conform to the AMA Guides.  Klein v. 
Whirlpool Corp., 2026 WL 44438 at *5 (Iowa Ct. App. 2026) (slip opinion) (noting 
nothing in the plain language of Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(x) precludes the 
commissioner from reviewing an expert’s impairment rating and verifying compliance 
with the AMA Guides, Fifth Edition). 
In this instance, Dr. Rondinelli did not specify how he arrived at his permanent 
impairment for right knee osteoarthritis.  Generally, arthritis in the lower extremity is 
governed by Section 17.2h of the AMA Guides, Fifth Edition, and Table 17-31 on page 
544 of the Guides, Fifth Edition.  Review of Table 17-31 reveals that permanent 
impairment is determined by the cartilage interval in the knee joint and patellofemoral 
joint.   
I can hypothesize, or speculate, that Dr. Rondinelli likely reached the conclusion 
that claimant sustained a 30 percent right leg impairment by combining the permanent 
impairment for a 2 mm joint interval for both the knee (20% lower extremity rating under 
Table 17-31) and a 2 mm joint interval for the patellofemoral joint (10% lower extremity 
rating under Table 17-31).  However, Dr. Rondinelli does not clearly specify this is how 
he calculated this impairment rating.  Moreover, I find no documentation of the joint 
intervals of either the knee or the patellofemoral joint within this evidentiary record.  
Therefore, to accept Dr. Rondinelli’s permanent impairment for arthritis, I must assume 
that Dr. Rondinelli used Table 17-31, even though he did not say so.  Then, I must also 
assume or speculate that Dr. Rondinelli accurately determined the joint space of each 
was 2 millimeters without documentation. 
Ultimately, I conclude that the evidence submitted does not clearly document 
claimant’s residual joint space in either his right knee or right patellofemoral joint.  The 
evidence further does not document how claimant’s arthritis was rated within the 
provisions of the AMA Guides, Fifth Edition.

The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established has the burden 
of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence.  Iowa R. App. P. 6.904(3)(e).  
Claimant is the party that would lose on this issue if it is not established. I conclude that 
claimant failed to prove by a preponderance of the evidence how Dr. Rondinelli rated 
his right knee and patellofemoral joint arthritis.  Similarly, I conclude that claimant failed 
to prove by a preponderance of the evidence that Dr. Rondinelli’s permanent 
impairment rating related to arthritis complies with the provisions of the AMA Guides, 
Fifth Edition. Therefore, I conclude claimant has proven only a 22 percent permanent 
impairment of the right leg. 
The Iowa legislature has established a 220-week schedule for leg injuries.  Iowa 
Code section 85.34(2)(p).  Claimant is entitled to an award of permanent partial 
disability benefits equivalent to the proportional loss of her leg.  Iowa Code section 
85.34(2)(x); Blizek v. Eagle Signal Company, 164 N.W.2d 84 (Iowa 1969).  Twenty-two 
(22) percent of 220 weeks equals forty-eight point four (48.4) weeks.  Claimant is, 
therefore, entitled to an award of 48.4 weeks of permanent partial disability benefits 
against the employer.  Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(p), (x).   
ORDER 
IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
The August 29, 2025 arbitration decision is affirmed in part, reversed in part, and 
amended as explained in this decision. 
Defendants shall pay claimant forty-eight point four (48.4) weeks of permanent 
partial disability benefits commencing on December 29, 2023. 
Defendants shall pay healing period benefits from January 23, 2023, through 
March 10, 2023. 
All benefits shall be paid at the rate of nine hundred thirty-nine and 48/100 dollars 
($939.48) per week. 
Defendants shall pay all accrued weekly benefits in lump sum with applicable 
interest pursuant to Iowa Code section 85.30. 
Defendants shall be entitled to credit for any weekly benefits paid to date. 
Costs are taxed to defendants pursuant to 876 IAC 4.33, as set forth in the 
decision. 
Defendants shall file subsequent reports of injury (SROI) as required by this 
agency pursuant to rules 876 IAC 3.1(2), and 876 IAC 11.7.

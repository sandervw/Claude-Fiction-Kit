Claimant filed a petition seeking alternate medical care. A telephonic hearing on 
this petition was scheduled for May 18, 2021.  At the commencement of the alternate 
medical care hearing, counsel notified the undersigned that the parties thought they 
could reach an amicable agreement.  Discussions were held and it was eventually 
determined that a consent order should be issued.   
 
Specifically, claimant requests authorization of claimant to return to see 
Christopher L. Wise, M.D., as recommended by Michael D. Stover, M.D. on May 17, 
2021.  Defendants have agreed to authorize the referral to Dr. Wise.  Prior to the 
hearing, defendants had contacted Dr. Wise’s office, but before Dr. Wise would agree to 
see Mr. Baker, he required defendants mail him the past two years of treatment records.  
Defendants have sent the records to Dr. Wise and are waiting for his response.  Prior to 
the hearing, defendants scheduled an appointment for Mr. Baker to see a foot specialist 
as recommended by Dr. Stover; that appointment is scheduled for June 1, 2021.  
Defendants are willing to authorize the CT recommended by Dr. Stover, but are waiting 
for the workers’ compensation coordinator at Dr. Stover’s office to provide defendants 
with the written referral so defendants may schedule the CT.  Both parties agree that 
a consent order is appropriate and that a formal hearing is unnecessary to resolve this 
contested case dispute. 
 
THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED:

On March 20, 2025, claimant filed a petition for alternate medical care, seeking 
treatment for alleged injuries to her left leg, lower back, and body as a whole. Hearing 
was scheduled to take place on April 1, 2025. 
Prior to the scheduled hearing, the parties advised that they had reached an 
agreement regarding the requested care. The parties requested formal entry of the 
consent order to confirm and document the agreement as follows: 
Claimant has an accepted work injury to her back. On October 29, 2024, 
Claimant requested further treatment be authorized with the authorized treating pain 
management provider, Tork Harman, M.D. Claimant repeated this request without 
authorization from Defendants prior to the filing of the Petition for Alternate Medical 
Care herein.  
After discussion between the parties, Defendants confirmed to Claimant’s 
counsel that the requested treatment is approved and authorized. As of this date, the 
medical provider has not confirmed the date and/or time of the follow-up exam. 
However, Defendants confirmed it is authorized and will provide the date and time of the 
appointment as soon as it is known.   
To avoid an unnecessary hearing, and to protect the interests of the clients, the 
parties agree to entry of a consent order, requiring Defendants to authorize and 
schedule treatment as described above. This consent order is not intended to reflect 
findings of fact or conclusions of law relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to

THEREFORE, IT IS ORDERED: 
The hearing scheduled for April 1, 2025, is cancelled. 
Pursuant to the agreement of the parties, Defendants agree to provide 
confirmation to Claimant, within fourteen days of this Order, that the requested 
appointment with Dr. Harman has been scheduled. If the medical provider fails to 
schedule such appointment within fourteen days, Defendants agree that Claimant 
retains the right to file another petition for alternate medical care regarding this matter if 
necessary.

STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
Claimant, Martin Valtierra, filed a petition in arbitration seeking workers’ 
compensation benefits from the Second Injury Fund of Iowa (Fund), as defendant.  This 
matter was heard on May 15, 2025, with a final submission date of June 12, 2025. 
The record in this case consists of Joint Exhibits 1-7, Claimant’s Exhibits 1-8, 
Fund Exhibits AA-CC, and the testimony of claimant.  Serving as interpreter was 
Christine Feagan.   
STIPULATIONS 
The parties filed a hearing report at the commencement of the arbitration 
hearing.  On the hearing report, the parties entered into various stipulations.  All of 
those stipulations were accepted and are hereby incorporated into this arbitration 
decision and no factual or legal issues relative to the parties’ stipulations will be raised 
or discussed in this decision.  The parties are now bound by their stipulations.  
ISSUES 
1. If claimant has a qualifying first injury for the purposes of Fund benefits. 
 
2. The extent of claimant’s functional loss regarding the first injury. 
 
3. Whether claimant is entitled to Fund benefits. 
 
4. The extent of the Fund’s credit for Fund benefits.

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
The first issue to be determined is whether claimant sustained a first qualifying 
injury that would entitle him to Fund benefits. 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established has the burden 
of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence.  Iowa R. App. P. 6.904(3)(e). 
Iowa Code section 85.64 governs Second Injury Fund liability.  Before liability of 
the Fund is triggered, three requirements must be met.  First, the employee must have 
lost or lost the use of a hand, arm, foot, leg, or eye.  Second, the employee must sustain 
a loss or loss of use of another specified member or organ through a compensable 
injury.  Third, permanent disability must exist as to both the initial injury and the second 
injury.   
The Second Injury Fund Act exists to encourage the hiring of handicapped 
persons by making a current employer responsible only for the amount of disability 
related to an injury occurring while that employer employed the handicapped individual 
as if the individual had had no preexisting disability.  See Anderson v. Second Injury 
Fund, 262 N.W.2d 789, 792 (Iowa 1978); 15 Iowa Practice, Workers’ Compensation, 
Lawyer, Section 17:1, p. 211 (2014-2015). 
The Fund is responsible for the industrial disability present after the second injury 
that exceeds the disability attributable to the first and second injuries.  Section 85.64.  
Second Injury Fund of Iowa v. Braden, 459 N.W.2d 467, 471 (Iowa 1990); Second Injury 
Fund v. Neelans, 436 N.W.2d 355, 358 (Iowa 1989); Second Injury Fund v. Mich Coal 
Co., 274 N.W.2d 300, 304 (Iowa 1979). 
The statute requires that for claimant to qualify for Fund benefits, he must have a 
loss of or loss of use of a scheduled member as detailed in the statute.  In this matter, 
claimant carries the burden of proof that he had loss of or loss of use of his left eye from 
the December 2022 work injury. 
As detailed, claimant’s December 20, 2022, injury resulted in a nondisplaced 
fracture of the orbit on the left.  Records from the emergency department indicate that 
claimant had no trauma to the eye. (JE 2, p. 19) 
Dr. Wymore, an ophthalmologist, treated claimant on two occasions following his 
orbital fracture.  Records from both of those visits indicate claimant’s vision was healthy 
and that he had equal vision in each eye.  (JE 3, pp. 37-38)  Claimant was returned to 
work at full duty following the orbital fracture.  Dr. Wymore noted on both visits that if 
claimant had problems with his left eye he was to return for evaluation.  (JE 3, pp. 37-
48)  There is no evidence in the record claimant ever returned to Dr. Wymore regarding 
the treatment of his eye for loss of vision regarding the December 20, 2022, injury.

Claimant did see an ophthalmologist in April of 2024 for cataracts.  There is no 
evidence in the record claimant’s cataracts were related to the December of 2022 work 
injury.  (JE 3, pp. 49-54) 
Both Dr. Bansal and Dr. Chen found claimant had a 3 percent permanent 
impairment to the body as a whole due to the orbital fracture.  Dr. Bansal found that 
claimant had a 3 percent impairment to the body as a whole based on Table 11-5 and 
Examples 11-9 and 11-10 of the Guides.  Table 11-5 concerns a finding of permanent 
impairment due to facial disfigurements or disorders.  Dr. Bansal also based his rating 
on the Guides Examples 11-9 and 11-10.  Example 11-9 concerns a rating for a 
zygomatic arch fracture.  Example 11-10 concerns a rating for a fracture to the nasal 
bones.  (Guides, pages 256-257)  Dr. Bansal’s ratings for claimant’s December 20, 
2022, orbital fracture makes no reference to Chapter 12 of the Guides concerning the 
impairment for the visual system.  (Ex. 1, pp. 15-17) 
Dr. Bansal’s notes indicate that claimant had some permanent impairment based 
upon deficits in ocular range of motion and double vision (diplopia).  As noted. Dr. 
Wymore, an ophthalmologist, found that claimant had full range of motion in his left eye.  
Dr. Chen also notes that claimant’s visual problems and headaches are not related to 
the December 20, 2022, work injury. 
Claimant fractured a bone in his face during his December 20, 2022, injury.  An 
ophthalmologist found on two occasions that claimant’s vision and range of motion of 
the left eye were normal.  Dr. Bansal rated claimant’s left eye injury based upon values 
for facial disfigurement.  Dr. Bansal does not use a rating method for the visual system 
as found in the Guides.  Dr. Bansal’s rating is to the body as a whole and is not a 
scheduled member rating.  Claimant has some impairment due to disfigurement caused 
by the orbital fracture.  However, based on the facts as detailed above, claimant has 
failed to carry his burden of proof he has a loss of or loss of use of his eye.  Claimant 
has failed to carry his burden of proof that he has a qualifying first injury for the 
purposes of Fund benefits.  
As claimant failed to carry his burden of proof that he has a qualifying first injury 
for the purposes of Fund benefits, all other issues are moot. 
ORDER 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
That claimant shall take nothing in the way of benefits from this proceeding. 
That both parties shall pay their own costs.

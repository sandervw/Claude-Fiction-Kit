On January 31, 2025 claimant filed an application for alternate medical care 
under Iowa Code section 85.27 and rule 876 IAC 4.48. A hearing was held on February 
12, 2025. All parties were given proper notice. 
Claimant sustained an injury on August 10, 2024, to the right shoulder, elbow, 
neck and head, which arose out of and in the course of employment. Defendants did not 
appear or answer. Claimant’s counsel properly served defendant employer and emailed 
the case manager handling the matter for the defendant insurer. There was no 
response. A party that fails to file an answer may be subject to a default judgment. Iowa 
R. Civ. Proc 1.971. Default is largely within the discretion of the adjudicator. (Avery v. 
Peterson, 243 N.W.2d 630, 631–32 (Iowa 1976). Defendants are found in default. 
Claimant is dissatisfied with the medical treatment offered by the defendant. 
The hearing was recorded via digital voice recorder and a complete decision was 
dictated into the record on the day of the hearing. The decision will be reproduced in 
typewritten form only if the decision is appealed, and the parties are advised to follow 
the procedures outlined under the administrative code. Any rights of appeal will run from 
the date the decision was dictated into the record. 
The deputy ordered that claimant’s petition for alternate medical care be 
GRANTED. Defendants shall provide the surgery to the shoulder as recommended by 
authorized treating physician Dr. White. The decision was rendered orally on the record.

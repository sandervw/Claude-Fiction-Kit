Claimant filed an alternate medical care petition on January 15, 2026.  
Defendants filed an answer on January 26, 2026.  The defendants are investigating the 
causes of claimant’s need for surgery.  Hearing was set for today, January 27, 2026.   
Prior to going on the record, the undersigned convened on a telephone 
conference with counsel for the parties.  The defendants sent a letter to the claimant’s 
treating physician requesting an opinion regarding causal connection.  Defendants have 
not received an answer at this time.  The parties, however, are in agreement that 
allowing the doctor a little more time to provide a response is in the best interest of all 
parties.  Consequently, this claim for treatment is not deemed admitted or denied at this 
time.  The defendants have agreed that if the treating physician provides a positive 
causation opinion, the treatment requested shall be provided promptly.  If the treating 
physician either provides an adverse causation opinion, or does not provide a response 
within fourteen days, the claim shall be deemed DENIED.  If the claim is denied, the 
defendants shall lose their authorization defense and claimant may direct his own 
treatment and seek reimbursement through normal claim proceedings.

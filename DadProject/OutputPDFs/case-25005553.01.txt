STATEMENT OF THE CASE   
Claimant, Scottie Scott, filed a petition in arbitration seeking workers’ 
compensation benefits from Country Inn & Suites by Radisson, defendant employer, as 
a result of an alleged injury sustained on approximately June 15, 2024.   
Claimant filed an original notice and petition with the Division of Workers’ 
Compensation (Agency) on September 9, 2024.  Proof of service of the petition was 
filed with the Agency on September 18, 2024, and September 25, 2024.  In those filings, 
the claimant included a copy of a Notice of Service from the Black Hawk County Sheriff. 
(See Proof of Service).  That Notice indicates that Officer Isaac Jerome served Satyre 
Martinez, the defendants’ designee at the Country Inn & Suites by Radisson located at 
4025 Hammond Avenue, Waterloo, Iowa 50702, on September 13, 2024. (Id.).  On 
November 18, 2024, claimant filed a notice of intent to file written application for default 
judgment against Country Inn & Suites by Radisson with the Agency.  On December 23, 
2024, the undersigned granted claimant’s application and entered default against the 
defendants.    
The matter was set for a default hearing on March 27, 2025, at 1:00 p.m.  The 
matter was heard and fully submitted to Deputy Workers’ Compensation Commissioner, 
Amanda Rutherford, as scheduled.  Defendants failed to appear or participate. 
Defendants did not file an appearance, motion, answer, or other pleading with the 
Agency.  The record consists of claimant’s exhibit 1 and the testimony of the claimant.  
The matter was recorded by means of digital audio recorder.

ISSUES 
The following issues were presented for determination:   
1. The nature and extent of claimant’s entitlement to permanent disability 
benefits from the June 15, 2024 date of injury.  
 
2.  Costs.  
As an entry of default has been entered against defendants, the issues of 
existence of an employee-employer relationship, whether claimant sustained an injury 
on June 15, 2024, that arose out of and in the course of employment, and whether there 
is a causal connection between the injury and the alleged disability, will not be 
discussed.   
FINDINGS OF FACT   
The undersigned, having considered all of the evidence and testimony in the 
record, finds as follows: 
The claimant’s name is Scottie Scott (hereinafter “Scott”).  At the time of the 
alleged injury in this case, Scott was 39 years old. (Testimony; see Cl. Ex. 1, p. 1).  
Scott resides in Waterloo, Iowa. (Id.; Cl. Ex. 1, p. 1).   
On the date of injury, Scott was employed by Country Inn & Suites by Radisson 
(hereinafter “Country Inn”), a hotel in Waterloo, Iowa. (Testimony).  Scott worked in the 
laundry room at the hotel. (Id.).  His job consisted of washing and folding bed sheets 
and towels. (Id.).  Scott testified his position required lots of bending and standing, and 
carrying laundry. (Id.).  Scott work full-time for Country Inn, 40 hours per week, and he 
was paid $14.00 an hour. (Id.).  At the time of the injury, Scott was single and entitled to 
one dependent on his taxes. (Id.).   
Scott is claiming that his work at Country Inn caused a permanent aggravation of 
a pre-existing condition in his right leg. (Testimony).  He testified that as a result of the 
constant walking and standing at work, his right leg got irritated, that it swelled up, and 
that liquid started coming out of his ankle. (Id.).   
Scott sought treatment for his right leg at MercyOne Medical Center in Waterloo, 
Iowa. (Testimony; Cl Ex. 1).  Scott has a history of recurrent deep vein thrombosis 
(DVT). (Cl Ex. 1, p. 1).  He has a prescription for Eliquis; he will need to take the 
medication for the rest of his life. (Id.).  
According to the medical records, Scott was evaluated by Suzanne Munns, M.D., 
and Amjed Katmeh, M.D. at MercyOne on February 28, 2024. (Cl Ex. 1, p. 1).  Scott 
was seen for a venous stasis ulcer on his right ankle. (Id.).  Dr. Munns instructed him to 
wash the wound 4 times a day and gave him prescriptions for Tramadol and Bactrim. 
(Id.).  She also wanted him to wear compression socks daily. (Id.).  The treatment note

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
The party who would suffer loss if an issue were not established ordinarily has 
the burden of proving that issue by a preponderance of the evidence. Iowa Rule of 
Appellate Procedure 6.14(6)(e). 
  
  
The claimant has the burden of proving by a preponderance of the evidence that 
the injury is a proximate cause of the disability on which the claim is based. A cause is 
proximate if it is a substantial factor in bringing about the result; it need not be the only 
cause. A preponderance of the evidence exists when the causal connection is probable, 
rather than merely possible. George A. Hormel & Co. v. Jordan, 569 N.W.2d 148 (Iowa 
1997); Frye v. Smith-Doyle Contractors, 569 N.W.2d 154 (Iowa App. 1997); Sanchez v. 
Blue Bird Midwest, 554 N.W.2d 283 (Iowa App. 1996). 
 
The question of medical causation is “essentially within the domain of expert 
testimony.” Cedar Rapids Community School Dist. v. Pease, 807 N.W.2d 839, 844-45 
(Iowa 2011). The commissioner, as the trier of fact, must “weigh the evidence and 
measure the credibility of witnesses.” Id. The trier of fact may accept or reject expert 
testimony, even if uncontroverted, in whole or in part. Frye, 569 N.W.2d at 156. When

considering the weight of an expert opinion, the fact-finder may consider whether the 
examination occurred shortly after the claimant was injured, the compensation 
arrangement, the nature and extent of the examination, the expert's education, 
experience, training, and practice, and “all other factors which bear upon the weight and 
value” of the opinion. Rockwell Graphic Systems, Inc. v. Prince, 366 N.W.2d 187, 192 
(Iowa 1985). Unrebutted expert medical testimony cannot be summarily rejected. Poula 
v. Siouxland Wall & Ceiling, Inc., 516 N.W.2d 910 (Iowa App. 1994). Supportive lay 
testimony may be used to buttress expert testimony, but it cannot take the place of 
expert testimony.   
 
 
The right of a worker to receive compensation for injuries sustained which arose 
out of and in the course of employment is statutory. The statute conferring this right can 
also fix the amount of compensation to be paid for different specific injuries, and the 
employee is not entitled to compensation except as provided by statute. Soukup v 
Shores Co., 268 N.W. 598 (Iowa 1936). Where an injury is limited to a scheduled 
member the loss is measured functionally, not industrially. Graves v. Eagle Iron Works, 
331 N.W.2d 116 (Iowa 1983).  Scott has suffered an injury to his right leg, a scheduled 
member.  His loss must be measured functionally.   
 
In 2017, the Iowa legislature made several changes to the workers’ 
compensation statutes.  One of these changes overturned a prior decision holding that 
functional disability encompassed more than doctor provided impairment ratings.  
As revised, Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(x) provides that when determining functional 
disability, the extent of loss or percentage of permanent impairment shall be determined 
solely by utilizing the guides to the evaluation of permanent impairment, published  
by the American medical association, as adopted by the workers' compensation 
commissioner by rule pursuant to chapter 17A. Lay testimony or agency expertise shall 
not be utilized in determining loss or percentage of permanent impairment. Iowa Code 
section 85.34(2)(x). 
There are no permanent impairment ratings in the hearing record, let alone an 
impairment rating in compliance with the AMA Guides.  Scott testified that he walks with 
a limp and has permanent impairment in his right leg, but he has never undergone an 
impairment evaluation with a doctor. This is what is required under the AMA Guides, 5th 
Edition, and therefore what is required under the statute.  See AMA Guides to the 
Evaluation of Permanent Impairment, Fifth Edition, p. 529 (stating that an impairment 
rating for gait derangement requires an evaluation and should be supported by 
pathologic findings, such as x-rays).  Scott has not met his burden to prove he suffered 
permanent impairment from the June 15, 2024 injury date.

STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
This matter is before the Iowa Workers’ Compensation Commission on remand 
from the Iowa Supreme Court. 
This matter was initially heard in arbitration on March 10, 2022.  The Arbitration 
Decision held, in part, that claimant was entitled to workers’ compensation benefits 
based upon a loss of earning capacity or an industrial disability.  That decision was 
affirmed on intra-agency appeal.   
Defendants moved for judicial review of the Appeal Decision.  The district court 
confirmed the commissioner’s decision. 
Defendants appealed that decision to the Iowa Supreme Court.  The appeal was 
transferred to the Iowa Court of Appeals.  In a 2-1 decision, the Iowa Court of Appeals 
affirmed the district court.  
Defendants filed an application for further review, which was granted by the Iowa 
Supreme Court.  The Iowa Supreme Court reversed the decision of the Iowa Court of 
Appeals, the district court and the workers’ compensation commissioner.  They 
remanded the case back to the commission to decide the claimant’s award based upon 
a functional disability. 
Upon written delegation of authority given to me by the interim workers’ 
compensation commissioner under Iowa Code section 10A.305, I render this decision

FINDINGS OF FACT 
The Findings of Fact in the Arbitration and Appeal Decision adequately detail the 
record in this case.  The Findings of Fact in the Remand Decision will only address the 
Findings of Fact relative to the issue on remand. 
Claimant worked for defendant-employer, Den Hartog Industries, as an outside 
loader and a material handler.  (Hearing Transcript, pages 18-19; Defendants’ Exhibit C, 
pages 1-2)  On July 24, 2019, claimant was replacing a hoop used to secure a tank.  
(Tr., pp. 24-25)  While replacing the hoop, claimant injured his lower back.  (Tr., p. 25; 
Joint Exhibit 5, page 1)   
Claimant was assessed as having a disk herniation at the L4-5 level and an 
annular tear at L5-S1.  (JE 5, pp. 3-5; JE 6, pp. 1-2)  Claimant received steroid 
injections for his injury.  Claimant also underwent physical therapy.  (Tr., pp. 30-31; JE 
5, pp. 6-7; JE 7, pp. 3-6, 9-12) 
At the time of injury, claimant earned $15.16 per hour.  After his injury, Den 
Hartog increased claimant’s pay to $15.50 per hour.  (Tr., p. 43) 
In February of 2020, claimant was limited by Hendrik Klopper, M.D., a 
neurosurgeon, to lifting up to 40 pounds.  (JE 5, p. 7) 
In June of 2020, claimant and his fiancée decided to move to be closer to family. 
Claimant left Sioux County and his employment with Den Hartog.  Claimant went to 
work for Meridian Manufacturing as a welder and earned $17.48 per hour.   
In June and September of 2020, claimant received more steroid injections at the 
L4-5 level for pain.  (JE 5, pp. 11-12; JE 7, pp. 24-25; JE 8, p. 4) 
At the time of hearing, claimant had left employment at Meridian.  Claimant was 
working as a welder for Gomaco in Ida Grove, Iowa.  At Gomaco, claimant earned 
$20.15 per hour.  When he began with Gomaco, claimant asked for and received a 
release from all of his work restrictions.  (Tr., pp. 47-48; JE 5, p. 21) 
In a March 10, 2021, letter, Dr. Klopper found that claimant had a 5 percent 
permanent impairment to the body as a whole.  (JE 5, p. 24)  Claimant underwent an 
independent medical evaluation (IME) with Sunil Bansal, M.D.  Dr. Bansal found that 
claimant had an 8 percent permanent impairment to the body as a whole due, in part, to 
claimant’s radiculopathy.  (Claimant’s Exhibit 1, page 10)

CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
Claimant sustained a back injury on July 24, 2019.  A back injury is an 
unscheduled injury and is compensated under Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(v).  
Bridgestone Ams. v. Anderson, 4 N.W.3d 676, 685-686 (Iowa 2024)  
Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(v) states, in relevant part,  
[1] In all cases of permanent partial disability other than those described or 
referred to in paragraphs “a” through “u”, the compensation shall be paid 
during the number of weeks in relation to five hundred weeks as the 
reduction in the employee’s earning capacity caused by the disability 
bears in relation to the earning capacity that the employee possessed 
when the injury occurred. [2] A determination of the reduction in the 
employee’s earning capacity caused by the disability shall take into 
account the permanent partial disability of the employee and the number 
of years in the future it was reasonably anticipated that the employee 
would work at the time of the injury. [3] If an employee who is eligible for 
compensation under this paragraph returns to work or is offered work for 
which the employee receives or would receive the same or greater salary, 
wages, or earnings than the employee received at the time of the injury, 
the employee shall be compensated based only upon the employee’s 
functional impairment resulting from the injury, and not in relation to the 
employee’s earning capacity. [4] Notwithstanding section 85.26, 
subsection 2, if an employee who is eligible for compensation under this 
paragraph returns to work with the same employer and is compensated 
based only upon the employee’s functional impairment resulting from the 
injury as provided in this paragraph and is terminated from employment by 
that employer, the award or agreement for settlement for benefits under 
this chapter shall be reviewed upon commencement of reopening 
proceedings by the employee for a determination of any reduction in the 
employee’s earning capacity caused by the employee’s permanent partial 
disability. 
The Iowa Supreme Court held that since claimant had returned to work at Den 
Hartog after his back injury and received the same or greater wages, claimant’s 
entitlement to permanent partial disability benefits was to be based on his functional 
impairment resulting from the injury.  Claimant’s entitlement to permanent partial 
disability benefits, as per Iowa Code section 85.34(2)(v)[3], was not to be compensated 
under a loss of earning capacity or industrial disability.  Den Hartog Industries v. 
Dungan, 26 N.W.3d 377, 382-384 (Iowa 2025)

The deputy that heard the arbitration case found that claimant had an 8 percent 
permanent impairment to the body as a whole.  Based upon the analysis found in the 
Arbitration and Appeal Decision, I find no reason to differ from that Findings of Fact.  As 
claimant had an 8 percent permanent impairment to the body as a whole, his 
entitlement to permanent partial disability benefits is determined by Iowa Code section 
85.34(2)(v)[3].  Claimant is entitled to 40 weeks of permanent partial disability benefits 
(500 weeks x 8 percent). 
ORDER 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
That defendants shall pay claimant forty (40) weeks of permanent partial 
disability benefits at the rate of four hundred thirty-nine and 89/100 dollars ($439.89) per 
week commencing on February 8, 2021, and shall receive credit for benefits previously 
paid. 
That defendants shall pay accrued weekly benefits in a lump sum together with 
interest at an annual rate equal to the one-year treasury constant maturity published by 
the federal reserve in the most recent H15 report settled as of the date of injury, plus 
two (2) percent. 
That the Appeal Decision of January 13, 2023, shall remain the same in all other 
respects.

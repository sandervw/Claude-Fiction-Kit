STATEMENT OF THE CASE 
 
This is a contested case proceeding under Iowa Code chapters 85 and 17A.  The 
expedited procedure of rule 876 IAC 4.48 is invoked by claimant, Juana Herrera de 
Gonzalez.  Claimant appeared personally and through her attorney, Dennis McElwain.  
Defendants failed to appear for hearing and were found to be in default. 
 
The alternate medical care claim came on for a telephonic hearing on 
September 3, 2020.  The proceedings were digitally recorded.  That recording 
constitutes the official record of this proceeding.  Pursuant to the Commissioner’s 
February 16, 2015 Order, the undersigned has been delegated authority to issue a final 
agency decision in this alternate medical care proceeding.  Therefore, this ruling is 
designated final agency action and any appeal of the decision would be to the Iowa 
District Court pursuant to Iowa Code section 17A. 
 
Claimant offered exhibits 1-8, which include a total of 11 pages.  Those exhibits 
were received.  Claimant testified on her own behalf, using an interpreter to provide her 
testimony.  No other witnesses were called to testify and the evidentiary record closed 
at the conclusion of the alternate medical care hearing. 
 
ISSUE 
 
The issue presented for resolution is whether the claimant is entitled to 
authorization of care through and at the direction of John W. McClellan, M.D., including 
but not limited to impending bilateral EMG tests on claimant’s arms and legs on 
September 11, 2020.

FINDINGS OF FACT 
 
The undersigned having considered all the evidence in the record finds: 
 
Claimant, Juana Herrera de Gonzalez, is a 62-year-old woman, who has worked 
at Seaboard Triumph Foods, L.L.C., since January 2018.  On July 16, 2019, Ms. 
Herrera de Gonzalez was struck by an automobile on the premises of the employer and 
sustained injuries.  The employer initially authorized and directed medical care through 
Douglas Martin, M.D.  (Claimant’s testimony)  Dr. Martin apparently declared maximum 
medical improvement and questioned whether claimant’s carpal tunnel and knee are 
personal or related to work.  The employer sent correspondence to claimant denying 
liability for the carpal tunnel syndrome and her knee claim.  (Claimant’s Exhibit 1) 
 
After Dr. Martin declared maximum medical improvement, claimant testified she 
continued to have symptoms and returned to work full duty.  Defendants offered no 
further medical care.  Therefore, Ms. Herrera de Gonzalez sought medical care through 
a surgeon, John W. McClellan, M.D., at Nebraska Spine and Pain Center in Omaha.  
(Claimant’s testimony; Claimant’s Ex. 2) 
 
Dr. McClellan evaluated claimant twice, once on June 15, 2020 and once in 
August 2020.  (Claimant’s testimony)  Dr. McClellan diagnosed claimant with cervical 
spinal stenosis, likely at the C5-6 level.  He also diagnosed her with chronic lumbar 
spinal claudication in an L2-3 radicular pattern.  Dr. McLellan recommends EMGs of 
both upper and both lower extremities and notes:  
 
I explained to Juana that the EMG will help to identify if the nerve 
compression or if the injury has caused any true nerve injury.  If the 
nerves are showing significant injury, then further evaluation and surgical 
options will be evaluated.  This is true for both the cervical and lumbar 
spine. 
 
(Claimant’s Ex. 2, page 4)  Dr. McLellan noted, “Based on the MRIs alone, the stenosis 
appears quite severe at C5-6 and C6-7 in the cervical spine.  In the lumbar spine, I am 
very concerned because the lateral listhesis at L3-4 suggests significant instability.”  
(Claimant’s Ex. 2, p. 4) 
 
 
Claimant has clearly proven that the recommended EMGs are reasonable and 
necessary medical care for her neck and low back.  However, the question is whether 
defendants are offering reasonable medical care.  Claimant concedes that defendants 
provided initial medical care through Dr. Martin.  In addition, defendants offered an 
orthopaedic evaluation at CNOS, which occurred July 27, 2020.  Claimant introduced a 
work restriction sheet from that appointment (Claimant’s Ex. 4) and testified that the 
surgeon recommended an injection for her low back.  Claimant has requested medical 
records for that appointment, but has not been able to obtain those records from 
defendants to date.  (Claimant’s Ex. 6-7)

Mr. McElwain, claimant’s counsel of record, provided a professional statement 
that defendants have offered one of the two EMG’s being recommended but claimant

the lack of care being offered.  (Claimant’s Ex. 3, 5-7)  
 
Defendants were clearly aware that there was a pending alternate medical care 
petition.  (Claimant’s Ex. 8)  Defendants failed to file an answer, appearance, or give 
notice of their intention to participate in the alternate medical care hearing.  Claimant’s 
counsel provided a statement at hearing that he notified defense counsel on September 
2, 2020 that there was not an agreement and that claimant intended to proceed with the 
alternate medical care hearing.  The undersigned sent an e-mail to presumed defense 
counsel, Meredith Ashley, prior to commencement of the alternate medical care hearing 
on September 3, 2020.   
 
Claimant’s counsel also provided the phone number he had for defense counsel 
prior to the commencement of the hearing.  The undersigned called said number and 
left a message with defense counsel, giving notice that the hearing was proceeding and 
giving his cell phone number for defense counsel to call if she received the voice 
message during the pendency of the hearing.  The hearing proceeded ten to eleven 
minutes after the scheduled start time to give defendants an opportunity to respond to 
the inquiries and messages. Defense counsel did not respond to either the e-mail or the 
voice message left by the undersigned and defendants were determined to be in 
default.   
It is found that claimant sustained injuries to her neck and low back as a result of 
her work injury on July 16, 2019.  It is found that claimant has ongoing symptoms as a 
result of the neck and low back injuries.  It is found that claimant requires additional 
medical care for her neck and low back injuries.  It is found that defendants have not 
authorized or scheduled recommended back injections for claimant since her evaluation 
at CNOS in July 2020.  
 
It is found that Ms. Herrera de Gonzalez obtained surgical consultation with Dr. 
McClellan and that Dr. McClellan offers specific recommendations to further evaluate 
claimant’s neck and low back, including bilateral EMG testing of claimant’s arms and 
legs.  Dr. McClellan then recommends a return evaluation, either live or via 
telemedicine, after the EMG test results are received.  (Claimant’s Ex. 2, p. 4) 
 
I find that defendants have not offered reasonable, necessary and prompt 
medical care for claimant’s neck and low back injuries.  Claimant has lost any 
confidence in CNOS physicians and desires treatment through Dr. McClellan.  
Defendants have not promptly authorized further medical care through CNOS or offered 
alternate medical care that is reasonable.  I find that the medical recommendations

REASONING AND CONCLUSIONS OF LAW 
 
The employer shall furnish reasonable surgical, medical, dental, osteopathic, 
chiropractic, podiatric, physical rehabilitation, nursing, ambulance and hospital services 
and supplies for all conditions compensable under the workers' compensation law.  The 
employer shall also allow reasonable and necessary transportation expenses incurred 
for those services.  The employer has the right to choose the provider of care, except 
where the employer has denied liability for the injury.  Section 85.27.  Holbert v. 
Townsend Engineering Co., Thirty-second Biennial Report of the Industrial 
Commissioner 78 (Review-Reopening October 16, 1975). 
 
By challenging the employer’s choice of treatment – and seeking alternate care – 
claimant assumes the burden of proving the authorized care is unreasonable.  See Iowa 
R. App. P 14(f)(5); Bell Bros. Heating and Air Conditioning v. Gwinn, 779 N.W.2d 193, 
209 (Iowa 2010); Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).  Determining 
what care is reasonable under the statute is a question of fact.  Long v. Roberts Dairy 
Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).  The employer’s obligation turns on the question of 
reasonable necessity, not desirability.  Id.; Harned v. Farmland Foods, Inc., 331 N.W.2d 
98 (Iowa 1983).   
An application for alternate medical care is not automatically sustained because 
claimant is dissatisfied with the care he has been receiving.  Mere dissatisfaction with 
the medical care is not ample grounds for granting an application for alternate medical 
care.  Rather, the claimant must show that the care was not offered promptly, was not 
reasonably suited to treat the injury, or that the care was unduly inconvenient for the 
claimant.  Long v. Roberts Dairy Co., 528 N.W.2d 122 (Iowa 1995).   
An employer’s right to select the provider of medical treatment to an injured 
worker does not include the right to determine how an injured worker should be 
diagnosed, evaluated, treated, or other matters of professional medical judgment.  
Assmann v. Blue Star Foods, File No. 866389 (Declaratory Ruling, May 19, 1988).   
Reasonable care includes care necessary to diagnose the condition and 
defendants are not entitled to interfere with the medical judgment of its own treating 
physician.  Pote v. Mickow Corp., File No. 694639 (Review-Reopening Decision 
June 17, 1986).

In Pirelli-Armstrong Tire Co. v. Reynolds, 562 N.W.2d 433, 437 (Iowa 1997), the 
supreme court held that “when evidence is presented to the commissioner that the 
employer-authorized medical care has not been effective and that such care is ‘inferior 
or less extensive’ than other available care requested by the employee, . . . the 
commissioner is justified by section 85.27 to order the alternate care.” 
 
Having found that the employer failed to offer prompt, reasonable, and necessary 
medical care for claimant’s low back and neck injuries, I conclude that claimant has 
established entitlement to an order for alternate medical care.  Claimant produced 
evidence demonstrating that additional diagnostic testing and follow-up evaluation are 
necessary for her neck and low back injuries.  Claimant proved that the 
recommendations of Dr. McClellan will provide reasonable, necessary and prompt care.  
Defendants failed to appear, were in default at the hearing, and there is no evidence 
defendants are providing reasonable, necessary, or prompt medical care to treat 
claimant’s condition.  Therefore, I conclude that claimant has established the need and 
reasonableness of the care offered by Dr. McClellan.  Claimant has established 
entitlement to an order directing that care be authorized through Dr. McClellan and that 
all care recommended by and through Dr. McClellan should be authorized, including the 
impending bilateral EMGs of claimant’s legs and arms on September 11, 2020. 
 
ORDER 
 
THEREFORE IT IS ORDERED: 
 
The claimant's petition for alternate medical care is granted.   
 
Defendants shall immediately authorize and pay for all medical care through and 
recommended by Dr. McClellan pertaining to claimants’ neck and low back injuries, 
including the recommended bilateral EMG’s of claimant’s legs and arms scheduled for 
September 11, 2020, as well as follow-up care with and through Dr. McClellan. 
 
Defendants shall make arrangements to pre-pay, guarantee, or reimburse 
claimant for the cost of the scheduled EMGs on September 11, 2020.

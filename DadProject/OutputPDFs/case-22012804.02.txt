 On September 26, 2024, claimant filed an original notice and petition for 
alternate medical care in this file under Iowa Code section 85.27, invoking the 
provisions of rule 876 IAC 4.48. Claimant has alleged a back/whole body injury on July 
18, 2022. Claimant seeks an order that defendants authorize treatment recommended 
by Hendrik Klopper, M.D. Hearing was scheduled for October 8, 2024. 
 
Defendants filed an answer on October 8, 2024. The answer states that claimant 
was originally released from care related to the injury in May of 2023. In July of 2024 
claimant requested additional care. Defendants authorized claimant to return to Dr. 
Klopper, who then recommended a repeat left L5-S1 microsurgical discectomy. 
Defendants have written to Dr. Klopper seeking his opinion as to whether the 
recommended procedure is causally related to the July 18, 2022 work injury, but have 
not yet received a response. As such, defendants cannot admit or deny liability for the 
requested treatment at this time. 
 
Ordinarily, a petition for alternate care will be dismissed if liability is at issue. 
However, in this case, defendants agree the procedure will be authorized in the event 
Dr. Klopper finds causation. The parties have communicated with the undersigned, and 
requested a consent order to document and record their agreement in this case.

IT IS THEREFORE ORDERED: 
 
The parties agree that defendants will immediately notify claimant upon receipt of 
Dr. Klopper’s causation opinion. If Dr. Klopper opines the need for the recommended 
procedure is causally related to the July 18, 2022 work injury, defendants will promptly 
authorize the procedure. 
      
 
The hearing scheduled for October 8, 2024 is cancelled.

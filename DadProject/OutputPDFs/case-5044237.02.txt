The claimant filed a petition seeking alternate medical care on April 12, 
2024. A telephonic hearing on this petition was scheduled to occur on April 23, 
2024, at 10:30 AM. Prior to the alternate medical care hearing, counsel notified 
the undersigned that the parties were able to reach an amicable agreement and 
that a consent order should be entered. 
With respect to the specific alternate medical care requested, claimant 
sought authorization of a compound sav (GACK), which was recommended and 
prescribed by the authorized treating physician, Dr. Lisa Snyder. Defendants will 
authorize and pay for the prescribed compound sav (GACK), which will be 
available to claimant at her pharmacy, Essential Wellness Pharmacy, by April 
30, 2024. 
The parties desire that a consent order be entered in this case, in lieu of a 
hearing. After discussion between the parties and an agreement to provide the 
requested care, there is no justiciable issue for hearing and determination. Both 
parties agree that a consent order is appropriate, and a formal hearing is 
unnecessary to resolve the contested case dispute. 
This consent order is not intended to reflect the findings of fact or conclusions 
of law relative to this claim. Rather, it is intended to document and enter an order 
that is consistent with both parties' positions and acceptable to both parties. A 
formal alternate medical care hearing was not conducted, as all parties 
consented to the entry of this order and waived the formal hearing.

# Scene Parameters: little-comfort

**Scene context:** A hunched old man skulks through Little Comfort's most frequented crypt — *The Last Rest* — at the opening of a dark-fantasy beat. The vault is busy with the murmur and echo of regular patrons. He must pass through it to reach his own tomb-home in the cliffs.

## Locked Parameters

- **Senses (rolled 3): touch, taste, sound**
  - Rationale: Sight is the LLM-default for a vaulted, fire-lit crypt; forcing touch (his sparky-dry fingers, sere cloth), taste (cold air, stone-dust on the tongue), and sound (echoing patron voices, his own mumbled fragments) yields the scene's actual texture without leaning on the obvious chiaroscuro.

- **Rhythms (rolled 2): long-coordinated, periodic-with-interruptions**
  - Rationale: The source already runs long-coordinated and periodic. Allowing periodic-with-interruptions lets the patrons' echoed fragments break the long sentences exactly where they would be heard.

- **POV distance (rolled 1): close**
  - Rationale: The old man is taking pains to be inconspicuous; close POV puts the reader inside his vigilance and his shame, which a wider lens would flatten.

- **Time handling (rolled 1): anticipatory**
  - Rationale: He has done this walk every day of his adult life. The scene is shaped by what he expects to overhear, what he is bracing for, and the tomb he is heading toward — anticipation, not pure present-tense report.

- **Scene shape (rolled 1): parallel-thread**
  - Rationale: Two threads run side by side — the patrons' overheard chatter and echoes, and the old man's own mumbled clipped fragments — and the scene's shape is their parallel motion through the columned hall.

*The writing agent treats every parameter above as a hard contract.*

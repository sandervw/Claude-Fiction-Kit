## Scene 1 - The Cubicle Garden

Here are the identified issues:

**Typos/Errors:**
- "no little distinct" → "no little distinction"
- "sink to level" → "sink to the level"
- "stepped up to cubicle" → "stepped up to the cubicle"
- "the partion" → "the partition"
- "his his full height" → "his full height"
- "Your lumbar angles below" → "Your lumbar angles below" (missing verb, e.g., "falls below")
- "diviniation" → "divination"
- "heaved a melancholy a sigh" → "heaved a melancholy sigh"
- "summonable one more only" → "summonable once more only"
- "meagerest" → "meagerest" (acceptable archaic, but "merest" reads cleaner)
- "accquire" → "acquire"

**Prose Issues:**
- "muttered-oath of the verse" — unclear referent; "verse" seems like it should be something else (perhaps "the averse"? or a workplace term needing earlier establishment).
- "harridanic" — inventive but may read as trying too hard; "harridanesque" or a simpler description might land better.
- "with oxen movements" — effective but slightly awkward; "with bovine movements" is more idiomatic.
- The comma after "Mr. Rafe Scigley," creates a sentence fragment issue; remove it.

**Structural Note:**
- The internal monologue paragraph about Forever Worlds drops considerable exposition mid-scene. Consider weaving this information more gradually to maintain the con-man energy established around it.

## Scene 2 - The Word Misapplied

Here are the identified issues:

**Grammatical:**
- "it's employee" and "it's exposed tube" — should be "its" (possessive, no apostrophe) in both cases.
- "rafe thought" — "Rafe" should be capitalized.

**Logical Inconsistencies:**
- Rafe plans to "answer every question incorrectly" to be escorted out, yet he rehearses a competent-sounding answer ("perseverance"). This tension could be intentional irony, but it reads as unresolved — a single line clarifying he's practicing *wrong* answers would sharpen it.
- "The building is cooperating" contradicts the increasingly surreal, hostile spatial distortions (disappearing doors, resistant axes, rearranging directories). If Rafe is oblivious, this needs a touch more interiority to sell his misreading.

**Repeated Beats:**
- "Rafe rubbed his eyes" appears twice within three sentences. Cut or vary the second instance.

**Prose Polish:**
- "metamorphosis-of-hue" and "vending-machine-mustard" — the hyphenated compound coinages are effective individually but cluster densely in one paragraph, slightly diluting their impact.
- The simile "like the flush that suffuses skin with color" feels somewhat generic compared to the otherwise inventive imagery surrounding it. Consider something more specific to the corporate-uncanny tone.

**Overall:** Strong, distinctive voice. Issues are minor and mostly mechanical.

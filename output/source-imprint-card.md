# Source Imprint Card: Little Comfort's Last Rest

## Features

- **Sentence length:** Long periodic sentences (40-70 words) braided with short echoed dialogue fragments. Almost no mid-range sentences.
- **Clause stacking:** Heavy left-branching openers ("In the town of...", "As he skulked..."), then nested appositives separated by semicolons and colons. Periodic closure on the noun.
- **Register:** Mock-archaic, slightly mannered. Idiosyncratic spellings (chrypt, tohmb). Latinate diction (limned, sere, convivial, inconspicuous) sat against plain Anglo nouns (fish, grave, grass).
- **Sensory channels:** Auditory (echoes, mumbling) and tactile-dry (wringing fingers, sere clothes, sparky dryness). Visual is shadow and stone, not color.
- **Tics:** Triple-echoed dialogue ("fish... fish... fish..."). Semicolon-chained appositive lists. Possessive town names. Italics for proper-noun landmarks.

## Do

- Open with a place or posture clause before the subject.
- Chain appositives with semicolons to layer setting.
- Pair Latinate adjective with plain noun.
- Echo dialogue in fading triplets.

## Don't

- Use em-dashes (use commas, colons, parentheses).
- Write punchy mid-length sentences.
- Name colors. Stay in shadow, dust, stone.
- Use contractions in narration.

## Extracted Features

1. **Sentence-length distribution** — strongly long-skewed with periodic structure. The opening sentence runs ~45 words; the second paragraph contains a single ~70-word periodic sentence chained by semicolons. Short sentences appear only as quoted echo-fragments ("Bonefish... fish... fish...") and as a terse setup ("Even so the man took pains to be inconspicuous."). No middle-range filler.
2. **Clause stacking** — left-branching and periodic. Sentences delay the main subject behind stacked locative and qualifying phrases: *"In the town of Little Comfort's, within its most frequented chrypt, the voices of the regular patrons repeated themselves..."* The second paragraph's long sentence chains appositives with semicolons: *"Little Comfort, with its repurposed Tohmbs, it's headstone tents, all set under the shadows of high cliffs..."*
3. **Register and vocabulary** — archaic-Latinate with deliberate orthographic drift. Words like *"limned," "plied," "sere," "tohmb," "chrypt," "convivial house of marble"* mix high-register diction with eccentric spellings. No contractions in narration. The register feels older than the events.
4. **Dominant sensory channels** — sound and texture above sight. Repeated voices and trailing echoes carry the opening; tactile detail dominates the body (*"wringing his fingers to a dryness so sparky that it endangered his sere clothes"*). Light is referenced negatively (*"least-limned in the light of the long fire"*).
5. **Signature tics** — (a) ellipsis-trail repetition for echoed speech (*"fish... fish... fish..."*); (b) semicolons used as appositive chains rather than clause separators; (c) recursive naming of the place inside its own description (*"Little Comfort, with its... Little Comfort's Last Rest"*); (d) compound-coined modifiers (*"least-limned," "long fire," "harbor of the living"*).

## Do This

- Open with a long, left-branching periodic sentence that delays its subject behind locative phrases.
- Use semicolons to chain appositive descriptions of place within a single breath.
- Prefer archaic, Latinate, or coined vocabulary where a plain word would do.
- Render echoed or repeated speech with ellipsis-trail repetition of the final word.
- Lean on tactile and auditory detail; treat light as something a thing fails to receive rather than something that illuminates.

## Don't Do This

- Don't write middle-length sentences. Use either very long periodic constructions or terse declaratives.
- Don't use contractions in narration.
- Don't render the setting in modern, plain, or journalistic diction.
- Don't lead with sight as the primary sensory channel.
- Don't separate every clause with a period when a semicolon-chain would carry the rhythm.

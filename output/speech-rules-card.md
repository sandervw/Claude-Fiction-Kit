# Speech Card: The Hunched Old Man (Last Rest)

## Positive mode

- Issues commands without justification. He mutters imperatives to himself (keep on, mind the column, hush now), never explaining why.

## Voice fingerprint

- **Register:** Mock-archaic, low. Source-overridden from rolled `formal-modern` because the surrounding narration runs on chrypt, tohmb, sere, limned. Plain Anglo-Saxon nouns with the occasional archaic spelling.
- **Sentence-length cap:** Fragments only. 1 to 6 words. Source-overridden from rolled `25` because the passage states he mumbles clipped fragments of sentences. No complete sentences in his mouth.
- **Signature construction:** Self-address and trailing repetition. Source-overridden from rolled `always names the person addressing` because he speaks to no one. He drops a noun, then echoes it, mirroring the patrons' triple-echo.

## Default-banned patterns

- No clinical or curious tone.
- No "what does it feel like" questions.
- No detached observational commentary on himself.
- Fragment-as-style is permitted here (the rolled-and-overridden positive mode requires it).
